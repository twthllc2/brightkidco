## 1. Copy (Copywriting OS)

**Source:** `/root/.hermes/skills/copy/SKILL.md` (6,071 bytes)

### Identity & Workflow
- Role: copywriting knowledge base. ONLY job is writing — no research, strategy, or building.
- Workflow: Brief → Audience + Goal + Tone → Draft → Self-audit (AI-isms, clarity, CTA strength) → Polish → Signal

### 1.1 Email Copy Frameworks

**Welcome Series (7 emails):**
| # | Day | Subject | Body Angle |
|---|-----|---------|------------|
| 1 | 0 | "Welcome to [Brand], [Name]!" | Thank + set expectations + lead magnet |
| 2 | 1 | "Help us pick your favorites" | Preference quiz/collect data |
| 3 | 3 | "Your personalized picks" | Value based on preferences |
| 4 | 5 | "Here's a free gift" | Offer + incentive to purchase |
| 5 | 7 | "What our readers say" | Testimonials + social proof |
| 6 | 10 | "Behind the brand" | Brand story + mission |
| 7 | 14 | "Your first month" | Recap + loyalty invite |

**Cart Abandonment (3 emails):**
- 1h: "Still thinking it over?" — product reminder, no pressure
- 24h: "Selling fast" — social proof + free shipping offer
- 72h: "Last chance" — discount + urgency ("Your cart expires")

**Post-Purchase (6 emails):**
- D0: Order confirmation + tracking
- D2: Cross-sell "Complete the set"
- D7: Review request + incentive
- D14: Referral invite ("Give $10, get $10")
- D30: Re-order reminder + new arrivals

**Re-engagement (3 emails):**
- "We miss you" → "What's new" → "Last chance to stay subscribed" (D0, D3, D7)

### 1.2 Landing Page Copy

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: 8 words max, one clear value prop
- Subheadline: 15 words max, supports headline
- CTA: action-oriented ("Get My Free Guide" not "Submit"), high contrast
- Trust signal visible immediately (testimonial, stat, logo)

**Below the Fold:**
- 3-5 benefits with supporting proof
- How it works: 3-step visual
- FAQ: overcome top 5 objections
- Risk reversal: guarantee, free trial
- Final CTA: repeat + urgency (limited time, limited availability)

### 1.3 Ad Copy by Platform

**Facebook/Instagram:**
- Hook (1-2 lines): stat, question, pattern interrupt
- Body: Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
- CTA button: Shop Now / Learn More / Take Quiz

**Google Search Ads:**
- Headline 1 (30): keyword + value
- Headline 2 (30): USP
- Headline 3 (30): offer
- Description (90): key benefit + action

**TikTok Scripts (15-30s):**
- 0-2s: Hook with text overlay, bold font
- 2-5s: Relatable problem
- 5-15s: Product reveal and demonstration
- 15-25s: Social proof / results
- 25-30s: CTA with urgency
- Must have captions, shot on phone, trending audio

**LinkedIn (B2B):**
- Thought-leadership angle, lead with insight not product
- 50-100 words, generous line spacing
- Data or original research shared
- CTA: "Read report" / "Register for webinar"

### 1.4 Social Content Formats

**Instagram Captions:** Hook (first 1-2 lines) → Story/Value → CTA. 3-5 hashtags max. Line breaks between sections.

**LinkedIn Posts:** Hook question or contrarian take → Personal experience → Data/insight → CTA. 2-3 line breaks between paragraphs.

**Twitter/X Threads:** First tweet is the hook. Number each tweet (1/5, 2/5 etc.). Last tweet: CTA + recap.

### 1.5 Sales Copy Frameworks

**Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS):**
- Problem: "Struggling to get your emails opened?"
- Agitate: "Every unopened email is a lost sale..."
- Solve: "Here's the exact 3-step framework that boosted our client's open rates by 40%..."

**Before-After-Bridge (BAB):**
- Before: "You're spending hours on email campaigns with low returns."
- After: "Imagine a set-it-and-forget-it system that delivers 4x ROI on autopilot."
- Bridge: "Our done-for-you email setup does exactly that."

**Features-Advantages-Benefits (FAB):**
- Feature: "Automated welcome sequence"
- Advantage: "Sends 7 emails over 14 days without manual work"
- Benefit: "You make money while you sleep"

### 1.6 Subject Line Formulas
1. Curiosity: "[Number] ways to [benefit]"
2. Social proof: "Join [Number] others who..."
3. Urgency: "Last chance to [benefit]"
4. Personalization: "[Name], your [personalized thing] is ready"
5. How-to: "How to [desired outcome]"
6. Announcement: "Introducing [new product/feature]"
7. Question: "Struggling with [problem]?"
8. Stat: "[Number]% of [audience] [action]"

### 1.7 AI-ism Banned List
Never use: dive into, unlock, game-changer, in today's fast-paced, leveraging, revolutionize, transformative, empower, disrupt, best-in-class, cutting-edge, world-class, reimagine, supercharge, synergy, holistic, think outside the box, low-hanging fruit, dot-com bubble, ninja, guru, rockstar

### 1.8 Quality Gate
- No AI-isms from banned list
- Brand voice matches brief
- Subject line A/B testable (2+ variations)
- CTA specific and action-oriented (one per section)
- Length matches brief requirements
- Read aloud: natural flow?
- Would you click/CVC this if you received it?

## 2. Content (Content & SEO System)

**Source:** `/root/.hermes/skills/content/SKILL.md` (4,347 bytes)

### 2.1 Content Strategy — 4-Pillar System
- **TOFU (40%):** acquire via guides, listicles, trends. KPIs: traffic, signups.
- **MOFU (30%):** nurture via case studies, comparisons. KPIs: leads, downloads.
- **BOFU (20%):** convert via landing pages, pricing. KPIs: conversions, revenue.
- **Retention (10%):** loyalty via newsletters, exclusives. KPIs: retention, NPS.

### 2.2 Topic Clusters
- **Pillar** (1 per cluster): 3,000-5,000 words, broad coverage, links to all cluster pages
- **Cluster** (10-20 per pillar): 1,500-2,000 words, specific subtopics, links to pillar
- **Quarterly mix:** 1/3 new, 1/3 updates, 1/3 experiments

### 2.3 Keyword Research

**Intent Classification:**
| Intent | Volume | Conv Rate | Content Type |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Informational | High | 1-3% | Blog/guide |
| Commercial | Mid | 3-8% | Comparison/best-of |
| Transactional | Low | 8-15% | Product page |
| Navigational | N/A | 15%+ | Brand page |

**Priority Scoring:**
- Score = Volume × Intent Multiplier × (10 - Difficulty)
- Multipliers: Transactional=1.0, Commercial=0.7, Informational=0.3, Navigational=0.5
- Tier 1 (>100): Pillar. Tier 2 (50-100): Cluster. Tier 3 (<50): Existing content.

**Sources:**
- Primary: GSC, Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs/SEMRush, Google Trends
- Secondary: AlsoAsked, AnswerThePublic, Autocomplete, Reddit/Quora, competitor URLs

### 2.4 Production Pipeline

**Brief:** Target + secondary keywords, intent, audience, angle, competing URLs (top 3), format, CTAs, internal links (3+), external sources (2+).

**Outline:** H1 (60 chars, keyword) → Meta (150-160, keyword+CTA) → Intro (100-200 words, keyword in first 100) → H2 sections (3-5, each answers sub-question) → Conclusion (summary + CTA)

**Writing Standards:**
- Paragraphs 3-4 sentences
- Bullets 3-7 items
- H2 every 300-500 words
- Image every 500-700 words
- Active voice (90%)
- Second person
- Data cited with source+date

**Quality Pass:**
- Facts checked, AI-isms removed, fluff cut
- Read aloud test, Grammarly, Hemingway < Grade 10
- Better than top 3 results?
- Would you share this?

### 2.5 Technical SEO

**Crawlability:** robots.txt allowed, XML sitemap, canonical on all pages, noindex on correct only, pagination rel next/prev, zero orphan pages, GSC coverage clean.

**Core Web Vitals:**
- LCP < 2.5s (WebP, CDN, lazy load)
- FID < 100ms (code split, defer JS)
- CLS < 0.1 (explicit w/h, font-display:swap)

**On-Page:**
- Title (50-60): keyword near start, brand at end
- Meta (150-160): keyword + value + CTA
- 1 H1, H2s natural, images with alt + WebP + max 100KB
- 3+ internal links per page, descriptive anchor

**Structured Data:** Product (name, description, image, offers), Article (headline, datePublished, author), FAQ, HowTo, BreadcrumbList, Review, Event, VideoObject

### 2.6 AI Search (SGE) Optimization
- Direct answer in first 100 words
- Structured lists and comparison tables
- FAQ schema for Q&A
- Cite authoritative sources
- Clear headers mirroring question phrasing
- Entity clusters with interlinking

### 2.7 Link Building
- Guest posting (unique angle, relevant sites)
- Skyscraper (improve existing, pitch linkers)
- Broken link (find broken, offer replacement)
- Digital PR (data stories, HARO)
- Resource pages (pitch inclusion)
- Priority: .edu/.gov > industry pubs > DA 50+ > partners

### 2.8 Distribution
- Blog → Email, LinkedIn (teaser), Twitter (thread), Reddit/Quora, Medium (canonical)
- Video → YouTube, TikTok clips, IG Reels, embed in blog
- Lead magnets → Gate with email, promote in CTAs

### 2.9 KPIs
- Traffic: organic sessions, new/returning, top 5 pages
- Engagement: time on page, scroll depth, shares
- Conversions: goal completions, assisted conv, revenue
- SEO: rankings top 3/10/30, DA, backlinks (+/-), indexed pages, CWV, SGE/featured snippet presence

## 3. CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)

**Source:** `/root/.hermes/skills/cro/SKILL.md` (5,379 bytes)

### 3.1 The CRO Cycle
```
Research → Hypothesis → Prioritize → Test → Analyze → Implement → Repeat
```
- Research: heatmaps, recordings, surveys, analytics
- Hypothesis: problem + solution + expected lift + rationale
- Prioritize: ICE (Impact × Confidence × Ease)
- Test: A/B, multivariate, split URL
- Analyze: 95% significance, 14d minimum
- Implement: winner published, document learnings

### 3.2 ICE Scoring
- Impact (40%): how much lift if correct
- Confidence (35%): how sure are we
- Ease (25%): how hard to implement
- Score > 6: do it. Score < 4: skip.

### 3.3 A/B Testing Rules

**Protocol:**
- Pre-calculate sample size (EvanMiller calculator)
- Run minimum 14 days (captures day-of-week effects)
- 95% statistical significance (p < 0.05)
- Minimum 100 conversions per variant
- NEVER peek and stop early
- Bonferroni correction for 2+ variants
- Check segment-level results (mobile/desktop, new/returning)
- Winner must show both statistical AND business significance (5%+ relative lift)

**What to Test (Priority Order):**
1. Headline / value proposition
2. CTA (text, color, placement, size)
3. Hero image/video
4. Social proof placement
5. Form length (reduce fields by 50%)
6. Offer type (% off vs $ off vs free shipping vs free gift)
7. Pricing page structure (tiers, decoy, annual discount)
8. Checkout flow (guest vs account, 1-step vs multi-step)

### 3.4 Landing Page Optimization

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: 8 words max, one clear value proposition
- Subheadline: 15 words max, supports headline
- Hero image/video: product in use or benefit visualization
- Primary CTA: high contrast, action text ("Get My Free Guide")
- Trust signal visible: testimonial quote, logo bar, stat
- Form (if lead gen): 3-5 fields max, visible without scroll
- Load time: < 2s on 3G

**Below the Fold:**
- Benefits: 3-5 with supporting proof (stats, testimonials)
- How it works: 3-step visual (problem → solution → result)
- FAQ: overcome top 5 objections
- Risk reversal: guarantee, free trial, refund policy
- Final CTA: repeat primary + urgency element

### 3.5 Pricing Page Design — Three-Tier (Decoy Effect)
| | Basic | Popular★ | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price | $19/mo | $39/mo | $79/mo |
| Value | ⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ |
| Best for | Individuals | Teams | Enterprise |

Rules: Popular badge drives 70%+ selections. Annual discount 20% boosts LTV 40-60%. Feature comparison with checkmarks. 30-day money-back guarantee. CTA: "Start Free Trial" > "Buy Now".

### 3.6 Checkout Optimization
- Guest checkout: MANDATORY (30%+ uplift)
- Progress indicator: 3 steps max
- Remove navigation links (stay on checkout)
- Trust badges: SSL, payment icons, guarantee
- Address auto-complete
- Payment methods: card + PayPal + Apple Pay + Google Pay
- Mobile: thumb-accessible, auto-fill enabled
- Error messages: inline, specific
- Exit-intent popup: 5-10% recovery
- Order summary: always visible, itemized

### 3.7 Form Optimization
- Reduce fields by 50% (test: what's the minimum we truly need?)
- Single column > multi-column
- Inline validation > error at submit
- Placeholder text > labels above fields (or both)
- Auto-fill enabled for known fields
- Progress bar for multi-step
- CAPTCHA: reCAPTCHA v3 invisible > v2
- Submit button: action-oriented ("Get My Free Guide" > "Submit")
- Trust: privacy link, no-spam guarantee

### 3.8 Mobile Conversion
- Thumb zone: CTAs in bottom 1/3 of screen
- One column layout
- 44px minimum tap targets
- Font: 16px minimum (prevents zoom on input)
- Auto-fill enabled
- Payment: Apple Pay / Google Pay one-tap
- Load time: < 3s on 4G

### 3.9 Popup & Exit-Intent Strategy
- Timing: exit-intent (desktop), scroll 50% + 30s delay (mobile)
- Offer: 10-15% off or free shipping (loss leader, recoups on LTV)
- Copy: problem + solution + urgency
- Design: clean, single column, image + text, no more than 3 fields
- Mobile: full-screen overlay with close button
- Frequency: once per session, once per 30 days after capture

### 3.10 CRO KPI Targets
| Metric | Target | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Landing page CVR | > 3% | < 2%: rebuild |
| Checkout CVR | > 60% | < 40%: audit flow |
| Form completion | > 30% | < 15%: reduce fields |
| A/B test win rate | > 25% | < 15%: rethink hypotheses |
| Popup CVR | > 3% | < 1%: change offer |
| Mobile CVR | > 80% of desktop | < 60%: mobile audit |

## 4. Strategy (Consigliere — Strategic Advisory System)

**Source:** `/root/.hermes/skills/strategy/SKILL.md` (6,638 bytes)

### 4.1 The LLM Council (Mandatory — Every Brief)

All 5 modes must be run for every task brief:

**1a: Contrarian**
- Challenge every assumption. What's the flaw in the brief?
- What if the opposite is true? What's being overlooked?
- What would a skeptic say? What data would prove us wrong?

**1b: First Principles**
- Strip the brief to fundamentals
- What immutable truths can't be broken?
- What physics/laws/constraints are non-negotiable?
- What's the core problem stripped of all assumptions?

**1c: Expansionist**
- What adjacent possibilities exist? What's the 10x version?
- What would we do with infinite budget?
- What adjacent markets, channels, or products are worth exploring?

**1d: Outsider**
- How would someone from a completely different industry solve this? (Hospitality, military, gaming, healthcare, non-profit)
- What's the non-obvious angle?
- What would a newcomer with no industry bias do?

**1e: Executor**
- Given the above, what's the actual plan?
- Steps, resources, timeline, risk mitigation, success criteria, fallback plan
- Be specific — who does what by when

**1f: Synthesis**
- Merge all 5 modes into ONE clear, actionable recommendation
- If you disagree with the Executor in favor of a different approach, say so explicitly

### 4.2 Strategic Analysis Frameworks

**SWOT:**
- Strengths: internal, positive, controllable → exploit
- Weaknesses: internal, negative, fixable → fix or outsource
- Opportunities: external, positive, time-bound → prioritize by impact
- Threats: external, negative, existential → mitigate or monitor

**Porter's Five Forces:**
1. Rivalry (existing competitors): intensity, differentiation, switching costs
2. New entrants: barriers to entry (capital, brand, regulation, network effects)
3. Substitutes: alternative solutions, price-performance tradeoff
4. Supplier power: concentration, switching costs, forward integration threat
5. Buyer power: concentration, price sensitivity, backward integration threat

**Blue Ocean Strategy:**
- Eliminate: factors the industry takes for granted that you can remove
- Reduce: below industry standard
- Raise: above industry standard
- Create: factors the industry never offered
- Canvas: value curve plotting your offering vs competitors on all factors

### 4.3 Review Process

**Research Review Gate:**
- Every finding has a source? [REQUIRED]
- [VERIFIED] vs [INFERRED] labeled? [REQUIRED]
- Confidence levels stated? High/Medium/Low + rationale
- Research scope followed? (no rabbit holes)
- Competitors documented with specifics?
- Benchmarks include numeric values?
- At least 2 sources for key claims?

**Copy Review Gate:**
- No AI-isms from banned list?
- Brand voice matches brief?
- Subject lines A/B testable? (2+ variations)
- CTAs specific and action-oriented?
- Length matches brief?
- Read aloud: natural flow?
- Claims in copy flagged where verification needed?

**Ops Review Gate (build/deploy/configure):**
- 5-gate check: Segments, Flows, Dynamic Blocks, Tracking, Testing
- All expected files/paths exist?
- Verification evidence provided?
- No side effects outside scope?
- Platform-specific: API responses logged, errors handled?

**Final Sign-off:**
- Pass: deliver with briefest summary
- Pass with notes: fix minor items directly, note changes
- Flag: route back to specialist with specific feedback (one retry only)
- Fail: explain why, involve stakeholder if threshold disagreement

### 4.4 Strategy Domains

**Marketing Strategy Evaluation:**
- ICP defined? Demographics + psychographics + buying triggers
- Positioning defensible? Unique relative to competitors
- Channel mix balanced? Owned + earned + paid
- Funnel complete? Awareness through advocacy
- Unit economics positive? CAC < 33% LTV, payback < 12 months
- Growth loops identified? Viral, content, paid, sales

**GTM Plan Evaluation:**
- Phases defined with clear milestones?
- Resources (budget, people, time) allocated?
- Risks identified with mitigation plans?
- Success metrics falsifiable?
- Fallback plan if phase X fails?

### 4.5 Strategic Risk Assessment

**Risk Classification:**
| Type | Example | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|----------|------------|
| Market | Demand doesn't materialize | High | Validate with pre-sales, MVP |
| Competitive | Copycat enters market | Medium | First-mover advantage, defensibility |
| Execution | Can't deliver on time | Medium | Buffer in timeline, scope control |
| Financial | Run out of cash | High | Clear unit economics, runway visibility |
| Technical | Integration fails | Low | POC first, fallback plan |
| Regulatory | Compliance issue | Medium | Legal review, privacy by design |

**Decision Framework:**
- Likely (> 50%) AND severe (> $50k impact)? → Do not proceed
- Likely but low severity? → Mitigate then proceed
- Unlikely but severe? → Have contingency plan
- Unlikely and low severity? → Accept and monitor

## 5. Voice Delivery Protocol (Supporting)

**Source:** `/root/.hermes/skills/content/voice-delivery-protocol/SKILL.md` (1,472 bytes)

### Protocol for Sending Audio/Voice Messages
- The `[[audio_as_voice]]` directive is required for inline voice playback
- Without it, .ogg files are delivered as documents (unplayable inline) on both Telegram and Discord
- When using `text_to_speech` tool: use the entire `media_tag` value as-is (including the `[[audio_as_voice]]` prefix)
- When using `send_message` tool: prefix the MEDIA tag with the directive
- The gateway's `extract_media()` checks for `[[audio_as_voice]]` to set `is_voice=True`
- Verification: check gateway logs for `send_voice` (success) vs `send_document` (wrong path)
