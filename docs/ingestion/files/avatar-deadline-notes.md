## Deadline/Urgency Triggers (10 Distinct)

1. **Preschool enrollment requirement** — "My kid starts preschool next week and the daycare says he needs to be 90% daytime trained." Schools require full potty training before the first day. No exceptions, no pull-ups allowed.

2. **Pre-K move-up gate** — "When he turns three he'll have to be potty trained in order to move up to the preschool class at daycare." Daycare refuses to advance the child to the next class until potty trained.

3. **Tuition already paid** — Parents have already committed financially. Losing the spot means losing money AND childcare.

4. **Parent returning to work** — School start date is tied to a parent's return-to-work timeline. If the child can't attend, the parent can't work.

5. **Calendar countdown** — "Checking the calendar and counting down: 'six weeks left.'" Parents mentally track days until the deadline, creating escalating anxiety.

6. **September deadline** — "I need this done before September." The fall school start is the most common concrete deadline.

7. **Losing childcare spot** — "I have six weeks to get him fully potty trained or we lose our childcare / he can't go to pre-K." No backup plan exists.

8. **Younger kids moving ahead** — "Watching younger kids move to the next class." Social comparison with peers who are already trained creates shame and urgency.

9. **School policy emails** — "Children must be potty trained before starting." The official communication from schools is a cold trigger moment.

10. **Regression approaching deadline** — "We had a week of success and then it all fell apart." When progress reverses and the deadline is still approaching, panic escalates to crisis.

## Behavioral Patterns Unique to Deadline Segment

### Avoidance Behaviors
- Avoids talking to school about flexibility
- Avoids reading full policy documents (stress trigger)
- Avoids other parents who seem "ready"
- Avoids committing to return-to-work plans
- Avoids booking family trips before school starts
- Avoids checking accident frequency honestly
- **Pattern:** Avoiding confirmation that time is running out.
