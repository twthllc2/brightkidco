## Product Marketing Rules

### 1. Promotional Calendar Strategy (4 Peaks Theory)
Plan promotions around 4 peak moments per year. Don't scatter random sales — anchor to major shopping events (Black Friday, holidays, product launches). Know how often to run promotions, where they fit in the calendar, which customers they target, and what foundational strategies govern success.
- Source: `02-the-calendar-the-big-picture-strategy-for-your-promotions.md`

### 2. Offer Is the #1 Driver of Promotion Success
No factor matters more than the offer itself. Construct offers to maximize both AOV and Conversion Rate simultaneously. Build urgency into every offer. Collaborate with supply chain, finance, and operations to ensure promotions feature high-inventory, high-margin products for maximum cash flow.
- Source: `03-the-offer-the-engine-of-an-effective-promotion.md`

### 3. Cash Conversion Cycle Determines Growth Speed
Ecommerce is capital-intensive. The more money you have and the more efficiently you use it, the faster you grow. The traditional cash conversion cycle formula is inaccurate for retention-focused ecommerce — use the adjusted formula. Find your ideal CLV/CAC ratio to determine scaling speed.
- Source: `04-capital-and-cash-conversion-cycle.md`

### 4. Advertising Feeds Remarketing Audiences
Build audiences in advance with advertising, then drive purchases during promotions with clear, direct creative to expanded remarketing lists. This is especially powerful before Black Friday/Cyber Monday weekend. Pre-promotion ads build the audiences; during-promotion ads convert them.
- Source: `04-the-ads-maximizing-impact-with-paid-promotion.md`

### 5. Subscription Model Suitability Criteria
Three key factors determine if a product suits subscriptions: (1) consumption rate predictability, (2) replenishment need, (3) convenience value. Not every D2C product should have a subscription — evaluate product fit before implementing.
- Source: `05-implement-successful-subscription-models-in-ecommerce-business.md`

## Conversion Patterns

### 6. Email & SMS = Highest Margin Channel
For ecommerce, no tool beats email & SMS for maximizing promotion profitability. Owned audiences are nearly free to reach, maintaining maximal contribution margin on purchases driven. This is the #1 key to high revenue AND high profit — not just revenue.
- Source: `05-the-emails-and-texts-your-main-source-of-profit.md`

### 7. SMS List Building Pre-Promotion
Collect SMS opt-ins during the week leading up to a promotion to build the SMS list, then reduce SMS volume during the promotion itself to maximize customer awareness and drive sales. Counter-intuitive but effective: build the list, then use it strategically.
- Source: `05-the-emails-and-texts-your-main-source-of-profit.md`

### 8. Email Frequency & Segmentation During Sales
Know how many emails to send during a sale, when to send them, and to which segments. Goal: drive maximum sales without harming deliverability. Use plain text emails at crucial moments to maximize deliverability when it matters most.
- Source: `05-the-emails-and-texts-your-main-source-of-profit.md`

### 9. Personalized vs Evergreen Value Approach
Two outreach workflows for conversion: (1) Personalized value approach — create custom audit/loom videos for high-value prospects. Higher conversion rates due to reciprocity bias, better pain point discovery, easier competitor differentiation. (2) Evergreen approach — use pre-recorded educational videos to scale. Lower conversion but frees time. Start personalized, graduate to evergreen after reps.
- Source: `1-evergreen-vs-personalized-value-approach-workflow.md`

### 10. Micro-Commitments Drive Funnel Conversion
Get prospects to micro-commit by saying "Yes" to receiving a video or giving their email address. Micro-commitments make conversion rates high at every funnel step. Never spam prospects with value they haven't accepted — zero results guaranteed.
- Source: `1-evergreen-vs-personalized-value-approach-workflow.md`

### 11. Content as Heavy Lifting in Sales Process
Use content to do the heavy lifting before sales calls. Every piece of content should: have a CTA, be directed to one person (use "you"), be structured as a story, and occasionally polarize against competitors. This separates you from 99.99% of competition. Increases: message-to-warm-lead rate, warm-lead-to-booked-call rate, closing rate. Decreases: sales process length.
- Source: `3-content-marketing-why-is-it-important.md`

## Retention Strategies

### 12. Repeat Customer Flow — Thank & Reward
When a customer places a second order, immediately acknowledge and thank them. Draw their attention with a thank-you gift (e.g., 20% discount code valid for 48 hours). This is the Repeat Customer Flow — a sequence of emails sent to customers placing their second order.
- Source: `006-repeat-customer-flow.md`

### 13. Email Marketing Platform Selection
Recommended platforms ranked: GoHighLevel (top pick), ActiveCampaign (second), then MailChimp, Benchmark, Mailerlite, GetResponse, HubSpot, SendinBlue. Use testing tools: mailgenius.com, coschedule subject line tester, mail-tester.com, mailmeteor spam checker.
- Source: `2-tools-automation-1.md`, `21-tools-automation-1.md`

### 14. Mastering Email Marketing Fundamentals
Three pillars: (1) Choose the right email marketing platform for your brand, (2) Write the best subject headers to win attention and increase open rates, (3) Create simple content that looks amazing and packs a punch. Klaviyo recommended as the tool of choice.
- Source: `06-mastering-email-marketing.md`

### 15. Content Compounds Exponentially (UOV Model)
Content value compounds: each piece of content multiplies your channel's value. Miss an upload and your value drops back to what it was TWO uploads ago. Inconsistency is punished at 2x the rate of consistency. This applies to email cadence too — consistent sends build deliverability; gaps destroy it faster than you built it.
- Source: `1-content-throughput.md`

## Checkout Optimization & Offer Construction

### 16. Offer Construction Worksheet (7-Step Framework)
Step-by-step offer building: (1) Niche — define needs, wants, desires, transformation. (2) Outcome — the big promise, tangible/measurable. (3) Timeframe — realistic, with compression tactics. (4) Methodology — 6-9 word steps, understandable by a 10-year-old. (5) Model — DFY/DWY/DIY. (6) Risk Reversal — guarantee the outcome. (7) Summary — combine into one compelling offer statement.
- Source: `16-imperium-academy-offer-construction-worksheet.md`

### 17. Risk Reversal / Guarantee Positioning
Position guarantees creatively: "guaranteed," "pay on results," "paid on performance," "you don't pay," "money back," "on the house," "we pick up the bill," "no win no fee," "charged on delivery," "outcome-based payment." The spin matters more than the mechanism.
- Source: `16-imperium-academy-offer-construction-worksheet.md`

### 18. Offer Formula Examples
Pattern: "[Niche] + [Desired Outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Risk Reversal] + [Methodology]." Examples: "Overcome binge eating in 90 days or you don't pay using our 5-step program." "100 qualified appointments in 30 days on pure pay-per-qualified-appointment basis."
- Source: `16-imperium-academy-offer-construction-worksheet.md`

## Copywriting & Content Strategy

### 19. Copywriting Content Structure (6 Parts)
(1) Headline — speaks to audience. (2) Problems/Biases/Limiting Beliefs/Dreams. (3) Solution — how to solve the problems. (4) Tangible Results/Proof/Success Stories. (5) Conclusion — 1-3 sentence summary. (6) Sign off — ending message or direct CTA.
- Source: `4-copywriting-content-creation-1.md`

### 20. Content Creation Rules
Write to one single person (your avatar). Keep it simple — talk as if to a child. Use a timer (10-15 min max per piece). Read copy out loud to check flow. Plan the next 10 posts before starting. Remove all distractions. Stay positive — market reads your post at the energy level it was created. Be genuine — no copy-paste.
- Source: `4-copywriting-content-creation-1.md`

### 21. SEO Copywriting for Product Pages
Optimize content and web copy for target keywords. Reverse-engineer the SERP to determine what should be on your page. Master foundational on-page basics required to optimize any page. Use tools: Surfer, Moz extension, Ahrefs Extension, Detailed SEO Extension.
- Source: `11-writing-copy-that-ranks.md`

### 22. Content Calendar Discipline
Daily rules: 50-80 new Facebook friends, max Instagram follows, 50-100 LinkedIn connections, DM everyone who engages, add creative to posts (selfies, videos), redistribute content across all channels, use niche-relevant tags. Give maximum value, don't ask for too much upfront. Consistency beats talent. Don't be a perfectionist.
- Source: `31-the-content-calendar.md`

Methods: post in niche Facebook groups asking for feedback (not "check out my channel"), DM people in your niche for feedback, email list announcement, influencer shoutouts, tell clients.
