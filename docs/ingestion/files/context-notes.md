### B1. BRAND VOICE FOUNDATION

**BrightKidCo IS:**
- A brand founded by Mary S. based on her own journey
- Led by Lena (Customer Support, Mom of two autistic sons) — first human touchpoint
- Clinically informed, but emotionally raw
- Knowledge-based, not hope-based

**BrightKidCo IS NOT:**
- A "wellness brand" with positive vibes
- An "expert brand" that knows everything
- A "miracle product brand"
- Polished, perfect, or predictable

**THE VOICE sounds like:**
- An OT who is also a mom of an autistic child
- A friend who went through the same thing
- An experienced caregiver with two sons on the spectrum

**Lena's Profile — Always the Same:**
- Name: Lena S.
- Role: Customer Support
- Profile subtitle: "Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons"

1. Observation of reality (specific scenario)
2. Validation without drama
3. Mechanism or information
4. Permission / soft action

- 4+ paragraphs of sign-off text

**ALLOWED:**
- "Lena S."
- "Mom of two autistic sons"

### B3. FORBIDDEN PHRASES (Universal — NEVER USE)

- "Easy" / "Simple" / "Effortless"
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Finally solved everything"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- "Suffering from autism" (autism = identity, not disease)
- "High-functioning" / "Low-functioning" (controversial)
- "Wait until they're ready" (trigger — she's heard it 100× and hates it)
- "Just try harder"
- "Be more consistent"
- "Your child should..."
- "Most children at this age..."
- "Have you tried [obvious method]" — she's tried everything
- "Don't worry" — minimizes real concerns
- "We understand" without substance
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix" — autism isn't to be fixed
- "Your child will be normal"
- "Get him trained!" — pressure language

**Level-Specific Forbidden Phrases exist for Level 1, 2, 3, and General Fallback** (see full guide for complete lists).

---

### B4. SENSITIVE AREAS (Handle with Extra Care)

1. **SELF-BLAME** — She's on a guilt seesaw. NEVER hint she's doing something wrong. Always absolve, never accuse.
2. **PARTNER CONFLICT** — Don't sound like she has to convince her partner.
3. **COMPARISON WOUND** — Playground comparisons with neurotypical peers are triggers. NEVER position neurotypical kids as standard.
4. **PEDIATRICIAN FRUSTRATION** — Gently acknowledge, never attack the entire medical profession.
5. **FINANCIAL HARDSHIP** — ~25% use Medicaid. $79.99 is real commitment. Math reframe OK, never preachy.
6. **CHILD'S IDENTITY** — NEVER "your son will be normal" — autism = identity, not disease. "Help his nervous system" > "fix his behavior".

### B5. AVATAR DEFINITIONS (4 Levels)

**LEVEL 1 (~35% of audience): "Talks about potty but won't actually do it"**
- Child: Verbal, 3-5 years
- Failed attempts: 1-3
- Hope: Intact, Skepticism: Low
- Tonality: 70% Hope / 30% Realism
- Decision period: ~2-7 days
- CTA aggressiveness: Medium
- Urgency allowed: High (Pre-K driven)

**LEVEL 2 (~40%, LARGEST): "Sensory issues, tried many methods"**
- Child: Mixed verbal, 5-7 years
- Failed attempts: 3-5+
- Hope: Cracks, Skepticism: Very high
- Tonality: 40% Hope / 60% Realism
- Decision period: ~7-21 days
- CTA aggressiveness: Low
- Urgency allowed: Little

**LEVEL 3 (~25%): "Non-verbal or high support needs"**
- Child: Non-verbal or minimal verbal, 6-10+ years
- Failed attempts: 5+ years
- Hope: Accepted resignation, Skepticism: Deep, calm
- Tonality: 20% Hope / 80% Realism
- Decision period: ~14-30 days
- CTA aggressiveness: Very low
- Urgency allowed: NEVER

**GENERAL FALLBACK (~50% real volume): "Still figuring out what's going on"**
- Cross-level: diagnostic entrants, label rejecters, sensory-different-non-ASD, overwhelmed
- Tonality: 50% Hope / 50% Realism
- Decision period: Variable

### B6. THE 5 BIG CONVERSION LEVERS

1. **"It's not the method. It's a sensory gap."** — D1+D2 absolution. Shift from "behavior issue" to "neurological issue."
2. **"Pull-ups make this worse"** — Reframe pull-ups as actively damaging. Creates urgency WITHOUT manipulation.
3. **"Try it for 60 days. By your judgment."** — Reduces hope-risk. "You decide" framing.
4. **"I have two autistic sons" (Lena's Voice)** — Brand becomes person. Trust +200%.
5. **"49% of autistic 4-5 year olds are not toilet trained"** — Normalizes. Removes self-blame. SPARK research credibility.
