## Contradictions Log

### Contradiction 1: ABA Success Rate
- **Claim A (DOC1):** "His BCBA has tried everything in her training. Nothing has worked."
- **Claim B (Autistic-Parent-full.txt):** "My son is apparently doing great at ABA and is basically potty trained there"
- **Resolution:** Not a true contradiction — ABA sometimes succeeds at clinic but fails at home transfer. Both are true simultaneously. The gap is TRANSFER, not ABA effectiveness per se.
- **Impact on messaging:** Emails must acknowledge ABA CAN work (clinic context) while addressing why it often doesn't transfer home (sensory environment change).

### Contradiction 2: BCBA Loyalty Intensity
- **Claim A (DOC1):** Objection B2 intensity is "LOW-MEDIUM" overall but "HIGH for Level 2-3"
- **Claim B (Round 01 analysis):** "BCBA Loyalty is the single biggest conversion gate for Level 2 and Level 3"
- **Resolution:** The LOW-MEDIUM rating is cross-level average. For the 65% of audience that IS Level 2-3, it's HIGH. The averaged rating may understate the obstacle for target segments.
- **Impact on messaging:** Don't treat BCBA objection as moderate — for the majority of paying customers, it's the #1 gate.

### Contradiction 3: ABA Controversy Awareness
- **Claim A (DOC3):** "Complement, not replace" — assume parent is pro-ABA
- **Claim B (Autistic-Parent-full.txt):** "We're still on the fence about whether ABA is right for her" + "I know there is some controversy around ABA"
- **Resolution:** A minority of parents are skeptical of ABA itself, not just its effectiveness for toileting. The "complement" framing assumes a pro-ABA parent.
- **Impact on messaging:** Need a secondary messaging track for ABA-skeptical parents who still need sensory solutions. These parents may respond BETTER to direct sensory framing without the "alongside your BCBA" language.

## Research Recommendations (Prioritized)

### Priority 1: BCBA Professional Validation (HIGH impact, MEDIUM effort)
**Question:** Do BCBAs actually endorse the "behavioral + sensory complement" framing?
**Method:** Survey 10-20 BCBAs who work with autism toileting; show them the fact-sheet concept and "complement" positioning
**Impact if confirmed:** Validates the entire BCBA messaging strategy; enables "BCBA-approved" claim
**Impact if NOT confirmed:** Requires fundamental repositioning — may need to go around BCBA rather than through them

### Priority 2: ABA Clinic-to-Home Transfer Research (HIGH impact, LOW effort)
**Question:** Why does ABA toileting success at clinic fail to transfer home?
**Method:** Literature search on generalization/skill transfer in ABA; parent interview subset
**Impact if resolved:** Strengthens the "sensory gap" argument with clinical backing; provides specific home-environment messaging angles
**Impact if NOT resolved:** Current "clinic works, home doesn't" claim is anecdotal (2 quotes)

### Priority 3: BCBA Fact-Sheet Effectiveness (MEDIUM impact, LOW effort)
**Question:** Does the BCBA fact-sheet actually convert? Has it been tested?
**Method:** Check if fact-sheet exists; if yes, review conversion data. If no, draft and A/B test.
**Impact if confirmed:** Highest-leverage conversion tool for 65% of audience
**Impact if NOT resolved:** Strategy is theoretical, may waste email real estate

### Priority 4: ABA-Skeptical Parent Sub-Segment (MEDIUM impact, MEDIUM effort)
**Question:** What % of Level 2-3 parents are ABA-skeptical vs ABA-loyal?
**Method:** Survey or community analysis; categorize quotes from Autistic-Parent-full.txt by sentiment
**Impact if confirmed:** Enables two-track BCBA messaging (pro-ABA complement + ABA-skeptical direct)
**Impact if NOT resolved:** One-size-fits-all BCBA messaging may alienate the skeptical minority

### Priority 5: Waitlist Family Dynamics (LOW impact, LOW effort)
**Question:** Are ABA-waitlist families a distinct sub-segment worth targeting separately?
**Method:** Count waitlist mentions across all source files; assess purchasing behavior differences
**Impact if confirmed:** New sub-segment with different messaging needs (no BCBA relationship to navigate)
**Impact if NOT resolved:** Minor — these families still benefit from standard sensory-gap messaging

## Summary

**Strongest area:** The "complement, not competitor" positioning is exhaustively documented with verbatim quotes, forbidden phrases, and specific language patterns. Client files provide a complete tactical playbook for BCBA-respectful messaging.

**Weakest area:** Zero validation from BCBA professionals themselves. All evidence is parent-side. The entire strategy assumes BCBAs will accept the "behavioral + sensory complement" framing, but no BCBA has been quoted endorsing it. This is the single biggest strategic risk.

**Three gaps that matter most for email execution:**
1. Will BCBAs endorse the complement framing? (CRITICAL)
2. Why does ABA toileting fail to transfer home? (HIGH)
3. Does the BCBA fact-sheet actually convert? (HIGH)

**Recommended next step:** Priority 1 research (BCBA professional validation) should be completed before writing Level 2 email sequences. If BCBAs reject the framing, the entire BCBA Loyalty layer needs rearchitecting.
