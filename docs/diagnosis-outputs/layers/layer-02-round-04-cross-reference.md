# Layer 2 (The Customer) Round 4 — Cross-Reference Matrix

**Generated:** May 24, 2026
**Source Data:** Layer 2 R1 (Client Files), Layer 2 R2 (Kommentare Mining), DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION
**Cross-Reference Targets:** Layer 3 (Emotional Phases), Layer 4 (Avatar Levels), Layer 5 (Emotional Dimensions), Layer 6 (Symptom Clusters), Layer 7 (Conversion Probabilities)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Layer 2 identified **4 customer avatars** (Autistic Parent, Deadline Parent, Social Pressure Parent, Overwhelmed At-Home Mom) with distinct demographics, emotional profiles, comorbidities, and behavioral patterns. This document maps each customer data point against Layers 3-7 to create a complete cross-reference matrix.

**Key Finding:** The 4 customer avatars from Layer 2 are NOT 1:1 with the 4 Avatar Levels from Layer 4. Instead, each avatar represents a PSYCHOLOGICAL PRESSURE that can coexist with any avatar level. An Autistic Parent at Level 2 who is also a Deadline Pressure mom is the highest-conversion segment. The emotional dimensions (Layer 5) are the bridge that connects customer avatars to avatar levels.

## 1. CUSTOMER AVATARS → LAYER 4 (4 AVATAR LEVELS)

The 4 customer avatars map differently onto the 4 avatar levels depending on the child's severity and the mother's journey stage.

### Primary Mapping: Avatar × Level

| Customer Avatar | Primary Level(s) | Rationale |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Autistic Parent** | Level 2-3 (dominant) / Level 1 (early stage) | Child has formal ASD diagnosis; sensory and interoception challenges are central. Level 2 (40%) if mixed-verbal with method cycling; Level 3 (25%) if non-verbal with years of attempts. Early-stage Autistic Parents with younger verbal children may be Level 1. |
| **Deadline Parent** | Level 1 (dominant) / Level 2 (if method-cycling) | School deadline is the master driver. Level 1 (35%) fits the Pre-K deadline + first Oh Crap failure profile. | **Overwhelmed At-Home Mom** | Cross-level (all levels) | Overwhelm is universal — it cuts across ALL levels. A Level 1 mom with high accidents can be overwhelmed. A Level 3 mom with years of daily mess is overwhelmed. This is the most universal avatar. |

## 2. CUSTOMER EMOTIONS → LAYER 3 (4 EMOTIONAL PHASES)

Each customer avatar has a dominant emotional phase they occupy, but they can cycle between phases.

### Phase × Avatar Dominance

| Emotional Phase | Autistic Parent | Deadline Parent | Social Pressure Parent | Overwhelmed Mom |

| **Phase 1: Slow Realization** | Rare — usually past this by time of ASD diagnosis | Common initial state — "he's just slow" before deadline crystallizes | Strong entry — first comparison pain, "should I be worried?" | Rare — overwhelm doesn't exist at this stage |
| **Phase 2: First Real Attempt** | Dominant for Level 1 Autistic Parent | PEAK — this is the Deadline Parent's defining phase | Strong — her "first attempt" IS comparing herself | Common — this is when she discovers how bad accidents can be |
| **Phase 3: Method Cycling** | PEAK for Level 2 Autistic Parent | Present — if deadline was missed, enters method cycling | Strong — has tried social stories, gentle methods, rewards | PEAK — has tried everything, nothing sticks |
| **Phase 4: Resignation Fork** | PEAK for Level 3 Autistic Parent | Rare — deadlines create urgency, not resignation | Present — deep shame can lead to giving up | Common — survival mode IS resignation |

## 7. HIGHEST CONVERSION INSIGHTS

### The Top-Converting Customer Profiles

**#1: Deadline Parent × Level 1 × Phase 2**
- Conversion probability: **15-25%**
- Why: Hope intact + Pre-K deadline + first Oh Crap failure = maximum emotional readiness
- Best levers: L1 (reframe) + L3 (60-day) + deadline urgency framing
- Key objection: D2 "Maybe I'm the problem" (Phase 2 peak)
- Email: Welcome E2-E4 with "Oh Crap didn't fail your kid — it failed your kid's nervous system"
- Decision window: 3-14 days (act fast, this phase is short)

**#2: Autistic Parent × Level 2 × Phase 3**
- Conversion probability: **8-15%**
- Why: Largest audience (~40%), mechanism-convertible, hyper-motivated + hyper-skeptical
- Best levers: L1 (deep reframe with named methods) + L3 (60-day) + L5 (49% stat)
- Key objection: D1 "I've tried everything" (Phase 3 peak)
- Email: Science-first (Kelly Mahler, SPARK), BCBA-respectful, 7-21 day nurture
- Key risk: Promise nothing, demonstrate mechanism

**#3: Social Pressure Parent × Level 1-2 × Phase 2-3**
- Conversion probability: **10-18%**
- Why: Shame-driven search for identity restoration; mechanism + validation combo works
- Best levers: L4 (Lena's voice) + L5 (normalization) + social proof
- Key objection: C2 "Show me kids LIKE MINE"
- Email: Peer-to-peer voice + "You're not alone" + specific testimonials

**#4: Overwhelmed Mom × Cross-level × Phase 3-4**
- Conversion probability: **4-10%** (but highest LTV potential)
- Why: Purchases for relief, not hope; high loyalty once converted
- Best levers: L2 (pull-ups make this worse) + L3 (60-day) + practical relief
- Key objection: C3 "Is this worth $30-50 when I've wasted so much?"
- Email: Practical relief framing — "The next accident doesn't have to mean cleaning the couch"

### The Triple-Dimension Prospect

The highest-value prospect in the entire ecosystem is a mom who experiences ALL THREE emotional dimensions simultaneously:

- **Deadline Pressure** — "School starts in 3 weeks"
- **Social Pressure** — "Everyone else's kid is trained"
- **Overwhelmed** — "I can't clean one more car seat"

This is the **Breaking Point Mom**. She has the highest conversion probability (potentially 20%+ if all three align), but she is also the **most fragile** — one wrong word and she unsubscribes forever.

**Email approach for this segment:** Maximum empathy, minimum pressure. Lena's voice. Mechanism specificity. 60-day guarantee. Single dimension per email — never combine deadline pressure with social pressure shame in the same email. Lead with her dominant dimension, acknowledge the others gently.
