## Finding 1: Authority Positioning in Copywriting — The BCBA as "Authority Source"

**Source:** Cashvertising, Chapter 4 Hot Lists + Index (lines 69, 131, 144-147)

The Cashvertising wiki notes reference Drew Eric Whitman's framework on authority positioning (p144-147) and the psychology of influence (Cialdini's 6 weapons, p57-63). Key principles relevant to BCBA Loyalty:

- **Authority positioning** (Cashvertising p144-147): People defer to perceived experts. The BCBA is the "authority source" in the Level 2 mom's mental model — the person with credentials, training, and years of behavioral data on her specific child.
- **Headline authority** (Chapter 4, line 131): "Is it authoritative, and not wimpy?" — Whitman emphasizes that authority must be communicated confidently, not apologetically. This maps to BCBA positioning: the BCBA speaks with clinical authority ("We've maxed out behavioral interventions"), which carries enormous weight.
- **Social proof psychology** (Index, p126): Social proof is particularly powerful when combined with authority. BCBA recommendations carry both: the BCBA is an authority figure AND represents years of "clinical social proof" (other families, other protocols, institutional backing).
- **6 Weapons of Influence** (Index, p57-63): Cialdini's framework — authority, social proof, reciprocation, commitment/consistency, liking, scarcity. BCBA Loyalty activates at least 3: authority (credentials), commitment/consistency (years of ABA investment), and social proof (other families trust BCBA).

**Connection:** The Cashvertising authority positioning framework explains WHY the BCBA objection is so powerful. It's not just about clinical effectiveness — it's about psychological deference to authority. The BCBA carries Cialdini's "authority" weapon (credentials, clinical language, institutional backing) plus "commitment/consistency" (the mom has invested years and thousands of dollars in ABA). Overcoming this requires NOT opposing the authority but repositioning the product as complementary — exactly what DOC1 recommends.

## Finding 2: Market Sophistication — BCBA-Experienced Parents at Highest Awareness

**Source:** Breakthrough Advertising, Chapters 2-3 (Eugene Schwartz framework)

Eugene Schwartz's market sophistication framework (5 stages) and prospect awareness states (5 levels) provide a precise lens for understanding BCBA Loyalty:

**Market Sophistication Stages:**
1. First-to-market (be direct)
2. Second entrant (be better)
3. Crowded market (find a new mechanism)
4. Skeptical market (reframe the problem)
5. Exhausted market (name the claim differently)

BCBA-experienced Level 2 moms are at **Stage 4-5**. They've seen every potty training approach — ABA protocols, sticker charts, social stories, timer schedules, naked method, Oh Crap. They are deeply skeptical of ANY new claim.

**Prospect Awareness States:**
1. Most aware (knows product, ready to buy)
2. Product aware (knows product, not convinced)
3. Solution aware (knows solutions exist, not your specific one)
4. Problem aware (feels the problem, doesn't know solutions)
5. Unaware (doesn't know the problem exists)

BCBA-loyal Level 2 moms are **Product Aware to Solution Aware**. They know ABA exists. They know OT exists. They know interoception is a concept (if their OT mentioned it). But they do NOT know that a sensory feedback mechanism (Body-Signal Learning Layer) can complement their BCBA program. They are solution-aware for behavioral approaches but unaware for sensory-specific solutions.

### Cross-Reference with DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt

DOC1's Phase 3 (Method Cycling) description confirms this:
- "Default mode: SKEPTICISM" (line 311)
- "'I've tried everything' is a trauma response, not just a statement" (line 313)
- "But: ready for solutions with SPECIFIC mechanism" (line 314)
- "No longer reacts to 'general parenting advice'" (line 315)
- "Reacts to: science (especially Kelly Mahler), mechanism specificity, peer-to-peer voice, brand founder authenticity" (line 316-317)

DOC1's Lever 1 ("It's not the method. It's a sensory gap.") is a Schwartz Stage 4 strategy — reframe the problem. Instead of competing with ABA (Stage 3 approach), it reframes: ABA solved the behavioral component, but the sensory component remains. This is exactly the "redefinition" technique Schwartz describes.

**Gap:** The wiki contains only the Breakthrough Advertising chapter indexes, not the full content. The Schwartz framework is referenced but the actual 5-stage detail and the redefinition/mechanization techniques are not fully documented in wiki notes. The framework must be applied from general copywriting knowledge rather than wiki-specific content.
