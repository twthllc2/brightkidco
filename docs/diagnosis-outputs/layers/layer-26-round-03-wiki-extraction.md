## 1. MEDICAID SENSITIVITY BY AVATAR LEVEL

### Source: overnight-plan.md (Layer 26, lines 463-476)

Medicaid usage varies dramatically by level and directly determines what financial messaging is appropriate:

| Level | Medicaid Usage | Financial Sensitivity | Math Reframe Rule |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Level 1 (Verbal, 3-5yr) | 10-15% | Low — most have disposable income | Math reframes work well |
| Level 2 (Mixed, 5-7yr) | 25% | Moderate — sensitivity present | Use carefully, avoid preachy tone |
| Level 3 (Non-verbal, 6-10+) | 40% (50% use Medicaid Waiver) | Extremely high — diapers often FREE through insurance | NEVER use math reframes |

**Critical rule:** Telling a Level 3 mom she is "wasting money" on diapers she gets for free through Medicaid is offensive and tone-deaf. For Level 3, the framing is "add alongside existing diapers" — not "replace your pull-ups." The product is positioned as a daytime trial supplement, not a replacement for specialty diapers.

## 4. CONVERSION BARRIERS RELATED TO FINANCIAL SENSITIVITY

### Source: layer-07-round-01-client-files.md (Section 9, lines 200-224)

**Level 2 barriers (25% Medicaid):**
- Hyper-skepticism from 3-5+ failed methods
- "I've tried everything" trauma response
- Financial sensitivity — BCBA context means ongoing therapy costs
- What works: Science-first, mechanism specificity, "Try-and-judge" framing
- What kills conversion: Hard sell, easy/quick language

**Level 3 barriers (40% Medicaid):**
- Deep resignation, "he's too old/severe"
- Medicaid reality (price sensitivity) — diapers often free through insurance
- Years of failed attempts, $500-$2,000 wasted
- What works: Dignity-first, late-trainer stories, "Late is not never"
- What kills conversion: Urgency manipulation, price pressure, any "fix" framing

**Objection C3 (all levels):** "Is this worth $30-50 when I've wasted so much?" — Counter: Math reframe (carefully), 60-day refund, what happens if you DON'T invest.

## 5. UNIT ECONOMICS AND PRICING PSYCHOLOGY FROM WIKI

### Source: wiki/about-me/psychology/02-transcript-unit-economics-for-ecommerce-improving-primary-component-1_-pricin.md

Key principles applicable to BrightKidCo's Medicaid-sensitive audience:

1. **Pricing power question:** Do customers perceive the value they receive to be greater than the cost? For Medicaid families receiving free diapers, the answer is NO unless the value proposition is reframed as supplementing (not replacing) existing coverage.

2. **Price elasticity:** The theoretical break point where a customer no longer feels value and price are in positive relation. For Level 3 Medicaid families, this break point is extremely low — the product must be positioned as an experiment, not a replacement.

3. **Promotional discount risk:** Running promotions increases the risk that customers permanently associate discounts with products. For BrightKidCo, the 60-day guarantee serves as the primary risk reducer rather than discounting.

4. **Bundling as disguised discounting:** The 3+3 and 5+5 bundles work by increasing perceived value without reducing per-unit price — critical for maintaining brand positioning while serving price-sensitive segments.

### Source: wiki/skills-tools/__enqueued__/price-anchoring-and-bracketing.md

Stub page — referenced from wiki as a concept. Price anchoring (showing $34.99 per pair before showing $79.99 for 6) creates a favorable comparison. The bracketing strategy (1-pair / 3+3 / 5+5) lets each level self-select into their comfort zone.

### Source: wiki/tenza-marketing/_hub-operations/tenza-pop-ups--31-direct-financial-incentives.md

- Free shipping removes friction without devaluing the product
- Physical gifts have higher perceived value than cash discounts
- Price integrity matters — avoid "discount forward" positioning

## 7. MESSAGING RULES FOR MEDICAID-SENSITIVE SEGMENTS

### What to DO:
- Position as "add alongside existing diapers" for Level 3
- Use "just trying it" framing with 1-pair offer ($34.99)
- Lead with dignity-first language
- Feature late-trainer stories (Maureen Bennie's son at 9.5)
- Use 60-day guarantee as primary risk reducer
- Acknowledge that families may already have coverage for diapers

### What NEVER to do:
- Never use math reframes for Level 3 ("you're wasting $876/year on pull-ups")
- Never position as "replacement" for specialty diapers
- Never use urgency manipulation or price pressure
- Never imply the parent is making bad financial decisions
- Never use "easy" or "quick" language
- Never suggest Medicaid-funded diapers are inferior
