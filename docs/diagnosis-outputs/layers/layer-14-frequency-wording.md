## 1. THE 6-STAGE EMOTIONAL CURVE (Per-Ad)

**Source:** Frequencies-Wording.txt, Lines 5-17
**Rule:** "Die Frequenz muss mit dem emotionalen Zustand matchen." — The frequency MUST match the emotional state.

Every ad must move the customer through this emotional curve:

| Stage | Name | Customer Feels | Frequency Level | Key Principle |
|-------|------|----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Mirror Frequency | Customer feels understood | Empathetic match | Reflect their exact emotional state back to them |
| 2 | Problem Amplification | Customer recognizes urgency | Rising tension | Make the problem feel real without manipulation |
| 3 | Hope Introduction | Customer sees solution | Lifting | Shift from problem to possibility |
| 4 | Mechanism Confidence | Customer believes in solution | Building trust | Explain the HOW, not just the WHAT |
| 5 | Calm Certainty | Customer feels safe | Grounded | Remove fear of failure |
| 6 | Action Frequency | Customer acts | Decisive | Clear, low-friction next step |

**Critical Rule:** Mismatched frequency is the #1 ad failure. A calm tone for a Deadline Parent does NOT work. An urgency tone for an Overwhelmed Mom does NOT work.

**Internal Thoughts:**
- "Wir haben keine Zeit mehr." (We have no more time.)
- "Preschool startet bald." (Preschool starts soon.)
- "Warum klappt das nicht?" (Why isn't this working?)

| Hook | Panic Mirror | preschool, next week, deadline, accidents, running out of time | "If your toddler needs to be potty trained before preschool…" / "If preschool starts soon and your toddler still has accidents…" |

**Priority Words:** preschool, deadline, progress, learn faster, before school

## 3. THE 4 EMOTIONAL PHASES (Buyer Journey — Per-Email-Sequence)

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt, Lines 218-386

These phases describe the autistic parent's journey OVER TIME, distinct from the 6-stage per-ad frequency curve above.

| Phase | Child Age | Skepticism | Hope | Tonality |
|-------|-----------|------------|------|----------|
| 1: Slow Realization | 2.5–3.5 | Low | High | Education, gentle |
| 2: First Real Attempt | 3–4 | Medium | Medium | Validation + Mechanism |
| 3: Method Cycling | 4–6 | Very High | Med-Low | Science + Trust |
| 4: Resignation Fork | 6+ | Deep, Calm | Hidden | Respectful, slow |

### Phase 1: Slow Realization (Lines 228-257)

**Inner World:** First comparison pain, pediatrician asks at 3-year check, still thinks "boys are slower"
**Emotional State:** Worry but not crisis, still has hope for "easy fix", open to new ideas
**Tonality:** Education-oriented, gentle, "You're not alone" + "there's a reason", mechanism-first, hope language OK
**Decision Triggers:** Pediatrician comment, comparison with peers, own research

### Phase 2: First Real Attempt (Lines 258-296)

**Inner World:** Has bought "Oh Crap" book, blocked weekend for bootcamp, set child naked on couch... that never comes, 3 days become 7 become 2 weeks, gives up
**Emotional State:** First real defeat, self-doubt, frustration with child, guilt seesaw
**Tonality:** Validation-first ("You did nothing wrong"), mechanism explanation critical, soft belief shift, specific failure acknowledgment
**BIGGEST conversion lever in entire avatar journey**
**Decision Triggers:** Painful failure of specific method, pediatrician follow-up, upcoming deadline

### Phase 3: Method Cycling (Lines 297-340)

**Inner World:** Tries sticker charts, social stories, visual schedules, ABA protocols, special seats, finds OT helpful, hears about "interoception", becomes cynical
**Emotional State:** Default SKEPTICISM, "I've tried everything" is a trauma response
**Tonality:** Hard-sell is POISON, science-first (Kelly Mahler, SPARK), mechanism specificity, realistic before/after, "Try-and-judge" framing, permission-not-to-buy
**Decision Triggers:** OT recommendation, BCBA feedback, community exchange, specific science

### Phase 4: Resignation Fork (Lines 341-386)

**Inner World:** Has accepted child is "late" or "maybe never", plans pull-up stocks, stopped actively trying, OR new energy push before event
**Emotional State:** Two versions — Version A: "I gave up" / Version B: "Trying one last time"
**Tonality:** Very respectful, no energy pushes, "It's not too late" (Maureen Bennie's son was 9.5), late-trainer stories, longer timeline promises, gentle never pressure
**Decision Triggers:** Upcoming life events, sibling comparison, pull-up size change, puberty

## 7. EMAIL CADENCE & TIMING RULES (From Industry Skills — R2)

**Source:** Layer 14 R2 — Skill Loading (strategy-campaign-calendar, analytics-performance-benchmarks-reporting, strategy-customer-journey, copy)

### 7.1 Optimal Campaign Send Frequency

| Frequency | Likely Outcome | Diagnostic Signal |
|---|---|---|
| 1x/week | Customers forget the brand; engagement drops; revenue suffers | Low repeat purchase rate |
| 2x/week | Borderline — some results but suboptimal | Inconsistent revenue per campaign |
| **3-4x/week** | **Optimal: high revenue, low unsubscribes** | Stable or growing RPR, unsubscribe rate <0.20% |
| 5-7x/week | Fatigue; unsubscribes rise; diluted messaging | Unsubscribe rate >0.30%, click rate declining |

**Frequency correction protocol:**
- If sending <2x/week: Add 1 campaign/week. Monitor unsub rate. Repeat until 3-4x/week or unsub rises above 0.20%.
- If sending >5x/week: Reduce by 1 campaign/week. Monitor RPR per campaign. Continue until unsub stabilizes below 0.20%.

### 7.2 Segment-Based Frequency Tiers

| Segment | Definition | Max Emails/Week | Campaign Type Mix |
|---|---|---|---|
| VIPs | Top 10% by CLTV, 3+ orders, $500+ LTV | 3-5 | Exclusive drops, early access, VIP-only offers |
| Engaged 30-60 days | Opened/clicked in last 30-60 days | 2-3 | Standard promotional + educational mix |
| Engaged 90+ days | Opened/clicked in last 90 days | 1-2 | Re-engagement focused with strong CTAs |
| New subscribers | Subscribed in last 30 days, no purchase | 2-3 | Welcome flow + educational content |
| Recent buyers | Purchased in last 30 days | 2-3 | Post-purchase flow + cross-sell/upsell |
| Lapsed | Last open/click 90-180 days ago | 1 maximum | Winback offers, "we miss you" messaging |
| Unengaged | No open/click in 180+ days | Sunset only | Final re-engagement → sunset flow → suppress |

**Frequency management rules:**
1. Never send to unengaged at standard volume — route through sunset flow first
2. VIPs get maximum frequency but every send must feel earned — never automate a standard blast
3. Lapsed subscribers: 1 email/week max with heavy discount or compelling reason to return
4. Recent buyers skip promotional sends during post-purchase window (7-14 days)
5. New subscribers in welcome flow get flow emails + reduced campaign load — don't double-saturate

### 7.4 Flow Cadence Templates (Industry Standard)

#### Welcome Series Cadence

| # | Day | Subject Angle | Body Angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | "Welcome to [Brand], [Name]!" | Thank + set expectations + lead magnet |
| 2 | 1 | "Help us pick your favorites" | Preference quiz/collect data |
| 3 | 3 | "Your personalized picks" | Value based on preferences |
| 4 | 5 | "Here's a free gift" | Offer + incentive to purchase |

| 7 | 14 | "Your first month" | Recap + loyalty invite |

**Welcome flow benchmarks:**
- Email 1 open rate: 40-55% (top quartile: 60-70%)
- Email 1 conversion rate: 3-8% purchase rate
- Welcome RPR: $2.50-$4.00
- Email 2 open rate: 20-35% (steep drop-off from Email 1)

#### Cart Abandonment Cadence

| Timing | Subject Angle | Body Angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1h | "Still thinking it over?" | Product reminder, no pressure |
| 24h | "Selling fast" | Social proof + free shipping offer |
| 72h | "Last chance" | Discount + urgency ("Your cart expires") |

**Benchmarks:**
- Abandoned checkout Email 1 open rate: 35-45% (top quartile: 50-60%)
- Abandoned checkout recovery: 5-8% single email, 8-12% for 3-email series
- Abandoned checkout RPR: $3.00-$6.00

#### Post-Purchase Cadence

| Day | Touchpoint | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| D0 | Order confirmation + tracking | Confirm details, set expectations |
| D2 | Cross-sell "Complete the set" | Increase AOV |

- Email 1 open rate: 45-55% (top quartile: 60-70%) — highest open rate window in customer journey

- Trigger: 60-90 days no open/click for engagement winback, 90-180 days for purchase winback
- 3-4 emails over 2-3 weeks
- Escalating urgency: reminder → offer → "last chance" → final email
- Discount depth increases with each email
- Winback Email 1 open rate: 10-20% (top quartile: 25-35%)
- Winback RPR: $0.50-$1.50

|---|---|---|

| D3 | "What's new" | Showcase new products/content |
| D7 | "Last chance to stay subscribed" | Final urgency, sunset threat |

## 8. CONTENT MIX & WORDING FRAMEWORKS (From Skills — R2)

### 8.1 Content Mix Framework

**Source:** analytics-program-diagnostics-optimization, strategy-campaign-calendar

| Content Type | % of Total | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Sales / Promotional | ~30% | Drive revenue |
| Educational / Value | ~50% | Build trust and authority |
| Brand / Personality | ~20% | Create emotional connection |

**Note:** Alternate framework from campaign-calendar skill suggests 50% promotional / 30% educational / 15% engagement / 5% brand. The right ratio depends on program maturity and audience tolerance. For BrightKidCo (autism parenting niche, high-sensitivity audience), the conservative 30/50/20 split is recommended — heavy promotional would trigger trust erosion.

**Common pitfalls:**
- Too much promotional (>40%): Trains audience to wait for discounts. Unsub rates climb.
- Too much educational (>70%): Revenue per campaign is lower. Building trust but not converting.
- Too little brand: No differentiation. Every email feels like a sales email.

### 8.2 Subject Line Formulas

**Source:** copy skill

1. Curiosity: "[Number] ways to [benefit]"
2. Social proof: "Join [Number] others who..."
3. Urgency: "Last chance to [benefit]"
4. Personalization: "[Name], your [personalized thing] is ready"
5. How-to: "How to [desired outcome]"
6. Announcement: "Introducing [new product/feature]"
7. Question: "Struggling with [problem]?"
8. Stat: "[Number]% of [audience] [action]"

**For BrightKidCo, adapted per avatar:**

| Avatar | Subject Line Pattern | Example |
|--------|---------------------|---------|
| Deadline Pressure | Question + deadline | "Preschool starts soon — is your toddler ready?" |
| Social Pressure | Stat + normalization | "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't toilet trained" |
| Overwhelmed | Question + empathy | "Tired of changing wet clothes 5 times a day?" |
| Autistic Parent | Gentle question + sensory | "Sensory-sensitive potty training — is there a gentler way?" |

### 8.3 AI-ism Banned List

Never use: dive into, unlock, game-changer, in today's fast-paced, leveraging, revolutionize, transformative, empower, disrupt, best-in-class, cutting-edge, world-class, reimagine, supercharge, synergy, holistic, think outside the box, low-hanging fruit, ninja, guru, rockstar

### 8.4 Content Quality Standards

1. Every email must provide value — "Would the recipient be glad they opened this?"
2. One topic per email — micro-topics beat information overload
3. Write like a human — no corporate jargon, conversational tone with personality
4. Respect the reader's time — get to the point, use scannable formatting
5. Always include a CTA — even educational emails need a next step

## 9. LENA VOICE — WORDING RULES

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt, Lines 874-943

### Lena's Voice Formula (4-Step Structure)
1. **Observation of reality** — specific scenario the parent recognizes
2. **Validation without drama** — acknowledge without amplifying pain
3. **Mechanism or information** — the WHY behind the product
4. **Permission / soft action** — let them decide

### Sign-Off Rules
- Every mail ends with Lena sign-off
- Maximum 2-3 sentences body + max 2 lines P.S.
- **Short variant:** "Thanks for being here. If you have a question, hit reply. You'll get me — not a template, not a bot."
- **Medium variant:** Adds sons reference ("One is 6, still in the middle of it. The other took us until he was almost 8.")
- **Long variant:** For Cart Recovery / Last Day — adds "Whatever you choose — thanks for reading this far."

### Mail Content Composition (DOC6)
- 30-50% authentic verbatim language or tightly paraphrased (from research)
- 30-50% Lena's voice (peer-to-peer, empathetic)
- 10-20% mechanism/brand language (doc-based)
- Every mail must contain 2-4 authentic verbatim quotes from research

## 10. FORBIDDEN PHRASES — WORDING BLACKLIST

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt, Lines 965-1008

**Never use:**
- "Easy" / "Simple" / "Effortless"
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Finally solved everything"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- "Suffering from autism" (autism = identity, not disease)
- "High-functioning" / "Low-functioning"
- "Wait until they're ready" (trigger — she's heard it 100x)
- "Just try harder"
- "Be more consistent"
- "Your child should…"
- "Most children at this age…"
- "Have you tried [obvious method]"
- "Don't worry"
- "We understand" without substance
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix"
- "Your child will be normal"
- "Get him trained!"

**Total: 18 forbidden phrases**

## 15. LIST GROWTH FREQUENCY CONSIDERATIONS (From Skills — R2)

**Source:** Layer 14 R2 — email-collection-list-growth

### 15.1 The Growth Math

**Growth rate vs. absolute list size:** A brand gaining 2,000 subscribers/month with a 10K list outperforms a brand with a 20K list gaining only 250/month.

**Real-world example (30K monthly visitors):**
- Unoptimized (2.5% conversion): 750 new subs/month → $7K welcome flow revenue
- Optimized (8.75% conversion): 2,625 new subs/month → $25K welcome flow revenue
- Annual impact: $84K vs $300K = $216K additional revenue

### 15.2 List Churn Management

- Monthly churn rate: ~5% (unsubscribes + bounces + inactivity)
- If churn is 5% on a 10K list = 500 lost/month
- **Target acquisition rate: 2x churn rate minimum**

### 15.3 Offer Performance for List Growth

| Offer Type | Expected Conversion Rate | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Signup Campaign (No Offer) | ~1% | Brand loyalists, luxury/exclusive |
| Coupon Campaign (Discount) | ~5% | Default choice — immediate revenue, broadest application |
| Enter to Win / Giveaway | ~10% | Rapid list growth, launch hype (lower lead quality) |

## 18. GAPS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### Gap 1: Avatar Segmentation Overlap
The Frequencies file defines 4 avatars (Deadline, Social Pressure, Overwhelmed, Autistic). DOC1/DOC6 define 4 avatar LEVELS (Level 1-3 + General). These are DIFFERENT segmentation schemes. The ad system uses emotional urgency; the email system uses autism severity. **Recommendation:** Create a crosswalk matrix mapping emotional avatars to autism levels for unified targeting.

### Gap 2: Autistic Parent Avatar Inconsistency
The Frequencies file treats "Autistic Parent" as one of four equal avatars. DOC1 treats the autistic parent as THE primary audience (the entire doc is built for them). **Recommendation:** Decide if the autistic parent is the core audience (DOC1 view) or one segment among four (Frequencies view). This affects ALL content strategy.

### Gap 3: Phase 3 & 4 Mechanism Messaging
Avatar word banks for the Mechanism section are incomplete for Overwhelmed Mom and Autistic Parent (no example messages provided). **Recommendation:** Draft mechanism messaging for these avatars using the mechanism confidence tone guidelines.

### Gap 4: No Frequency Cap for Ads
Email flows have a max 1x per 7 days rule (from Layer 08). No equivalent ad frequency cap exists in the source files. **Recommendation:** Define ad frequency caps per avatar (Deadline Parent may tolerate higher frequency than Overwhelmed Mom).

### Gap 5: Missing R3-R4 Upstream Outputs
R3 (Wiki Extraction) and R4 (Cross-Reference) were not produced. The synthesis above is based on R1 (client files) and R2 (skill loading). Any additional findings from wiki or cross-reference rounds should be integrated when available.

### Gap 6: No A/B Testing Framework for Frequency
The source files define WHAT frequency to use but not HOW TO TEST it. **Recommendation:** Build frequency A/B testing protocols — test calm vs. urgent for Deadline Parents, test hope-heavy vs. realism-heavy for Level 3 avatars. Industry priority: abandoned cart discount depth, welcome series email count, subject line formula, send day of week.

### Gap 7: Frequency Decay Across Sequences
No rules exist for how frequency tone should shift across multi-email sequences. The 6-stage curve is per-ad; how does it apply to an 8-email Welcome Flow? **Recommendation:** Map each Welcome Flow email to a primary emotional stage (e.g., Email 1 = Mirror, Email 3 = Hope, Email 7 = Action).

### Gap 8: Subject Line Wording Per Avatar
R2 provided generic subject line formulas, and we adapted them per avatar. However, the client files (DOC1/DOC6) may contain avatar-specific subject line guidance not yet extracted. **Recommendation:** Re-read DOC1/DOC6 for subject line rules during Section 2 ingestion.

### Gap 9: Content Mix Discrepancy Between Skills
- strategy-campaign-calendar says: 50% promotional, 30% educational, 15% engagement, 5% brand
- analytics-program-diagnostics-optimization says: 30% promotional, 50% educational, 20% brand
- **Resolution:** For BrightKidCo's high-sensitivity audience, the conservative 30/50/20 split is recommended. Heavy promotional content would erode trust with autism parents who are skeptical of marketing.
