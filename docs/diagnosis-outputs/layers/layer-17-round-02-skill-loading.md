## 2. BRAND VOICE RULES FROM BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

### 2.1 Brand Identity — How BrightKidCo Sounds

**WE ARE:**
- Led by Lena (Customer Support, Mom of two autistic sons)
- Clinically informed, but emotionally raw
- Knowledge-based, not hope-based

**WE ARE NOT:**
- A "wellness brand" with positive vibes
- An "expert brand" that knows everything
- A "miracle product brand"
- Polished, perfect, or predictable

**THE VOICE:**
- Like an OT who is also a mom of an autistic child
- Like a friend who went through the same thing
- Like an experienced caregiver with two sons on the spectrum

### 2.2 Lena Voice Formula (4 Steps)
1. Observation of reality (specific scenario)
2. Validation without drama
3. Mechanism or information
4. Permission / soft action

### 2.3 Lena Sign-Off Rules
- EVERY mail ends with Lena sign-off
- NEVER founder framing in regular mails
- Sign-off body max 2-3 sentences. P.S. max 2 lines.
- **Three variants:** Short (transactional), Medium (with sons reference), Long (Cart Recovery/Last Day)

### 2.4 Tonality Calibration Per Level (BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE)

| Dimension | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | General Fallback |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|------------------|
| Hope Level | 70% Hope / 30% Realism | 40% Hope / 60% Realism | 20% Hope / 80% Realism | 50/50 |
| Realism Level | Medium | High | Very High | Medium |
| Urgency Level | High (Pre-K) | Low | ZERO | Low-Medium |
| Science Density | Medium-Low | High | Medium-High | Medium |
| Empathy Intensity | Medium | High | Very High | High |
| Testimonial Length | Short-Medium | Long | Long + Messy | Medium |
| CTA Aggressiveness | Medium | Low | Very Low | Medium-Low |
| General Energy | Optimistic but grounded | Clinical warmth | Quiet warmth + clinical reality | Inclusive warmth |

### 2.5 Hope vs Realism Language Per Level

**Level 1 (70/30):**
- Hope: "This may be the thing your child needs", "Many families see real change", "Pre-K acceptance is achievable"
- Realism: "Some kids take 2 weeks, others 8", "Not every kid is the same", "60 days to know if it fits your kid"

**Level 2 (40/60):**
- Hope: "This may close one piece of what's been missing", "Some families see real change after 4-8 weeks"
- Realism: "Some kids take 8-12 weeks. Some longer.", "Regression cycles are normal — that's not failure", "Mini-wins count: pause, look, pull at pants"

**Level 3 (20/80):**
- Hope: "It may add one piece to what you're already doing", "Some families see real change. Some don't. Both happen."
- Realism: "Some kids take 12+ weeks. Some 6+ months.", "Wins look different at this stage — pause, look, tolerate", "We're not promising training. We're offering a missing piece."

**General Fallback (50/50):**
- Hope: "This may help close a piece of what's been missing"
- Realism: "Some kids take 2 weeks. Others 12+ weeks. Both normal.", "Mini-wins look different per kid"

### 2.6 BCBA Respect Language (Critical for Level 2 & 3)

**DO:**
- "Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years"
- "ABA addresses behavior. The sensory piece needs different input."
- "This complements your ABA program, not replaces it"

**DON'T:**
- "ABA doesn't work for potty training"
- "Your BCBA missed this"
- "BCBA protocols are flawed"

### 2.7 Dignity-First Language (Critical for Level 3)

**DO:** "Your child", "He is doing the best his nervous system allows", "Whatever progress means for your family is enough", "Whether he trains in 8 weeks or never, you've been doing the right thing"

**DON'T:** "Your child will be normal", "Cure/Fix/Solve", "Save him from...", "Become like other kids"

## 3. DOC1/DOC6 TONALITY RULES MAPPED TO FLOW STAGES

### 3.1 Tonality Per Flow Stage (DOC1 Section 21)

| Flow Stage | Tone | Language Style | Energy | Do | Don't |
|------------|------|---------------|--------|-----|-------|
| **Pre-Purchase** (Welcome, Cart, Browse, Checkout) | Education + Soft Conversion | Specific, mechanism-led, peer-to-peer | Calm, confident, "we're not in a hurry" | Validate failures, explain mechanism, soft CTA | Hard-sell, urgency manipulation |
| **Conversion Moment** (Cart-Recovery final, Checkout) | Friendly Reminder + Permission Not To Buy | "If today isn't right, that's a real answer" | Decision-respect, "you decide" | Reduce friction, offer guarantee, soft permission | Pressure tactics, fake scarcity, guilt-tripping |
| **Transactional** (Order Confirm, Shipping) | Warm + Functional | Direct, informative, branded | Professional but human | Clear info, set expectations, soft upsell spot | Too much sales talk |
| **Post-Purchase Early** (Arrived, Day 0-7) | Warm + Reassurance + Education | "Here's what to expect" | Welcome to the family | Set realistic Week-1 expectations, sensory prep tips | Hard re-sell, oversell ease |
| **Post-Purchase Mid** (Day 14-60) | Realistic + Patient + "It's normal" | "Week 1 plateau is real. Hold the line." | Coach + cheerleader | Manage expectations, celebrate mini-wins | Promise quick wins, abandon if no progress |
| **Lifecycle** (Replenishment, Winback, Sunset) | Respectful Re-engagement | "How are you doing?" | Genuinely curious, low-pressure | Acknowledge time, no guilt, reopen softly | "You forgot us" guilt-tripping |

### 3.2 Cross-Level Calibration Rules (DOC1 Section 22)

| Rule | Never | Yes |
|------|-------|-----|
| Symptom over level label | "If your child is Level 2..." | "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..." |
| 3-5 recognition anchors per mail | Generic single scenario | Level 1 + Level 2 + Level 3 specific situations |
| Age ranges not fixed ages | "For 5-year-olds..." | "For kids 3 to 10+..." |
| Verbal/non-verbal inclusivity | Only verbal examples | "Saying 'wet' OR showing you with a gesture" |
| Timeline flexibility | "In 30 days your child will..." | "Some kids show first signals after 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks." |
| Success metrics low bar | "Fully trained" | "Look at the wet spot for the first time" |

### 3.3 The 4 Emotional Phases (DOC1 Section 6) — Phase-to-Tonality Map

| Phase | Child Age | Skepticism | Hope | Tonality | Decision Triggers |
|-------|-----------|------------|------|----------|-------------------|
| Phase 1: Slow Realization | 2.5-3.5 | Low | High | Education, gentle | Pediatrician comment, peer comparison |
| Phase 2: First Real Attempt | 3-4 | Medium | Medium | Validation + Mechanism | Method failure, Pre-K deadline |
| Phase 3: Method Cycling | 4-6 | Very High | Med-Low | Science + Trust | OT recommendation, BCBA feedback |
| Phase 4: Resignation Fork | 6+ | Deep, calm | Hidden | Respectful, slow | Life events, pull-up size change |

### 3.4 Trust Mechanic Pyramid (DOC1 Section 9) — Non-Negotiable Order

1. **Mechanism Specificity** (most important)
2. **Peer-to-Peer Voice** (Lena, real parents)
3. **Prior Failure Acknowledgment**
4. **Realistic Timelines** (Weeks, not days)
5. **60-Day Guarantee** (risk removal)

**CRITICAL:** If the marketer brings #5 (discount) before #1 (mechanism), they lose the avatar.

- "Finally solved everything"
- "Guaranteed results"

- "We understand" without substance
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix"

### 4.4 Level 3 Specific Forbidden Phrases

- "Easy" / "Quick" / "Fast" / "Simple"
- "Your child WILL train"
- "Don't give up!"
- "It's never too late!" (as sole statement)

- "Make this the year he trains"
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix"

### 4.5 General Fallback Specific Forbidden Phrases

- "For Level 1/2/3 kids..."
- "Diagnosed autism only"
- "Verbal kids will..."

- Aggressive math reframes
- "Just like other kids"

- "Get him trained!"
- Heavy resignation language

### 4.6 AI-ism Banned List (Copy Skill)

- dive into, unlock, game-changer, in today's fast-paced, leveraging, revolutionize, transformative, empower, disrupt, best-in-class, cutting-edge, world-class, reimagine, supercharge, synergy, holistic, think outside the box, low-hanging fruit, ninja, guru, rockstar

### 4.7 Structural Don'ts (DOC1 Section 23)

- Lead with the product
- Use "easy/quick/simple" anywhere
- Promise outcomes tied to timelines
- Compare to neurotypical kids as standard
- Suggest the parent isn't doing enough
- Use level labels (Level 2/3) in copy
- Stack social proof early
- Use urgency manipulation ("only 24 hours!")
- Use exclamation marks in body text
- Use "we know how you feel"
- Use generic stock images of neurotypical happy families
- End mails with hard-sell CTAs ("BUY NOW!")

### 4.8 Sensitive Areas (Handle with Extra Care)

1. **SELF-BLAME** — Never hint she's doing something wrong. Always absolve.
2. **PARTNER CONFLICT** — Don't sound like she has to convince her partner.
3. **COMPARISON WOUND** — Never position neurotypical kids as standard.
4. **PEDIATRICIAN FRUSTRATION** — Gently acknowledge, never attack entire medical profession.
5. **FINANCIAL HARDSHIP** — ~25% use Medicaid. Math reframe OK, never preachy.
6. **CHILD'S IDENTITY** — Autism = identity, not disease. "Help his nervous system" > "fix his behavior".

## 6. SKILL GAPS — WHAT'S MISSING THAT LAYER 17 NEEDS

### 6.1 Gap: Copy Skill Has No Avatar-Level Tonality Rules
The copy skill is a generic copywriting OS. It has ZERO BrightKidCo-specific tonality calibration. All level-specific rules come from BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md and DOC1. Layer 17 must build its own tonality engine from the doc rules, not the skill.

### 6.2 Gap: No Flow-Stage-to-Tonality Mapping in Copy Skill
The copy skill defines flow types (Welcome, Cart, Post-Purchase) but maps them to generic ecom tonalities. BrightKidCo requires a completely different mapping:
- Cart Abandonment = "permission not to buy" (NOT urgency)
- Welcome = "mechanism-first education" (NOT promotional)
- Post-Purchase = "realistic expectations" (NOT upsell-first)

### 6.3 Gap: No Forbidden-Phrase-to-Level Matrix in Copy Skill
The copy skill has a flat AI-ism banned list. BrightKidCo needs a 4-level forbidden phrase matrix with different severity per level. The BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE provides this but the skill does not.

### 6.4 Gap: No Emotional Phase Integration
The copy skill has no concept of the 4 emotional phases (Slow Realization → First Attempt → Method Cycling → Resignation Fork). These phases fundamentally change tonality. Layer 17 must integrate phase detection into tonality selection.

### 6.5 Gap: No "Permission-Not-To-Buy" Framework
The standard copy skill assumes conversion-oriented CTAs. BrightKidCo requires a "permission-not-to-buy" framework where the CTA actively reduces pressure. This is antithetical to standard copywriting and must be built fresh.

### 6.6 Gap: No RS (Raw Research) Integration Pattern
The copy skill has no mechanism for integrating verbatim customer quotes. DOC6 specifies: "30-50% authentic verbatim language, 30-50% Lena's voice, 10-20% mechanism/brand language." This ratio system must be built into Layer 17.

### 6.7 Gap: No Trust Pyramid Enforcement
The copy skill doesn't enforce the trust mechanic pyramid order (Mechanism → Peer Voice → Failure Acknowledgment → Timelines → Guarantee). Violating this order kills conversion. Layer 17 needs to enforce sequence.

### 6.8 Gap: No Cross-Level Calibration Engine
The copy skill has no concept of writing one mail that works for all 4 avatar levels simultaneously. The 6 cross-level calibration rules (symptom-first, 3-5 anchors, age ranges, verbal/non-verbal inclusivity, timeline flexibility, low-bar success metrics) must be built into Layer 17.

### 6.9 Gap: No Sign-Off Template System
The copy skill has generic CTA guidance. BrightKidCo requires 3 Lena sign-off variants (Short/Medium/Long) matched to flow stage and emotional weight. Must be templated.

### 6.10 Gap: No Anti-Fabrication Guardrails
The copy skill doesn't address quote sourcing. DOC6 requires: all verbatim quotes from RS PDFs only, no fabrication, informal citation. Layer 17 needs a verification layer.
