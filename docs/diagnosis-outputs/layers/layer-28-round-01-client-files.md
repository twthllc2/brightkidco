## 1. TESTIMONIAL FRAMEWORKS AND STRUCTURES

### 1A. Three-Family-Pattern (Cross-Level) — DOC 5, Section 15
**The primary testimonial framework for General Fallback (cross-level) emails.**
Instead of ONE testimonial, offer THREE (short, compact) — one per level — explicitly labeled.

**Format:**
```
"Three families. Three different situations.
— Sarah, mom of 4yo verbal son:
'He went from "almost" to "mostly" in 6 weeks. Pre-K accepted him in underwear.'
— Lisa, mom of 6yo daughter (sensory dominant):
'After 3 years of trying everything, she finally paused when she felt wetness. That was Week 4.'
— Maria, mom of 9yo non-verbal son:
'We're 5 months in. He's not trained. But he's feeling something for the first time. That's enough for now.'
You'll see in 60 days where you fit."
```

**Why it works (DOC 5):**
- Everyone finds themselves in a testimonial
- Implicitly acknowledged: different levels exist
- No "miracle" promises
- Realistic outcomes per profile
- Creates implicit migration signal (she identifies with one)

**When to use:** Welcome E3 (Last Day), Cart Recovery E2, FAQ "Will this work for my kid", Browse Abandonment E2

### 1B. Single Cross-Level Testimonial — DOC 5, Section 16
**Alternative to Three-Family: ONE testimonial using cross-level language that works for all levels simultaneously.**

**Example:**
```
"My son is 7. We don't focus on labels in our family. He has sensory differences,
communication delays, and never showed any sign of feeling wet. We tried Oh Crap,
sticker charts, scheduled sits, social stories. None worked the way they were
'supposed' to.
This isn't a 'fully trained' story. After 4 months, he sometimes initiates. He
sometimes feels the signal. He still has accidents. But for the first time in 4 years,
his nervous system is registering something.
I'd buy it again."
— Anonymous parent
```

**Why it works:**
- No diagnosis labels
- No fixed age (7 is middle for GF)
- Multiple methods mentioned (resonates broadly)
- Realistic outcome ("not fully trained, but...")
- Symptom-First Language ("sensory differences", "communication delays")

**When to use:** Welcome E2 (mechanism mail), Welcome E4 (Education), Cart Recovery E1, Sunset Flow

### 1C. Specificity Without Level-Lock-In — DOC 5, Section 17
**Principle: Generic = not trust-building. But Level-specific = misses cross-level audience.**
**Solution: Specific details, but level-agnostic.**

**DO:**
- "After 3 years of trying" (specific time, level-agnostic)
- "His sensory differences" (descriptive, not diagnostic)
- "He's never been verbal about bathroom needs" (verbal-inclusive)
- "Communication delays / non-verbal moments"
- Specific mini-wins (pause, look, hold the pants)

**DON'T:**
- "Level 2 with severe sensory" (too level-lock-in)
- "ASD Level 1 child" (misses non-ASD audience)
- "Diagnosed Level 3" (misses Sub-Group B+C)
- Specific diagnosis-code language

### 1D. Testimonial Structure by Length — DOC 3, Section 11 (Level 2)
**For longer testimonials (PDP, FAQ mails, 20+ sentences):**
- PARAGRAPH 1: Child profile + diagnosis + therapy history
- PARAGRAPH 2: Specific symptoms (Sensory + Prompt-Dep + GI)
- PARAGRAPH 3: Methods tried (detail!) + why failed
- PARAGRAPH 4: Skepticism at purchase + expectations
- PARAGRAPH 5: Week-by-week progress with concrete wins
- PARAGRAPH 6: Where they are now + honest "still working on"
- PARAGRAPH 7: Recommendation (peer-to-peer, not marketing)

### 1E. Testimonial Placement by Flow Position — DOC 1, Section 9 + DOC 2, Section 11 + DOC 3, Section 12
**Proof placement in mails:**
- Welcome E1: only reviews stat in footer
- Welcome E2: OT citation + mechanism
- Welcome E3: 1 short testimonial
- Welcome E4-5: full testimonials allowed
- Cart Recovery: 1 strong Pre-K testimonial
- Post-Purchase: wins-pattern testimonials
- FAQ mails: Multiple testimonials depending on question

## 3. RULES FOR TESTIMONIAL USAGE

### 3A. Anti-Fabrication Rule (NON-NEGOTIABLE) — DOC 6, Section + BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE Section 10
**This is the single most important rule about testimonials.**

- You may ONLY use verbatim quotes that exist in the RS PDFs provided
- You may NOT: Invent customer quotes, Fabricate testimonials, Imagine "what a mom might say", Generate plausible-sounding but fake reviews, Adapt quotes so heavily they become fictional
- You MAY: Lightly anonymize (change first names, ages within range), Combine themes from multiple real quotes into one representative statement (only if noted internally), Paraphrase for length keeping core meaning and voice
- If you cannot find a real quote that matches what the mail needs, FLAG IT instead of fabricating
- This is brand-trust-critical. Fabricated quotes that get exposed = brand reputation damage.

### 3B. Testimonial Patterns by Level — DOC 2, Section 9 + DOC 3, Section 10

**LEVEL 1 Testimonial Requirements:**
- Child Profile: Age 3-5 years (sweet spot 4), Verbal, "Diagnosed Level 1" or "On the spectrum, mild support needs"
- Context: Pre-K pressure as context (very powerful), Mainstream daycare/preschool, 1-3 failed attempts mentioned
- Timeline: 2-6 weeks to first mini-wins, 6-12 weeks to daytime training, Realistic: "We're still working on poop"
- Language: "He was so close to being trained but kept regressing", "Pre-K starts in September and I was terrified", "Within 3 weeks, he started telling me BEFORE", "Still working on it, but he's no longer in pull-ups", "We made it for pre-K with weeks to spare"
- Details that build trust: Birthday mentioned (3rd, 4th, 5th), OT/Speech therapist mentioned, Specific Pre-K school or daycare name, "He's so verbal — that's not the issue", Authentic struggle moment
- Testimonial length: Short-Medium (3-8 sentences ideal, multiple short > 1 long)

**LEVEL 2 Testimonial Requirements:**
- Child Profile: Age 5-7 years (sweet spot 6), "Diagnosed Level 2" or "moderate support needs", Partly verbal, sensory dominant, 3-5 failed attempts mentioned, BCBA/OT context prominent, Optional: comorbidity (GI, anxiety)
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks to first mini-wins, 3-6 months to daytime independence, Acknowledged: night training comes MUCH later, Realistic: "Still have accidents, but...", Regression cycles mentioned + acknowledged
- Language: "After 3 years of trying everything...", "His BCBA was skeptical but...", "OT recommended sensory regulation, this seemed to be the missing piece", "Week 6 was when it clicked", "Still working on poop, but pee is mostly there", "Some regression but overall trending up"
- Details that build trust: BCBA/OT mentioned with name or specific years, Specific therapy hours ("20 hours of ABA"), Comorbidities mentioned (Miralax, anxiety, sensory), Authentic struggle moment, Honest "still not done" closing
- Testimonial length: Long (10-15 sentences ideal, mention multiple wins, realistic "not finished yet" closing)

**LEVEL 3 Testimonial Requirements:**
- Child Profile: Age 6-10+ years, Non-verbal/minimal verbal, 5+ years of attempts, GI/comorbidity history
- Timeline: 12+ weeks to first signals, 6+ months to any independence, Many never reach "fully trained"
- Details: Late-trainer stories prominent (Maureen Bennie's son was 9.5), Dignity-first framing, Honest "still in pull-ups at night" closing
- Testimonial length: Long + messy (realistic, detailed, no sugar-coating)

### 3C. Testimonial Selection Rules — BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE, Section 6
- Testimonials must be from the DOC files (DOC 2/3/4/5 contain approved sample testimonials)
- All verbatim quotes must come from the RS PDFs (Autistic_Parent.pdf, Autistic_language_Bank.pdf, Autistic_MIRO_m.pdf)
- Statistics must be sourced (49% SPARK stat → cite "SPARK study, Wiggins et al.", Kelly Mahler quotes → cite "Kelly Mahler, interoception researcher", 16,511 reviews → cite as "on-site reviews")
- Lena's voice is the only fictional persona — and even she must stay within her defined profile (mom of two autistic sons, ages 6 and 8)

### 3D. RS-Holistic Workflow for Testimonials — DOC 6, Section 8 + BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE, Section 9
**Every mail must contain 2-4 authentic verbatim quotes or tightly paraphrased lines from the RS. Cite informally ("one mom in our research described..." or directly in mail body as quote).**

**Mail text composition:**
- 30-50% authentic verbatim language or tightly paraphrased
- 30-50% Lena's voice (peer-to-peer, empathetic)
- 10-20% mechanism/brand language (doc-based)

**BAD (only DOC-based, no RS):**
"Many parents describe their child as 'not feeling wet.' This is a sensory-neurological issue."
→ Generic. Sounds like marketing.

**GOOD (RS-integrated):**
"One mom in our research wrote: 'He doesn't realize he needs to go until it's happening.' Another: 'She'll pee herself and just keep playing.' These aren't isolated moments. They're the same sensory-neurological signal gap, showing up in different families."
→ Specific. Real. Trustworthy.

## 5. SOCIAL PROOF TECHNIQUES

### 5A. Trust Mechanic Pyramid — DOC 1, Section 9
**How trust is built with this avatar (ordered most important to secondary):**

1. **MECHANISM SPECIFICITY** (the most important) — explain HOW the product works neurologically
2. **PEER-TO-PEER VOICE** (Lena, real parents) — brand becomes person
3. **PRIOR FAILURE ACKNOWLEDGMENT** — name the methods that didn't work and WHY
4. **REALISTIC TIMELINES** (Weeks, not days) — "6-12 weeks" not "3 days"
5. **60-DAY GUARANTEE** (Risk removal) — "Try it, decide, no pressure"

**IMPORTANT: This order is NON-NEGOTIABLE.** If the marketer brings #5 (discount) before #1 (mechanism), they lose the avatar.

**Additional Trust Boosters (secondary):**
- Kelly Mahler references (gold standard in interoception)

- OT/BCBA co-signs
- Real-parent testimonials with messy details

- 16,511 reviews / 4.9 stars / 100,000+ parents (brand-backed)
