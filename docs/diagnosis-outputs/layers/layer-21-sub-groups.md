# Diagnosis: Layer 21 — Sub-Groups Within General Fallback (Synthesis)

## Objective
Synthesize all Layer 21 findings into the definitive diagnosis file for the 5 Sub-Groups Within General Fallback.

## Background
General Fallback is ~50% of all real email traffic. It is NOT homogeneous. It consists of 5 very different sub-groups that must all receive the same mail set simultaneously. This synthesis consolidates all client file extractions (R1), skill loading (R2), wiki extraction (R3), and cross-referencing (R4) into one comprehensive diagnosis file.

---

## Executive Summary

The 5 General Fallback sub-groups span 4 emotional phases, 3 avatar levels, and wildly different diagnostic and psychological states. **3 of 5 sub-groups (A, B, C) have NO clean Level match** — they exist in gaps between the 3 avatar levels. Only Sub-Group D has a real (but undisclosed) level, and Sub-Group E is completely variable.

**Critical findings:**

1. **The "Identity Gap" is the biggest challenge.** Sub-Group A is pre-identity (doesn't yet identify as "autism mom"). Sub-Group B is anti-identity (rejects labels entirely). Sub-Group C is parallel-identity (identifies as "neurodivergent parent" but NOT "autism mom"). The General Fallback flow must serve all three identity states simultaneously.

2. **Sub-Group B requires the most linguistic care.** She has Level 2-3 experience but rejects the identity framework all Level-specific content assumes. She needs symptom language ONLY — never label language.

3. **Sub-Group C is the hidden high-value segment.** She has money, education, and mechanism understanding. Lever GF-D ("Not just for autism") is the key to unlocking her.

4. **Sub-Group D is the migration goldmine.** She has a real level but didn't share it. Day 14 Level-Detection Mail expected: 35-50% click rate, ~40-50% migration.

5. **Sub-Groups B and C are essentially PERMANENT General Fallback residents.** The General flow must be designed to convert them IN the General segment, not migrate them out.

## Sub-Group A: The Diagnostic Entrants (~25% of GF, ~10% of total)

### Profile
- **Age:** 28-38 years old
- **Child age:** 2-4 years old, often first child
- **Geography:** US, UK, AU mix
- **Education:** Higher education over-represented (she researches actively)

### Who She Is
> "No formal ASD diagnosis yet. Waiting for evaluation or has appointments in pipeline. Strong suspicion that autism is the case. Pediatrician has hinted 'developmental concerns.' Other families have said: 'Get that checked.'" — DOC5

### Mental State
- Confusion dominant: "Is this autism or just delays?"
- Doesn't yet identify as "autism mom"
- Very active in research
- Hasn't decided yet which communities to join
- Very curious, very open to education

### What She Reads
- "is my 3 year old autistic"
- "speech delay potty training"
- "developmental delays toilet training"
- "early signs of autism"

### Why She's in General Fallback
**Reason 1: No Diagnostic Level.** She doesn't know what to choose. "Still figuring out" is most honest answer. She can't choose a level because she doesn't have one.

### Language Preferences
- Education-First approach
- NO diagnosis language as prerequisite
- "If you're starting to wonder..." openers
- Soft mechanism explanation
- Gentle, non-alarming

### Content Needs
- Education about why standard methods fail
- Gentle introduction to interoception concept
- "You're not alone" framing
- No hard sales hooks
- Mechanism-first approach

### Triggers
- Pediatrician comment about "developmental concerns"
- Comparison with peers whose kids are trained
- Own research initiative discovering autism signs
- Upcoming evaluation appointment

### Fears
- Getting hopes up before diagnosis is confirmed
- Being labeled prematurely
- Missing a "normal" developmental window
- Not knowing enough to make good decisions

### Avatar Level Mapping
- **Primary overlap:** Phase 1 (Slow Realization) — child 2.5-3.5 years, first comparison pain, still has hope for "easy fix," open to new ideas
- **Partial overlap with Level 1** — similar age range, similar hope level, but Sub-Group A lacks even the diagnosis that Level 1 has (~70% of Level 1 is formally diagnosed)
- **No overlap with Level 2 or 3** — she hasn't accumulated years of therapy experience or method cycling

## Messaging Rules and Guidelines

### Universal Rules (All Sub-Groups)

**Every GF mail MUST:**
1. Never assume a diagnosis exists (misses A, C)
2. Never assume the mom knows ASD concepts (misses A, B, C)
3. Never assume the mom identifies as "autism mom" (misses B, C)

5. Include at least one "Whether...Or...If..." sentence (Pattern A or B)

7. Include GF-D lever ("Not just for autism") for Sub-Groups B and C

- **Science Density:** MEDIUM — Kelly Mahler yes, but not 3 paragraphs
- **Empathy Intensity:** HIGH — across all sub-groups, mom is tired
- **CTA Aggressiveness:** MEDIUM-LOW — "See if this fits your situation"

### 4-Phase Mix Dynamics (All Sub-Groups Simultaneously)

Each GF mail must contain elements for all 4 phases:

- **Phase 1 elements:** "If you're just starting to wonder..." / Education approach / Gentle mechanism intro
- **Phase 2 elements:** "If you tried Oh Crap or similar and it didn't work..." / Validation of method failure / Reframe: "wrong problem, not wrong parent"
- **Phase 3 elements:** "If you've cycled through methods for years..." / Science (Kelly Mahler, SPARK) / 60-Day Guarantee as risk removal

**Integration Tip:** Instead of 4 separate sections, integrate through: "Wherever you are on this journey..." / "Whether you're 3 weeks in or 3 years in..." / "Some parents have just started looking, others have tried everything..."

### Migration Strategy

General Fallback is NOT an end state. It is a TEMPORARY state until mom self-identifies.

**4 Migration Paths:**
1. **Click-Based:** Customer clicks level-specific story links → automatic Klaviyo tag update
2. **Reply-Based:** Customer replies with details → tag set based on content
3. **Purchase-Based Hints:** Underwear size → age hint → level hint
4. **Post-Purchase Level-Detection (Day 14):** Strongest method. 4 buttons. Expected: 65-75% open rate, 35-50% click rate, ~40-50% migration

**Sub-Group Migration Likelihood:**
- A → Will likely self-select Level 1 or 2 after diagnosis (weeks to months)
- B → Unlikely to migrate; stays General or drops off
- C → Unlikely to migrate to ASD levels; may stay General permanently
- D → Highest migration potential (has a real level, just didn't share it)
- E → Variable; depends on which level she actually is

### Monitoring Recommendations

Track these metrics per sub-group:
- **Engagement Velocity:** Open rate + click rate trends by sub-group
- **Migration Rate:** % of Sub-Group D migrating to level-specific segments
- **Revenue Per Subscriber:** Monetary value per sub-group
- **Churn Rate:** Unsubscribes + bounces by sub-group
- **Conversion Rate:** Purchase rate by sub-group
