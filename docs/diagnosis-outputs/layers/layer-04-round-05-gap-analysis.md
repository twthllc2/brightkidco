## 2. LEVEL 1 GAPS

### What's Well-Covered
- Demographics (age, income, education, employment) — good detail
- Symptom clusters (5 clusters, well-described) — comprehensive
- Conversion triggers (Pre-K, Oh Crap failure, OT reco, comparison) — strong
- Language patterns and forbidden phrases — thorough
- Tonality calibration — clear and actionable

### What's Missing

**Gap L1-1: Hope-Crash Risk Quantification**
- R1 mentions "Vulnerable to hope-crash if promises break" and "Refund Risk: High (hope-crash)" in the R4 master matrix
- No data on what percentage of L1 purchasers actually request refunds
- No characterization of what triggers the hope-crash (timeline slippage vs unmet expectations vs product mismatch)
- **Recommendation:** Analyze refund/return data segmented by avatar level to quantify this risk

**Gap L1-2: Pre-K Deadline Seasonality Data**
- Pre-K enrollment is identified as the #1 conversion trigger
- No data on seasonal timing: when do most Pre-K deadlines fall? How should email calendar align?
- No data on how many L1 moms are in Pre-K zone vs those with younger children (2-year-olds not yet at Pre-K)
- **Recommendation:** Cross-reference with enrollment calendar data; model Pre-K timing into email campaign sequencing

**Gap L1-3: The "Almost Trained" Loop Duration**
- The language pattern "Almost trained" is identified as an L1-specific hook
- No data on how long L1 moms stay in the "almost trained" state before either converting or cycling to L2
- This is critical for email nurture timing — how many emails before she either buys or escalates to skepticism?
- **Recommendation:** Analyze time-to-conversion from first opt-in for L1 tag; estimate typical "almost trained" duration

**Gap L1-4: Sibling/Parent Dynamic Missing**
- R1 identifies L1 mom demographics (married ~85%, often NT children)
- No sibling dynamics data: how does potty training an autistic child affect the NT sibling? How does NT sibling being already trained affect mom?
- No partner conflict assessment specific to L1 (noted as common in L2 but not quantified for L1)
- **Recommendation:** Add sibling dynamic and partner alignment questions to L1 email content strategy

**Gap L1-5: ADHD/PDA Comorbidity Depth**
- R1 mentions ADHD ~25-30% comorbidity for Level 1
- PDA (Pathological Demand Avoidance) mentioned as "common at L1/L2" but not quantified
- The "verbal refusal" symptom cluster (L1-C) strongly resembles PDA behavior — is this being properly differentiated?
- **Recommendation:** External research needed on PDA prevalence in Level 1 population; verify whether "verbal refusal" responses are PDA or interoception-based

## 3. LEVEL 2 GAPS

### What's Well-Covered
- Core characteristics (skepticism, method-cycling, BCBA context) — excellent depth
- L2 Paradox and email formula (6 elements, clear) — standout insight
- Symptom clusters (6 clusters, well-defined) — comprehensive
- BCBA respect language — highly specific and actionable
- Conversion lever strengths — well-articulated

### What's Missing

**Gap L2-1: Method Cycling Timeline Data**
- L2 Phase 3 (Method Cycling, ages 4-7) is identified as the longest and most important phase (~55% of L2)
- No data on how many methods the typical L2 mom cycles through before either converting or reaching Phase 4
- No data on the average length of each "method cycle" (weeks? months?)
- **Recommendation:** Survey data or purchase path analysis to quantify method-cycle duration; informs email timing between attempts

**Gap L2-2: Prompt-Dependency Duration Threshold**
- Prompt-dependency frustration is the #1 conversion trigger
- No data on how long prompt-dependency must persist before it becomes a conversion trigger
- Is a 6-month prompt-dependent mom different from a 2-year prompt-dependent mom in conversion behavior?
- **Recommendation:** Cross-reference purchase timing against how long the child has been in scheduled-sit routines

**Gap L2-3: GI Crisis → Purchase Conversion Data**
- Withholding + GI issues (L2-B) is identified as a high-investment-ready hook
- No data on actual conversion rates from GI-related email content vs non-GI content
- No data on seasonal patterns in GI-related conversion (known: GI issues in autistic children often worsen with dietary changes, stress, seasonal transitions)
- **Recommendation:** A/B test GI-focused vs sensory-focused emails; track conversion timing relative to GI crisis triggers

**Gap L2-4: Single Mom Segment Fidelity**
- 15-20% of L2 audience noted as single moms
- No differentiation in messaging for single moms vs partnered moms
- Different financial constraints, different support network, different time availability
- A single mom who is the sole decision-maker (L2: "Potty training ruining marriage" mentioned, but no solo-parent path)
- **Recommendation:** Add single-mom sub-segment considerations to L2 email strategy

**Gap L2-5: Comorbidity Interaction Effects**
- L2 has multiple comorbidities: SPD (95%), ADHD (40%), GI (50%), Anxiety (35%), Sleep (45%)
- These interact in nonlinear ways (fatigued mom + anxious child + sensory overwhelm + GI pain = different conversion profile than sensory-only)
- No analysis of which comorbidity combinations are most/least responsive to specific email hooks
- **Recommendation:** Analyze purchase patterns by reported comorbidity; identify highest-value comorbidity profiles

## 4. LEVEL 3 GAPS

### What's Well-Covered
- Resignation reality — exceptional emotional depth
- Dignity-first principle — clear and consistently applied
- Symptom clusters (6 clusters) — thorough
- Late-trainer storytelling priority — well-understood
- Financial sensitivity (40% Medicaid) — acknowledged

### What's Missing

**Gap L3-1: Late-Trainer Success Statistics**
- "Maureen Bennie's son was 9.5" is cited as the central testimonial
- No data on late-trainer success rates — how many Level 3 children eventually achieve daytime training?
- No data on age ranges for late training (9.5 is one data point; what's the distribution?)
- This is THE core proof element for L3 and it currently rests on a single anecdote
- **Recommendation:** Research published data on late toilet training in severe autism; or collect internal case studies with age-at-training data

**Gap L3-2: Pull-Up as Safety Object — Insufficient Depth**
- L3 conversion lever 2 ("Pull-ups make this worse") is rated Medium with a CAUTION: "pull-ups are existential safety"
- This is a CRITICAL nuance but not explored in depth
- For L3, pull-ups may be the ONLY consistent, non-traumatic toileting solution after years of failed attempts
- Framing them as "bad" could cause profound alienation
- **Recommendation:** Dedicated analysis of when and how to address pull-ups for L3 without triggering defensiveness; consider whether to address at all in early emails

**Gap L3-3: 60-Day Guarantee — Realistic for Level 3?**
- 60-day guarantee is rated "Very High" as a conversion lever for L3
- But if L3 children have literally never shown a signal (Complete Signal Absence, L3-A), is 60 days realistic for any observable progress?
- The gap between "you have 60 days to try" and "your child may need 6+ months" is dangerous for L3
- **Recommendation:** Evaluate whether the 60-day guarantee needs an L3-specific modification (extended timeline, or progress-based rather than result-based)

**Gap L3-4: Physical Size Progression — Forward-Looking Strategy**
- L3-F: Size Progression Fear — emotional milestone: "Next step is adult diapers"
- This is a time-triggered event — as the child grows, this concern becomes acute
- No data on age/size distribution of L3 audience relative to this trigger point
- No email sequence planned around this specific transition
- **Recommendation:** Map age/size cohorts in L3; build a dedicated "Size Progression" email trigger

**Gap L3-5: Self-Injury and Bathroom Trauma — Missing Content Strategy**
- L3-E Physical Resistance is described ("kicks me when I change him")
- Self-injury (mentioned as comorbidity ~40%) and bathroom trauma are acknowledged but have no dedicated content strategy
- If toilet training attempts have caused trauma (child and parent), how does the email sequence address this?
- **Recommendation:** Add trauma-informed email content for L3 including acknowledgment of past traumatic training attempts

**Gap L3-6: Professional Team Dynamics — Insufficient Detail**
- L3 has the deepest professional team relationships (4-7 years with BCBA, OT, SLP, sometimes neurologist)
- R1 mentions "position as complement" but provides no specific co-marketing or trust-transfer strategy
- How does the brand earn trust from a mom who has a 7-year relationship with a BCBA and has seen multiple products fail?
- **Recommendation:** Develop a specific "professional team validation" pathway for L3; consider clinical validation partnerships

- Universal symptom hooks — practical and well-chosen

**Gap GF-1: Sub-Group Proportion Validation**
- Sub-group percentages (A: 25%, B: 15%, C: 25%, D: 25%, E: 10%) are estimated from the archive
- No data source is cited for these proportions — are they based on survey data, traffic analysis, or expert estimation?
- **Recommendation:** Validate proportions through pop-up button selection analysis or customer survey

**Gap GF-2: Migration Rate Projections — No Data Source**
- Expected migration rates are stated (e.g., "Sub-Group A: 15-25% click through to level story; 40-50% post-purchase self-selection")
- These appear to be expert estimates, not data-driven projections
- **Recommendation:** Track actual migration rates from Day 14 post-purchase detection mail; adjust flow design based on real data

**Gap GF-3: Sub-Group C (Sensory-Difference) — No SPD-to-ASD Overlap Data**
- Sub-Group C is defined as "SPD-only or L2-like" — sensory processing disorder without autism
- No data on what percentage of the audience is actually SPD-only vs what percentage has undiagnosed Level 1 autism
- The email strategy difference between these two populations is significant
- **Recommendation:** Include SPD diagnostic overlap analysis in R3 (external research); ensure email content works for both populations

**Gap GF-4: The "Never Migrate" 50% — Content Strategy Gap**
- R4 notes that ~50% of GF (~25% of total audience) will NEVER migrate to a level
- This is a very large segment that receives only GF-level content for their entire customer journey
- No dedicated content strategy for permanent GF subscribers — all GF strategy currently focuses on migration
- **Recommendation:** Develop a dedicated email strategy for the "stays-in-GF-forever" segment; they need self-contained, level-independent content

## 6. CROSS-LEVEL GAPS

**Gap CL-1: No Geographic/Cultural Adaptation**
- Geography data exists (US 65%, UK 15%, AU/NZ 15%, CA 5%)
- No analysis of how avatar levels manifest differently across geographies
- Pre-K deadlines are US-centric; UK uses Reception Year; Australia has different enrollment patterns
- No cultural adaptation of language patterns (UK vs US: "nappy" vs "diaper", "toilet" vs "loo")
- **Recommendation:** Add geographic localization requirements to each avatar level profile

**Gap CL-2: No Pricing Sensitivity Calibration by Level**
- Pricing mentioned as brand constant: $34.99 single / $79.99 bundle / $119.99 premium
- No analysis of price sensitivity by level (anecdotally L3 is highest sensitivity at 40% Medicaid)
- No willingness-to-pay data for any level
- CTA aggressiveness varies by level but pricing framework does not
- **Recommendation:** Evaluate whether level-specific pricing or offers would increase conversion without alienating

**Gap CL-3: No Feedback Loop Between Levels**
- The avatar model is currently static — L1 mom who doesn't convert may become L2 mom over time
- No mechanism for detecting level migration (other than Day 14 PP detection, which only catches GF)
- A L1 mom who has been in email flow for 6 months without purchasing may actually have progressed to L2 psychology
- **Recommendation:** Build a level-migration detection system based on engagement patterns (declining hope, increasing skepticism signals in click behavior)

**Gap CL-4: Welcome Flow Variant — No Differentiation Detail**
- Tag routing logic says "Level 1 = DOC 2 + DOC 1, Level 2 = DOC 3 + DOC 1, etc."
- No differentiation detail beyond document assignment
- How many emails in each welcome variant? What's the sequence? What's the CTA cadence difference?
- **Recommendation:** Define full welcome flow structure per level for the implementation phase

**Gap CL-5: No Level 0 / Pre-Diagnosis Path**
- GF Sub-Group A (Diagnostic Entrants) is the closest to a "Level 0"
- No defined path for a mom whose child is being evaluated but not yet diagnosed
- This is a growing segment as autism awareness increases and diagnostic wait times extend (6-18 months in many regions)
- **Recommendation:** Consider defining a Level 0 segment for the pre-diagnosis journey, with different content needs than GF

## 8. RECOMMENDATIONS

### Priority 1 (Must Fix Before Implementation)

**P1-A: Produce R3 (External Research)**
The single biggest gap is the missing external research. Before implementing any avatar-level email flows, external validation is needed on:
- Competitor messaging and positioning by audience segment
- Academic literature on interoception training across autism severity levels
- Published data on late toilet training success rates (for L3)
- Market benchmarks for hope-level expectations and conversion timing

**P1-B: Fix L3's 60-Day Guarantee Problem**
The 60-day guarantee is rated "Very High" for L3 conversion but may be counterproductive if progress takes longer. Determine whether L3 needs a different guarantee structure or additional narrative framing.

**P1-C: Validate L1 Hope-Crash Rate**
Obtain actual refund/return data segmented by avatar level. If L1 has a disproportionately high refund rate due to hope-crash, the email strategy needs to under-promise and over-deliver more aggressively.

### Priority 2 (Should Fix Before Full Flow Design)

**P2-A: Develop Permanent GF Content Strategy**
With ~25% of total audience never migrating from GF, the email flows must be self-contained for this segment. Current strategy is migration-heavy; add permanent GF content pillars.

**P2-B: Build Level Migration Detection System**
Create engagement-based triggers to detect when a subscriber has moved between levels (especially L1→L2 after failed method cycling) and update their content path accordingly.

**P2-C: Map L3 Size Progression Trigger**
With adult-diaper-size fear as the primary L3 conversion trigger, map age/size cohorts in the existing subscriber base to align email timing with this transition.

### Priority 3 (Nice-to-Have Improvements)

**P3-A: Address Single-Mom Sub-Segment**
Create differentiated messaging for single mothers, especially in L2 and L3 where single-mom rates are highest (15-20% and 20-25% respectively).

**P3-B: Add Geographic Localization**
Build UK/AU-specific language variants into each avatar level profile (UK: "nappy", "reception year"; AU: "kindy", "prep").

**P3-C: Define Level 0 Pre-Diagnosis Path**
Consider creating a dedicated path for the pre-diagnosis subscriber who doesn't yet identify as an autism parent but is clearly in the evaluation process.

**P3-D: Revisit GF Sub-Group Proportion Assumptions**
Once the pop-up has been live for 3+ months, analyze actual button selection distribution to validate (or adjust) the estimated sub-group percentages.
