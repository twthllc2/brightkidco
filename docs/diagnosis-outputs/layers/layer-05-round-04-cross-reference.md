### Critical Insight: The Level 1 Uniqueness

**Level 1 is the ONLY level where Deadline Pressure dominates.** No other level has an external, non-negotiable timeline (Pre-K enrollment). This makes Level 1 the easiest to convert — and the most vulnerable to mis-calibrated messaging (amplifying panic instead of calming it).

## 6. EMAIL STRATEGY BY DOMINANT DIMENSION

### When Deadline Pressure (Panic) Is Dominant

**Most likely:** Level 1, Phase 2. Pre-K deadline approaching. 3-14 day decision window.

**Email Architecture:**
```
E1 (Welcome/Inbox): L1 — "It's not the method" validation
  → "He gets it but doesn't get it. That's not defiance. That's a signal-timing gap."
E2 (Mechanism): L1 + L4 — Science + Lena
  → "Most methods assume the signal arrives. For autistic kids, it often doesn't."
E3 (Last Day): L3 — Guarantee + L2 — Pull-up urgency
  → "60 days. Pre-K in 4 months. You have time."
```

**Forbidden:** Amplify panic ("You're running out of time!"), hard-sell urgency, "easy/quick" promises, neurotypical comparisons.

### When Social Pressure (Shame) Is Dominant

**Most likely:** Level 2-3, Phase 2-4. Chronic shame from years of comparison. 7-30+ day decision window.

**Email Architecture:**
```
E1 (Welcome/Inbox): L4 — Lena's voice, shame reduction
  → "I have two autistic sons. One's 6, still in the middle of it. I know what it's like."
E2 (Mechanism): L1 — Absolution + L5 — Normalization
  → "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't trained. You're not alone. Your child isn't broken."
E3 (Last Day): L3 — No-pressure guarantee
  → "We're not asking you to hope. We're asking you to observe. 60 days."
```

**Forbidden:** "Don't worry" (minimizing), "most children this age" (comparison trigger), "before/after" with shame implications, stacking social proof early.

### When Overwhelmed (Exhaustion) Is Dominant

**Most likely:** Level 2-3, Phase 3-4A. Years of cleanup, therapy, failure. 7-30+ day decision window.

**Email Architecture:**
```
E1 (Welcome/Inbox): L1 — Mechanism hope
  → "You didn't fail. The methods failed you. Here's what's different."
E2 (Mechanism): L1 + L4 — Practical relief
  → "Your house smells like pee. We know. Here's what changes that."
E3 (Education): L3 — Guarantee + relief framing
  → "60 days. Not asking for more energy. Just an observation period."
```

**Forbidden:** Adding to her to-do list, energetic approaches ("try this exciting new method!"), talking over her head about theory (interoception without practical), "easy/quick" promises.

## 7. STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

### 7.1 The Dimension Hierarchy

Each emotional dimension has a clear hierarchy across avatar levels:

```
Deadline Pressure:  Level 1 (★★★★) > Level 2 (★★★) > Level 3 (★★)
Social Pressure:    Level 2-3 (★★★★) > Level 1 (★★★) > GF (★★)
Overwhelmed:        Level 3 (★★★★) > Level 2 (★★★★) > Level 1 (★★) > GF-A (★★)
```

**One-Line Rule:** Deadline Pressure is Level 1. Social Pressure is everyone. Overwhelmed is Level 2 + Level 3.

### 7.3 The Overlap Coping Sequence

When multiple dimensions are active, use this sequence order:

```
ALL THREE → L4 (empathy) → L1 (mechanism) → L3 (guarantee)
FEAR + EXHAUSTION → L1 (mechanism gives hope) → L4 (empathy) → L3 (guarantee)
GUILT + EXHAUSTION → L4 (shame reduction first) → L1 (absolution) → L3 (no-pressure)
FEAR + GUILT → L1 (validation) → L4 (peer voice) → L3 (risk removal)
```

**Rule:** The dimension that causes the most emotional pain (Shame > Exhaustion > Panic) must be addressed first. Lead with the dimension that, if ignored, would cause the avatar to stop reading.
