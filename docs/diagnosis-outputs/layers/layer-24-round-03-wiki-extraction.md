## Finding 1: School/IEP Context Per Level

### Level 1 (Verbal, 3–5 years, ~35% of audience)

**School Context:** Pre-K enrollment is THE defining Level 1 event. It is an external, non-negotiable timeline that no other level experiences with the same intensity.

- **Pre-K enrollment conditional on daytime dryness** — the child must be potty trained to attend (DOC1 line 281, DOC6 line 39, BRAND_VOICE line 210)
- **Deadline Pressure: VERY HIGH ★★★★** — "He'll be the only one in pull-ups." This is the #1 conversion driver in the entire ecosystem (L5 R4 cross-reference, line 86)
- **Conversion potential: 15-25% ★★★** — the "Cash Cow" combination: Deadline Pressure × Phase 2 × Level 1 (L5 R4, line 176)
- **Decision period: 3-14 days** — fastest of all levels (L5 R4, line 292)
- **Urgency language: ALLOWED** — "Pre-K starts in X months — you have more time than you think" (L5 R4, line 161)
- **Tonality: 70% Hope / 30% Realism** (BRAND_VOICE line 215)
- **Primary triggers:** Pre-K deadline, Oh-Crap failure, OT recommendation (BRAND_VOICE line 216)
- **Unique:** Level 1 is the ONLY level where Deadline Pressure dominates as the primary emotional dimension (L5 R4, line 105)

**IEP Context for Level 1:** Minimal. At ages 3-5, IEPs are typically newly established or not yet in place. The Pre-K deadline is the dominant institutional pressure, not the IEP.

### Level 2 (Mixed verbal, 5–7 years, ~40% — LARGEST segment)

**School Context:** School transitions (Kindergarten, grade progression) are the institutional trigger, but IEP accommodations REDUCE the urgency compared to Level 1.

- **Deadline Pressure: MEDIUM ★★★** — School transitions exist but are less acute. IEP accommodations buffer the pressure (L5 R4, line 86)
- **IEP accommodations ≠ solution** — the email strategy for L2 Deadline Pressure must frame IEP as accommodation, not resolution (L5 R4, line 162)
- **School context change** is a primary trigger for Level 2 (BRAND_VOICE line 238)
- **Urgency BASELINE: LOW** — but TRIGGER-SPIKE: HIGH on school changes, withholding crises (L7 R4, line 755 — resolution from tonality cross-reference)
- **Tonality: 40% Hope / 60% Realism** (BRAND_VOICE line 237)
- **Decision period: 7-21 days** (BRAND_VOICE line 239)
- **Forbidden: Pre-K urgency hooks** — "Use Pre-K hooks for Level 2+3 audiences" is listed as a FORBIDDEN practice (DOC6 line 308)
- **BCBA context: Almost always** — Level 2 moms almost always have BCBA involvement, which intersects with school IEP planning (BRAND_VOICE line 233)

**IEP Context for Level 2:** IEPs are actively in place. The IEP provides accommodations (e.g., bathroom schedules, pull-up allowances) that reduce the acute pressure of potty training. However, this creates a false comfort — the IEP masks the underlying sensory gap. Email messaging must NOT oppose the IEP but position the product as "the missing complement."

### Level 3 (Non-verbal, 6-10+ years, ~25%)

**School Context:** No Pre-K deadline. School is already established. The urgency comes from puberty, hygiene, and adult diaper transitions — NOT school enrollment.

- **Deadline Pressure: LOW ★★** — No Pre-K deadline. Different urgency axis (L5 R4, line 86)
- **Primary triggers:** Adult diaper size approaching, school transition, puberty approaching, another mom's late-trainer story, GI crisis repeated (BRAND_VOICE line 261)
- **Phase 4B trigger:** Life events (puberty, adult diapers, school start) create "one more try" energy (DOC1 lines 350-351, 361)
- **Social Pressure from school: EXTREME** — "School looks. Office staff. 'He's 9 and uses words like unfortunately'" (L5 R4, line 45)
- **Tonality: 20% Hope / 80% Realism** (BRAND_VOICE line 260)
- **Decision period: 14-30 days** — longest of all levels (BRAND_VOICE line 262)
- **Forbidden:** NO Pre-K urgency, NO "easy/quick/fast/simple" language, NO "Don't give up!" (BRAND_VOICE lines 150-153, L22 R2 line 200)

**IEP Context for Level 3:** IEPs are deeply entrenched, multi-year plans with extensive accommodations. The school has adapted. The urgency is NOT about school readiness — it's about dignity, hygiene, and the approaching transition to puberty/adult diapers. The IEP is a settled reality, not a pressure point.

## Finding 2: Cross-Reference Connections

### Connection 1: Deadline Pressure × Phase × Level Matrix (from L5 R4)

The cross-reference layer mapped Deadline Pressure intensity across all 4 emotional phases and all 4 levels:

| Phase | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|
| Phase 1 (2.5-3.5y) | Weak | N/A | N/A |
| Phase 2 (3-4y) | STRONG ★★★★ | N/A | N/A |
| Phase 3 (4-6y) | MODERATE ★★★ (IEP reduces) | Active | Active |
| Phase 4 (6+y) | N/A | VERSION B: VERY STRONG ★★★★ | Low (Version A: given up) |

**Key insight:** IEP accommodations act as a PRESSURE RELEASE VALVE in Phase 3 for Level 1, reducing Deadline Pressure from STRONG to MODERATE. For Level 2, the school transition creates MEDIUM pressure. For Level 3, school is irrelevant to urgency — puberty/hygiene dominates.

### Connection 2: Trust Pyramid Tier 4 — IEP Adjustments (from L13)

The Trust Pyramid layer identified specific messaging adjustments for school/IEP context:

- **NO Pre-K urgency** for Tier 4 customers (school already settled with IEP)
- **"Before next school year" gently at most** — soft seasonal framing only
- **Regression cycles acknowledged as normal** — school transitions often trigger regressions

### Connection 3: Urgency Baseline vs Trigger-Spike (from L7 R4)

The tonality cross-reference resolved a contradiction between layers:
- L4 said Level 2 urgency = LOW (baseline)
- L3 said Level 2 urgency spikes HIGH on trigger events

**Resolution:** Distinguish BASELINE (LOW) from TRIGGER-SPIKE (HIGH). Level 2 urgency is low day-to-day but spikes HIGH during school changes, withholding crises, and comparison moments. Email strategy must detect which state the customer is in.

### Connection 4: School as Structural Urgency Category (from L5 R3)

The PLOS ONE research validated that school enrollment deadlines are not isolated events but one manifestation of **chronic urgency** — one of four universal stress categories for autism caregivers. The Deadline Pressure mom is in **pre-crisis mode** — the deadline hasn't passed, but the anticipation creates "panic with a thin layer of control."

### Connection 5: Customer Extraction — IEP as Repeated Word (from BRIGHTKIDCO_CUSTOMER_EXTRACTION)

The customer extraction document lists "IEP" as a **repeated word** in parent language, alongside autism, non-verbal, overwhelmed, ABA, OT, sensory, and interoception. IEP appears in the "methods tried" section as "IEP accommodations — School-based plan" (line 264).

## Summary

School/IEP context is the SECOND most important trigger ecosystem (after Overwhelmed) across the BrightKidCo diagnosis. It manifests differently per level:

1. **Level 1:** Pre-K enrollment = THE primary conversion driver. Urgency language allowed. 15-25% conversion potential.
2. **Level 2:** IEP accommodations = pressure buffer that masks the sensory gap. Must NOT use Pre-K hooks. "IEP ≠ solution" is the key message.
3. **Level 3:** School is settled. Urgency comes from puberty/hygiene, not enrollment. Dignity-first messaging only.

The wiki (23 prior diagnosis layers + client documentation) provides a complete framework for school/IEP messaging across all levels, with clear allowed/forbidden rules, tonality calibration, and email sequencing strategies.
