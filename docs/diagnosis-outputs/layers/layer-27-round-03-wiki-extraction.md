### Source A: Layer 27 — The Adult Diaper Transition (overnight-plan.md, lines 479-487)

> This is a unique emotional milestone specific to Level 3. As the child grows, they outgrow the largest specialty diaper size. The next option available is adult diapers. This transition represents a parent's deepest fear: that their child will never be independent.

> Raw quotes from research: "He has outgrown size 7 pull-ups. We are moving to adult diapers. I cried in the store." "He is 9. He has been in pull-ups his entire life. Specialty diapers now. The next size up is adult diapers."

> This milestone must be handled with extreme care. It cannot be used as a fear-based selling tactic. It can only be acknowledged with deep empathy and dignity. The appropriate framing is: "We know some families are facing size transitions that feel overwhelming. You are not alone in that."

### Source B: Layer 3 — Phase 4: Resignation Fork (overnight-plan.md, line 107)

> Phase 4 is called Resignation Fork. The child is typically 6 or older. The mom has accepted that her child is "late" or "maybe never." She plans stocks of pull-ups, maybe Medicaid. She has stopped actively trying. OR she has a new energy push before an important event like school start, travel, sibling birth, or school trip. Two versions coexist: Version A is "I gave up, he'll do it when he's ready" and Version B is "I'm trying one last time because X is coming." She is deeply hurt by failed hopes. She reacts to hope-reawakening through real science. She does not react to standard sales pitch or "easy" language. Decision triggers include upcoming life events, intensifying sibling comparison, pull-up size change to adult diapers, or approaching puberty with hygiene concerns.

### Source C: Layer 4 — Level 3 Avatar (overnight-plan.md, line 121)

> Level 3 represents approximately 25% of the audience. The child is non-verbal or minimally verbal, typically 6 to 10 or older, with high support needs. The pop-up selection is "Non-verbal or high support needs." The tag is avatar_level3. The hope level is 20% — hope is very low. The tonality is 20% hope and 80% realism. Phase 4 (Resignation Fork) is dominant. Dignity-first language is non-negotiable. The Level 3 mom is like the mother of Daniel — he has never been potty-trained. He wears specialty diapers size XL ordered through a Medicaid Waiver. He is non-verbal, has significant sensory processing differences, and attends special education each morning. She has tried a 3-week residential potty program, multiple ABA-based protocols, specialty toilet seats with side bars, and every sensory accommodation available. When nothing worked, she told the pediatrician: "I want to focus on dignity." He agreed. Daniel's special education school supports with diaper changes as part of his IEP. He is approaching the largest specialty diaper size. The next option is adult diapers. A comment from another mom in a special needs Facebook group caught her eye: "Maureen Bennie's son, 9.5 years, finally trained." That one sentence became her lifeline. She is not buying for quick results. She is buying for hope — carefully measured, scientifically grounded hope. The Level 3 audience is Medicaid-heavy (40%), has the longest history of failed attempts (5+ years), and requires the most careful messaging. Adult diaper transition is an approaching emotional milestone. Late-trainer stories are the master anchor. Urgency language is forbidden. "Add alongside specialty diapers" is the framing, never "replace your pull-ups."

## CROSS-REFERENCE FINDINGS (from L27 R4)

### Adult Diaper Purchase as Phase 4 Decision Trigger
The adult diaper purchase is a psychological breaking point that splits Phase 4 into Version A (acceptance/resignation) and Version B (last-push energy). This moment is the single strongest re-engagement trigger for Version B parents.

### Phase 3 "Pull-Up Size Crisis" as Pre-Adult Diaper Warning
Phase 3 parents experience an intermediate version of adult diaper fear — the pull-up size upgrade (size 7/8) signals they are approaching the adult diaper threshold. This is a "pre-trauma" moment that can drive conversion BEFORE the full adult diaper crisis.

### Fear of Inaction as Hidden Psychological Engine
The fear that "doing nothing = permanent adult diapers" operates as the underlying driver behind multiple conversion levers. For Version A (resigned), Fear of Action > Fear of Inaction. For Version B (last push), Fear of Inaction > Fear of Action.
