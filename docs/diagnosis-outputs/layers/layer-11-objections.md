### D1 — "I've tried everything. Why would this be different?"

**Intensity:** Very High (all levels)
**Kommentare Frequency:** 150+ direct mentions
**Phase:** Phase 2 (first attempt)

#### Giuliano's Counter-Language (R1)

"Sticker charts. Social stories. Timed sits. ABA protocols. 4 different potty seats. We've heard about all of them. You already know they don't work for your kid. Here's why none of them COULD work."

**Counter-Strategy Logic:**

#### Critical Gap (R2)

Giuliano names METHODS. Parents name THEMSELVES. The emotional weight is on personal exhaustion, not method inventory. Parents say "I've tried everything... I'm at a loss" — the counter-strategy must acknowledge the parent's exhaustion BEFORE reframing the method gap. Current copy reads like a product comparison, not a lifeline.

## TOP 10 RECOMMENDATIONS FOR COPYWRITERS

1. **Lead with empathy, follow with science.** Every counter-strategy should start with emotional validation ("We hear you. You've given everything you have.") BEFORE explaining the mechanism. Current copy is clinically correct but emotionally insufficient.

2. **Reframe "doesn't care" as "can't feel it."** B1 is the most dangerous objection because it appears to disprove the product. The reframe from defiance to sensory gap is the single most important copy move.

3. **Create a Daycare Battle Plan.** The #2 objection by volume (964 mentions) has zero counter-strategy. Template letter, pull-up-over-underwear compromise, staff communication guide.

4. **Add Night vs. Day educational content.** The #1 objection by volume (1,166 mentions) is completely absent from the framework. Night wetting is hormonal. Daytime pull-ups at night don't undo progress.

5. **Write a Regression Recovery Protocol.** Parents who experienced regression have a DIFFERENT emotional profile than those who never succeeded. "Regression means the skill exists" is the key reframe.

6. **Elevate B5 (Partner Not On Board) from "Additional" to core.** 112 mentions and growing. "You don't need anyone's permission to try this. 60-day guarantee = no commitment."

7. **Create a dedicated GI/Withholding lever.** B4 has 730 mentions — the #1 raw-frequency objection — but no dedicated conversion lever. "Withholding isn't defiance — it's a sensory feedback loop."

8. **Separate poop training from GI issues.** Many parents have poop-specific fear without diagnosed GI conditions. B4 partially covers this but needs broader treatment.

9. **Add a "give before ask" trust mechanism for C1.** Free readiness assessment, Lena's 2-minute video, or sensory checklist. Builds consistency bias before purchase.

10. **Create L3-specific B3 variant.** "He's not fighting you. He's fighting the sensation." For parents whose children kick, scream, and self-injure — gradual transition tips are too gentle.
