### LEVER 1: "It's not the method. It's a sensory gap."

**Full Definition (DOC1):**
Reframes the parent's experience from "I failed" to "the method was wrong." Simultaneously triggers D1 (shame — "Maybe I'm the problem") and D2 (method fatigue — "I've tried everything") absolution. Shifts the belief from "behavior issue" to "neurological issue."

**Psychological Effect:**
- D1+D2 absolution simultaneously
- Belief shift: from "behavior issue" to "neurological issue"
- Avatar feels seen, not judged

**How It Feels to the Avatar (DOC1):**
> "Finally, someone gets it. It was never my fault. It was never his fault. It was the wrong approach all along."

**Use Cases by Flow Type:**
- Welcome E2-E4
- FAQ mails
- Cart Recovery
- Browse Abandonment
- Winback

**DOC1 Line 286 — Phase 2 Context:**
> "This is the BIGGEST conversion lever in the entire avatar journey"

**DOC1 Line 616 — Counter Strategy:**
1. Name each method explicitly (sticker chart, social story, ABA, naked method, scheduled sits, timers, 4 different potty seats)
2. Explain WHY each one failed (sensory gap argument)
3. Reframe: "You haven't failed. You solved the wrong problem."

**Verbatim Language (DOC1):**
> "Sticker charts. Social stories. Timed sits. ABA protocols. 4 different potty seats. We've heard about all of them. You already know they don't work for your kid. Here's why none of them COULD work."

**Level-Specific Calibrations:**

| Level | Strength | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Level 1 | MEDIUM-HIGH | Level 1 mom has 1-3 failed attempts. Reframe acts as validation but not as heavy as Level 2. "You didn't fail, the method assumed something your kid couldn't do yet" works very well. Use: Welcome E2/E4, Cart Recovery E1, FAQ E1 |
| Level 2 | VERY HIGH | She has MASSIVE method cycling behind her. "I've tried everything" is her reality. Named methods + why failed = resonance. Use: Welcome E2 prominently, Welcome E4 expanded, FAQ E1 central, Cart Recovery E2 |
| Level 3 | HIGH | She has tried all methods, long and deep. BUT: careful — "method" for Level 3 isn't "Oh Crap," but "BCBA + OT + residential + private consultant." Acknowledge the REAL depth of her attempts. Use: Welcome E2/E4, FAQ mails |
| General Fallback | VERY HIGH | Universally applicable. Triggers D1+D2 for all levels. "You haven't been failing" reframe very strong. Use: Welcome E2/E4, Cart Recovery E1, FAQ E1, Browse Abandonment |

**Level-Specific Additional Levers (DOC2):**
- L1-B: "Gets it but doesn't get it" reframe — "Your kid understands potty conceptually but can't execute. That's a signal-timing gap, not a comprehension gap."
- L1-C: "Verbal doesn't mean independent" — "Verbal kids on the spectrum often understand more than they can act on. That's not defiance — it's signal regulation."

**Forbidden Usage:**
- Never say "It's not the method" without following up with the sensory gap explanation
- Never use for Level 3 without acknowledging the depth of their professional attempts (BCBA, OT, consultants)
- Never imply the parent was "using the wrong method" — reframe as "the method assumed something your kid couldn't do yet"
