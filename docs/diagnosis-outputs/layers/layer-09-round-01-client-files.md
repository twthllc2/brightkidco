## 3. TONALITY CALIBRATION PER FLOW STAGE (DOC1, Section 21)

### Pre-Purchase (Welcome, Cart, Browse, Checkout)
- **TONE:** Education + Soft Conversion
- **LANGUAGE:** Specific, mechanism-led, peer-to-peer
- **ENERGY:** Calm, confident, "we're not in a hurry"
- **DO:** Validate failures, explain mechanism, soft CTA
- **DON'T:** Hard-sell, urgency manipulation
- **Sample Opening:** "You came for the code. Stay for the reason nothing else worked."

### Conversion Moment (Cart-Recovery final, Checkout)
- **TONE:** Friendly Reminder + Permission Not To Buy
- **LANGUAGE:** "If today isn't right, that's a real answer"
- **ENERGY:** Decision-respect, "you decide"
- **DO:** Reduce friction, offer guarantee, soft permission
- **DON'T:** Pressure tactics, fake scarcity, guilt-tripping
- **Sample Opening:** "You left the 3+3 bundle in your cart. I'm not chasing — but I want to answer the question you might be sitting with."

### Transactional (Order Confirm, Shipping)
- **TONE:** Warm + Functional
- **LANGUAGE:** Direct, informative, branded
- **ENERGY:** Professional but human
- **DO:** Clear info, set expectations, soft upsell spot
- **DON'T:** Too much sales talk, treat like marketing mail

### Post-Purchase Early (Arrived, Day 0-7)
- **TONE:** Warm + Reassurance + Education
- **LANGUAGE:** "Here's what to expect"
- **ENERGY:** Welcome to the family
- **DO:** Confirm purchase, set realistic Week-1 expectations, introduce sensory prep tips
- **DON'T:** Hard re-sell, oversell ease of use
- **Sample Opening:** "Your underwear is here. Open the box together — let your kid touch it first. Sensory familiarity helps."

### Post-Purchase Mid (Day 14-60)
- **TONE:** Realistic + Patient + "It's normal"
- **LANGUAGE:** "Week 1 plateau is real. Hold the line."
- **ENERGY:** Coach + cheerleader
- **DO:** Manage expectations, celebrate mini-wins, troubleshoot
- **DON'T:** Promise quick wins, abandon them if no progress
- **Sample Opening:** "It's been 3 weeks. If you're not seeing what you hoped — that's expected. Here's what Week 4 typically looks like."

### Lifecycle (Replenishment, Winback, Sunset)
- **TONE:** Respectful Re-engagement
- **LANGUAGE:** "How are you doing?" / "Whichever path you take"
- **ENERGY:** Genuinely curious, low-pressure
- **DO:** Acknowledge time, no guilt, reopen conversation softly
- **DON'T:** "You forgot us" guilt-tripping, aggressive re-engage
- **Sample Opening:** "It's been a while. No pressure — but if your kid is still in pull-ups, here's what I'd suggest looking at next."

## 4. LEVEL-SPECIFIC VOICE CALIBRATIONS

### Level 1 Voice (DOC2 — Verbal, 3-5 years, ~35% audience)
- **Hope Level:** 70% Hope / 30% Realism
- **Science Density:** Medium-Low (introduce "interoception", don't assume)
- **Empathy Intensity:** Medium (she is not in deepest crisis)
- **Testimonial Length:** Short-Medium (3-8 sentences ideal)
- **CTA Aggressiveness:** Medium ("See the options" OK, "Make it happen" too direct)
- **Urgency:** High (Pre-K driven) — "Before school starts in September" OK, but not manipulative
- **Key Language:** "verbal", "almost trained", "gets it but doesn't get it"
- **Dominant Phase:** Phase 2 (First Real Attempt) — the "fat middle"
- **Empathy Approach:** "I see your situation" (not "I see your pain")
- **Sample Opener:** "If you tried Oh Crap last month and it fell apart, you're in the right place."

### Level 2 Voice (DOC3 — Mixed verbal, 5-7 years, ~40% audience)
- **Hope Level:** 40% Hope / 60% Realism
- **Science Density:** High (Kelly Mahler quote prominent, SPARK stat)
- **Empathy Intensity:** High (she is tired and hurt)
- **Testimonial Length:** Long (10-15 sentences ideal)
- **CTA Aggressiveness:** Low ("See options when you're ready")
- **Urgency:** Low (school usually already settled via IEP)
- **Key Language:** "prompt-dependent", "sensory regulation", "regression cycles"
- **Dominant Phase:** Phase 3 (Method Cycling) — the longest phase
- **Empathy Approach:** "I see how exhausting this has been" — deep observation > shallow sympathy
- **BCBA Respect:** Critical — always position as partner, "complement, not replace"
- **Sample Opener:** "You've done BCBA. You've done OT. You've done social stories, sticker charts, scheduled sits. Nothing's fully working — and you're starting to wonder if you missed something fundamental."

### Level 3 Voice (DOC4 — Non-verbal, 6-10+ years, ~25% audience)
- **Hope Level:** 20% Hope / 80% Realism
- **Science Density:** Medium-High
- **Empathy Intensity:** Very high
- **Testimonial Length:** Long+messy
- **CTA Aggressiveness:** Very low
- **Urgency:** Never
- **Key Language:** "dignity", "late trainer", "Maureen Bennie's son at 9.5"
- **Dominant Phase:** Phase 4 (Resignation)
- **Dignity-First:** NON-NEGOTIABLE
- **Empathy Approach:** Deep respect for years of trauma
- **Sample Opener:** "Your code's below. Maureen Bennie's son was 9.5 when he finally trained. They had stopped trying years before."

### General Fallback Voice (DOC5 — Cross-level, ~50% real volume)
- **Hope Level:** 50/50 Hope / Realism
- **Science Density:** Medium
- **Key Pattern:** "Whether...Or...If..." structure
- **Migration Goal:** Move subscribers to level-specific segments
- **Sample Opener:** "Whether you're 3 weeks into wondering, or 3 years into trying — if your kid isn't progressing like 'every method' promises they will — here's what most parents miss."
