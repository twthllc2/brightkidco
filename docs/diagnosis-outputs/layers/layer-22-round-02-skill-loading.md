### Skill 2: `copy` (TENZA Copywriting Operating System)
**Category:** Copywriting
**Relevance:** HIGH — Contains universal copywriting frameworks that must be adapted for the Level 2 audience's unique psychology.

**Key Frameworks Extracted:**

1. **Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)** — The most reliable framework for e-commerce. For Level 2:
   - **Problem:** "Your child sits on the toilet when prompted but never initiates. After years of ABA, you're stuck in compliance, not training."
   - **Agitate:** "Every scheduled sit reinforces prompt-dependency. The longer the pattern continues, the deeper it embeds. Meanwhile, GI complications from withholding are getting worse."
   - **Solve:** "The missing piece is interoception — the body's ability to feel internal signals. Body-Signal underwear creates sustained wetness sensation that builds the neural pathway rewards cannot."

2. **Before-After-Bridge (BAB)** — Transformation narrative:
   - **Before:** "3 years of BCBA protocols, OT sensory work, sticker charts, social stories. He goes for the teacher but not for you. You've spent $2,000 on methods that didn't address the root issue."
   - **After:** "Week 6: he pauses. He feels the wetness. He looks at you. First time in 6 years. His BCBA notices the shift."
   - **Bridge:** "The Body-Signal Learning Layer adds what behavior-based approaches cannot — sustained interoceptive input designed for hypo-responsive nervous systems."

3. **Storytelling Arc (Hook → Conflict → Resolution → CTA)** — For Level 2 testimonials:
   - **Hook:** "He's 6 and we're still on a schedule. He hasn't initiated once."
   - **Conflict:** "BCBA says fade prompts. OT says interoception. Neither explains what to DO."
   - **Resolution:** "Week 4: he paused. He felt something. Week 8: 30% initiation at home."
   - **CTA:** "See options when you're ready. 60 days to decide."

4. **AI-ism Banned List** — Critical for Level 2: words like "game-changer," "unlock," "revolutionize" trigger immediate skepticism. The Level 2 mom recognizes marketing language instantly.

5. **Subject Line Formulas** — Adapted for Level 2:
   - Curiosity: "Why she goes for the teacher, not for you"
   - Stat: "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't trained"
   - Question: "After 3 years of ABA, still in pull-ups?"
   - Personalization: "Your BCBA protocol + the missing piece"

**Application to Level 2 Paradox:**
The PAS framework maps perfectly to the Level 2 Paradox because the mom already KNOWS the problem (she's lived it for years). The "Agitate" step must be surgical — naming specific failed methods (Oh Crap, sticker charts, scheduled sits) without sounding like marketing. The "Solve" must be mechanism-first, not product-first. The BAB framework works because Level 2 moms respond to transformation narratives with specific timelines ("Week 4," "Week 8") rather than vague promises.

### Skill 3: `copywriting-body-copy-frameworks`
**Category:** Email Marketing Strategy
**Relevance:** HIGH — Provides the structural templates for email body copy that must be calibrated for Level 2's unique reading behavior.

**Key Frameworks Extracted:**

1. **AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)** — Adapted for Level 2:
   - **Attention:** "Your kid goes for the teacher but not for you. That's not defiance. That's prompt-dependency."
   - **Interest:** "After 20 hours of ABA per week for 3 years, your child has learned to comply — but not to feel. The sensory piece was never addressed."
   - **Desire:** "The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates sustained wetness sensation for 30-60 seconds. Designed for nervous systems that need MORE signal, not less."
   - **Action:** "Try it for 60 days. By your judgment. If it doesn't fit, full refund."

2. **"So What?" Test** — Every sentence must pass this filter for Level 2:
   - Does this inform? (She wants science, not fluff)
   - Does this build desire? (Through mechanism, not hope-selling)
   - Does this overcome an objection? (BCBA compatibility, timeline realism)
   - Does this create urgency? (Gently — no pressure tricks)
   - Does this establish credibility? (Kelly Mahler, SPARK stat, BCBA testimonials)

3. **Tone Calibration for Level 2:**
   - NOT promotional (triggers skepticism)
   - NOT urgent (school pressure is already resolved via IEP)
   - Educational + Empathetic combination
   - "Like an experienced OT who has seen many families"
   - Patient, deliberate, science-first

4. **Long-Form Email Rules** — Level 2 moms READ:
   - They tolerate 200-500+ word emails if the content is deep
   - Longer testimonials work (10-15 sentences)
   - Science boxes and mechanism explanations hold attention
   - "Bold reading path" technique: bold the key phrases so scanning still delivers the message

5. **CTA Optimization for Level 2:**
   - Soft CTAs: "See options when you're ready"
   - "60 days to decide" as primary CTA
   - Permission-not-to-buy energy
   - NO urgency tricks
   - Action verb + benefit: "Try the mechanism — 60-day guarantee"

6. **PS Lines for Level 2:**
   - Reinforce mechanism, not urgency
   - "PS: Kelly Mahler says rewards can't create internal body signals. This addresses what behavior-based approaches cannot."
   - Overcome BCBA objection: "PS: Many BCBAs incorporate sensation-based feedback once they see results."

**Application to Level 2 Paradox:**
The body copy frameworks must be inverted for Level 2. Standard e-commerce email logic says "urgency + discount = conversion." For Level 2, that formula BACKFIRES. The AIDA model's "Attention" step must use clinical specificity (not hype). The "Desire" step must be built through mechanism explanation, not feature lists. The CTA must feel like permission, not pressure. The Level 2 Paradox means the same mom who will read a 500-word email about interoception will instantly delete a "30% off — hurry!" email.
