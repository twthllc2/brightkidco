## 7. CONTRADICTION AND GAP ANALYSIS

### Contradiction C1: Level 1 Urgency vs. Cross-Level 50/50 Balance

**Layers in Conflict:** L18 (50/50 tonality) vs. L4 (L1 = 70% hope, HIGH urgency) vs. L17 (L1 Pre-Purchase = "Pre-K pressure OK")

**Description:** L18's cross-level tonality averages hope to 50/50 and urgency to Low-Medium. But Layer 4 defines Level 1's master driver as Pre-K urgency (70% hope), and Layer 17 explicitly allows "Pre-K pressure OK" for L1 Pre-Purchase. Cross-level mails at 50/50 hope will underserve Level 1 moms who need urgency to convert (15-25% conversion rate driven by Pre-K deadline).

**Severity:** 🟡 MEDIUM — Cross-level mails are designed for mixed audiences; level-specific Welcome Flows handle L1 urgency separately. But General Fallback (50% of volume) includes many L1-leaning moms who never selected a level.

**Resolution:** Accept the 50/50 compromise for cross-level flows. Add "soft urgency" language that doesn't alienate L3 but gives L1 a nudge: "considering this for the year ahead" instead of "Pre-K is coming." L18 §12 already acknowledges this ("Soft urgency OK").

### Contradiction C2: Level 3 Anchors Concentrated on L3-A Only

**Layers in Conflict:** L18 (Rule 2 anchors) vs. L4 (6 Level 3 symptom clusters)

**Description:** L18's cross-level anchors for Level 3 focus almost entirely on signal absence (L3-A: "doesn't react to wet diaper," "no progress in 4 years," "never shown a sign"). Layer 4 defines 6 Level 3 clusters (L3-A through L3-F), but L18 doesn't provide cross-level anchors for sensory overload (L3-B), withholding/GI (L3-C), communication lack (L3-D), physical resistance (L3-E), or size progression (L3-F).

**Severity:** 🟡 MEDIUM — Level 3 moms whose primary issue is sensory overload or GI withholding won't find themselves in cross-level mails. Since Level 3 is 25% of audience and GF is 50%, many L3-leaning moms in GF won't be reached.

**Resolution:** Add 2-3 Level 3 anchors to L18's cross-level library:
- L3-B: "Kids who scream when the toilet flushes"
- L3-C: "Kids who hold their bowel movements until bedtime"
- L3-F: "Kids who are outgrowing the largest pull-up size"

### Contradiction C3: L18 "No Fixed Ages" vs. L17 Level-Specific Ages

**Layers in Conflict:** L18 (Rule 3: age ranges only) vs. L17 (uses "3-5 years" for L1, "5-7 years" for L2)

**Description:** L18 Rule 3 says "NEVER write 'for 5-year-olds'" and mandates "3 to 10+" ranges. But L17's per-level calibrations use specific age ranges (L1: "3-5 years," L2: "5-7 years," L3: "6-10+ years") and age-specific openers ("Pre-K starts in [month]"). These operate in different contexts (cross-level vs. level-specific), but the transition between contexts isn't defined.

**Severity:** 🟢 LOW — This is a context-dependent rule, not a true contradiction. Cross-level flows use ranges; level-specific flows use targeted ages. But the boundary needs explicit documentation.
