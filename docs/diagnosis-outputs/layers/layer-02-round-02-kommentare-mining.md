## 14. Key Insights Summary

1. **Primary Customer: Parents of neurodivergent toddlers (age 2-4).** The vast majority of children discussed are aged 0-4 (95%), with age 3 being the single most common age mentioned (217 mentions).

2. **Top Diagnoses:** Autism (46), Speech Delay (38), and PDA (20) are the most common neurodevelopmental conditions. Constipation/Encopresis (153) is the most common physical comorbidity — a known issue in autistic children.

3. **Service Gap:** Daycare/school resistance to potty training methods is a massive unmet need. 304 parents mention daycare struggles. This represents a significant product/service opportunity.

4. **Emotional Profile:** Parents cycle between hope (666 mentions of progress/celebration) and burnout (104). They are highly motivated but systemically unsupported. Guilt and self-doubt (78) indicate they internalize struggles.

5. **Family Structure:** Most families are dual-parent, dual-income (305 daycare mentions). Only 6 explicit single parents identified. Twin parents (59) are overrepresented, suggesting unique challenges.

6. **Therapy Preferences:** This group strongly prefers relationship-based/play-based approaches over ABA. OCPT Method (gentle potty training) is the dominant framework, not clinical behavioral intervention.

7. **Global Reach:** While primarily English-speaking, the group has international membership across Europe, North America, UK, Australia, and New Zealand.
