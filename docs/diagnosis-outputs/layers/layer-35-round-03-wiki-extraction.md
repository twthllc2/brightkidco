**Scope constraint:** The 28 Buckets cannot be applied as-is to BrightKidCo. Each of the 4 avatar levels needs its own bucket set. Level 3 Maria's "trauma" (resigned exhaustion, adult diaper threshold) is categorically different from Level 1 Sarah's "trauma" (deadline pressure, daycare exclusion fear).

### D1 — The "Single Avatar" Assumption Is Pervasive

Every avatar-related wiki note assumes ONE ideal client persona:
- Tenza's 28 Buckets → Marcus (one person)
- Avatar emotional drivers → Marcus (one person)
- Avatar purchase triggers → Marcus (one person)
- Avatar Marcus core → Marcus (one person)

**Constraint for BrightKidCo:** The wiki's entire avatar infrastructure is built for single-persona brands. BrightKidCo's 4-level system requires a MULTI-PERSONA extension that doesn't exist in the wiki. This means:
1. Every avatar bucket needs 4× variants
2. Every emotional driver needs per-level calibration
3. Every purchase trigger needs per-level objection mapping
4. Every copy template needs per-level tonality rules

### D2 — Segmentation Is Axis-Limited

The wiki's segmentation knowledge operates on 5 axes:
1. **Engagement** (recency of opens/clicks/activity)
2. **Value** (AOV, CLV, purchase frequency)
3. **Behavioral** (viewed, carted, purchased)
4. **Demographic** (location, gender)
5. **Predictive** (Klaviyo algorithms)

BrightKidCo adds a 6th axis: **Avatar Level** (L1/L2/L3/General). This axis is:
- Not behavioral (it's about the child's communication level)
- Not demographic (it's about autism severity)
- Not predictive (it requires explicit tagging)
- Not engagement-based (a Level 3 parent can be Hot or Cold)

**Constraint:** The wiki has NO framework for a non-behavioral, non-demographic segmentation axis. The closest analog is "gender-based segmentation" (segmentation.md line 212-226), but even that is demographic. Avatar level is PSYCHOGRAPHIC-NEUROLOGICAL — a category the wiki doesn't address.

### D3 — Hope/Realism Ratio Is Uncharted

Layer 35 defines per-level tonality:
- Level 1: 70% hope, 30% realism
- Level 2: 40% hope, 60% realism
- Level 3: 20% hope, 80% realism
- General: 50/50

The wiki's tonality frameworks (from L34 R3 extraction) include:
- Tone spectrum: Promotional, Educational, Personal, Urgent, Celebratory, Empathetic
- Segment-based tone: VIP (exclusive), New (educational), Lapsed (empathetic)
- Voice matrix: Formal↔Casual, Serious↔Playful, Respectful↔Irreverent, Practical↔Aspirational

**Constraint:** NONE of these frameworks include a hope/realism ratio. The wiki has no vocabulary for expressing "how much hope vs hard truth should this email contain." This is a BrightKidCo-specific constraint that must be built from scratch.
