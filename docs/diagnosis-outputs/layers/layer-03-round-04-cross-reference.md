## 1. PHASE → EMOTIONAL DIMENSIONS MAPPING

Each emotional dimension (Layer 5) triggers different phases at different intensities:

### Matrix: Dimensions × Phases

| Emotional Dimension | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 | Phase 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Deadline Pressure** | Weak — no formal deadline yet (child 2.5-3.5) | **STRONG** — Pre-K deadline creating panic; Oh Crap failed; "6 weeks left" | **MODERATE** — School transition / IEP-driven; less urgent than P2 | **VERY STRONG** — Life event triggers (puberty, adult diapers, school start) |
| **Social Pressure** | **EMERGING** — First comparison pain; playground observation; pediatrician comment | **STRONG** — "Everyone else's kid is ahead"; MIL comments; Facebook groups | **INTENSE** — Deep shame; "my kid is 5 and still in diapers"; past judgment trauma | **EXTREME** — School looks; office staff; "He's 9 and uses words like 'unfortunately'" |
| **Overwhelmed** | Absent — still optimistic, hasn't hit cleanup fatigue | **RISING** — First bootcamp failure; carpet cleaning begins | **DOMINANT** — "My house is ruined"; "I'm constantly covered in pee"; 3 years of cleanup | **EXTREME** — "I don't have it in me anymore"; adult diaper logistics; total exhaustion |

### Detailed Mapping

**Phase 1 — Slow Realization:**
- Dominant Dimension: **None yet** (too early for pressure dimensions)
- Emerging Dimension: Social Pressure (first comparisons)
- Absent: Deadline Pressure, Overwhelmed
- Primary emotion: Worry (not crisis-level)
- The mom hasn't been hit hard by any dimension — she's still in the "wondering" phase

**Phase 2 — First Real Attempt:**
- Dominant Dimension: **Deadline Pressure** (Pre-K enrollment creates the strongest trigger)
- Secondary: Social Pressure (comparison with peers who succeeded)
- Emerging: Overwhelmed (first cleanup fatigue)
- Primary emotion: Defeat, Guilt, Self-doubt
- **This is where Deadline Pressure first strikes hardest** — the school deadline becomes real

**Phase 3 — Method Cycling:**
- Dominant Dimension: **Overwhelmed** (years of cleanup, laundry, failed methods)
- Secondary: Social Pressure (deep shame of being "that mom")
- Weakening: Deadline Pressure (many Level 2 families have IEP accommodations)
- Primary emotion: Skepticism, Exhaustion, Burnout
- The exhaustion of method cycling makes Overwhelmed the most active dimension

**Phase 4 — Resignation Fork:**
- Dominant Dimension (Version A — Given Up): **Overwhelmed** (extreme, passive exhaustion)
- Dominant Dimension (Version B — Last Push): **Deadline Pressure** (life event triggers)
- Undercurrent: Social Pressure (deepest shame, identity collapse)
- Primary emotion: Defeat, Resignation, Hidden Hope
- **Extreme sensitivity** to all dimensions — one wrong word in any dimension kills trust

### Objection Profile by Phase

**Phase 1 — Primary Objection: D2 ("I'm the problem")**
- At this phase she has tried very few methods, so D1 is weak
- But she's starting to doubt herself — "Should I be worried?", "Maybe I started too late"
- C1 (brand unknown) is significant — she's at top of funnel
- **Email focus:** Absolution → Normalization → Education (no objection-heavy copy needed yet)
