### Mapping Table: Pattern Type × Avatar Level

|---------|----------|----------|----------|---------------|----------|
| **A: Whether...Or...If...** | ✅ High | ✅ High | ✅ High | ✅ CRITICAL | Universal — bridges all levels through shared symptom |
| **B: Wherever...Or...If...** | ✅ Medium | ✅ High | ✅ Very High | ✅ High | Strongest for Phase 3-4 (L2-L3). Phase 1 sees "Wherever" as inclusive |

| **D: Some...Others...Wherever** | ✅ High | ✅ Medium | ✅ Medium | ✅ High | Phase 1 self-selects ("just started"), Phase 3-4 self-selects ("tried everything") |

### Level-Specific Pattern Preferences

**Level 1 (Sarah — Verbal, Hope-Intact):**
- Best patterns: **A** (journey bridge), **E** (symptom list — she finds "almost trained")
- Why: L1 moms have verbal kids who "almost trained." Pattern E's symptom list lets her self-select immediately. Pattern A's "3 weeks into wondering" mirrors her Phase 1 journey.
- Voice alignment: V1 (peer-to-peer), V11 (mechanism gentle), urgency OK
- Avoid: Pattern B heavy variants — "Wherever you are" feels too resigned for her hope level

**Level 2 (Lisa — Mixed, Method-Cycling):**
- Best patterns: **A** (method-count bridge), **B** (journey acknowledgment), **E** (symptom list)
- Why: L2 moms have tried 3-5+ methods. Pattern A's "tried 2 things or 12" validates her effort. Pattern B's "Wherever you are" respects her skepticism.
- Voice alignment: V7 (deep validation), V11 (mechanism heavy), V13 (science citations)
- Avoid: Pattern D without substance — "Some parents have just started looking" feels dismissive to a 5-year veteran

**Level 3 (Maria — Non-Verbal, Resigned):**
- Best patterns: **B** (journey acknowledgment), **C** (demographic inclusivity), **E** (symptom list)
- Why: L3 moms are deep in resignation. Pattern B's "Wherever you are — still in pull-ups, on a new method, or taking a break" acknowledges without judgment. Pattern C's "whatever your child's profile" avoids diagnosis pressure.
- Voice alignment: V10 (autism = identity), V12 (permission non-negotiable), V14 (long timelines)
- Avoid: Pattern A with urgency — "3 weeks into wondering" doesn't resonate with a 6-year journey

**General Fallback:**
- Best patterns: ALL — but **A** and **E** are mandatory in every GF mail
- Why: GF is the "holding pattern" until level detection. Every pattern type works because the audience is mixed.
- Voice alignment: Symptom-first, no labels, V6 strict
- Special: Pattern A MUST include the "If" pivot (mechanism hook). Without it, the pattern is just a list of situations.

### Stage-Specific Pattern Guidance

**Pre-Purchase (Welcome, Cart, Browse, Checkout):**
- **Mandatory:** Pattern A (opener) + Pattern E (recognition anchors)
- **Recommended:** Pattern D (for Welcome E1 — "Some parents have just started looking")
- **Forbidden:** None — all patterns work here
- **Cross-level rules active:** R1 (symptom over label), R2 (3-5 anchors), R3 (age ranges), R4 (verbal/non-verbal), R5 (timeline flexibility), R6 (low-bar success)
- **Tonality:** Education-first, no hard sell. 50/50 hope/realism for GF

**Conversion (Cart Recovery Final, Checkout):**
- **Mandatory:** Pattern B (permission framing) or Pattern A with permission close
- **Recommended:** Pattern E (symptom recognition to re-engage)
- **Forbidden:** Pattern D without substance — "Some parents" feels dismissive in a cart recovery context
- **Cross-level rules active:** ALL 6 — especially R6 (permission-not-to-buy)
- **Tonality:** Permission-not-to-buy. 45/55 hope/realism for GF. No urgency, no scarcity

**Transactional (Order Confirm, Shipping, Arrived):**
- **FORBIDDEN:** ALL pattern types
- **Why:** Transactional mails are information-first (order details, tracking, timeline). The "Whether...Or...If..." pattern is persuasion language — inappropriate when she just bought.
- **Exception:** NONE. Even the Arrived mail should use direct language ("Your underwear is here"), not pattern language.
- **Cross-level rules active:** R1 (medium), R3 (low), R5 (medium — set expectations)
- **Tonality:** Warm + functional. 70/30 hope/realism (celebrate the purchase)

**Post-Purchase Early (Day 0-7):**
- **Mandatory:** Pattern E (recognition anchors for "what to look for")
- **Recommended:** Pattern C (demographic inclusivity — "Whatever your child's profile")
- **Avoid:** Pattern A (full "Whether...Or...If..." feels sales-heavy in a post-purchase context)
- **Cross-level rules active:** R1 (high), R2 (high), R3 (high), R4 (high), R5 (CRITICAL), R6 (CRITICAL)
- **Tonality:** Reassurance + education. 50/50 hope/realism for GF. Mini-wins as success

**Post-Purchase Mid (Day 14-60):**
- **Mandatory:** Pattern E (mini-win recognition anchors)
- **Recommended:** Pattern B (journey acknowledgment — "Wherever you are at Day 30")
- **Avoid:** Pattern D — "Some parents have just started looking" is tone-deaf to a Day 30 customer
- **Cross-level rules active:** R2 (high), R5 (high — timeline ranges), R6 (CRITICAL — celebrate mini-wins)
- **Tonality:** Coach + patience. 45/55 hope/realism for GF. Plateau normalization

**Lifecycle (Replenishment, Winback, Sunset, Review):**
- **Mandatory:** Pattern B (permission + re-engagement)
- **Recommended:** Pattern A (journey bridge for winback), Pattern E (symptom re-engagement)
- **Avoid:** Pattern D without context — "Some parents" feels generic after months of silence
- **Cross-level rules active:** R1 (high), R2 (medium), R3 (high), R4 (high), R5 (high), R6 (CRITICAL)
- **Tonality:** Respectful re-engagement. 40/60 hope/realism for GF. Zero guilt

## 6. PATTERN VARIANTS NEEDED FOR DIFFERENT FLOW STAGES

### Currently Existing Variants (from L20 R1)

| Variant | Structure | Best Stage |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| A1: Journey bridge | "Whether [short time] into [exploring], or [long time] into [trying]" | Pre-Purchase |
| A2: Demographic bridge | "Whether [communication A] or [B], [age A] or [B], [diagnosis A] or [B]" | Pre-Purchase, PP-Early |
| A3: Method validation | "Whether you tried [method A], [B], [C], or something else" | Pre-Purchase (Welcome E4) |
| B1: Permission closer | "Wherever you are — [state A] or [state B]" | Conversion, Lifecycle |
| C1: Profile inclusivity | "Whatever your child's profile — [variable A] or [B]" | PP-Early |
| D1: Journey segment | "Some [group A]. Others [group B]. Wherever you are" | Pre-Purchase, Lifecycle |
| E1: Symptom OR-List | "[Symptom A], [B], [C], or [D] — [mechanism pivot]" | All except Transactional |
| F1: Conditional pivot | "If [scenario A] or [B] — [universal mechanism]" | Conversion, Lifecycle |

### Missing Variants Needed by Stage

**Post-Purchase Mid — Coach Variant (MISSING):**
```
"Wherever you are at Day [N] — [mini-win A], [mini-win B], or still
waiting — if [shared signal], that's the nervous system wiring."
```
**Why needed:** Current variants are pre-purchase focused. Post-purchase mid needs a COACH energy variant that celebrates mini-wins while acknowledging the plateau. The "Wherever" opener works, but the "If" pivot must deliver REASSURANCE, not MECHANISM.

**Post-Purchase Early — Expectation Variant (MISSING):**
```
"Whatever your child's profile — [demographic A] or [B] — the first
[timeframe] often looks like [normal struggle]. Here's what to
actually look for: [mini-win list]."
```
**Why needed:** Current C1 variant works for demographics, but doesn't set TIMELINE expectations. PP-Early needs to combine demographic inclusivity with timeline flexibility (R5) in one pattern.

**Lifecycle — Re-engagement Variant (MISSING):**
```
"Wherever you are now — [current state A], [B], or [C] — maybe this
lands, maybe it doesn't. [Single value piece]."
```
**Why needed:** Current B1 variant is permission-focused. Lifecycle re-engagement needs a SOFTER variant that offers value before asking for action. "Maybe it lands, maybe it doesn't" is the permission energy, but the content after must be VALUE, not PRODUCT.

**Conversion — Guarantee Variant (MISSING):**
```
"Whether you've tried [N] things or [N+10] — the 60-day guarantee
covers you. Either your kid [responds] within 60 days, or full
refund. No pressure either way."
```
**Why needed:** Current variants don't embed the guarantee IN the pattern. Cart recovery needs the guarantee as the "If" pivot — the mechanism promise IS the guarantee.

**SMS Variant (MISSING — V24 alignment):**
```
"[Symptom hook]. [CTA link]. -Lena"
```
**Why needed:** SMS is max 160 chars. No room for "Whether...Or...If..." structure. Need a COMPRESSED symptom-first variant that uses ONE symptom from Pattern E, not the full list.
