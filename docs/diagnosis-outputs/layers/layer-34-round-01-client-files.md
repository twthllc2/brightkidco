## 8. KEY INSIGHTS FOR BRIGHTKITCO

### The Emotional Journey (what parents go through):
1. **Excitement** -- "She's showing all the signs!"
2. **Early success** -- "Day 1 was pretty good!"
3. **Setback** -- "Day 2 was terrible"
4. **Determination** -- "We've got five days to nail this"
5. **Frustration** -- "Nothing is working"
6. **Despair** -- "I feel like a failure"
7. **Isolation** -- "I don't know what else to do"
8. **Seeking help** -- "Please help, any advice welcome"

### The Poop Problem (biggest single issue):
Poop refusal/withholding is THE dominant theme across all 8 files. It accounts for more distress than any other single issue. Parents describe:
- Children holding poop for 7-12 days
- Hospital visits for impaction
- Laxative dependency concerns
- Fear-based refusal after painful constipation
- Children hiding to poop in underwear
- Strategic holding until pull-up/nap time

### The School Pressure Point:
School enrollment is the #1 external pressure driver. Parents of 3.5-4 year olds are most desperate because:
- Preschool requires potty training
- Kindergarten requires full independence
- Teachers won't help with toileting
- Social stigma ("those parents who haven't bothered")

### The Daycare Disconnect:
Multiple parents report daycare undoing their progress:
- Daycare puts pull-ups back on
- Daycare won't implement reward systems
- Child won't use unfamiliar toilets
- No bathroom in classroom
- Staff can't do individual prompting

### Gender Differences (as perceived by parents):
- Boys are perceived as harder to train
- Boys are more likely to pee standing/asleep
- Girls are perceived as more verbal about needs
- Both genders equally struggle with poop
