## 4. COMPLETE Banned Claims List

### 4.1 Outcome Promises — NEVER MAKE
- "Your child will..."
- "In 30 days your child will..."
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Your child WILL train" (L3)
- "Make this the year he trains" (L3)

**Source:** DOC6-USAGE-GUIDE.txt, Claude-Project-Instructions.txt, DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt

### 4.2 Medical/Therapeutic Claims — NEVER MAKE
- "His sensory issues will improve"
- "Reduce meltdowns"
- Product cures, solves, or fixes autism
- Any claim the product treats or mitigates a medical condition

**Source:** BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

**Cross-reference (L11):** Objection B4 (GI issues, 730 mentions) is a medical crisis area. Never position the product as a medical intervention for GI/withholding. Always defer to "your GI doctor leads."

### 4.3 Normalcy Claims — NEVER MAKE
- "Your child will be normal"
- "He'll be normal"
- "Become like other kids"
- "Other kids his age..."
- "Just like neurotypical kids"

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt, BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

**Cross-reference (L11):** Objection D4 (too old/severe, 339 mentions) and C2 (kids like mine, 76+ mentions) are directly triggered by comparison language. Parents of autistic children experience comparison as identity erasure.

### 4.4 Comparison Claims — NEVER MAKE
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- Position neurotypical kids as the standard
- Compare Level 1/2/3 success stories across levels

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt

### 4.5 Financial Claims — NEVER MAKE
- "$876 a year wasted!" — feels marketing-like
- Math reframes for Level 3 (Medicaid reality)

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION.txt

**Cross-reference (L11):** Objection C3 (worth the money, 11 mentions) shows price sensitivity. ~25% of audience uses Medicaid. Financial pressure language triggers shame for L3 parents.

### 4.6 Fabricated Entities — NEVER CREATE
- Fake parent names
- Fake quotes
- Fake success stories
- Fake research
- Fake statistics

**Source:** BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

**This is the absolute floor.** Any fabricated attribution that surfaces publicly damages brand irreparably.

## 7. Escalation Procedure

### When No Real Quote Exists

**Step 1:** Search the RS PDFs thoroughly (Autistic_Parent.pdf, Autistic_language_Bank.pdf, Autistic_MIRO_m.pdf)

**Step 2:** Search Kommentare PDFs for tone-adjacent language (not for direct quotes — for emotional register)

**Step 3:** If no match found, **STOP. Do NOT fabricate.**

**Step 4:** Use universal symptom descriptions instead of a specific quote
> "When in doubt, use the universal symptom descriptions instead of specific quotes" — BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

**Step 5:** FLAG to Ayoub/Yurishizu with:
- What the email needs (the emotional beat, the objection being addressed)
- What was searched (which RS files, which Kommentare sections)
- Why no existing quote fits
- Suggested universal symptom description as alternative

**Step 6:** Ayoub escalates to Brand Strategist or accepts a different approach

**Source:** DOC6-USAGE-GUIDE.txt, Claude-Project-Instructions.txt, BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md

### When a Claim Cannot Be Verified

**Scientific claims:** If a statistic or claim cannot be traced to a peer-reviewed source or the BKC survey data, do NOT include it. Flag for verification.

**Unverified claims to watch for (from L1):**
- "98% of autistics have interoceptive differences" — Source is Autism Parenting Magazine (not peer-reviewed). Use with extreme caution or omit.
- "3x more absorption than regular underwear" — No third-party verification. Use as supporting claim only, never as lead claim.
- "Less than 1% of parents use the guarantee" — No external audit. Do not use.

**Source:** Layer 1 Product Diagnosis (Section 8.3)
