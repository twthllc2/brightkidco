## SECTION A: ANTI-FABRICATION RULES FOUND IN WIKI

### A1 — AI Ad Compliance Framework (7-Step)
**Source:** /root/wiki/tenza-marketing/_hub-operations/__enqueued__/ai-ad-compliance-framework.md

A structured compliance framework from MIM AI Masterclass:

1. **Know Why Compliance Matters** — Ad platforms align with FTC government guidelines. Platforms dislike anything misleading or risky.
2. **Avoid "Money + Time" Promises** — "Make $10,000 in 30 days" triggers immediate flags. Never combine specific dollar amounts with specific timeframes.
3. **Use Creative Phrasing** — Swap "Passive Income" with "Passive Returns." Mention client numbers, not direct dollars.
4. **Provide Proper Disclaimers** — Explain big wins aren't typical. State average outcomes when possible.
5. **Watch Trademark Usage** — Avoid brand names (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok) in ads.
6. **Keep Up with Updates** — Visit FTC press releases regularly. Rules shift when industries face fines.
7. **Simplify Messaging** — Hype-free ads get flagged less. Serious buyers prefer honest messaging.

**Key Principle:** "Compliance is a quality filter. Writing honest, substantiated, non-hype ads forces genuine value communication."

### A2 — TCPA Compliance Checklist (SMS Marketing)
**Source:** /root/wiki/tenza-marketing/_hub-operations/__enqueued__/compliance-checklist.md

Pre-launch compliance rules for SMS campaigns (TCPA violations carry $500-$1,500 fines per message):

- Written opt-in consent documented for every phone number
- Opt-in record must include: timestamp, source, IP address, exact consent language
- No pre-checked opt-in boxes
- "Reply STOP to opt out" in every promotional SMS
- Brand name disclosed in message
- Frequency disclosure included
- "Msg & data rates may apply" disclosure
- STOP keyword processes immediately
- Quiet hours: 9PM-8AM recipient local timezone
- Frequency cap: max 5-10 SMS per month per subscriber

### A3 — Trust Asymmetry Principle
**Source:** /root/wiki/tenza-marketing/_hub-business-strategy/__enqueued__/trust-asymmetry.md

Core principle: "Small, trust-building actions produce disproportionately large effects on conversion." Trust operates through:
- Understanding bias (if you understand me, I trust you)
- Disarming honesty (brutal transparency disarms scepticism)
- Relatability bias (I trust people like me)
- Effort perception bias (I trust those who've invested effort)

**Anti-fabrication implication:** Dishonest claims destroy the trust asymmetry that drives conversion. Fabrication is anti-conversion.

### A4 — Brand Congruency Rule (POP-UPS Rule 9)
**Source:** /root/wiki/tenza-marketing/__enqueued__/(TENZA)_POP_UPS--59-rule-9-brand-congruency.md

"Your pop-up is not an isolated advertisement; it is the brand's first direct conversation with a new visitor." Must feel like a natural, trustworthy extension of the website experience. Generic templates create "a jarring and untrustworthy experience."

**Anti-fabrication implication:** All customer-facing elements must authentically represent the brand — no bait-and-switch, no misleading visual cues.

### A5 — Copy Skill: AI-ism Banned List
**Source:** /root/.hermes/skills/copy/SKILL.md (Section 6)

Never use: dive into, unlock, game-changer, in today's fast-paced, leveraging, revolutionize, transformative, empower, disrupt, best-in-class, cutting-edge, world-class, reimagine, supercharge, synergy, holistic, think outside the box, low-hanging fruit, dot-com bubble, ninja, guru, rockstar.

**Anti-fabrication implication:** These phrases fabricate authority and value where none exists. They are empty superlatives that erode trust.

### A6 — Copy Skill: Quality Gate
**Source:** /root/.hermes/skills/copy/SKILL.md (Section 8)

Pre-send verification checklist:
- No AI-isms from banned list
- Brand voice matches brief
- Subject line A/B testable (2+ variations)
- CTA specific and action-oriented (one per section)
- Length matches brief requirements
- Read aloud: natural flow?
- Would you click this if you received it?

- Quiet hours legally required for SMS (9PM-8AM)

### B3 — Consent & Transparency Standards
- Written opt-in required for SMS
- Opt-out must be immediate and frictionless
- Brand name must be disclosed in messages
- Frequency disclosure required
- "Msg & data rates may apply" mandatory

### B4 — Brand Safety Through Congruency
- Pop-ups must match brand aesthetic exactly
- Generic templates = untrustworthy experience
- All touchpoints must feel like natural brand extensions
- Visual consistency builds trust; inconsistency destroys it

A search for "anti-fabrication" returns zero results — the concept exists only in client files.

### Gap 2 — No FTC Compliance Hub
FTC is mentioned once (in the AI Ad Compliance Framework) but there is no dedicated FTC compliance page covering:
- Endorsement guidelines (16 CFR Part 255)
- Testimonial disclosure requirements
- "Results not typical" / "Your results may vary" standards
- Substantiation requirements for health/performance claims

### Gap 3 — No Health Claim Substantiation Rules
BrightKidCo operates in a health/wellness adjacent space (autism, sensory, toileting). The wiki has no guidance on:

- What constitutes a "health claim" vs. "structure/function claim"
- Required disclaimers for products touching developmental/medical territory
- Substantiation standards for implied health benefits

### Gap 4 — No Testimonial Ethics Framework
The wiki has testimonial CAPTURE protocols (testimonial-request-protocol, testimonial-request-system) but NO rules about:
- What testimonials can and cannot claim

- How to handle testimonials that imply health outcomes

### Gap 5 — No Email-Specific Anti-Fabrication Rules
The copy skill has quality gates and banned lists but no email-specific anti-fabrication rules like:

- No misleading subject lines (beyond "A/B testable")
- No deceptive "RE:" or "FWD:" prefixes
- No fake personalization that implies a real relationship

### Gap 6 — No Children's Privacy / COPPA Awareness
BrightKidCo targets parents of children. The wiki has zero content on:

### Gap 7 — No Disability / Autism-Sensitive Language Guide

- Avoiding inspiration porn / pity-based marketing
