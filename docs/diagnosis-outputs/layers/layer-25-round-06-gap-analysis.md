## CONTRADICTIONS IDENTIFIED

### C1: Level 1 Partner Conflict — Mild Disagreement vs. Active Blame
- **R1 (Level 1 section):** "Partner conflict is less common at Level 1... more about disagreement on approach than active sabotage"
- **R4 (Section 3.1):** Level 1 partner conflict = "★★ Medium" with "Blame & method disagreement" as conflict type, partner "Engaged but blaming"
- **Resolution:** R1 frames L1 conflict as mild (timing/method disagreement). R4 frames it as moderate with blame dynamics. The verbatim quotes in R1 (K1: "my husband is pointing the finger at me") support R4's stronger framing. R1's "less common" is relative to L2, not absolute — but R4 may overstate by importing L2 dynamics into L1.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM — need to verify whether L1 blame quotes (K1) are from L1 or L2 families

### C2: Partner Conflict Intensity Ranking — L2 Highest vs. L3 Highest
- **R1:** "Partner conflict is explicitly documented as most common at Level 2"
- **R4 (Section 3.1):** L2 = ★★★★ High, L3 = ★★★★★ Very High
- **Resolution:** R1 measures frequency (how often it's discussed). R4 measures severity (how damaging it is). L2 has the most partner conflict discussions; L3 has the most severe consequences (denial, abandonment, divorce). Both can be true — L2 is the epicenter by volume, L3 by intensity.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — reconcilable with different metrics

### C3: "Share This With Your Partner" — Forbidden vs. Recommended
- **R2 (Forbidden language):** "❌ 'Share this with your partner' — adds to her burden"
- **R2 (Recommended CTA pattern):** "Primary CTA: 'Get the Evidence Guide' (button — tangible to share)"
- **Resolution:** The distinction is between PUSHING sharing ("share this with your partner" as a CTA) vs. ENABLING sharing ("get the evidence guide" as something she can choose to share). The former adds pressure; the latter removes friction. R2's own framework resolves this — but a copywriter reading both sections could be confused.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — resolved by framing distinction

### C4: B5 Objection Ranking — #11 by Frequency vs. "Cross-Cutting Amplifier"
- **R4 (Section 2.1):** "Layer 11 ranks it #11 by raw frequency (112 mentions) but underestimates its emotional weight"
- **R4 (Section 2.1):** "Estimated true partner conflict surface area: 400-600+ mentions"
- **Resolution:** Direct partner mentions are 112 (#11). But partner conflict appears as a secondary factor in D1 (tried everything), D2 (I'm the problem), Overwhelmed (518 mentions), and NEW-2 (daycare conflicts). The 400-600 estimate is plausible but unverified — it's an extrapolation, not a count.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM — the 400-600 estimate needs actual re-counting to validate
