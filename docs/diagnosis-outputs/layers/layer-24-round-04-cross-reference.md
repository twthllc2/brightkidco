## Connection Analysis

### Connection A: The Unified School/IEP/BCBA Gatekeeper System

For Level 2 (40% of audience, LARGEST segment), three professional systems overlap:

```
BCBA (therapy authority)
  ↓ writes goals for
IEP (school authority)
  ↓ accommodates
School Setting (daily reality)

All three gate the purchase decision:
- BCBA says "this makes sense alongside what we're doing"
- IEP team says "bring data to the next review"
- School says "we'll support the transition"
```

L24, L23, and L4 all independently confirm this triad. The conversion strategy must address ALL THREE gatekeepers, not just one.

**Evidence:**
- L24: "Discuss with your IEP team" + "Bring it up at the next IEP meeting"
- L23: "Bring 30 days of data to your next BCBA session" + fact-sheet strategy
- L4: "Your BCBA has built the behavioral foundation. This adds the sensory layer."

### Connection B: The Pressure Release Valve Mechanism

IEP accommodations act as a PRESSURE RELEASE VALVE that reduces Deadline Pressure across phases:

```
Phase 2 Level 1: Deadline Pressure = STRONG (no IEP yet)
  → Pre-K enrollment creates acute crisis
  → Fastest conversion (3-14 days)

Phase 3 Level 2: Deadline Pressure = MODERATE (IEP in place)
  → School adapted, urgency reduced
  → But school CHANGE can spike it back to HIGH
  → Slower conversion (7-21 days)

Phase 4 Level 3: Deadline Pressure = LOW (IEP with diaper support)
  → School fully adapted, zero urgency
  → Urgency from puberty/hygiene, not school
  → Slowest conversion (14-30+ days)
```

L24's finding that "Pre-K pressure is GONE for Level 2" and "NO school pressure for Level 3" is EXPLAINED by the IEP accommodation mechanism. The IEP doesn't just reduce pressure — it eliminates the specific institutional barrier (school enrollment requirement) that drives Level 1 conversion.

### Connection C: The ABA-School Paradox

L24's "School trained ≠ home trained" paradox and L23's "ABA failure mode" are the SAME finding described from different angles:

| L24 Language | L23 Language | Underlying Reality |
|---|---|---|
| "He goes for the teacher but won't even try at home" | "Behavioral compliance without sensory awareness" | ABA taught compliance at school; interoception missing at home |
| "He's prompt-dependent, the BCBA confirms it" | "Child learns to sit but not to feel" | Schedule-based training created prompt dependency |
| "After 30 hours of ABA, he sits — but doesn't go on his own" | "The child is trained to comply, not trained to feel" | Behavioral protocol succeeded; sensory protocol needed |
| "She trained at school then forgot it all over summer break" | "Transfer to home life is the critical gap" | Context-dependent learning without internalized signals |

This connection is THE conversion mechanism for Level 2. The parent sees: "My child CAN do it at school (ABA compliance) but CAN'T do it at home (no interoception)." The product message: "ABA handles behavior. This handles the sensory gap. Together, they close both gaps."

### Connection D: School as Phase Transition Trigger

School events trigger phase transitions across all levels:

| School Event | Phase Transition | Level | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-K enrollment deadline | Phase 1 → Phase 2 | Level 1 | External deadline forces first real attempt |
| Oh Crap failure before Pre-K | Phase 2 → Phase 3 | Level 1 | Failed method + deadline = method cycling |
| Elementary school transition | Phase 3 spike | Level 2 | IEP review, new environment, peer awareness |
| IEP team "maxed out" | Phase 3 → Phase 4 | Level 2 | Professional acknowledgment of limitation |
| Middle school transition | Phase 4B trigger | Level 3 | "Diaper support won't transfer easily" |
| Adult diaper size approaching | Phase 4A deepening | Level 3 | Hygiene crisis, not school-related |

## Contradictions or Gaps Identified

### Contradiction 1: Level 2 School Status Numbers

**L24 R1 states:** "Mainstream with 1:1 aide: ~40%, Special needs preschool/school: ~50%"
**L4 states:** Therapy load 20-30 hrs/week ABA for Level 2

**Issue:** If 50% of Level 2 kids are in special needs schools, the "school context change" trigger (elementary transition) applies to only ~40% in mainstream settings. The L24 R3 wiki extraction says "School context change is a primary trigger for Level 2" but this may only apply to the mainstream subset.

**Resolution:** The school context change trigger applies DIFFERENTLY by school setting:
- Mainstream + aide (40%): Elementary transition = acute trigger (IEP review, peer awareness)
- Special needs (50%): School context change = softer trigger (program change, new staff)
- Mainstream without support (10%): Highest urgency, but smallest segment

**Gap:** L24 R3 does not distinguish between school settings when listing triggers. Should specify which 40% experiences acute school transition pressure.

### Contradiction 2: Level 3 Deadline Pressure Rating

**L24 R2 states:** Level 3 Deadline Pressure = "LOW ★★ (no Pre-K deadline) for Version A, VERY STRONG for Version B"
**L4 states:** Level 3 urgency = NEVER
**L3 states:** Phase 4B Deadline Pressure = "VERY STRONG (life event triggers)"

**Issue:** L24 R2 assigns "VERY STRONG" Deadline Pressure to Level 3 Version B, but L4 says urgency is "NEVER" for Level 3. L3 (Emotional Phases) sides with L24 R2: Phase 4B has VERY STRONG Deadline Pressure from life events.

**Resolution:** L4's "NEVER" applies to SCHOOL-RELATED urgency specifically (Pre-K enrollment). L24 R2 and L3 are correct that non-school life events (puberty, adult diapers, school transition for dignity) create deadline pressure. The contradiction is definitional: L4 means "never use school as urgency lever" while L3 means "life events create deadline energy."

**Recommendation:** Clarify L4's "NEVER" to "NEVER for school enrollment urgency. Life event urgency (puberty, adult diapers) is allowed with dignity-first framing."

### Contradiction 3: IEP Rate for Level 1

**L24 R1 states:** Level 1 IEP rate is not explicitly stated but implied LOW ("IEP Language: Not applicable" in delta table)
**L24 R3 states:** "IEP Context for Level 1: Minimal. At ages 3-5, IEPs are typically newly established or not yet in place."

**Issue:** Some Level 1 children DO have IEPs — early intervention IEPs exist for 3-5 year olds. The blanket "not applicable" may miss a subset of Level 1 parents who have IEP experience.

**Gap:** No data on what percentage of Level 1 children have early intervention IEPs. This affects whether "IEP as trust marker" language could work for a Level 1 sub-segment.

### Contradiction 4: School Transition as Level 3 Trigger — Timing Mismatch

**L24 R1 states:** Level 3 school transition = "Middle school starts next year. The diaper support won't transfer easily."
**L4 states:** Level 3 child age = 6-10+ years, Phase 4 dominant at ~55%

**Issue:** Middle school starts at age 11-12. If Level 3 children are 6-10+ years, the middle school trigger applies only to the older end of the range. For 6-8 year olds, the school transition trigger is weaker or absent.

**Gap:** L24 R1 does not specify which age range within Level 3 experiences the middle school transition trigger. Should distinguish between:
- Ages 6-8: Elementary school is settled, no imminent transition
- Ages 9-10+: Middle school approaching, diaper support transfer anxiety
- Ages 11+: Active middle school transition

### Gap 1: No School/IEP Data for General Fallback

L24 R1 covers Levels 1-3 comprehensively but provides NO data on how school/IEP affects the General Fallback segment (~50% of real email volume). L4 identifies 5 sub-groups within GF, some of which (Diagnostic Entrants, Overwhelmed Non-Selectors) may have school-related triggers.

**Impact:** The General Fallback segment receives cross-level flows where school/IEP language must work WITHOUT level-specific calibration. L24 provides no guidance for this.

### Gap 2: No BCBA-IEP Overlap Data

L23 provides extensive BCBA data. L24 provides extensive IEP data. Neither layer quantifies the OVERLAP: what percentage of Level 2 parents have BOTH active BCBA involvement AND active IEP involvement simultaneously?

**Inference:** Given L23's "almost always" BCBA for Level 2 and L24's 85% IEP rate for Level 2, the overlap is likely 75-85% of Level 2 parents. But this is inferred, not measured.

**Impact:** The "unified gatekeeper system" (Connection A) assumes both BCBA and IEP are present. If 15-25% of Level 2 parents have IEP but no BCBA (or vice versa), the strategy needs variants.

### Gap 3: No International School/IEP Data

L24's data is US-centric (IEP is a US legal framework). L4 notes: "Geography: US 65%, UK 15%, AU/NZ 15%, CA 5%." The UK, Australia, and Canada have different special education frameworks (EHCP in UK, IEP in Australia with different legal weight, IPRC in Ontario).

**Impact:** L24's IEP-specific language ("Discuss with your IEP team," "30 days of data for IEP review") may not resonate with 35% of the audience using different systems.
