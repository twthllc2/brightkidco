**Lena Rules:**
- Lena S., Customer Support, Mom of two autistic sons (6 and 8)
- Peer-to-peer, NEVER founder framing

## 2. LEVEL 1

**Audience share:** ~35% of Autistic Parent audience
**Tag:** avatar_level1
**Pop-up option:** "Talks about potty but won't actually do it"
**Child age:** 3-5 years (dominant)
**Verbal profile:** Fully verbal

### 2.1 Core Characteristics
- She STILL HAS HOPE
- She is NOT cynical (yet)
- Has money to invest
- Actively searches for solutions
- Time pressure (Pre-K)
- Open to mechanism explanations
- Vulnerable to hope-crash if promises break

### 2.2 The Level 1 Child
- Fully verbal, can form sentences, knows letters/numbers
- Cognitive: often academically advanced
- Sensory: mild to moderate differences
- Potty profile: KNOWS the concept, CAN sit, shows interest, has occasional successes (1-2x/week) BUT can't reliably initiate, can't feel timing-accurately, inconsistent day to day
- THE PARADOX: "He gets it — but he doesn't get it"

### 2.3 Demographics
- Mom age: 28-40
- Often first ASD child in family
- Middle to upper-middle income
- 70% working (often part-time)
- College-educated: ~70%
- ~10-15% Medicaid usage

### 2.4 The 4 Phases for Level 1

| Phase | Share | Conversion | Key |
|-------|-------|-----------|-----|
| 1 — Slow Realization (2.5-3.5) | ~15% | 3-5% | Light worry, "boys are slower" |
| 2 — First Attempt (3-4) | ~50% | 15-25% | **BIGGEST CONVERSION MOMENT** — Oh Crap failure, guilt |
| 3 — Method Cycling (4-5) | ~30% | 10-18% | Pre-K deadline looming, "tried everything" |
| 4 — Rare (6+) | ~5% | 5-8% | Has given up temporarily |

### 2.5 Symptom Clusters (5 clusters)
- **A: Timing Problems** (~70% recognition) — "He says I need to pee AFTER it's happened"
- **B: Inconsistent Performance** (~80% recognition) — "Some days great, other days zero"
- **C: Verbal Refusal** — "He says no when I ask, then has accident 10 min later"
- **D: Potty Interest Without Action** — "Loves potty time but won't actually go"
- **E: "Gets it but doesn't get it"** — THE Level 1 marker sentence

### 2.6 Conversion Triggers
- **PRIMARY #1: Pre-K Deadline** — enrollment happening, timeline ~3-4 months
- **PRIMARY #2: Oh Crap Failure** — has tried standard method, failed
- **PRIMARY #3: OT Recommendation** — OT mentioned sensory/interoception
- **Secondary: Comparison Moment** — sees other kids trained

## 3. LEVEL 2

**Audience share:** ~40% of Autistic Parent audience (LARGEST)
**Tag:** avatar_level2
**Pop-up option:** "Sensory issues, tried many methods"
**Child age:** 5-7 years (dominant)
**Verbal profile:** Mixed verbal (scripts, echolalia)

### 3.1 Core Characteristics
- Has HOPE WITH CRACKS (intact but wounded)
- Skepticism is default mode
- "I've tried everything" is TRAUMA RESPONSE
- Has deep experience with therapies
- Trusts science, NOT marketing
- Reacts allergically to "miracle product" language
- Ready to invest if mechanism convinces
- Has accepted identity as "autism mom"
- HYPER-SKEPTICAL + HYPER-MOTIVATED simultaneously

### 3.2 The Level 2 Child
- Mixed verbal — often scripts, echolalia, sometimes AAC
- Highly variable cognitive — some advanced, some delayed
- MODERATE TO SEVERE sensory differences
- Hyper-sensitive AND hypo-sensitive (often both)
- Meltdowns 1-3x/week typical
- Sensory triggers in bathroom environment
- Prompt-dependent: goes when brought, never initiates
- Withholds bowel movements until pull-up

### 3.3 Demographics
- Mom age: 30-42
- Second child often NT (comparison pain real)
- 15-20% single moms
- Middle income dominant, ~25% Medicaid
- 75% college-educated (often very informed)
- BCBA + ABA: 20-30 hrs/week, OT: 1-2x/week, Speech: 1-2x/week

**Comorbidities:** SPD ~95%, ADHD ~40%, Anxiety ~35%, Speech delays ~70%, GI issues (chronic) ~50%, Sleep disorders ~45%

### 3.4 The Level 2 Paradox
"Level 2 is the HARDEST mom to convert — because she has seen enough to be skeptical, but not enough to have given up."

**The Level 2 Mail Formula:**
1. REAL symptom recognition (she tests immediately)
2. Mechanism specificity
3. Acknowledge BCBA/OT work
4. Realistic timelines
5. 60-day guarantee
6. Permission-not-to-buy
→ If ONE element is missing, conversion breaks.

### 3.5 The 4 Phases for Level 2

| Phase | Share | Conversion | Key |
|-------|-------|-----------|-----|
| 1 — Slow Realization (2.5-3.5) | ~5% | ~2% | Diagnosis processing, potty NOT priority |
| 2 — First Attempt (3.5-4.5) | ~25% | 10-15% | Deep shock, "my kid is REALLY different" |
| 3 — Method Cycling (4-7) | ~55% | 8-15% | **LONGEST & MOST IMPORTANT** — Years of methods |
| 4 — Resignation Fork (7+) | ~15% | 6-10% | ~40% of Level 2 reach Phase 4 |

### 3.6 Symptom Clusters (6 clusters)

- **B: Withholding + GI Issues** — "Holds poop until bedtime pull-up, chronic constipation"
- **C: Delayed Verbal Signals** — "Says 'wet' after the accident, never before"
- **D: Schedule Dependency Without Internal Signal** — "Goes when prompted, never initiates" (PROMPT-DEPENDENCY)
- **E: Regression Cycles** — "3 weeks of success then total regression"
- **F: Location-Specific Training** — "Goes at school but not at home"

## 4. LEVEL 3

**Audience share:** ~25% of Autistic Parent audience
**Tag:** avatar_level3
**Pop-up option:** "Non-verbal or high support needs"
**Child age:** 6-10+ years (dominant)
**Verbal profile:** Non-verbal or minimal verbal
**CRITICAL:** DIGNITY-FIRST IS NON-NEGOTIABLE

### 4.1 Core Characteristics
- HOPE is accepted as lost (but not entirely dead)
- Resignation is her default mode
- "Maybe never" is permitted reality
- Active searching she doesn't do — she reacts to signals
- When she buys, it's a last attempt
- Hyper-sensitive to language
- Comparison pain is 6-9 years deep
- She is caregiver, not "potty-training mom"
- Has accepted identity as mom of a severely disabled child
- Most EMOTIONALLY SENSITIVE audience

### 4.2 The Level 3 Child
- 6-10+ years dominant (teenagers exist too)
- Non-verbal or minimal verbal (0-50 functional words)
- Often AAC device
- SEVERE sensory differences (hyper AND hypo)
- Meltdowns daily to multiple times daily
- Self-injury when frustrated possible
- Has NEVER been potty-trained despite years of attempts
- Holds bowel movements (withholding extreme)
- Cannot show when he needs to
- Eventually adult diaper sizes

### 4.3 Demographics
- Mom age: 35-48
- 20-25% single moms (higher than other levels)
- Lower-middle income dominant
- ~40% Medicaid usage (significantly higher)
- 60% stay-at-home/full-time caregiver
- 55% college-educated

**Comorbidities:** SPD ~98% (severe), Intellectual Disability ~50%, Speech non-verbal ~80%, GI chronic ~60-70%, Seizure ~25%, Self-injury ~40%, Sleep disorders ~70%, Feeding issues ~50%

### 4.4 The Resignation Reality
"Resignation is NOT giving up. It is acceptance after years of pain. It is realistic peace with what is. It is energy preservation for what's actually possible."

**Mom's words on resignation:**
- "I stopped trying actively 2 years ago. We focus on dignity now."
- "He'll get there when he gets there. Or he won't. Either way, he's my son."
- "I don't 'hope' anymore. I just keep him clean and safe."
- "Maybe one day. Maybe never. We're at peace either way."

**DO NOT:** "Don't give up!" / "Your child can still do this!" / Cheerleader language
**DO:** "If you've moved past active training — that's not failure"
**DO:** "If something shifts, it shifts. If not, you've been doing the right thing"
