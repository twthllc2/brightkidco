## LEVEL 1: Pre-K as Master Driver

### School/Daycare Status
- Mainstream daycare/preschool: ~60%
- Mainstream + 1:1 or aide: ~25%
- Special preschool: ~15%
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 2)

### Pre-K Deadline Pressure — Core Driver
- Pre-K is the MOST IMPORTANT conversion driver for Level 1
- Spring/Summer creates pressure (school starts in fall)
- "If he doesn't train, no school" fear
- Investment readiness: High
- Timeline tolerance: ~3-4 months
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 8 — Conversion Trigger Map)

### Pre-K Timing Calendar
| Month | Pre-K Relevance | Mail Focus |
|-------|-----------------|------------|
| Jan-Feb | Low | Education, low urgency |
| Mar | Medium (enrollment) | Mention gently |
| Apr | High | Pre-K enrollment peak |
| May | Very high | "Last chance for summer training" |
| Jun | Highest | "Summer training season" |
| Jul | Very high | "Halftime to Pre-K" |
| Aug | High | "Final stretch" |
| Sep | Medium | School start, less urgency |
| Oct-Dec | Low | Birthday-driven, off-season |
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 17)

### Pre-K Messaging Rules — DO
- "Pre-K starts in [season]"
- "Most schools require daytime training"
- "60 days × consistency = real change before August"
- "Give your kid runway to adjust"
- "Pre-K means trained daytime, night training is later"
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 17)

### Pre-K Messaging Rules — DON'T
- "Your child HAS to be trained by September!"
- "Time is running out!"
- "What will you do if he doesn't start school?"
- "Don't let your kid be the only one in diapers"
- Shaming language
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 17)

### Pre-K Urgency — How to Convey Without Manipulation
DO:
- "Pre-K starts in 4 months. That gives most kids enough runway."
- "Many parents start in summer to leverage indoor time."
- "Fall birthday — Pre-K is coming anyway."

DON'T:
- "Only 24 hours left!"
- "Limited stock!"
- "Your child can't wait!"
- "Time is running out!"
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 14)

### Level 1-Specific Lever: Before Pre-K Summer Window
Language: "4 months of summer = ideal runway for body-signal training. Most families see real progress by end of August."
Effect: Concretizes timeline, season-specific, realistic + motivating
Use: Spring/Summer Welcome, Browse Abandonment (Spring/Summer), Pre-Birthday mails
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 16 — Additional Lever L1-E)

### Level 1 Additional Lever: Pre-K Readiness
Language: "Many schools require daytime training before enrollment. The 3+3 bundle gives you 60 days to see real progress — enough runway before September."
Effect: Very strong lever, intensified by season (Spring/Summer), triggers active decision-making
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 16 — Additional Lever L1-A)

### Verbatim Parent Quotes About School (Level 1)

- "They said he has to be fully trained by September."

### Testimonial Patterns With School Context

- "We made it for pre-K with weeks to spare"

### Subject Lines With Pre-K Hooks
- "Pre-K starts in [X months]. Here's the plan."
- "Pre-K parents' biggest question"
- "Pre-K parents' #1 question"
- "60 days = before Pre-K"
- "Most Level 1 kids see signs in 4-6 weeks"

### Cart Recovery With School Context

### Winback With School Context

(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 19)

### Image Direction — School Cues for Level 1
- Backpack, small school look
- Kid with hat/water bottle (park, outdoor)
- Backpack hanging up
- Subtle "growing up" cues
- Mainstream daycare/preschool settings possible
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 22-23)

### Forbidden for Level 1
- NO Level 2/3 stories (misses her)
- NO heavy resignation language
- NO "BCBA protocol" (she often has no BCBA)
(Source: DOC2-LEVEL1, Section 4, 18)

## LEVEL 2: IEP Context + School Accommodation

### School Status
- Mainstream with 1:1 aide: ~40%
- Special needs preschool/school: ~50%
- Mainstream without support: ~10%
- IEP (Individualized Education Plan): ~85%
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 2)

### Key Insight: Pre-K Pressure Is GONE for Level 2
- Pre-K pressure GONE for Level 2 (school already adapted with IEP)
- "Pre-K pressure less acute" — school is usually already settled (IEP)
- School as main pressure is FORBIDDEN for Level 2
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 4, 13, 19)

### IEP as Trust Marker
DO:
- "Discuss with your IEP team"
- "Bring it up at the next IEP meeting"
- IEP as trust marker (you know what she means)
- Acknowledge school as supportive system

DON'T:
- Use school as pressure/urgency (that's Level 1)
- Criticize IEP limitations
- "School should accommodate this" demanding tone

LANGUAGE PATTERNS:
- "Your IEP team already supports him in pull-ups. This just adds a piece you can build alongside."
- "30 days of data = useful for the next IEP review."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 27 — School/IEP Context)

### Level 2 Paradox: School Trained ≠ Home Trained
- "She goes for the teacher, but not for me." — THE Level 2 paradox
- Child goes at school, refuses at home
- School trained ≠ home trained
- Prompt-dependent: goes when brought, never initiates
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 3, 5)

### Verbatim Quotes About School (Level 2)
- "He goes for the teacher but won't even try at home."
- "She goes for the teacher but not for me."
- "She trained at school then forgot it all over summer break."
- "He's compliant when prompted, never initiates."
- "His BCBA says he's prompt-dependent and we need to fade prompts"
- "After 30 hours of ABA, he sits — but doesn't go on his own."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Sections 3, 7, 8)

### School Context Change as Conversion Trigger
PRIMARY TRIGGER #3: SCHOOL CONTEXT CHANGE
What she thinks: "He transitions to elementary school in fall. His IEP includes pull-up support but other kids notice now."
Mail lever: "Add alongside IEP support. Build independence piece by piece."
Investment readiness: High
Timeline tolerance: 3-6 months
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 9)

### Symptom Cluster D: Schedule Dependency Without Internal Signal
- "He'll go when I take him every 90 minutes, but never on his own."
- "She's been on a timed schedule for 2 years — still no independence."
- "He's prompt-dependent, the BCBA confirms it."
- "She goes for the teacher but not for me."
- "He's compliant when prompted, never initiates."
- "After 30 hours of ABA, he sits — but doesn't go on his own."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 7 — Cluster D)

### Symptom Cluster F: Location-Specific Training
- "He goes at home but never in public."
- "She goes at school but not at home."
- "He has a specific toilet he'll use — anywhere else, nothing."
- "She's trained at grandparents' but not at our house."
- "Travel is impossible — different bathroom = no go."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 7 — Cluster F)

### Regression Cycles With School
- "She trained at school then forgot it all over summer break."
- "Every change in routine sets us back to zero."
- "Vacation always means starting over."
- "COVID set us back 2 years of progress."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 7 — Cluster E)

### Forbidden for Level 2
- Direct Pre-K urgency (school is usually already adapted)
- "School as main pressure" (school is not her acute pain)
- Pre-K classroom contexts in visuals (too Level 1)
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 19, 23)

### Subject Line No-Gos for Level 2
- "Pre-K is coming" (too Level 1)
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 21)

### Visual Cues — No School Context for Level 2
- Pre-K classroom contexts: AVOID (too Level 1)
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 23)

### BCBA + School Team Loyalty
- "Your BCBA has built the behavioral foundation. This adds the sensory layer."
- "Many BCBAs integrate sensation-based feedback once they see results."
- "Bring 30 days of data to your next BCBA session — they'll recognize the patterns."
(Source: DOC3-LEVEL2, Section 26)

## LEVEL 3: Special Needs School + IEP Diaper Support

### School Status
- Special needs school: ~70%
- Mainstream with 1:1 aide: ~25%
- Homeschool: ~5% (sometimes burnout consequence)
- IEP with diaper support: ~95%
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 2)

### Key Insight: NO School Pressure for Level 3
- NO Pre-K pressure
- NO school pressure as lever
- NO "running out of time"
- School is already adapted
- Level 3 mails must NEVER use Pre-K or school pressure as lever
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Sections 4, 13, 19)

### School as Daily Reality
- "School handles changes during the day. I do mornings and nights."
- "School handles changes during the day. I do night."
- Daniel's special ed school supports with diaper changes as part of his IEP
- Maria packs 3 change diapers in his school bag for special ed each morning
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Sections 1, 8)

### School Transition as Conversion Trigger
PRIMARY TRIGGER #2: SCHOOL TRANSITION
What she thinks: "Middle school starts next year. The diaper support won't transfer easily. Other kids will notice."
Mail lever: Privacy + Late-Trainer + Dignity
Investment readiness: High
Timeline tolerance: Long
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 9)

### Verbatim Quotes About School (Level 3)
- "School handles changes during the day. I do mornings and nights."
- "School handles changes during the day. I do night."
- "We've structured our life around this. Limited outings, specific timing."
- "It's not a battle anymore. It's just life."
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 8 — Category 5)

### Forbidden for Level 3
- Pre-K, school enrollment as driver → "Misses. School is already adapted."
- "Make this the year he trains" → Pressure language. Block.
- "Don't let your child miss out" → Pressure + Pre-K language.
- Comparisons with Level 1+2 successes → "Our 4-year-old in Pre-K..." → instant disconnect.
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 19)

### Subject Line No-Gos for Level 3
- "Pre-K coming!"
- "Save your kid from..."
- "Don't miss out!"
- "Limited time"
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 21)

### Image Direction — No School Context for Level 3
- Pre-K classic settings: AVOID
- Special ed school context: OK (calm settings)
(Source: DOC4-LEVEL3, Section 24)
