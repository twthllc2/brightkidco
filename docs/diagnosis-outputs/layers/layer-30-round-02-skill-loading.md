### Rule 2: Quality Gate (Pre-Send Checklist)
**Source:** Copy Skill, Section 8
- No AI-isms from banned list
- Brand voice matches brief
- Subject line A/B testable (2+ variations)
- CTA specific and action-oriented (one per section)
- Length matches brief requirements
- Read aloud: natural flow?
- Would you click/CVC this if you received it?

**BrightKidCo mapping:** This quality gate must be COMBINED with the BrightKidCo-specific quick-reference checks (DOC6 Section 6).

### Rule 3: Subject Line Formulas
**Source:** Copy Skill, Section 7
1. Curiosity: "[Number] ways to [benefit]"
2. Social proof: "Join [Number] others who..."
3. Urgency: "Last chance to [benefit]"
4. Personalization: "[Name], your [personalized thing] is ready"
5. How-to: "How to [desired outcome]"
6. Announcement: "Introducing [new product/feature]"
7. Question: "Struggling with [problem]?"
8. Stat: "[Number]% of [audience] [action]"

**BrightKidCo mapping:** Formula #3 (Urgency) is RESTRICTED — no urgency manipulation allowed. Formula #7 (Question) maps to symptom recognition pattern. Formula #8 (Stat) maps to SPARK study (49%).

## PART B: FABRICATION-PREVENTION RULES (FROM CLIENT FILES)

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 1: Source-Restricted Quotes
**Source:** DOC6, Section "ANTI-FABRICATION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)"
You may ONLY use verbatim quotes that exist in the RS PDFs provided in this project.

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** Every quote in every email must be traceable to a specific RS PDF (Autistic_Parent.pdf, Autistic_language_Bank.pdf, Autistic_MIRO_m.pdf).

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 2: Prohibited Actions
**Source:** DOC6, Section "ANTI-FABRICATION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)"
You may NOT:
- Invent customer quotes
- Fabricate testimonials
- Imagine "what a mom might say"
- Generate plausible-sounding but fake reviews
- Adapt quotes so heavily they become fictional

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** If a quote cannot be found in RS PDFs, FLAG IT to Yurishizu instead of fabricating. Brand-trust-critical.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 3: Permitted Adaptations
**Source:** DOC6, Section "ANTI-FABRICATION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)"
You MAY:
- Lightly anonymize (change first names, change ages within range)
- Combine themes from multiple real quotes into one representative statement (only if you note this internally)
- Paraphrase for length, keeping core meaning and voice

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** Any combination of quotes must be noted internally. Paraphrasing must preserve core meaning and voice.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 4: RS Integration Mandate
**Source:** DOC6, Section "RS-INTEGRATION (CRITICAL)"
Each mail must contain 2-4 authentic verbatim quotes or tightly paraphrased lines from the RS. Cite informally ("one mom in our research described..." or directly in mail body as quote).

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** No email ships without RS quotes. This is a structural requirement, not optional.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 5: RS Holistic Workflow
**Source:** DOC6, Section "RS-HOLISTIC WORKFLOW"
When writing a mail, think: "What would a real mom from the RS say in this moment?" — and find that voice in the PDFs.

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** The RS PDFs are the UNFILTERED VOICE source. The DOCs are ABSTRACT GUIDANCE. Both are required.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 6: Quote Mix Requirement
**Source:** DOC6, Section "RS-INTEGRATION"
Mail text should be:
- 30-50% authentic verbatim language or tightly paraphrased
- 30-50% Lena's voice (peer-to-peer, empathetic)
- 10-20% mechanism/brand language (doc-based)

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** This is a STRUCTURAL MIX requirement. Not just "include some quotes" — the ratio is specified.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 7: Verbatim Language Preservation
**Source:** DOC1, Section 7
RULE: NEVER rewrite into "nicer English." The ROUGH edges are the trust anchor.

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** Raw quotes from RS must preserve their rough edges. Cleaning up grammar or making quotes "nicer" destroys trust.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 8: Forbidden Phrases List
**Source:** DOC1, Section 15
PHRASES THAT KILL TRUST — NEVER USE:
- "Easy" / "Simple" / "Effortless"
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Finally solved everything"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- "Suffering from autism" (autism = identity, not disease)
- "High-functioning" / "Low-functioning"
- "Wait until they're ready"
- "Just try harder"
- "Be more consistent"
- "Your child should..."
- "Most children at this age..."
- "Have you tried [obvious method]"
- "Don't worry"
- "We understand" without substance
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix"
- "Your child will be normal"
- "Get him trained!"

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** These are TRUST-KILLING phrases. Using any of them = brand damage.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 9: Sensitive Areas (Extra Care)
**Source:** DOC1, Section 15
SENSITIVE AREAS — WITH EXTRA CARE:
1. SELF-BLAME — She's already on a guilt seesaw. Mails must NEVER hint that she's doing something wrong. Always absolve, never accuse.
2. PARTNER CONFLICT — Mails must not sound like she has to convince her partner.
3. COMPARISON WOUND — Playground comparisons with neurotypical peers are triggers. Mails must NEVER position neurotypical kids as standard.
4. PEDIATRICIAN FRUSTRATION — Gently acknowledge, never attack the entire medical profession.
5. FINANCIAL HARDSHIP — ~25% use Medicaid. $79.99 is real commitment. Math reframe OK, never preachy.
6. CHILD'S IDENTITY — NEVER "your son will be normal" — autism = identity, not disease. "Help his nervous system" > "fix his behavior".

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** These are ETHICAL BOUNDARIES. Violating any of them = harm to vulnerable parents.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 10: Brand Voice Integrity
**Source:** DOC1, Section 12 + DOC6
WE ARE NOT:
- A "wellness brand" with positive vibes
- An "expert brand" that knows everything
- A "miracle product brand"
- Polished, perfect, or predictable

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** Copy must sound like "an OT who is also a mom of an autistic child" — not marketing.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 11: Structural Do's & Don'ts
**Source:** DOC1, Section 23
DO'S:
- Open with the symptom (not the solution)
- Acknowledge prior failures BEFORE proposing mechanism
- Use 3-5 specific scenarios per mail
- Make Lena's voice the trust anchor
- Offer permission not to buy
- Realistic timelines with ranges
- Cite Kelly Mahler / SPARK research when relevant
- Mini-wins as success metrics

DON'TS:
- Lead with the product
- Use "easy/quick/simple" anywhere
- Promise outcomes tied to timelines
- Compare to neurotypical kids as standard
- Suggest the parent isn't doing enough
- Use level labels in copy
- Stack social proof early
- Use urgency manipulation
- Use exclamation marks in body text
- Use "we know how you feel"
- Use generic stock images
- End with hard-sell CTAs

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** These are STRUCTURAL RULES. Every email must pass this checklist.

### Anti-Fabrication Rule 12: Lena Sign-Off Integrity
**Source:** DOC1, Section 13
FORBIDDEN:
- "I'm the founder of BrightKidCo"
- "I'm not the founder, I'm..."
- "I started this company because..."
- 4+ paragraphs of sign-off text
- Story drama about sons

ALLOWED:
- "Lena S."
- "Mom of two autistic sons"
- "If you reply, you get me"
- Sign-off body max 2-3 sentences
- P.S. max 2 lines

**BrightKidCo enforcement:** Lena is a PERSONA, not a lie. The sign-off must be consistent and authentic-feeling.

AVATAR:

RS-INTEGRATION:
- 2-4 verbatim quotes from RS used?
- Quotes cited informally?
- NO fabricated quotes or testimonials?
