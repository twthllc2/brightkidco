## 1. Psychology Frameworks Found in Wiki

### 1.1 Asymmetric Psychological Leverage (88 Cognitive Heuristics)
**Source:** `/root/wiki/reference/__enqueued__/asymmetric-psychological-leverage.md`
**Relevance:** HIGH — Complete framework for understanding unconscious decision-making in client acquisition.

**Core Model:**
- Human + Stimulus = Action
- 95% of human decisions happen unconsciously (Harvard Prof. Gerald Zaltman)
- Lock & Key Theory: the human mind is the lock; stimuli are the key. Design the key to fit the lock.

**Key Heuristics Applicable to Autism Parent Psychology:**
1. **Reward super-response tendency** — Parents are driven by the incentive of their child's improvement
2. **Punishment avoidance** — Parents avoid decisions that could "punish" their child (wrong product, wasted money)
3. **Doubt avoidance** — Parents hate sitting with doubt about their child's condition; they'll act to escape it
4. **Inconsistency avoidance** — Parents resist acting against their self-image as "good parents"
5. **Commitment consistency bias** — Once parents commit to a solution path, they seek confirming evidence
6. **Curiosity tendency** — Parents are information-gatherers about their child's condition
7. **Kantian fairness tendency** — "My child deserves the same comfort as any other child"
8. **Excessive self-regard** — Parents believe they know their child best
9. **Pain avoiding denial** — Some parents deny the severity of SPD to avoid emotional pain
10. **Social proof tendency** — Parents rely heavily on other parents' experiences
11. **Authority misinfluence** — Therapists, doctors, and specialists carry enormous weight
12. **Familiarity bias** — Repeated exposure to a brand builds trust over time
13. **Reciprocation tendency** — Free resources (guides, tips) create obligation to reciprocate
14. **Scarcity bias** — Limited editions or "while supplies last" drive action
15. **Zero risk bias** — Parents prefer risk-free options (money-back guarantees, free trials)
16. **Deprival super-reaction (loss aversion)** — "Don't miss out on your child's progress"
17. **Barnum effect** — Personalized-feeling descriptions resonate even when general
18. **Subjective validation** — Copy that names exact problems validates the parent's struggle

### 1.2 Forces of Irrationality (7 Forces)
**Source:** `/root/wiki/about-me/psychology/__enqueued__/__enqueued__/10-forces-of-irrationality.md`
**Relevance:** HIGH — Explains the psychological forces that prevent rational decision-making.

**The 7 Forces:**
1. **Bias** — Pre-disposed inclinations that create mental inertia and feedback loops
2. **Emotion** — The #1 enemy of rational decision-making; both positive and negative emotions cloud judgment
3. **Belief** — Limiting beliefs restrict what's perceived as possible; beliefs are the main bottleneck
4. **Ego** — Taking things personally destroys opportunities; ego is the enemy in acquisition
5. **Resistance** — 30 forms including fear, instant gratification, doubt, anxiety, rationalization, perfection, procrastination
6. **Experience** — Past experiences predict future behavior irrationally; "in my experience" is often a trap
7. **Projection** — Humans see themselves in others; fear of judgment comes from judging others

**Application to Autism Parents:**
- Parents PROJECT their fears onto the product ("will others judge my child for wearing this?")
- Past EXPERIENCE with failed products creates resistance to new solutions
- EMOTION (guilt, anxiety, hope) drives purchasing decisions more than logic
- BELIEF systems about autism/SPD shape what solutions parents consider viable
