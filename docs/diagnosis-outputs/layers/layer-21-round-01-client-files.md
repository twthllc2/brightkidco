## The 5 Sub-Groups — Overview

General Fallback is ~50% of all real email traffic. It is NOT homogeneous. It consists of 5 very different sub-groups that must all receive the same mail set simultaneously.

| Sub-Group | Name | Share of GF | Share of Total |
|-----------|------|-------------|----------------|
| A | Diagnostic Entrants | ~25% | ~10% |
| B | Diagnosis-Rejecters | ~15% | ~6% |
| C | Sensory-Difference Parents | ~25% | ~10% |
| D | Overwhelmed Non-Selectors | ~25% | ~10% |
| E | Cart/Browse Without Opt-In | ~10% | ~14% |

**Decisive Insight:** Universal Symptom Hooks > Level Specifics. Mails must address all 5 simultaneously.

## SUB-GROUP A: THE DIAGNOSTIC ENTRANTS (~25% of GF)

### Verbatim Description from DOC5
> "No formal ASD diagnosis yet. Waiting for evaluation or has appointments in pipeline. Strong suspicion that autism is the case. Pediatrician has hinted 'developmental concerns.' Other families have said: 'Get that checked.'"

### Who She Is
- No formal ASD diagnosis yet
- Waiting for evaluation or has appointments in pipeline
- Strong suspicion that autism is the case
- Pediatrician has hinted "developmental concerns"
- Other families have said: "Get that checked"

### Demographics
- 28-38 years old
- Child 2-4 years old
- Often first child
- US, UK, AU mix
- Higher education over-represented (she researches actively)

### Mental State
- Confusion dominant
- "Is this autism or just delays?"
- Doesn't yet identify as "autism mom"
- Very active in research
- Hasn't decided yet which communities to join
- Very curious, very open to education

### What She Reads
- "is my 3 year old autistic"
- "speech delay potty training"
- "developmental delays toilet training"
- "early signs of autism"

### Why She's in General Fallback
**Reason 1: No Diagnostic Level.** She doesn't know what to choose. "Still figuring out" is most honest answer. She can't choose a level because she doesn't have one. But she's still primary target for BrightKidCo in Phase 1 of her journey.

### Language Preferences
- Education-First approach
- NO diagnosis language as prerequisite
- "If you're starting to wonder..." openers
- Soft mechanism explanation
- Gentle, non-alarming

### Content Needs
- Education about why standard methods fail
- Gentle introduction to interoception concept
- "You're not alone" framing
- No hard sales hooks
- Mechanism-first approach

### Triggers
- Pediatrician comment about "developmental concerns"
- Comparison with peers whose kids are trained
- Own research initiative discovering autism signs
- Upcoming evaluation appointment

### Fears
- Getting hopes up before diagnosis is confirmed
- Being labeled prematurely
- Missing a "normal" developmental window
- Not knowing enough to make good decisions

### Mapping to Avatar Levels
- **Primary overlap: Phase 1 (Slow Realization)** from DOC1 — child 2.5-3.5 years, first comparison pain, still has hope for "easy fix," open to new ideas, ready to invest money, not yet skeptical toward brands
- **Partial overlap with Level 1 profile** — similar age range, similar hope level, but Sub-Group A lacks even the diagnosis that Level 1 has (~70% of Level 1 is formally diagnosed)
- **No overlap with Level 2 or 3** — she hasn't accumulated years of therapy experience or method cycling

### Messaging Rules
- Hope level: HIGH (70%+)
- Tonality: Education-oriented, gentle, "You're not alone" + "there's a reason"
- No hard sales hooks
- Mechanism-first approach works particularly well
- Hope language OK ("This may help...")
- NEVER assume diagnosis exists
- NEVER use Level labels

## SUB-GROUP B: THE DIAGNOSIS-REJECTERS (~15% of GF)

### Verbatim Description from DOC5
> "Has a child with diagnosis, but doesn't identify primarily as 'autism mom.' Consciously answered the pop-up 'I'm skipping that.' Rejects labels ('My kid is just my kid'). But has experienced ASD-typical symptoms."

### Who She Is
- Has a child with diagnosis, but doesn't identify primarily as "autism mom"
- Consciously answered the pop-up "I'm skipping that"
- Rejects labels ("My kid is just my kid")
- But has experienced ASD-typical symptoms

### Demographics
- 30-45 years old
- Child 3-8 years old
- Sometimes HSP-oriented parents or anti-diagnosis movement
- Very own language, dislikes medical terms

### Mental State
- Values self-determination over diagnosis
- Often has positive self-concept ("we're just different")
- Still wants help for potty-training
- Sensitive to "autism marketing"

### What She Reads
- "potty training my unique kid"
- "alternatives to autism diagnosis"
- "neurodivergent without diagnosis"

### Why She's in General Fallback
**Reason 2: Label Rejection.** She doesn't consciously identify. Chooses General or skips pop-up. She has a child with diagnosis but rejects the framework.

### Language Preferences
- Language WITHOUT diagnosis terms
- "For kids who process the world differently"
- Symptom language, never label
- "Whatever you call this..." acknowledgment
- Inclusive, identity-flexible language

### Content Needs
- Symptom-based framing (not diagnosis-based)
- Acknowledgment that "different" is valid
- Help with practical problems (potty training) without requiring label acceptance
- Mechanism explanation that works regardless of diagnostic framework
- Respect for her choice not to use labels

### Triggers
- Practical problem (potty training) that won't resolve
- Seeing symptom descriptions that match her child without requiring diagnosis language
- Peer recommendations from other "label-free" parents
- Frustration with methods that assume diagnosis context

### Fears
- Being forced into a diagnostic box
- Her child being reduced to a label
- Marketing that uses autism as fear lever
- Losing autonomy over how she defines her family

### Mapping to Avatar Levels
- **Partial overlap with Phase 2-3** — she has likely tried methods, has experience, but her resistance to labels is unique
- **No clean overlap with any Level** — Levels 1/2/3 all assume diagnosis acceptance; Sub-Group B explicitly rejects this
- **Unique position:** She has the EXPERIENCE of a Level 2-3 parent but the IDENTITY RESISTANCE of someone who won't accept the framework

### Messaging Rules
- Language WITHOUT diagnosis terms
- "For kids who process the world differently"
- Symptom language, never label
- NEVER "As an autism mom you know..."
- NEVER "Your autistic child..."
- NEVER presuppose a diagnosis
- Lena's language should extend: "I'm mom of two boys who didn't follow the typical timeline"

## SUB-GROUP C: THE SENSORY-DIFFERENCE PARENTS (~25% of GF)

### Verbatim Description from DOC5
> "Child has SPD (Sensory Processing Disorder), ADHD, or other neurological differences. NO ASD diagnosed. OT has confirmed interoception issues. Identifies as 'neurodivergent parent.'"

### Who She Is
- Child has SPD (Sensory Processing Disorder), ADHD, or other neurological differences
- NO ASD diagnosed
- OT has confirmed interoception issues
- Identifies as "neurodivergent parent"

### Demographics
- 30-42 years old
- Child 3-7 years old
- Often academic background
- Knows sensory language, OT concepts
- Higher income tends to be present

### Mental State
- Sees autism marketing as "similar but not mine"
- Wants mechanism science
- Compares with ADHD/SPD-specific resources
- Makes purchase decision intellectually
- Very informed

### What She Reads
- "sensory processing disorder potty training"
- "ADHD child won't potty train"
- "interoception SPD"
- "neurodivergent kids and toileting"

### Why She's in General Fallback
**Reason 3: No ASD Diagnosis Despite Symptoms.** ADHD, SPD, others — doesn't fit Level 1/2/3. "General" or skip. She has a child with real neurological differences but they don't map to the ASD level framework.

### Language Preferences
- Science prominent
- "For kids with sensory differences of any kind"
- Mechanism-oriented mails
- NO ASD-specific language as prerequisite
- Kelly Mahler and interoception research resonates strongly
- OT/therapeutic language she already knows

### Content Needs
- Science-first approach (interoception, signal processing)
- Mechanism explanation with neurological specificity
- Acknowledgment that SPD/ADHD kids face the same toileting challenges
- "Not just for autism" framing
- Intellectual engagement (she researches before buying)

### Triggers
- OT recommendation mentioning interoception
- Finding science-based explanation for why standard methods fail
- Seeing product positioned for "sensory differences" (not just autism)
- Intellectual conviction about mechanism

### Fears
- Being lumped into "autism" marketing when her child isn't autistic
- Products that only work for ASD kids
- Generic "special needs" language that lacks specificity
- Wasting money on something not designed for her child's profile

### Mapping to Avatar Levels
- **IMPORTANT: This group is OFTEN overlooked, but is highly profitable** — she has money, education, and mechanism understanding
- **Partial overlap with Level 2 demographics** — similar age range (30-42), similar education level, similar sensory-awareness
- **No overlap with Level 2 psychology** — she doesn't have BCBA context, doesn't have years of ASD-specific therapy, doesn't carry ASD identity
- **Unique position:** She has the INTELLECTUAL PROFILE of a Level 2 parent but the DIAGNOSTIC CONTEXT of someone outside the ASD framework entirely

### Messaging Rules
- Science prominent
- "For kids with sensory differences of any kind"
- Mechanism-oriented mails
- NO ASD-specific language as prerequisite
- Combine SPARK stat with "for sensory-different kids more broadly": "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't trained. For kids with sensory differences of any kind, the percentage is similar."
- Lever GF-D ("Not just for autism") is critical: "For autistic kids. For ADHD kids. For SPD kids. For kids who just process signals differently."

### Who She Is
- Has absolutely ASD child with clear level (1, 2, or 3)
- But didn't select the pop-up out of overwhelm
- No conscious act — more "forgot / not relevant"
- Falls into default flow
- Clicked away pop-up too fast

### Demographics
- Mix of all three levels
- Often Phase 3-4 parents, tired of filling out forms
- Possibly just had a test/IEP meeting behind them

### Mental State
- Default skepticism
- Wants help without further forms
- Will read first 1-2 mails skeptically
- If relevant: can later migrate to level-specific segment

### What She Reads
- Varies by real level
- But lands on brand site often via Meta ad

### Why She's in General Fallback
**Reason 4: Overwhelm.** Clicked pop-up away too fast. "I'll deal with details later." She has a clear level but didn't communicate it.

### Language Preferences
- Symptom hooks that match her actual level
- Migration opportunities (links to level stories)
- "Wherever you are..." acknowledgment
- Non-pressuring, low-friction

### Content Needs
- Mails must offer symptom hooks across all levels so she finds herself
- Build in migration opportunities (links to level stories)
- Acknowledge: "Wherever you are..."
- Content that resonates with her ACTUAL level even though she's tagged general
- 3-5 recognition anchors per mail (cross-level symptom lists)

### Triggers
- Seeing a symptom description that exactly matches her child
- Clicking on a level-specific story link (migration trigger)
- Finding content that feels targeted rather than generic
- The Day 14 Level-Detection Mail

### Fears
- More forms, more questions, more hoops
- Being marketed to generically when her situation is specific
- Missing out on level-specific support
- Another product that doesn't understand her child's actual level

### Mapping to Avatar Levels

- **Could map to ANY of the three levels** depending on actual child profile
- **Most likely Phase 3-4** (tired of filling out forms, years of experience)
- **Migration target:** Day 14 Level-Detection Mail is designed specifically for this group. Expected click rate: 35-50%, resulting in ~40-50% migration from General to specific Level

### Messaging Rules
- Mails must offer symptom hooks across all levels
- Build in migration anchors in every mail
- Three-Family Testimonial Pattern is especially effective (she identifies with one story)
- Level-1-Anchor: "If you have a verbal child under 5 who 'almost trained' multiple times, here's Sarah's story"
- Level-2-Anchor: "If your kid has sensory issues around the bathroom and you've worked with a BCBA, here's Lisa's path"
- Level-3-Anchor: "If your child is 7+ and non-verbal, here's Maria's journey"
- Cross-Level-Anchor: "If you're not sure yet — here's the general framework"

### Universal Symptom Hooks (Cross-Sub-Group)

These symptoms are recognizable across ALL 5 sub-groups. Every parent finds at least 1-2 here:

**Priority 1:**
- "Kids who sit on the toilet for 20 minutes and nothing happens" → Hits all sub-groups
- "Kids who pee right after the pull-up comes off" → Hits all sub-groups
- "Kids who play in a wet pull-up without reacting" → Especially D (Level 2-3), but also others
- "Kids who refuse to even try the toilet" → Universal
- "Kids whose pediatrician keeps saying 'wait until they're ready' — but the readiness never arrives" → Especially A and D

**Priority 2:**
- "Kids who hold their bowel movements until pull-ups go on"
- "Kids who can talk about pottying but can't do it"
- "Kids who 'almost trained' multiple times"
- "Kids who pee or poop seconds after leaving the toilet"
- "Kids who don't seem to notice or care that they're wet"
- "Kids who scream when the toilet flushes"
- "Kids who need the same toilet, anywhere else triggers meltdowns"

**Rule:** In every General Fallback mail, 3-4 symptoms from Priority 1 or a combination of 1+2 should be used.

### Forbidden Phrases (Cross-Level Specific, from DOC5)

- "For Level 1/2/3 kids..." — Only hits Sub-Group D, misses others
- "Diagnosed autism only" — Misses Sub-Groups A and C
- "Verbal kids will..." — Misses Level 3
- "Your toddler will..." — Some kids are 8-10
- School-specific urgency (Pre-K) — Some kids are in special education
- Aggressive math reframes — Universally off-putting
- "Just like other kids" — Misses all neurodivergent families
- Strictly behavioral language — Misses neurologically-focused parents
- "Get him trained!" — Never pressure
- Heavy resignation language — Misses Level 1 and Sub-Group A
- "Easy" / "Quick" / "Simple" — Universal block
- "Cure" / "Fix" / "Solve" — Misses all neurodivergent families
- "He'll be normal" — Misses Sub-Group B+C+others
- "When she's potty trained..." — Implies success is guaranteed. Misses Level 3.

### Mail Structure Formula (Every GF Mail)

1. **Opener:** Universal Recognition (Level 1 psychological pyramid)
2. **Body:** Specific Symptoms (Level 2 psychological pyramid)
3. **Why-Section:** Mechanism (Level 3 psychological pyramid)
4. **CTA:** Universal Permission ("if this fits, here's the option")

Every mail must touch all 3 levels — otherwise you lose at least one sub-group.

### 4-Phase Mix Dynamics (All Sub-Groups Simultaneously)

Each GF mail must contain elements for all 4 phases:

- **Phase 1 elements:** "If you're just starting to wonder..." / Education approach / Gentle mechanism intro
- **Phase 2 elements:** "If you tried Oh Crap or similar and it didn't work..." / Validation of method failure / Reframe: "wrong problem, not wrong parent"
- **Phase 3 elements:** "If you've cycled through methods for years..." / Science (Kelly Mahler, SPARK) / 60-Day Guarantee as risk removal
- **Phase 4 elements:** "Whether your kid is 3 or 10..." / "Late is not never" hint / Permission-not-to-buy

**Integration Tip:** Instead of 4 separate sections, integrate through: "Wherever you are on this journey..." / "Whether you're 3 weeks in or 3 years in..." / "Some parents have just started looking, others have tried everything..."

### Migration Strategy (Critical for Sub-Groups A, B, C, D)

General Fallback is NOT an end state. It is a TEMPORARY state until mom self-identifies.

**4 Migration Paths:**
1. **Click-Based:** Customer clicks level-specific story links → automatic Klaviyo tag update
2. **Reply-Based:** Customer replies with details → tag set based on content
3. **Purchase-Based Hints:** Underwear size → age hint → level hint
4. **Post-Purchase Level-Detection (Day 14):** Strongest method. 4 buttons. Expected: 65-75% open rate, 35-50% click rate, ~40-50% migration

**Sub-Group Migration Likelihood:**
- A → Will likely self-select Level 1 or 2 after diagnosis (weeks to months)
- B → Unlikely to migrate; stays General or drops off
- C → Unlikely to migrate to ASD levels; may stay General permanently
- D → Highest migration potential (has a real level, just didn't share it)
- E → Variable; depends on which level she actually is

### Tonality Calibration (50/50 Balance)

Since this audience is SO mixed, tonality must be medium-balanced:
- **Hope Level:** MEDIUM (50/50) — not too high (loses Level 3), not too low (loses Level 1)
- **Urgency:** LOW-MEDIUM — soft urgency OK, never manipulative
- **Science Density:** MEDIUM — Kelly Mahler yes, but not 3 paragraphs
- **Empathy Intensity:** HIGH — across all sub-groups, mom is tired
- **CTA Aggressiveness:** MEDIUM-LOW — "See if this fits your situation"
- **General Energy:** Inclusive, broad, like an experienced advisor who doesn't assume

### Cross-Level Bridge Language (Universal, from DOC5)

**Universal Openers:**
- "Wherever you are on this journey..."
- "Whether you're just starting or 3 years in..."
- "If you've tried 2 things or 12 things..."
- "For kids of all ages and profiles..."
- "Whatever you call this..."

**Universal Child Descriptors:**
- "Kids who don't feel when they need to go"
- "Kids whose nervous systems process signals differently"
- "Kids who 'should be ready' but aren't"
- "Kids whose pediatrician said 'wait'"

**Universal Validation:**
- "You've already worked harder than most parents ever do."
- "Whatever you've tried, you tried because you care."
- "The fact that you're here means you've been doing the work."

**Universal Mechanism:**
- "There's a signal that needs to come from the body."
- "Most methods assume the signal arrives. What if it doesn't?"
- "The brain needs sustained sensation to make the connection."
- "Pull-ups remove the signal entirely."

**The "Universal Path" Language Pattern:**
"Whether [Sub-Group A situation], or [Sub-Group C situation] — if [common symptom], here's what most parents miss."

Example: "Whether you're 3 weeks into wondering, or 3 years into trying — if your kid isn't progressing like 'every method' promises they will — here's what most parents miss."

### Visual & Design Calibration (from DOC5)

- **Age-Diverse Imagery:** Kids in different ages (3, 5, 7, 9)
- **Diverse Families:** Different ethnicities, family setups, single-mom visuals
- **Diverse Child Profiles:** Some with AAC devices, some with sensory tools, some mainstream
- **Calm, Inclusive Palette:** Brand-Green dominant, soft warmth
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Universal Recognition (Hero) → Specific Symptoms → Mechanism → Three-Family testimonial → Migration anchors → Universal Permission closer
