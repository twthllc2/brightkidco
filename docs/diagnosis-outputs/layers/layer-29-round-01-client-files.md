## 3. HERO IMAGE GUIDELINES

### Hero Images — DO'S
1. Real parents and real kids in real homes
2. Soft natural lighting, neutral cream/sage/sand tones
3. Diverse representation (different ethnicities, family setups)
4. Kids 3-10 years (range, not just toddlers)
5. Hands, silhouettes, half-faces (less invasive)
6. Product context (visible underwear) — not packaging
7. Mood: calm, real-life, not over-staged

**Source:** DOC1 Section 25 (lines 1636-1644)

### Hero Images — DON'TS
1. ❌ Stock images of neurotypical happy families
2. ❌ Bright cartoon colors for toddler-brand look
3. ❌ Cleaning chaos as drama image
4. ❌ Children's faces full-on (privacy + over-personal)
5. ❌ "Before/After" style with shame implications
6. ❌ Pull-ups staged as "evil"

**Source:** DOC1 Section 25 (lines 1646-1652)

### Avatar-Specific Image Implications

**Level 1 (Verbal, 3-5 years, ~35%):**
- Show younger kids (3-5 range)
- Pre-K / school readiness visual context OK
- Hope-forward imagery: kids near bathroom, looking at potty
- Testimonial imagery: short-medium format

**Level 2 (Mixed verbal, 5-7 years, ~40%):**
- Show kids in 5-7 range
- Sensory context: soft textures, gentle environments
- BCBA-respectful imagery (therapy settings OK but not dominant)
- Testimonial imagery: longer, with messy details
- GI-awareness imagery: no "clean success" staging

**Level 3 (Non-verbal, 6-10+ years, ~25%):**
- Show older kids (6-10+ range)
- Dignity-first imagery: no pity, no "sad child" staging
- High-support-needs context: adaptive equipment visible OK
- Testimonial imagery: long + messy, realistic
- Adult-diaper-size context: handled with care, never as shock value
- NEVER: urgency imagery, countdown timers as visual elements

**General Fallback (~50% real volume):**
- Cross-level imagery: show range of ages and abilities
- Symptom-focused, not diagnosis-focused
- Migration-anchor visuals: multiple family types in one image
- Neutral, inclusive tone

**Source:** DOC1 Section 25 + BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md Section 3 (Avatar Definitions)

## 12. CROSS-LEVEL VISUAL CALIBRATION RULES

### Rule 1: Symptom Description Over Level Label
- NEVER: "If your child is Level 2 or Level 3..."
- YES: "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..."
- Symptoms are cross-level universal.

### Rule 2: 3-5 Recognition Anchors Per Mail
Every mail must have a block with at least 3 specific situations so all levels recognize themselves:
- Level 1 typical: "Sits 20 minutes on toilet and nothing happens" / "Sometimes succeeds, sometimes doesn't"
- Level 2 typical: "Pees immediately when pull-up comes off" / "Plays while peeing"
- Level 3 typical: "Doesn't react to wet diaper" / "Made no progress in 4 years"

### Rule 3: Age Ranges Instead of Fixed Ages
- NEVER: "For 5-year-olds..."
- YES: "For kids 3 to 10+..."

### Rule 4: Verbal/Non-Verbal Inclusivity
- Always include both: "saying 'wet'" (verbal) + "showing you with a gesture" (non-verbal)

### Rule 5: Timeline Flexibility
- NEVER: "In 30 days your child will..."
- YES: "Some kids show first signals after 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal."

### Rule 6: Success Metrics Low Bar
- "Look at the wet spot for the first time"
- "Pull at their pants in awareness"
- "Walk toward the bathroom (even after the accident)"
- "Stay dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes"
