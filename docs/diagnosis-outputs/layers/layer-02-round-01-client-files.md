## 4. BEHAVIORAL PATTERN CATALOG

### 4.1 Parent Daily Routines

**Hypervigilant Mode (all avatars)**
- Watching child for body cues constantly (shifting, leg crossing, face turning red)
- Counting minutes between last pee and next attempt
- Setting phone alarms/timers for bathroom breaks
- Asking "Do you need to go potty?" every 15–30 minutes (becomes automatic)
- Carrying spare clothes/underwear/towels everywhere
- Pre-emptive parking lot potty stops before entering stores

**Laundry & Cleaning Cycle (Overwhelmed Avatar dominant)**
- Multiple outfit changes per day (sometimes 10+)
- Car seat covers washed repeatedly
- Couch cushions cleaned or covered in plastic bags
- Floors mopped multiple times daily
- Urine smell management (air fresheners, enzymatic cleaners)
- "Washing pile taller than me"

**Research & Information-Seeking Pattern (all avatars)**
- Googling at 2am: *"autistic 5 year old not potty trained"* → Autistic-MIRO
- Searching Reddit threads during sleepless nights → All files
- Joining autism parent Facebook groups → SocialPressure-AvatarG
- Screenshots of tips (but overwhelmed by volume) → Autistic-MIRO
- Watching sensory OT content → Autistic-MIRO
- Reading academic studies → Autistic-MIRO
- Asking pediatrician repeatedly → Autistic-MIRO
- Reading teacher forums about enforcement → Deadline-MIRO

**Shopping/Purchasing Pattern**
- Multiple potty types purchased (exact same potty in every room + car) → Autistic-1-full
- Visual charts, sticker systems, reward items → Autistic-1-full
- Favorite character underwear (to incentivize) → Autistic-MIRO
- Sensory supports (weighted blankets, sensory swings) → Autistic-1-full
- Books about autism & toileting → Autistic-MIRO
- Professional potty training courses → Deadline-MIRO
- Multiple cleaning products → Overwhelmed-MIRO
- Returns products that "didn't work" → Autistic-MIRO
- Will pay premium for "fast results" framing → Deadline-MIRO
- Price-sensitive (Social Pressure) vs. desperate (Deadline) → SocialPressure-MIRO
- Buys based on social proof / mom influencer recommendations → SocialPressure-MIRO

**Social Avoidance Pattern (Social Pressure Avatar)**
- Avoids playdates with fully trained kids
- Avoids family gatherings (MIL asks questions)
- Avoids posting about potty training online
- Changes subject when topic arises
- Avoids certain mom group threads
- Avoids booking family trips before school starts
- Leaves playgrounds early if embarrassed

**Coping Behaviors Across Avatars**
- Keeps spare clothes everywhere (car, diaper bag, daycare cubby)
- Uses overnight pull-ups during day "just in case"
- Makes jokes about it to reduce tension (dark humor)
- Over-celebrates small wins (party for one pee in potty)
- Switches methods weekly in desperation
- Uses phrases like "Big kids stay dry" repeatedly (mantra)
- Mentally rehearses conversations with teachers

### 4.2 Child Behavioral Patterns

**Avoidance/Resistance Patterns**
- Holding pee/poop for hours (withholding)
- Peeing the second they stand up after sitting on potty
- Hiding around corners to pee on floor
- Screaming when prompted to sit on potty
- Running away when parent suggests bathroom
- Removing clothes/pull-ups and running naked
- Rolling on floor to avoid pants
- Digging poop out of diaper with hands
- Flailing/hitting parent during bathroom battles

**Sensory/Neurological Patterns**
- No awareness of wetness (sits in soaked clothes, doesn't care)
- Body signal arrives too late — already peeing when they realize
- Interoception failure — cannot feel bladder fullness until emergency
- Underwear feels like diaper to them → treats training undies like pull-up
- Naked = success, underwear = accidents (consistently in research)
- Better at home, fails at daycare/school (context-dependent)
- Hyperfocus on play → ignores body signals completely

**Daycare/School Patterns**
- Holds pee all day at school, then releases tiny amount
- Multiple wet pants sent home in bag
- Accident reports in daycare app
- Refuses to use school bathrooms (different, scary)
- Regresses at school despite being trained at home
- Teacher assistance varies — some help, some don't

## 9. KEY INSIGHTS FOR EMAIL MESSAGING

### 9.1 The Core Problem (not about lacking a product)

The research shows the REAL customer problem:
> **"I am a mother of an autistic child. I have tried every method. Nothing works. I feel like a failure. I don't know what to do anymore."**
