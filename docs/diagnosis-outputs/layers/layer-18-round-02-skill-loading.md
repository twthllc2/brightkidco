# Layer 18 — Cross-Level Calibration: Skill Loading (Round 2)

## 1. Cross-Level Writing Rules from the Copy Skill

The `copy` skill (TENZA Copywriting Operating System) provides general email copywriting frameworks. For cross-level calibration, the relevant rules are:

### 1.1 Email Structure Framework
- Welcome Series: 7 emails over 14 days (Day 0, 1, 3, 5, 7, 10, 14)
- Cart Abandonment: 3 emails (1h, 24h, 72h)
- Post-Purchase: 6 emails (D0, D2, D7, D14, D30)
- Re-engagement: 3 emails (D0, D3, D7)

### 1.2 Subject Line Formulas (Cross-Level Applicable)
1. Curiosity: "[Number] ways to [benefit]"
2. Social proof: "Join [Number] others who..."
3. Urgency: "Last chance to [benefit]"
4. Personalization: "[Name], your [personalized thing] is ready"
5. How-to: "How to [desired outcome]"
6. Question: "Struggling with [problem]?"
7. Stat: "[Number]% of [audience] [action]"

### 1.3 Quality Gate (Cross-Level Check)
- No AI-isms from banned list (dive into, unlock, game-changer, etc.)
- Brand voice matches brief
- Subject line A/B testable (2+ variations)
- CTA specific and action-oriented (one per section)
- Read aloud: natural flow

### 1.4 Copy Skill Gaps for Cross-Level
The copy skill does NOT address:
- Multi-level audience calibration (Level 1/2/3 simultaneously)
- Symptom-first vs. label-first language
- Diagnosis-neutral messaging
- Migration strategy from general to level-specific
- The "Whether...Or...If..." rhetorical structure
- Cross-level testimonial patterns (Three-Family format)
- Interoception-based mechanism explanation

## 2. General Fallback Rules from DOC5

### 2.1 The 5 Sub-Groups (Must Serve Simultaneously)
| Sub-Group | Share | Key Characteristic |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| A — Diagnostic Entrants | ~25% | No ASD diagnosis yet, confusion dominant |
| B — Diagnosis-Rejecters | ~15% | Has diagnosis but rejects labels |
| C — Sensory-Difference Parents | ~25% | SPD/ADHD, no ASD, highly informed |
| D — Overwhelmed Non-Selectors | ~25% | Has level but clicked away pop-up |
| E — Cart/Browse without opt-in | ~10% | Captured via Klaviyo cookie, no level tag |

### 2.2 Core Principle: Symptom-First Language
- NEVER level labels: "For Level 2/3 kids..." → "For kids who don't feel when they need to go..."
- NEVER fixed ages: "If your 5-year-old..." → "If your child is anywhere from 3 to 10+..."
- NEVER diagnosis prerequisites: "For autistic children..." → "For children whose nervous systems process signals differently..."
- NEVER verbal-exclusive: "If he says 'I need to pee'..." → "Look for signals: pulling at pants, looking down, saying 'wet,' or showing you with a gesture..."

### 2.3 3-Level Psychological Pyramid (Every GF Mail Must Touch All 3)
1. **Surface — Universal Recognition**: "You have a child who isn't progressing in potty training like mainstream methods promise."
2. **Middle — Specific Symptoms**: "Kids who don't feel when they need to / keep playing while peeing / don't notice they're wet."
3. **Deep — Mechanism**: "This isn't behavior. This is signal processing."

**Structure Formula:**
1. Opener: Universal Recognition (Level 1)
2. Body: Specific Symptoms (Level 2)
3. Why-Section: Mechanism (Level 3)
4. CTA: Universal Permission ("if this fits, here's the option")

### 2.4 4-Phase Mix Dynamics (Simultaneous in Each Mail)
- Phase 1 elements: "If you're just starting to wonder..."
- Phase 2 elements: "If you tried Oh Crap or similar and it didn't work..."
- Phase 3 elements: "If you've cycled through methods for years..."
- Phase 4 elements: "Whether your kid is 3 or 10..."

**Composition tip:** Instead of 4 separate sections, integrate through: "Wherever you are on this journey..."

### 2.5 Universal Symptom Hooks (Priority 1)
- "Kids who sit on the toilet for 20 minutes and nothing happens"
- "Kids who pee right after the pull-up comes off"
- "Kids who play in a wet pull-up without reacting"
- "Kids who refuse to even try the toilet"
- "Kids whose pediatrician keeps saying 'wait until they're ready' — but the readiness never arrives"

### 2.6 Tonality Calibration (Medium-Balanced)
- Hope Level: 50/50 (not too high for Level 3, not too low for Level 1)
- Urgency: Low-Medium (soft urgency OK, never manipulative)
- Science Density: Medium (Kelly Mahler yes, but not 3 paragraphs)
- Empathy Intensity: High
- CTA Aggressiveness: Medium-Low ("See if this fits your situation")

### 2.7 Cross-Level Bridge Language
**Universal Openers:**
- "Wherever you are on this journey..."
- "Whether you're just starting or 3 years in..."
- "If you've tried 2 things or 12 things..."

**Universal Validation:**
- "You've already worked harder than most parents ever do."
- "Whatever you've tried, you tried because you care."

**Universal Mechanism:**
- "There's a signal that needs to come from the body."
- "Most methods assume the signal arrives. What if it doesn't?"

### 2.8 Forbidden Phrases (Cross-Level Specific)
- "For Level 1/2/3 kids..." — only hits Sub-Group D
- "Diagnosed autism only" — misses Sub-Groups A and C
- "Verbal kids will..." — misses Level 3
- "Your toddler will..." — some kids are 8-10
- School-specific urgency (Pre-K) — misses kids in special education
- "Just like other kids" — misses all neurodivergent families

### 2.9 Testimonial Strategy
**Three-Family-Pattern:** One testimonial per level (short, compact):
- Sarah, mom of 4yo verbal son: "He went from 'almost' to 'mostly' in 6 weeks."
- Lisa, mom of 6yo daughter (sensory dominant): "After 3 years of trying everything, she finally paused when she felt wetness."
- Maria, mom of 9yo non-verbal son: "We're 5 months in. He's not trained. But he's feeling something for the first time."

**Single Cross-Level Testimonial:** Uses symptom-first language, no diagnosis labels, realistic outcomes.

### 2.10 Migration Strategy (4 Paths)
1. **Click-Based:** Level-specific story links in GF mails → automatic tag update
2. **Reply-Based:** Customer replies with details → tag set based on content
3. **Purchase-Based Hints:** Underwear size → age hint; bundle size → experience hint
4. **Post-Purchase Level-Detection (Day 14):** 4-button mail asking "How would you describe your kid?" → 40-50% migration rate

### 2.11 GF-Specific Additional Levers
- **GF-A:** "For any kid whose signal doesn't come" — universal mechanism
- **GF-B:** "Wherever you are on this journey" — phase-agnostic
- **GF-C:** "Multiple paths to recognition" — outcome-flexible
- **GF-D:** "Not just for autism" — addresses Sub-Group C directly

### 2.12 The "Whether...Or...If..." Pattern
The DNA of cross-level language:
"Whether [Sub-Group A situation], or [Sub-Group C situation] — if [common symptom], here's what most parents miss."

Example: "Whether you're 3 weeks into wondering, or 3 years into trying — if your kid isn't progressing like 'every method' promises they will — here's what most parents miss."

## 3. Cross-Level Rules from DOC1 (Master Foundation)

### 3.1 Section 22: Cross-Level Calibration Rules
6 core rules for reaching all ASD levels simultaneously:

1. **Symptom Description Over Level Label**
   - NEVER: "If your child is Level 2 or Level 3..."
   - YES: "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..."

2. **3-5 Recognition Anchors Per Mail**
   - Every mail must have a block with at least 3 specific situations
   - Level 1: "Sits 20 minutes on toilet and nothing happens"
   - Level 2: "Pees immediately when pull-up comes off"
   - Level 3: "Doesn't react to wet diaper"

3. **Age Ranges Instead of Fixed Ages**
   - NEVER: "For 5-year-olds..."
   - YES: "For kids 3 to 10+..."

4. **Verbal/Non-Verbal Inclusivity**
   - Always give both: "saying 'wet'" (verbal) + "showing you with a gesture" (non-verbal)

5. **Timeline Flexibility**
   - NEVER: "In 30 days your child will..."
   - YES: "Some kids show first signals after 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal."

6. **Success Metrics Low Bar**
   - Define success as mini-wins: "Look at the wet spot for the first time", "Pull at their pants in awareness", "Stay dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes"

### 3.2 Section 6: The 4 Emotional Phases
| Phase | Child Age | Skepticism | Hope | Tonality |
|-------|-----------|------------|------|----------|
| 1 — Slow Realization | 2.5-3.5 | Low | High | Education, gentle |
| 2 — First Real Attempt | 3-4 | Medium | Medium | Validation + Mechanism |
| 3 — Method Cycling | 4-6 | Very high | Med-Low | Science + Trust |
| 4 — Resignation Fork | 6+ | Deep, calm | Hidden | Respectful, slow |

### 3.3 Section 11: 5 Big Conversion Levers (Universal)
1. "It's not the method. It's a sensory gap." — D1+D2 absolution
2. "Pull-ups make this worse" — creates urgency without manipulation
3. "Try it for 60 days. By your judgment." — reduces hope-risk
4. "I have two autistic sons" (Lena's Voice) — peer-to-peer trust
5. "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds are not toilet trained" — normalizes situation

### 3.4 Section 15: Forbidden Phrases (Brand-Wide)
- "Easy" / "Simple" / "Effortless"
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- "Suffering from autism" (autism = identity, not disease)
- "High-functioning" / "Low-functioning"
- "Wait until they're ready"
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix"
- "Your child will be normal"
- "Get him trained!"

### 3.5 Section 13: Lena Voice Rules
- Observation of reality → Validation without drama → Mechanism → Permission
- Sign-off: max 2-3 sentences + P.S. max 2 lines
- Never: founder framing, brand strategist voice, salesperson
- Always: peer-to-peer, "mom of two autistic sons"

### 3.6 Section 21: Tonality Calibration Per Flow Stage
| Stage | Tone | Energy |
|-------|------|--------|
| Pre-Purchase | Education + Soft Conversion | Calm, confident |
| Conversion Moment | Friendly Reminder + Permission | Decision-respect |
| Transactional | Warm + Functional | Professional but human |
| Post-Purchase Early | Warm + Reassurance + Education | Welcome to family |
| Post-Purchase Mid | Realistic + Patient + "It's normal" | Coach + cheerleader |
| Lifecycle | Respectful Re-engagement | Genuinely curious |

### 3.7 Section 23: Structural Do's & Don'ts
**Do's:**
- Open with the symptom (not the solution)
- Acknowledge prior failures BEFORE proposing mechanism
- Use 3-5 specific scenarios per mail
- Make Lena's voice the trust anchor
- Offer permission not to buy
- Realistic timelines with ranges
- Mini-wins as success metrics

**Don'ts:**
- Lead with the product
- Use "easy/quick/simple"
- Promise outcomes tied to timelines
- Compare to neurotypical kids
- Suggest the parent isn't doing enough
- Use level labels in copy
- Use urgency manipulation

- 3 emails (1h, 24h, 72h)
- E1 (1h): Mechanism reminder + symptom recognition
- E2 (24h): Social proof + 60-day guarantee
- E3 (72h): Final reminder + soft permission
- **Cross-Level Rules:** Universal symptom hooks, no diagnosis prerequisites, 60-day guarantee prominent

### 5.3 Checkout Abandonment (Cross-Level)
- 2 emails
- Similar to Cart but more direct
- **Cross-Level Rules:** Same as Cart Abandonment

### 5.4 Browse Abandonment (Cross-Level)

### 5.5 Transactional (Order Confirm, Shipping, Arrived)
- Cross-level by default

- **Cross-Level Rules:** Set realistic expectations, soft upsell spot, no hard-sell

### 5.6 Post-Purchase Early (Day 0-7)
- Cross-level until Level Detection
- "Here's what to expect" tone

### 5.7 Post-Purchase Level Detection (Day 14)
- The strongest migration method
- 4-button mail: "How would you describe your kid?"

- **Cross-Level Rules:** Diagnosis-neutral buttons, symptom-based descriptions

### 5.8 Winback (Cross-Level)
- Respectful re-engagement
- "Wherever you are now" energy
- **Cross-Level Rules:** No guilt-tripping, soft reopening, permission-not-to-buy

### 5.9 Sunset (Cross-Level)
- 2 emails
- 120 days inactive trigger
- **Cross-Level Rules:** Last chance with dignity, no pressure

## 6. Skill Coverage Gaps Identified

### 6.1 Gap: No Cross-Level Calibration in Copy Skill
The `copy` skill provides generic email frameworks but has NO coverage of:
- Multi-level audience calibration
- Symptom-first language rules
- Diagnosis-neutral messaging
- The "Whether...Or...If..." structure
- Cross-level testimonial patterns
- Migration strategy from general to level-specific

**Impact:** When using the copy skill alone, mails will default to generic frameworks that miss the nuanced cross-level calibration required for BrightKidCo's heterogeneous audience.

### 6.2 Gap: No Interoception Mechanism in Copy Skill
The copy skill has no coverage of:
- Interoception as the core mechanism
- Kelly Mahler references
- SPARK study statistics (49% stat)
- Signal-timing as the explanation for failed methods

**Impact:** Mails will lack the scientific credibility that differentiates BrightKidCo from generic potty training products.

### 6.3 Gap: No Lena Voice Specificity in Copy Skill
The copy skill provides general brand voice guidance but has no coverage of:
- Lena's specific profile (mom of two autistic sons)
- Peer-to-peer vs. founder framing
- The sign-off rules (max 2-3 sentences, P.S. max 2 lines)
- The "observation → validation → mechanism → permission" formula

**Impact:** Mails may sound like generic marketing rather than peer-to-peer communication.

### 6.4 Gap: No Forbidden Phrase Sensitivity in Copy Skill
The copy skill has a general AI-ism banned list but has no coverage of:
- BrightKidCo-specific forbidden phrases ("easy", "cure", "fix", "normal")
- Sensitive areas (self-blame, partner conflict, comparison wound)
- Level-specific forbidden phrases (Pre-K hooks for Level 3, etc.)

**Impact:** Mails may accidentally use phrases that destroy trust with this highly sensitive audience.

### 6.5 Gap: No Migration Strategy in Copy Skill
The copy skill has no coverage of:
- Click-based migration anchors
- Reply-based migration
- Purchase-based hints
- Post-purchase level-detection mail format

**Impact:** General Fallback mails will convert but fail to migrate subscribers to level-specific segments, reducing long-term LTV.

### 6.6 Gap: No 5 Sub-Group Awareness in Copy Skill
The copy skill treats audiences as homogeneous. It has no coverage of:
- Diagnostic Entrants (Sub-Group A)
- Diagnosis-Rejecters (Sub-Group B)
- Sensory-Difference Parents (Sub-Group C)
- Overwhelmed Non-Selectors (Sub-Group D)
- Cart/Browse without opt-in (Sub-Group E)

**Impact:** Mails will miss entire sub-groups, especially Sub-Group C (sensory-different, no ASD) who are highly profitable but overlooked.

### 6.7 Gap: No Visual/Design Calibration in Copy Skill
The copy skill has no coverage of:
- Age-diverse imagery requirements
- Diverse representation in hero images
- Brand-specific color palette (#039902, #DBFFCD, etc.)

**Impact:** Visual design may not match the cross-level inclusive tone required for General Fallback mails.

### What the Copy Skill Provides:
- Generic email structure frameworks (Welcome, Cart, Post-Purchase, Re-engagement)
- Subject line formulas (8 patterns)
- Quality gate checklist
- AI-ism banned list
- General brand voice guidance
- Landing page, ad, social content frameworks

### What's Missing for Cross-Level Calibration:
- Symptom-first language rules
- 3-Level Psychological Pyramid structure
- 4-Phase mix dynamics
- Universal symptom hooks
- Cross-level bridge language library
- Three-Family testimonial pattern
- Migration strategy (4 paths)
- Tonality calibration (50/50 balance)
- Forbidden phrases (BrightKidCo-specific)
- Lena voice rules
- Interoception mechanism explanation
- 5 Sub-Group awareness
- Visual/design calibration
- RS-holistic workflow

### Recommendation:
The copy skill should be used as a FOUNDATION for email structure, but all cross-level calibration rules must come from DOC1 (Master Foundation) and DOC5 (General Fallback Profile). The copy skill cannot replace these specialized documents for BrightKidCo's unique multi-level, diagnosis-neutral, symptom-first email ecosystem.
