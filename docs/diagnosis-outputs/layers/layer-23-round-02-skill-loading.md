## Key Frameworks Extracted

### Framework 1: Trust Mechanic Pyramid (from DOC 1)
**Source:** DOC 1, Section 9

```
#1: MECHANISM SPECIFICITY (most important)
#2: PEER-TO-PEER VOICE (Lena, real parents)
#3: PRIOR FAILURE ACKNOWLEDGMENT
#4: REALISTIC TIMELINES (Weeks, not days)
#5: 60-DAY GUARANTEE (Risk removal)
```

**Application to BCBA Loyalty:** This order is NON-NEGOTIABLE. For BCBA parents:
- #1 (Mechanism) must explain WHY BCBA protocols alone don't address the sensory gap — without criticizing BCBA
- #2 (Peer voice) = Lena, who has two autistic sons and understands BCBA context
- #3 (Failure acknowledgment) = "Your BCBA tried everything in her training. Nothing has worked." (verbatim quote from research)
- #4 (Timelines) = Realistic expectations alongside existing BCBA programs
- #5 (Guarantee) = 60-day trial that COMPLEMENTS BCBA, not replaces it

### Framework 2: BCBA-Specific Objection Handling (from DOC 1)
**Source:** DOC 1, Section 10, Objection B2

**Objection B2:** "My BCBA has a whole protocol. I trust that more."
- **Intensity:** LOW-MEDIUM (HIGH for Level 2-3)
- **Counter strategy:** Don't oppose ABA. Position as missing complement.
- **Language that works:** "ABA protocols work for the behavioral component. Body-Signal underwear works for the sensory component. Together, they close both gaps."
- **Use in:** Welcome E2-E4, FAQ E1, Cart Abandonment E2

**Additional BCBA-related objections from research:**

### Framework 3: Voice Pillars for BCBA Context
**Source:** brand-voice-definition + DOC 1 (Brand Voice Foundation)

**Pillar 1: Science-Backed Empathy**
- Definition: Speak to the BCBA parent's frustration with understanding, then back it with interoception science
- DO: "His BCBA confirms prompt-dependency. You're not imagining it. The sensory gap is real."
- DON'T: "Your BCBA's approach is wrong."

**Pillar 2: Complement, Don't Oppose**
- Definition: Position BrightKidCo as the MISSING PIECE alongside BCBA protocols, never as replacement
- DO: "ABA protocols work for the behavioral component. Body-Signal underwear works for the sensory component."
- DON'T: "Forget what your BCBA told you."

**Pillar 3: Peer-to-Peer Authority**
- Definition: Lena speaks as a mom who's been through BCBA context, not as a brand
- DO: "I have two autistic sons. One's 6, still in the middle of it. The other took us until he was almost 8."
- DON'T: "Our clinical team recommends..."

## Practical Application Notes for Email Copy

### BCBA Loyalty Email Copy Principles

1. **Never oppose BCBA.** Position BrightKidCo as the sensory complement to behavioral protocols.
2. **Lead with interoception science.** Kelly Mahler is the gold standard reference. SPARK stat (49%) normalizes the experience.
3. **Use Lena's voice.** She's a mom who navigated BCBA context herself — peer-to-peer, never founder framing.
4. **Validate before proposing.** Every email must acknowledge BCBA parents' years of effort before introducing mechanism.
5. **Permission-not-to-buy energy.** BCBA parents in Phase 3 are deeply skeptical. "Try alongside your BCBA for 60 days. If it doesn't complement, full refund."
6. **Verbatim quotes are gold.** Use 2-4 authentic quotes from RS research per email. Never fabricate.
7. **Symptom description over level label.** Never say "Level 2" in copy. Describe symptoms: "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..."

### BCBA-Specific Subject Line Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Verbatim quote | "His BCBA tried everything. Nothing worked." |
| Mechanism tease | "Why ABA protocols miss this one thing" |
| Normalization | "49% of autistic kids in BCBA programs aren't trained" |
| Complement frame | "What your BCBA can't teach: the sensory gap" |
| Peer voice | "From one BCBA mom to another" |

### BCBA-Specific Email Structure

```
1. Hook: Verbatim BCBA parent quote or symptom recognition
2. Validation: "Your BCBA is doing everything right. This isn't about behavioral compliance."
3. Mechanism: Interoception science — why the sensory gap exists
4. Complement positioning: "ABA handles behavior. Body-Signal underwear handles sensation."
5. Social proof: BCBA parent testimonial with messy details
6. Soft CTA: "Try alongside your BCBA for 60 days"
7. Lena sign-off: 2-3 sentences, peer-to-peer
```
