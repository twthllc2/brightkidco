## Level 1 Symptom Clusters (5 Clusters)

**Source:** DOC2-LEVEL1.txt (Section 6), DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION (Section 7), Autistic-LanguageBank-full.txt
**Audience share:** ~35% of Autistic Parent audience
**Child profile:** Verbal, low support needs, 3-5 years, Pre-K pressure

### Cluster A: Timing Problems
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He says 'I need to pee' AFTER it's already happened."
- "She tells me 5 seconds before, not in time."
- "He'll start to pee, then run (still peeing) to the potty."
- "He realizes he needs to go, but only after he's wet."
- "She tells me when she's done, never when she's about to."
- "He'll start to pee and theeen run (while peeing) to his potty like WHAT DO I DO."

**What it means:** The signal comes — but too late or too weak. The child feels something, but the connection "signal → action" is not trained.

**Email implication:** ~70% of Level 1 moms recognize. Very strong recognition hook.

---

### Cluster B: Inconsistent Performance
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "Some days he's great, other days zero."
- "Last week he did so well, now he won't even try."
- "He went to the potty 4 times yesterday, today he's refusing."
- "He goes when reminded, but never on his own."
- "He had a perfect week, then a month of accidents."
- "I just can't seem to get him to consistently use the potty."

**What it means:** The child can sometimes do it, but not reliably. Sensory regulation fluctuates.

**Email implication:** "Sometimes good, sometimes not" is THE Level 1 signal. ~80% of Level 1 moms identify.

---

### Cluster C: Verbal Refusal
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He just says 'no' when I ask if he needs to pee."
- "She tells me she doesn't need to go, then has an accident 10 minutes later."
- "He says 'later' or 'not now'."
- "She refuses to even try when she clearly needs to."
- "He says he doesn't have to go, even when I can SEE he does."
- "If I try prompting her she just freaks out and refuses to sit on the toilet."

**What it means:** The child has verbal resistance, but not sensory refusal. He doesn't FEEL that he has to go — so "I don't have to" is true from his perspective.

**Email implication:** Important to reframe: this is not defiance, but missing internal signal.

---

### Cluster D: Potty Interest Without Action
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He LOVES potty time but won't actually go."
- "She sits on it for 20 minutes, nothing happens."
- "He wants to be like daddy, but can't follow through."
- "She'll bring me the potty, but won't sit on it."
- "He pretends to potty, mimics it perfectly, but no actual go."
- "Sitting on the potty watching Bluey but not releasing."
- "She knew how to go, but she just didn't want to."

**What it means:** Motivation and interest are there. What's missing: the internal signal that triggers action.

**Email implication:** Highly specific for Level 1 — Level 2/3 often don't have this motivation.

---

### Cluster E: "Gets It But Doesn't Get It"
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He gets it, but he doesn't get it."
- "She knows what to do, she just won't do it."
- "He understands potty, talks about it, but won't follow through."
- "She can explain the steps but can't execute them."
- "He's so smart in everything else."
- "He's 4 and still in pull-ups, but he's so smart in everything else."
- "She can sing the alphabet but won't tell me she needs to pee."

**What it means:** THE central Level 1 pain. Mom sees intellectual understanding, but can't explain the disconnect to action — until they understand interoception.

**Email implication:** This phrase IS the Level 1 marker. When you use this language, you signal: "I see exactly your situation."

## Level 2 Symptom Clusters (6 Clusters)

**Source:** DOC3-LEVEL2.txt (Section 7), DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION (Section 7), Autistic-LanguageBank-full.txt
**Audience share:** ~40% of Autistic Parent audience (LARGEST SEGMENT)
**Child profile:** Mixed verbal, moderate support needs, 5-7 years, BCBA/OT context

### Cluster A: Sensory Defensiveness Around Toilet
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He won't sit on the toilet — the seat feels weird."
- "The flushing sound makes her cry."
- "The bathroom light is too bright for him."
- "He hates the cold seat in winter."
- "She panics when the toilet flushes."
- "The bathroom has terrible acoustics for him."
- "Public bathrooms are impossible — we just don't go."
- "She needs the same toilet at home, anywhere else triggers meltdowns."

**What it means:** The bathroom itself is sensory hell. Before toilet training can start, sensory tolerance must be built.

**Email implication:** ~80% of Level 2 moms recognize themselves. Very strong recognition hook.

---

### Cluster B: Withholding + GI Issues
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He holds his poop until bedtime when the pull-up goes on."
- "She refuses to go for hours."
- "He has chronic constipation."
- "She'll sit on the toilet for 30 minutes, get up, and immediately go in her pull-up."
- "He's afraid to poop, will hold it for days."
- "He's on Miralax daily for 2 years."
- "She has overflow incontinence — soils without realizing."
- "He's been hospitalized for impaction twice."
- "My son is autistic as well and dribbles a lot and withholds his pee forever."

**What it means:** Withholding is not behavior — it's fear/sensory trigger. GI complications are not periphery, but central.

**Email implication:** Very emotional trigger. Mails must not promise "miracle cure" for withholding. Validate, but don't oversell.

---

### Cluster C: Delayed Verbal Signals
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He says 'wet' after the accident, never before."
- "She uses scripted speech, can't tell me her needs in real time."
- "He echoes 'do you need to pee?' instead of answering."
- "She can label body parts but not feelings."
- "He has the words but can't connect them to internal sensations."
- "She uses AAC for everything except bathroom needs."
- "He'll talk about peeing in past tense after the fact."

**What it means:** Verbal available, but not for internal signals. Communication tools (AAC, social stories) help for external things, not for interoception needs.

**Email implication:** Important for Level 2 mom: "verbal doesn't mean independent" hits her deeply.

---

### Cluster D: Schedule Dependency Without Internal Signal
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He'll go when I take him every 90 minutes, but never on his own."
- "She's been on a timed schedule for 2 years — still no independence."
- "He's prompt-dependent, the BCBA confirms it."
- "She goes for the teacher but not for me."
- "He's compliant when prompted, never initiates."
- "After 30 hours of ABA, he sits — but doesn't go on his own."

**What it means:** The child is "trained to comply", not "trained to feel". BCBA has perfected the behavioral component, but sensory component is missing.

**Email implication:** THAT is the most resonant frustration for Level 2 moms. They know prompt-dependency intimately.

---

### Cluster E: Regression Cycles
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "We had 3 weeks of success then total regression."
- "She trained at school then forgot it all over summer break."
- "He goes through phases — months of accidents, then weeks of dryness."
- "Every change in routine sets us back to zero."
- "Vacation always means starting over."
- "COVID set us back 2 years of progress."
- "Every illness erases what we built."
- "We had a few hard weeks where he wasn't sleeping at night."

**What it means:** Progress is not linear, often frustratingly cyclical. Routine sensitivity makes stability very difficult.

**Email implication:** Mail language must acknowledge: regressions are not failure, are sensory recalibration.

---

### Cluster F: Location-Specific Training
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He goes at home but never in public."
- "She goes at school but not at home."
- "He has a specific toilet he'll use — anywhere else, nothing."
- "She's trained at grandparents' but not at our house."
- "Travel is impossible — different bathroom = no go."

**What it means:** Generalization is missing. Child has local skills, but can't generalize them. Sensory triggers differ per location.

**Email implication:** Medium strength, but very Level 2 specific.

## Level 3 Symptom Clusters (6 Clusters)

**Source:** DOC4-LEVEL3.txt (Section 7), DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION (Section 7), Autistic-LanguageBank-full.txt
**Audience share:** ~25% of Autistic Parent audience
**Child profile:** Non-verbal/minimal verbal, high support needs, 6-10+ years, Medicaid context

### Cluster A: Complete Signal Absence
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He's never shown a single sign of feeling wet or dirty."
- "She has no awareness of bodily functions at all."
- "He's been in pull-ups for 9 years and never reacted to a single wet diaper."
- "There are no readiness signs. There never have been."
- "He doesn't pull at his diaper, doesn't ask for changes, doesn't react to soiled."
- "Neither boy was bothered or even seemed to notice if they are wet or dirty."
- "He doesn't realize he needs to go. He doesn't realize he's gone."
- "He's never given me a signal in 8 years."
- "He doesn't care about peeing himself one bit."

**What it means:** The interoceptive system either sends no signal, or the signal cannot be translated to awareness. This is not behavioral — it's neurologically fundamental.

**Email implication:** Mom has often spent years searching for "readiness signs" that never came. This cluster is deeply painful.

---

### Cluster B: Sensory Overload Bathroom
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He won't even enter the bathroom. The lights, the sound, everything is too much."
- "She has meltdowns just from the toilet seat texture."
- "He needs noise-cancelling headphones in the bathroom."
- "The flushing sound triggers self-injury."
- "She has a fear of falling into the toilet — won't sit."
- "He won't go in public bathrooms — we're trapped at home."

**What it means:** Bathroom = sensory war zone. Training cannot start before sensory acclimation happens.

**Email implication:** Sensory awareness in mail language is critical. Acknowledge bathroom specifics.

---

### Cluster C: Withholding + Severe GI Issues
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He withholds bowel movements for 5-7 days at a time."
- "She's been hospitalized for impaction twice."
- "He's on a daily Miralax dose for years."
- "GI specialist appointments every 3 months."
- "He has overflow incontinence — soils without realizing."
- "She's been on a strict diet for 4 years to manage GI."
- "He holds his bowel movements for 5-7 days."

**What it means:** GI complications are not periphery — they are often the central medical theme. Withholding is sensory + fear cycle.

**Email implication:** Careful: NO promises for GI healing. Medical team as primary, body-signal as adjunctive.

---

### Cluster D: Communication Lack
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He can't tell me when he needs to go — he's non-verbal."
- "She uses AAC for food and toys but can't request bathroom."
- "He has 30 words. Bathroom isn't one of them."
- "She points to things but can't communicate body needs."
- "He hits his diaper sometimes — I don't know if that's a signal."
- "There's no way for him to let me know."
- "She can't communicate, doesn't understand. Mines level 3."
- "He is unable to let me know when he has to go, no signals or anything."

**What it means:** Even when child feels something, they can't communicate. This is a double barrier: signal + communication.

**Email implication:** Important: body-signal training targets the feeling (signal), not the speaking. Mom must understand: this is just step 1.

---

### Cluster E: Physical Resistance
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He kicks me in the face when I try to change him."
- "My 6 year old son kicked me in the face today, while having a meltdown when I changed his diaper."
- "She self-injures when I try to put her on the toilet."
- "He hits his head when we're in the bathroom."
- "It takes two adults to change him now that he's bigger."
- "He's stronger than me — I can't physically guide him anymore."
- "He hits his head against the wall when I try to take him to the toilet."

**What it means:** Physical resistance is real and results from sensory overload + communication lack + frustration. This mom often has daily physical confrontations.

**Email implication:** Very emotional and painful. Mails should acknowledge this without dramatizing. NO promise that the product solves this resistance — it cannot.

---

### Cluster F: Size and Progression Fear
**══════════════════════════════════════════**

**Exact parent formulations:**
- "He's outgrown size 7. We're moving to specialty diapers."
- "The next step is adult diapers. I'm not ready."
- "She's getting too big for me to carry to the changing table."
- "He's 65 pounds. I can't lift him anymore."
- "Where do you even buy adult diapers for kids?"
- "I've started looking at adult-size pull-ups online."
- "The pull-up upgrade — the first time I had to order size 7 diaper I cried."
- "He's outgrown size 7 pull-ups. We're moving to adult diapers. I cried in the store."

**What it means:** Size progression shows permanence of the situation. Adult diapers feel like "final defeat."

**Email implication:** Very sensitive. Acknowledging adult diapers OK, but NEVER as sales pressure. This is trauma material.

## The 10 Failed Methods (Why Each One Failed)

**Source:** DOC1-MASTER-FOUNDATION (Section 8)

| # | Method | Why It Failed |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Sticker charts / Reward-based | Rewards cannot create or clarify internal body signals |
| 2 | Oh Crap / 3-day bootcamp | Expects "pee-pee dance" signal that never arrives |
| 3 | Timer-based schedules | Creates prompt-dependency, replaces internal signal with external |
| 4 | Social stories / Picture symbols | Explains the WHAT but not the HOW-IT-FEELS |
| 5 | ABA-based protocols | Behavioral compliance without sensory awareness |
| 6 | Special potty seats / Stairsteps | Addresses physical comfort, not signal gap |
| 7 | Naked method | Child has no sensory reaction to wet legs |
| 8 | Pull-ups worn like underwear | Wicking technology removes any signal |
| 9 | Punishment / Shaming | Creates anxiety, withholding, regression |
| 10 | "Just wait until he's ready" | Readiness signs often NEVER come spontaneously for ASD kids |

**Email implication:** These 10 failures can be used in any Welcome Education / FAQ / Cart Recovery email. Format: "You've tried [method]. Here's why it couldn't work for your child's nervous system: [neurological explanation]."
