## Finding 1: Wiki Psychology Frameworks — Conflict in Relationships

**Source:** `identity/brute-de-force-teachings-small--decoding-conflict-in-relationships.md` (stub, 15 lines)

The wiki contains a stub note on "decoding conflict in relationships" within the Brute de Force teachings framework. Key principles:

- Conflict in relationships is "not merely an abstract idea but a lived reality forged through experience, pressure, and conscious choice"
- Understanding conflict requires seeing it as "part of an integrated system of personal development rather than an isolated trait"
- The note links to causal law, discipline mindset, and spiritual frameworks for context

**Source:** `psychology/core-principle-duality-and-the-conflict-of-man.md` (stub, 21 lines)

A stub on duality and internal conflict. Links to scarcity mindset and the polarity principle.

**Source:** `psychology/relationships-as-oxygen.md` (stub, 21 lines)

A stub on relationships as a fundamental need. Links to motivations and sacrifice-audit frameworks.

### Cross-Reference with Client Files

The wiki's psychology stubs provide a GENERAL framework: conflict is real, relationships are essential, and duality creates tension. But the BrightKidCo partner conflict is SPECIFIC — it's not about personal development or philosophical duality. It's about a mother who does 80% of the potty training work while her partner undermines the effort, blames her, or checks out entirely.

DOC1's Sensitive Areas section (line 991-993) explicitly identifies partner conflict as a protected zone:
- "PARTNER CONFLICT — Mails must not sound like she has to convince her partner."

The Autistic-Parent Reddit thread (Post 8, lines 3221-3242) provides the defining verbatim quote:
- "My partner is FURIOUS at me that she isn't potty trained, and it turned into a massive argument. He insists that it's my 'unwillingness to try' and 'lack of motivation', but completely disregards her complete fear and clear signs that she isn't ready. In his words, 'if kids 100 years ago were potty trained by 18 months, you have no excuse with her'."

**Connection:** The wiki's psychology frameworks are too abstract to directly inform partner conflict copy. The Brute de Force "pressure and conscious choice" framing could theoretically apply (the partner's frustration comes from societal pressure + lack of autism understanding), but the stubs contain no actionable content. The real insight comes from the client files: partner conflict in autism potty training is not about relationship dynamics in general — it's about one partner not understanding the neurological reality of autism.

## Finding 2: The Unbalanced State — Relationship Stress as Trade-Off

**Source:** `about-me/identity/__enqueued__/__enqueued__/brute-de-force-teachings-small--the-unbalanced-state-of-a-man-on-a-mission.md`

This wiki note discusses how pursuing ambitious goals creates imbalance:

### Cross-Reference with Client Files

This framing inverts the BrightKidCo partner conflict dynamic. In the wiki's framework, relationship stress is a BARRIER to action. In the BrightKidCo context, the ACTION (potty training) IS the source of relationship stress.

The Kommentare reveal this inversion clearly:
- "I CANNOT go through this any longer with my 5 year old (and my husband)." (Kommentare-4, line 1194)
- "The worst part is my husband is getting so frustrated and angry with him." (Kommentare-4, line 1200)

- "Unfortunately I believe the main reason for her regression this time is my ex husband and I got divorced a few months back." (Kommentare-4, line 1488)

**Connection:** The wiki's "imbalanced state" framework is relevant to BrightKidCo's COPY STRATEGY, not to the customer's reality. The email copy must NOT create additional imbalance by asking the mom to "convince her partner" or "show your family you have a plan." Instead, the copy should REDUCE the imbalance — give her confidence without requiring partner buy-in. This aligns with DOC1's partner conflict sensitivity rule.

### Gap 2: No Relationship Conflict Psychology Applied to Purchase Decisions
The wiki's conflict frameworks (Brute de Force, duality) are personal-development-oriented, not consumer-psychology-oriented. There's no content on:
- How partner disagreement affects purchase decisions
- Whether the buyer needs the partner's approval
- How "solo decision" framing affects conversion rates
- The psychology of "permission-free" purchasing

**Impact:** The Layer 11 E2 counter-strategy ("empower solo decision") is derived from the client file and general copywriting intuition, not from any wiki knowledge base.
