## 1. CORE ANTI-FABRICATION RULES (VERBATIM)

### From Claude-Project-Instructions.txt (Section: ANTI-FABRICATION, NON-NEGOTIABLE)

> "You may ONLY use verbatim quotes that exist in the RS PDFs provided in this project."

**You may NOT:**
- Invent customer quotes
- Fabricate testimonials
- Imagine "what a mom might say"

## 6. SENSITIVE AREAS — EXTRA CARE RULES

From DOC1 Section 15 (lines 987-1007) and BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md (lines 193-199):

1. **SELF-BLAME** — She's already on a guilt seesaw. Mails must NEVER hint that she's doing something wrong. Always absolve, never accuse.
2. **PARTNER CONFLICT** — Mails must not sound like she has to convince her partner.
3. **COMPARISON WOUND** — Playground comparisons with neurotypical peers are triggers. Mails must NEVER position neurotypical kids as standard.
4. **PEDIATRICIAN FRUSTRATION** — Gently acknowledge, never attack the entire medical profession.
5. **FINANCIAL HARDSHIP** — ~25% use Medicaid. $79.99 is real commitment. Math reframe OK, never preachy.
6. **CHILD'S IDENTITY** — NEVER "your son will be normal" — autism = identity, not disease. "Help his nervous system" > "fix his behavior".

## 9. WHAT HAPPENS WHEN NO REAL QUOTE EXISTS

This is the critical escape hatch for Layer 30:

1. **FLAG IT** — Tell Ayoub (the marketer) that no matching real quote exists in the RS PDFs. He escalates to the Brand Strategist or accepts a different approach. (Source: CPI line 280, DOC6 line 247)

2. **Use universal symptom descriptions** — Instead of a specific quote, use the pre-approved symptom language from the Universal Child Descriptors, Universal Validation, or Universal Mechanism libraries in BRAND_VOICE_GUIDE.md Appendix. (Source: BVG line 942)

3. **NEVER fabricate** — The cost of a fabricated quote being exposed is brand reputation damage. The brand serves a vulnerable population (parents of autistic children). Trust is the #1 asset. (Source: CPI line 284, DOC6 line 248)
