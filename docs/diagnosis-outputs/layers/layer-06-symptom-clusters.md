## SECTION 6: TEN FAILED METHODS — NEUROLOGICAL EXPLANATIONS

| # | Method | Why It Failed | Neurological Explanation |
|---|--------|---------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Sticker charts / Reward-based | Rewards cannot create or clarify internal body signals | Dopamine-based extrinsic motivation cannot generate interoceptive awareness. The child may WANT the reward but still cannot FEEL the signal. |
| 2 | Oh Crap / 3-day bootcamp | Expects "pee-pee dance" signal that never arrives | Assumes neurotypical signal pattern: urgency → awareness → action. In ASD, the signal-to-awareness pathway is delayed or absent. |
| 3 | Timer-based schedules | Creates prompt-dependency, replaces internal signal with external | The brain learns "Mom/BCBA reminds me = time to go" instead of "my body signals = time to go." External cue overrides internal awareness. |
| 4 | Social stories / Picture symbols | Explains the WHAT but not the HOW-IT-FEELS | Cognitive understanding (prefrontal cortex) does not create sensory awareness (insula/interoception). Two different brain systems. |
| 5 | ABA-based protocols | Behavioral compliance without sensory awareness | Behavioral reinforcement shapes EXTERNAL behavior, not INTERNAL awareness. Child learns to comply, not to feel. |
| 6 | Special potty seats / Stairsteps | Addresses physical comfort, not signal gap | Mechanical access solution does not solve the sensory-signal problem. |
| 7 | Naked method | Child has no sensory reaction to wet legs | If interoception is impaired, skin-wetness is also not processed as a salient signal. Same root cause. |
| 8 | Pull-ups worn like underwear | Wicking technology removes any signal | Modern pull-ups are engineered to keep the child dry — which means NO sensory feedback from wetness. |
| 9 | Punishment / Shaming | Creates anxiety, withholding, regression | Stress hormones (cortisol) SHUT DOWN interoceptive awareness. Anxiety amplifies sensory avoidance and withholding. |
| 10 | "Just wait until he's ready" | Readiness signs often NEVER come spontaneously for ASD kids | Readiness framework assumes spontaneous interoceptive development. For many autistic children, interoception needs active training — waiting is not a strategy. |
