## PART 1: THE 6 CORE CROSS-LEVEL CALIBRATION RULES (DOC 1, Section 22)

These are the foundational rules for writing mails that reach all ASD levels simultaneously.

### Rule 1: Symptom Description Over Level Label

**NEVER:** "If your child is Level 2 or Level 3..."
**YES:** "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..."

> "Symptoms are cross-level universal." — DOC 1, Section 22

This is the #1 rule. In mail copy, NEVER say "Level 2." Instead describe symptoms. The tag is backend logic, not content. (DOC 1, Section 22; DOC 6, Principle 1; DOC 5, Rule 1)

**DOC 5, Section 5 deepens this into 4 sub-rules:**
- **Rule 1: NEVER LEVEL LABELS** — "NEVER: 'For Level 2/3 kids...' / INSTEAD: 'For kids who don't feel when they need to go...'"
- **Rule 2: NEVER FIXED AGE** — "NEVER: 'If your 5-year-old...' / INSTEAD: 'If your child is anywhere from 3 to 10+...'"
- **Rule 3: NEVER DIAGNOSIS PREREQUISITE** — "NEVER: 'For autistic children...' / INSTEAD: 'For children whose nervous systems process signals differently...'"
- **Rule 4: NEVER VERBAL-EXCLUSIVE** — "NEVER: 'If he says I need to pee...' / INSTEAD: 'Look for signals: pulling at pants, looking down, saying wet, or showing you with a gesture...'"

> "Avoid labels. Describe behavior patterns." — DOC 5, Section 5, Core Principle

### Rule 2: 3-5 Recognition Anchors Per Mail

In EVERY mail there must be a block with at least 3 specific situations so all levels recognize themselves.

**Level 1 typical anchors:**
- "Sits 20 minutes on toilet and nothing happens"
- "Sometimes succeeds, sometimes doesn't"

**Level 2 typical anchors:**
- "Pees immediately when pull-up comes off"
- "Plays while peeing"

**Level 3 typical anchors:**
- "Doesn't react to wet diaper"
- "Made no progress in 4 years"

> "Mom reads the list, finds 1-2 that exactly match her situation." — DOC 1, Section 22

Each level-specific doc reinforces this: Level 1 uses 5 symptom clusters, Level 2 uses 6 symptom clusters, Level 3 uses 6 symptom clusters. Every mail should include 2-3 clusters as recognition anchors. (DOC 2, Section 6; DOC 3, Section 7; DOC 4, Section 7)

### Rule 3: Age Ranges Instead of Fixed Ages

**NEVER:** "For 5-year-olds..."
**YES:** "For kids 3 to 10+..."

— DOC 1, Section 22

### Rule 4: Verbal/Non-Verbal Inclusivity

When giving verbalization examples, always include both:

> "Look for signals: pulling at their pants, looking down, saying 'wet,' or showing you with a gesture."
> → Verbal: "saying 'wet'"
> → Non-verbal: "showing you with a gesture"

— DOC 1, Section 22; DOC 5, Rule 4

### Rule 5: Timeline Flexibility

**NEVER:** "In 30 days your child will..."
**YES:** "Some kids show first signals after 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal."

— DOC 1, Section 22

Per-level timeline expectations:
- Level 1: 2-6 weeks to first mini-wins, 6-12 weeks to daytime training (DOC 2)
- Level 2: 4-8 weeks to first mini-wins, 3-6 months to daytime independence (DOC 3)
- Level 3: 8-16 weeks to first mini-wins, 4-12 months to substantial change (DOC 4)

### Rule 6: Success Metrics — Low Bar

Define "success" as mini-wins, not "fully trained":
- "Look at the wet spot for the first time"
- "Pull at their pants in awareness"
- "Walk toward the bathroom (even after the accident)"
- "Stay dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes"

— DOC 1, Section 22

Level 3 redefines wins even smaller: "He let me clean him without biting today," "She didn't melt down when I changed her," "He tolerated the bathroom for 10 minutes today." (DOC 4, Section 8)

### Specific Shift Rules

**Level 1 → Level 2 shifts:**
- MORE science density (Kelly Mahler, BCBA references)
- LESS hope language
- MORE BCBA-respectful framing
- LONGER timelines
- NO Pre-K pressure (often already irrelevant)
- Acknowledge regression cycles as normal (DOC 3)

**Level 2 → Level 3 shifts:**
- DIGNITY in every sentence (DOC 4, non-negotiable)
- NO urgency, never (DOC 4)
- MINI-WINS redefined even smaller
- Acknowledge adult diapers as reality (DOC 4, carefully)
- NO "miracle" or "easy" (stricter than Level 2)
- Late-trainer stories as master anchor (Maureen Bennie 9.5 years)
- 12-18 sentence testimonials with "still not fully trained" honest closings (DOC 4)

## PART 3: GENERAL FALLBACK WRITING RULES (DOC 5)

### 3-Level Psychological Pyramid

Every General Fallback mail must simultaneously serve three levels:

**LEVEL 1: SURFACE — Universal Recognition**
> "You have a child who isn't progressing in potty training like mainstream methods promise."
> → Reaches: ALL sub-groups, all levels.

**LEVEL 2: MIDDLE — Specific Symptoms**
> "Kids who don't feel when they need to / keep playing while peeing / don't notice they're wet."
> → Reaches: those with interoception issues, regardless of diagnosis.

**LEVEL 3: DEEP — Mechanism**
> "This isn't behavior. This is signal processing."
> → Reaches: those who want to know WHY.

> "EVERY MAIL must touch all 3 levels — otherwise you lose at least one sub-group." — DOC 5, Section 6

**STRUCTURE FORMULA FOR EACH GF MAIL:**
1. Opener: Universal Recognition (Level 1)
2. Body: Specific Symptoms (Level 2)
3. Why-Section: Mechanism (Level 3)
4. CTA: Universal Permission ("if this fits, here's the option")

— DOC 5, Section 6

### 4-Phase Mix Dynamics

The 4 emotional phases must all be addressable simultaneously in General Fallback mails:

- **Phase 1 elements:** "If you're just starting to wonder..." — Education approach, gentle mechanism intro
- **Phase 2 elements:** "If you tried Oh Crap or similar and it didn't work..." — Validation of method failure, reframe
- **Phase 3 elements:** "If you've cycled through methods for years..." — Science (Kelly Mahler, SPARK), 60-Day Guarantee
- **Phase 4 elements:** "Whether your kid is 3 or 10..." — "Late is not never" hint, permission-not-to-buy

> "These 4 phase elements must be present simultaneously in each mail without making the mail feel overloaded." — DOC 5, Section 7

**Example opener touching all 4 phases:**
> "Whether you're just starting to wonder if something's off, or you've been trying methods for years — if your kid isn't progressing like 'every method' promises they will — here's what most parents miss. It's not behavior. It's not motivation. It's signal-timing." — DOC 5, Section 7

### Universal Symptom Hooks (Cross-Level)

These symptoms are recognizable across ALL levels and sub-groups:

| Symptom | Levels Hit |
|---------|-----------|
| "Kids who sit on the toilet for 20 minutes and nothing happens" | L1-3, all ages |
| "Kids who pee right after the pull-up comes off" | L1-3, especially 3-7 years |
| "Kids who say 'I don't have to go' and have an accident 10 minutes later" | L1-2 (verbal) |
| "Kids who don't react to wetness at all" | L2-3 (hypo-responsive) |
| "Kids who've been 'almost trained' more than once" | L1 dominant, L2 |
| "Kids who go at school but not at home" | L2 dominant |
| "Kids who hold bowel movements until the pull-up returns" | L2-3 |
| "Kids who've tried 3+ methods with no lasting result" | L1-3 |

— DOC 5, Section 8

## PART 4: "WHETHER...OR...IF..." LANGUAGE PATTERN (DOC 5, Section 23)

This is the most important rhetorical structure for General Fallback:

**Pattern:**
> "Whether [Sub-Group A situation], or [Sub-Group C situation] — if [common symptom], here's what most parents miss."

**Example 1:**
> "Whether you're 3 weeks into wondering, or 3 years into trying — if your kid isn't progressing like 'every method' promises they will — here's what most parents miss."

**Example 2:**
> "Whether your kid is verbal or not, 3 or 10, diagnosed or watching — if the signal isn't arriving, behavior-training alone won't bridge the gap."

**Example 3:**
> "Whether you've tried 2 things or 12 — if your kid has 'almost trained' more than once, there's a piece worth considering."

> "This 'Whether...Or...If...' structure is the DNA of cross-level language. It embraces diversity without losing specificity." — DOC 5, Section 23

**Usage across flows (from DOC 5 sample openers):**
- Welcome E1: "Whether you're 3 weeks into wondering, or 3 years into trying — your code's below."
- Welcome E4: "Whether you tried Oh Crap, sticker charts, scheduled sits, ABA protocols, or something else — every standard method assumes a signal exists in the body."
- Cart Abandonment E2: "Wherever you are on this journey — whether you've tried 2 things or 12 — the 60-day guarantee covers you."
- Post-Purchase Early: "Whatever your child's profile — verbal or not, 3 or 10, diagnosed or watching — the first week often looks like nothing's happening."
- Winback: "Wherever you are now — still in pull-ups, on a new method, or taking a break — I wanted to share one new piece."

**DOC 6 confirms:** DOC 5 has the "Whether...Or...If..." language, the universal symptom hooks and the migration strategy. It is the operationally most important doc. (DOC 6, Section 7)

## PART 5: MIGRATION MESSAGING GUIDELINES (DOC 5, Sections 28-33)

### Why Migration Is Critical

> "General Fallback is not an end state. It is a TEMPORARY state until mom self-identifies." — DOC 5, Section 28

**Goals of migration:**
1. Level-specific mails convert better
2. Customer Lifetime Value higher with accurate segmentation
3. Post-purchase education can be personalized
4. Reduce refunds through accurate expectation management
5. Future marketing can be targeted

> "General Fallback mails are not just conversion tools. They are also DATA COLLECTION tools." — DOC 5, Section 28

### 4 Migration Paths

**PATH 1: Click-Based Migration**
Customer clicks on level-specific story links in General Fallback mails. Each link triggers automatic Klaviyo tag update. 3-4 migration anchors built into every GF mail:

- **Level 1 Anchor:** "If you have a verbal child under 5 who 'almost trained' multiple times, here's Sarah's story → [Link]"
- **Level 2 Anchor:** "If your kid has sensory issues around the bathroom and you've worked with a BCBA, here's Lisa's path → [Link]"
- **Level 3 Anchor:** "If your child is 7+ and non-verbal, here's Maria's journey → [Link]"
- **Cross-Level Anchor:** "If you're not sure yet — here's the general framework → [Link]"

— DOC 5, Section 30

**Etiquette:** "Migration anchors should be organically placed in mail. Never as 'click here if you're Level X' cold-tag. Instead as story recommendation. Mom thinks: 'That's my kid!'" — DOC 5, Section 30

**PATH 2: Reply-Based Migration**
Customer replies with details about child. Level indicators in replies:
- **Level 1:** "Pre-K is coming," "He's 3/4/5 years old," "Oh Crap didn't work," "He's verbal but..."
- **Level 2:** "BCBA / ABA / OT," "5/6/7 years old," "Sensory issues," "Prompt-dependent," "Withholding"
- **Level 3:** "Non-verbal / AAC," "8/9/10 years old," "Never been trained," "Specialty diapers," "Medicaid"

— DOC 5, Section 31

**PATH 3: Purchase-Based Hints**
- Underwear size → age hint: 2T-3T likely Level 1, XL/XXL likely Level 3
- Bundle size → experience hint: 1 Pair = tentative, 5+5 = high-investment (often Level 2-3)

— DOC 5, Section 32

**PATH 4: Post-Purchase Level-Detection Mail (STRONGEST)**
Day 14 mail asks "How would you describe your kid right now?" with 4 buttons:
1. "Verbal, 'almost trained' but inconsistent" → avatar_level1
2. "Sensory issues, tried many methods" → avatar_level2
3. "Non-verbal or high support needs" → avatar_level3
4. "I'm still figuring it out — keep it general" → avatar_general

> Conversion rate: Open 65-75%, Click 35-50%, Result ~40-50% migration from General to specific Level. — DOC 5, Section 33

## PART 6: LEVEL TRANSITION RULES — HOW COPY CHANGES PER LEVEL PAIR

### What Distinguishes Each Level (Delta Comparisons)

**Level 1 vs Level 2 (DOC 2, Section 4; DOC 3, Section 4):**
- L1 has 1-3 training attempts / L2 has 3-5+
- L1 child is verbal / L2 is mixed verbal
- L1 mom has less BCBA experience / L2 has years of BCBA
- L1 is more optimistic / L2 is skeptical
- Pre-K is strong driver for L1 / often already irrelevant for L2
- L1 hope intact / L2 hope has cracks

**Level 2 vs Level 3 (DOC 3, Section 4; DOC 4, Section 4):**
- L2 still has "maybe trained" hope / L3 has "maybe never"
- L2 child significantly younger / L3 older (6-10+)
- L2 has not resigned / L3 has accepted resignation
- L2 reacts to science / L3 reacts to dignity
- L2 child is verbal / L3 often non-verbal

**Level 1 vs Level 3 (DOC 2, Section 4; DOC 4, Section 4):**
- L1 has no "resignation" history / L3 does
- L1 child is significantly younger
- L1 mom has no Medicaid pull-up routine / L3 often does
- L1 would be surprised by "non-verbal" language
- L1 mom doesn't think "maybe never" / L3 has accepted it

### How Copy Must Shift Between Levels

**Mails for Level 1 MUST (DOC 2, Section 4):**
- MORE hope language (but realistic)
- LESS science density
- MORE Pre-K urgency
- NO heavy resignation language
- NO Level 3 stories (misses her)

**Mails for Level 2 MUST (DOC 3, Sections 4-5):**
- Science-first (Kelly Mahler, SPARK, OT quotes)
- BCBA-respectful language throughout (never criticize BCBA)
- Acknowledge regression cycles as normal
- Permission-not-to-buy strongly prominent
- NO Pre-K pressure
- NO "easy" / "quick" / "miracle"

**Mails for Level 3 MUST (DOC 4, Section 4):**
- Dignity in every sentence
- "Mini-wins count" framing
- Acknowledge late-trainer reality
- Understand specialty sizes
- Reference adult diapers (carefully)
- Medical team respect
- NO urgency, never
- NO standard mom voice (she is different)

### Cross-Level Rule: DOC 1 Is ALWAYS Foundation

> "Regardless of avatar: DOC 1 (Master Foundation) is ALWAYS consulted. DOC 2-5 are sub-profiles, not substitutes." — DOC 6, Section 7, Principle 4

**DOC lookup rule:**
- Before level tag known → Cross-Level (DOC 5)
- After level tag known → Level-specific (DOC 2/3/4)

In addition to DOC 1 universal forbidden phrases, these are forbidden in cross-level mails:

| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "For Level 1/2/3 kids..." | Only hits one sub-group, misses others |
| "Diagnosed autism only" | Misses diagnostic entrants and label rejecters |
| "Verbal kids will..." | Misses Level 3 |

| "Just like other kids" | Misses all neurodivergent families |
| Strictly behavioral language | Misses neurologically-focused parents |
| "Get him trained!" | Never pressure |
| Heavy resignation language | Misses Level 1 and diagnostic entrants |

| "He'll be normal" | Misses label rejecters, sensory-only, neurodivergent |

## PART 8: STRUCTURAL DO'S & DON'TS — CROSS-LEVEL (DOC 1, Section 23)

**DO'S:**
- Open with the symptom (not the solution)
- Acknowledge prior failures BEFORE proposing mechanism
- Use 3-5 specific scenarios per mail (cross-level recognition)
- Make Lena's voice the trust anchor (not brand voice)
- Offer permission not to buy (reduces resistance)
- Realistic timelines with ranges (not fixed promises)
- Cite Kelly Mahler / SPARK research when relevant
- Mini-wins as success metrics (not "fully trained")
- End with peer-to-peer Lena moment + soft CTA

**DON'TS:**
- Lead with the product
- Use "easy/quick/simple" anywhere
- Promise outcomes tied to timelines
- Compare to neurotypical kids as standard
- Use level labels (Level 2/3) in copy
- Use urgency manipulation ("only 24 hours!")
- Use "we know how you feel" — too cheap
- End mails with hard-sell CTAs ("BUY NOW!")

— DOC 1, Section 23

## PART 9: THREE-FAMILY TESTIMONIAL PATTERN (DOC 5, Section 15)

For cross-level mails, use THREE testimonials (one per level) instead of one:

> "Three families. Three different situations.
> — Sarah, mom of 4yo verbal son: 'He went from "almost" to "mostly" in 6 weeks. Pre-K accepted him in underwear.'
> — Lisa, mom of 6yo daughter (sensory dominant): 'After 3 years of trying everything, she finally paused when she felt wetness. That was Week 4.'
> — Maria, mom of 9yo non-verbal son: 'We're 5 months in. He's not trained. But he's feeling something for the first time. That's enough for now.'
> You'll see in 60 days where you fit." — DOC 5, Section 15

**Why it works:** Everyone finds themselves in a testimonial. Implicitly acknowledges different levels exist. No miracle promises. Realistic outcomes per profile. Creates implicit migration signal (she identifies with one).
