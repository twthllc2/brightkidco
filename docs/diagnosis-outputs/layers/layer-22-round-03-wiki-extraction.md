## Finding 1: Interoception Science — Gut-Brain Axis

**Source:** `your-lack-of-certainty-is-a-gut-issue.md` (lines 88-100, 163-189)

The wiki contains a detailed article on interoception as the brain's ability to read the internal state of the body. Key scientific findings:

- **Interoception defined:** "The brain's ability to read the internal state of the body" (line 100). This is the exact mechanism DOC3 identifies as the missing piece for Level 2 children.
- **Gut-brain signaling:** "Over 500 million neurons line the gut wall and send signals up to the brain via the vagus nerve. Of the signals travelling along the vagus nerve between gut and brain, 80% travel upward." (line 94). The gut talks to the brain far more than the brain talks to the gut.
- **Neurotransmitter production in gut:** Serotonin (90-95% made in gut), GABA (produced by gut bacteria), Dopamine (gut microbiota regulate availability), Oxytocin (gut bacteria stimulate release via vagus nerve) (lines 168-171).
- **Gut dysbiosis cascade:** "Gut dysbiosis leads to imbalances in neurotransmitters, inflammation and heightened activity of the hypothalamus–pituitary–adrenal axis that regulates the stress response." (line 173).
- **Microbiome-personality link:** "Sociable people have a higher abundance of certain types of gut bacteria and also more diverse bacteria." "Differences in gut microbiome composition and diversity are shown to be linked to personality traits in the general population." (lines 185-188).

### Cross-Reference with DOC3-LEVEL2.txt

DOC3 identifies interoception as the core mechanism gap:
- "Hypo-sensitive for interoception (FEELS LITTLE body signals)" (line 200-201)
- "OT says interoception but didn't explain what to do" (line 651)
- Kelly Mahler referenced as authority on interoception (lines 374, 829-833)
- Cluster C (Delayed Verbal Signals): "He has the words but can't connect them to internal sensations" (line 510-512)

**Gap:** The wiki article discusses interoception in adults (gut-brain personality link) but does NOT address pediatric interoception deficits in autistic children. The science is relevant but needs reframing for the Level 2 context — the article focuses on gut health restoration for adults, not sensory signal building for children with ASD.

## Finding 2: Body-Signal Learning Layer™ Mechanism — Interoception Bridge

**Source:** `Unique-Mechanism-BSL.txt` (28 lines)

The client's own mechanism document directly addresses the Level 2 Paradox:

- **Core mechanism:** "Die Unterhose lässt das Kind Nässe spüren — gerade genug, damit das Gehirn die Verbindung 'ich hab gepinkelt → ich fühl es → ich muss aufs Töpfchen' lernt. Pull-Ups blocken genau dieses Feedback komplett." (The underwear lets the child feel moisture — just enough for the brain to learn the connection "I peed → I feel it → I need to go to the potty." Pull-ups block this feedback completely.)
- **3-Layer system:** Layer 1 (cotton, light moisture feel), Layer 2 (smart absorption, 3x normal underwear, preserves learning signal), Layer 3 (leak-resistant outer barrier, NOT leak-proof).
- **ASD-specific framing:** "Bei autistischen Kindern ist das Kernproblem Interozeption — die Fähigkeit, interne Körpersignale wahrzunehmen. Pull-Ups blockieren die einzige externe Feedback-Schleife, die dem Gehirn hilft, diese Verbindung zu lernen." (For autistic children, the core problem is interoception — the ability to perceive internal body signals. Pull-ups block the only external feedback loop that helps the brain learn this connection.)
- **Villain framing:** Pull-ups positioned as active blockers of the neurological feedback loop.

### Cross-Reference with DOC3-LEVEL2.txt

DOC3's Level 2 Paradox maps directly to BSL mechanism:
- "She goes for the teacher, but not for me" (line 222) — the child has learned compliance in structured settings but lacks the internal signal for independent initiation.
- Cluster D (Schedule Dependency): "The child is 'trained to comply', not 'trained to feel'" (line 536-537).
- Lever L2-D ("Beyond Prompt-Dependency"): "Prompt-dependent kids comply when reminded but never independently initiate. That requires building the internal signal, not just the routine." (lines 1077-1080).

**Connection:** The BSL mechanism is the product-level answer to the Level 2 Paradox. The paradox exists because ABA/BCBA protocols train behavioral compliance (sit when prompted) without building interoceptive awareness (feel the signal → initiate). The BSL restores the external feedback loop that pull-ups block.
