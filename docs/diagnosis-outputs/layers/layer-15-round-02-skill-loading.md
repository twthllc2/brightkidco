## 1. Offer Optimization Frameworks

### From CRO Skill

**ICE Scoring for Offer Prioritization:**
- Impact (40%): how much lift if correct
- Confidence (35%): how sure are we
- Ease (25%): how hard to implement
- Score > 6: do it. Score < 4: skip.

**A/B Testing Priority Order for Offers:**
1. Headline / value proposition
2. CTA (text, color, placement, size)
3. Hero image/video
4. Social proof placement
5. Form length (reduce fields by 50%)
6. Offer type (% off vs $ off vs free shipping vs free gift)
7. Pricing page structure (tiers, decoy, annual discount)
8. Checkout flow (guest vs account, 1-step vs multi-step)

**Key CRO Offer Tests:**
- Offer type testing: % off vs $ off vs free shipping vs free gift
- Pricing page: three-tier with decoy effect (Popular badge drives 70%+ selections)
- Annual discount 20% boosts LTV 40-60%
- Exit-intent popup: 5-10% recovery, offer 10-15% off or free shipping
- Popup CVR target: > 3%, change offer if < 1%

### From Strategy Skill

**Decision Framework for Offers:**
- Is the risk likely (> 50%) AND severe (> $50k impact)? Do not proceed
- Likely but low severity? Mitigate then proceed
- Unlikely but severe? Have contingency plan
- Unlikely and low severity? Accept and monitor

**Unit Economics for Offers:**
- CAC < 33% LTV, payback < 12 months
- Growth loops: viral, content, paid, sales

## 2. Pricing Psychology Principles

### Decoy Effect (Three-Tier Pricing)
```
|           | Basic     | Popular★  | Premium   |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Price     | $19/mo    | $39/mo    | $79/mo    |
| Value     | ⭐        | ⭐⭐⭐     | ⭐⭐       |
```
- Popular badge drives 70%+ selections toward middle tier
- Annual discount of 20% boosts LTV 40-60%
- Feature comparison with checkmarks
- 30-day money-back guarantee
- CTA: "Start Free Trial" > "Buy Now"

### Discount Depth by Segment
| Segment | Typical Discount | When to Offer |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| VIPs | 20-40% off or exclusive products | Early access, VIP-only sales, birthday/anniversary |
| Existing customers | 15-25% off | Cross-sell, replenishment, post-purchase upsell |
| New subscribers | 10-20% off | Welcome flow (first purchase incentive) |
| Lapsed customers | 20-30% off | Winback flow, "come back" campaigns |
| Abandoned cart | 10-15% off | Cart recovery (email 2 or 3, not first) |
| BFCM/holiday | 30-50% off sitewide | Seasonal mega-sale events |

### Offer Principles (from strategy-campaign-calendar)
1. **Don't always discount.** Educational content sells without discounting. Position the product as the solution.
2. **Tier your offers.** VIPs get deeper discounts than new subscribers. Reward loyalty.
3. **Free shipping often outperforms % off.** Test free shipping vs. 15% off.
4. **Scarcity works.** Limited time, limited stock, limited quantities.
5. **Exclusivity beats discount.** "Members-only" access can outperform "50% off."

### Exclusivity Strategy
- VIPs get first access — 24-48 hour exclusive window before public launch
- VIP-only products — limited editions, exclusive colorways, member-only collections
- Early access emails — separate send 24 hours before public campaign
- Invite-only events — virtual stylist sessions, founder Q&As for top tier
- Surprise & delight — unannounced gifts, handwritten notes, anniversary bonuses

## 3. Upsell/Cross-Sell Frameworks

### From Copy Skill — Post-Purchase Flow (6 emails)
| Day | Email | Offer Angle |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| D0 | Order confirmation + tracking | None (trust building) |
| D2 | Cross-sell "Complete the set" | Upsell complementary products |
| D7 | Review request + incentive | Social proof + future discount |
| D14 | Referral invite ("Give $10, get $10") | Viral growth offer |
| D30 | Re-order reminder + new arrivals | Replenishment + novelty |

### From campaign-bundle — Bundle Types for AOV Lift
1. **Curated Sets (AESOP model)** — Brand selects specific products. Best for controlled brand experience and higher perceived value. Discount: 15-30% savings.
2. **Mix-and-Match** — Customer chooses X products from a category for a fixed price. Best for fashion/apparel where personal taste varies.
3. **Partner Bundles (KOPARI + Vanity Planet model)** — Two brands collaborate combining products sold separately on two different stores. Discount: up to 48% savings ($116.99 → $59.99).

**Bundle AOV Lift Target:** 15%+ over standalone AOV.

### From campaign-bundle — Value Contrast Pricing
- Show original combined price vs. bundle price (e.g., "Worth $116.99 — yours for $59.99")
- Clear value-messaging line for email body
- Savings callout: Bold, explicit comparison — "If purchased separately: $116.99. Bundle price: $59.99. You save $57."

### From campaign-refer-a-friend — Referral Offer Structures
- **Parade model:** Unique 20% discount code after first purchase; referrer earns up to $45 credit for first 3 friends who spend $40+, then $10 per friend after. Friend gets 20% off first $40+ purchase.
- **Beam model:** Double the previous reward. Friend gets $20 off $75+ purchase. Referrer gets $20 toward next order.
- **Twice model:** Simple $5 off for both referrer and friend. No complex calculations.
- Referred customers have 16% higher LTV and 37% higher retention rate (Nielsen/Wharton/Deloitte data).

### From strategy-customer-journey — Cross-Sell/Upsell Touchpoints by Stage
| Stage | Offer Touchpoints | Goal |
|-------|------------------|------|
| First Purchase → Repeat | Post-purchase flow, cross-sell/upsell | Drive second purchase (target: 15-25% repeat rate within 60 days) |
| Repeat Buyer | Replenishment, cross-sell, VIP program invite, loyalty enrollment | Increase purchase frequency |
| VIP | Early access, exclusive offers, loyalty rewards, surprise & delight | Retain at all costs, increase share of wallet |
| Advocate | Referral program, UGC campaigns, review incentives | Amplify word-of-mouth, reduce CAC |

## 5. Specific Offer Campaign Types

### Flash Sale (from campaign-flash-sale)
- Time-limited discount: 24-48 hours
- For rare discounters: emphasize urgency, short window, larger discount %
- For frequent discounters: focus on product value, tiered discounts to maintain AOV
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday (highest B2C engagement)
- Send at 10 AM in subscriber's timezone for 24-hour sales
- For 48-hour: Day 1 at 10 AM + Day 2 at 6 PM (last 4 hours reminder)

### Free Shipping (from campaign-free-shipping)
- Shipping cost is #1 reason for cart abandonment
- Free shipping often outperforms % off — test both
- Minimum order threshold recommended to protect margin
- Hero element must be most prominent above the fold

### Bundle (from campaign-bundle)
- Year-round strategy, not seasonal
- Target AOV lift: 15%+
- Three types: curated sets, mix-and-match, partner bundles
- Simpler is better (ALO anti-pattern: don't overcomplicate)
- Value contrast pricing: show savings explicitly
- Can re-run every 30-60 days with rotated products

### BFCM/Holiday (from campaign-bfcm-holiday)
- Q4 = 30-50% of annual revenue
- 6 Days of Deals: daily BFCM deals with daily reveals
- Shipping deadline urgency sequence: Heads up → Reminder → Last Chance
- Sale Extended: re-capture non-openers from BFCM sends
- VIP early access: 24-48 hour exclusive window

### Refer-a-Friend (from campaign-refer-a-friend)
- Referred customers: 4x more likely to purchase, 16% higher LTV, 37% higher retention
- Reward structures: give $10/get $10 (simple), tiered credits, or doubling model
- Post-purchase trigger: send referral prompt 24 hours after order confirmation

### Giveaway (from campaign-giveaway)
- Prize must relate to what the brand sells — unrelated prizes attract wrong audience
- Entry form: email + name, optionally SMS opt-in
- Social share buttons for viral reach
- Prize claim deadline: 48 hours recommended

### Back-in-stock (from campaign-back-in-stock)
- Combines popularity (sold out) + urgency (might sell out again)
- Minimum stock threshold: enough for 7 days projected demand
- Subject lines: "It's back!", "Remember this?", "RE-STOCKED"
- Optional discount for first-restock buyers

## 6. CTA Design for Offers (from email-cta-conversion-design)

### CTA Hierarchy of Impact
1. **Placement** — Is it visible without scrolling? (Highest impact, ~50% of conversion difference)
2. **Copy** — Does it communicate a benefit or just an action?
3. **Visual contrast** — Does it stand out from everything else?
4. **Size / tap target** — Minimum 44x44px for mobile
5. **Surrounding context** — Layout guides the eye toward it
6. **Color** — Signals urgency or calm

### Button Specs
- Height: 44px min, 48-56px recommended
- Width mobile: full-width (100%)
- Width desktop: 220-300px recommended
- Padding: 14px vertical, 36px horizontal
- Font: 16-18px, bold
- Border-radius: 4-6px

### CTA Text Examples for Offers
- "Shop the Sale," "Get 30% Off," "Claim Your Discount"
- "Shop the Set," "Get the Bundle," "Buy Now — Save 48%"
- "Grab One," "Get Yours Before It's Gone"
- Avoid: "Submit," "Click Here," generic text

## 7. Revenue Forecasting for Offers

### Core Formula
```
Email Revenue Target = Total Revenue Goal × Email Attribution %
```

### Per-Campaign Revenue Estimate
```
Projected Revenue = Segment Size × Open Rate × Click Rate × Conversion Rate × AOV
```

### Revenue Split
- Automated flows (welcome, cart, post-purchase, winback): 40-60% — highly predictable
- Campaigns (promotional, seasonal, educational): 40-60% — variable by offer/season

### Benchmarks
| Program Maturity | Email % of Total Revenue |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Launch (0-6 months) | 5-15% |
| Growth (6-18 months) | 15-25% |
| Mature (18+ months) | 25-40% |
| Best-in-class | 35-45% |
