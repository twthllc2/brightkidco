## 5. THE 4 EMOTIONAL PHASES (DOC1 — Autistic Parent Avatar)

### Phase 1: Slow Realization (Child 2.5-3.5 years)
- Emotional State: Worry, not crisis. Still has hope for "easy fix." Open to new ideas. Not yet skeptical.
- Tonality: Education-oriented, gentle. "You're not alone" + "there's a reason." No hard sales hooks. Mechanism-first. Hope language OK.
- Decision Triggers: Pediatrician comment, comparison with peers, own research
- Skepticism: Low | Hope: High

### Phase 2: First Real Attempt (Child 3-4 years)
- Emotional State: First real defeat. Self-doubt. Frustration with child. Guilt seesaw.
- Tonality: Validation-first: "You did nothing wrong." Mechanism explanation critical. Soft belief shift: "It was the wrong method, not her." BIGGEST conversion lever.
- Decision Triggers: Painful failure of specific method, pediatrician follow-up, upcoming Pre-K deadline
- Skepticism: Medium | Hope: Medium

### Phase 3: Method Cycling (Child 4-6 years)
- Emotional State: Default mode SKEPTICISM. "I've tried everything" is a trauma response. Ready for solutions with SPECIFIC mechanism. No longer reacts to "general parenting advice."
- Tonality: Hard-sell is poison. Science-first (Kelly Mahler, SPARK study). Mechanism specificity. Realistic stories with messy details. "Try-and-judge" framing (60-day guarantee). Permission-not-to-buy energy.
- Decision Triggers: OT recommendation, BCBA feedback, community exchange, specific science
- Skepticism: Very High | Hope: Medium-Low

### Phase 4: Resignation Fork (Child 6+ years)
- Emotional State: Two versions coexist: A) "I gave up" / B) "Trying one last time." Deeply hurt by failed hopes. Reacts to hope-reawakening through real science.
- Tonality: Very respectful, no energy pushes. "It's not too late" as anchor. Late-trainer stories. Longer timeline promises (8 weeks). Pull-up difficulties as trust bridge. Gentle, never pressure.
- Decision Triggers: Upcoming life events, sibling comparison, pull-up size change, puberty approaches
- Skepticism: Deep, calm | Hope: Hidden

**Phase Summary Table:**
| Phase | Child Age | Skepticism | Hope | Tonality |
|-------|-----------|------------|------|----------|
| 1 | 2.5-3.5 | Low | High | Education, gentle |
| 2 | 3-4 | Medium | Medium | Validation + Mechanism |
| 3 | 4-6 | Very high | Med-Low | Science + Trust |
| 4 | 6+ | Deep, calm | Hidden | Respectful, slow |

## 7. TONALITY CALIBRATION PER FLOW STAGE (DOC1)

### Pre-Purchase (Welcome, Cart, Browse, Checkout)
- Tone: Education + Soft Conversion
- Language: Specific, mechanism-led, peer-to-peer
- Energy: Calm, confident, "we're not in a hurry"
- Do: Validate failures, explain mechanism, soft CTA
- Don't: Hard-sell, urgency manipulation

### Conversion Moment (Cart-Recovery final, Checkout)
- Tone: Friendly Reminder + Permission Not To Buy
- Language: "If today isn't right, that's a real answer"
- Energy: Decision-respect, "you decide"

### Transactional (Order Confirm, Shipping)
- Tone: Warm + Functional
- Language: Direct, informative, branded

### Post-Purchase Early (Arrived, Day 0-7)
- Tone: Warm + Reassurance + Education
- Language: "Here's what to expect"
- Energy: Welcome to the family

### Post-Purchase Mid (Day 14-60)
- Tone: Realistic + Patient + "It's normal"
- Language: "Week 1 plateau is real. Hold the line."
- Energy: Coach + cheerleader

### Lifecycle (Replenishment, Winback, Sunset)
- Tone: Respectful Re-engagement
- Language: "How are you doing?" / "Whichever path you take"
- Energy: Genuinely curious, low-pressure

## 8. THE 5 BIG CONVERSION LEVERS (Universal Wording Rules)

### Lever 1: "It's not the method. It's a sensory gap."
- Psychological Effect: D1+D2 absolution simultaneously. Belief shift from "behavior issue" to "neurological issue."
- Use In: Welcome E2-E4, FAQ, Cart Recovery, Browse Abandonment, Winback
- Avatar Feels: "Finally, someone gets it. It was never my fault."

### Lever 2: "Pull-ups make this worse"
- Psychological Effect: Reframe — pull-ups actively damage. Creates urgency WITHOUT manipulation. Shifts blame from mother to product.
- Use In: Welcome E2, E4-E5, Browse Abandonment, Cart Recovery, Winback
- CAUTION for Level 3: Pull-ups are often Medicaid-funded. "Add alongside" not "abandon."

### Lever 3: "Try it for 60 days. By your judgment."
- Psychological Effect: Reduces hope-risk (D3 objection). "You decide" framing. Brand trust through risk transfer.
- Use In: ALL flows, especially conversion-oriented

### Lever 4: "I have two autistic sons" (Lena's Voice)
- Psychological Effect: Brand becomes person. Trust increases 200%+ when marketing voice becomes peer-to-peer.
- Use In: Lena sign-off in ALL mails, reply encouragement, post-purchase empathy

### Lever 5: "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds are not toilet trained"
- Psychological Effect: Normalizes her situation. Removes self-blame. Data anchor. SPARK research credibility.
- Use In: Welcome E2, FAQ E1, Winback E1, whenever self-blame pattern recognized

**Source:** DOC1 lines 750-840

## 9. FORBIDDEN PHRASES (Trust-Killing Wording)

NEVER USE:
- "Easy" / "Simple" / "Effortless"
- "Train your kid in 3 days"
- "Finally solved everything"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Just like neurotypical kids"
- "Suffering from autism" (autism = identity, not disease)
- "High-functioning" / "Low-functioning"
- "Wait until they're ready" (trigger — she's heard this 100x)
- "Just try harder"
- "Be more consistent"
- "Your child should..."
- "Most children at this age..."
- "Have you tried [obvious method]" — she's tried everything
- "Don't worry" — minimizes real concerns
- "We understand" without substance
- "Cure" / "Solve" / "Fix" — autism isn't to be fixed
- "Your child will be normal"
- "Get him trained!" — pressure language

## 10. LENA VOICE FORMULA (Wording Structure)

Every mail follows this 4-part formula:
1. Observation of reality (specific scenario)
2. Validation without drama
3. Mechanism or information
4. Permission / soft action

**Example:** "Your kid sits on the toilet, you wait, nothing happens. Then you put the pull-up back on, and 30 seconds later there's pee in it. I know that one. Both my sons did it for years. It's not defiance — it's a signal timing issue. Here's what helped us..."

**Sign-Off Rules:**
- Every mail ends with Lena sign-off
- Max 2-3 sentences body + max 2 lines P.S.
- Profile subtitle: "Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons"
- NEVER founder framing in regular mails

**Source:** DOC1 lines 874-943

## 11. CROSS-LEVEL CALIBRATION RULES (Wording Across Levels)

**Rule 1:** Symptom description over level label. NEVER "Level 2." YES "If your child doesn't feel when they need to go..."

**Rule 2:** 3-5 recognition anchors per mail:
- Level 1: "Sits 20 minutes on toilet and nothing happens" / "Sometimes succeeds, sometimes doesn't"
- Level 2: "Pees immediately when pull-up comes off" / "Plays while peeing"
- Level 3: "Doesn't react to wet diaper" / "Made no progress in 4 years"

**Rule 3:** Age ranges instead of fixed ages. YES: "For kids 3 to 10+..."

**Rule 4:** Verbal/non-verbal inclusivity. Always both: "saying 'wet'" + "showing you with a gesture"

**Rule 5:** Timeline flexibility. NEVER "In 30 days." YES: "Some kids show first signals after 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal."

**Rule 6:** Success metrics low bar. Define success as mini-wins: "Look at the wet spot for the first time," "Pull at their pants in awareness," "Walk toward the bathroom (even after the accident)," "Stay dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes."

**Source:** DOC1 lines 1476-1519

## 12. STRUCTURAL DO'S & DON'TS (Wording Patterns)

**DO'S:**
- Open with the symptom (not the solution)
- Acknowledge prior failures BEFORE proposing mechanism
- Use 3-5 specific scenarios per mail (cross-level recognition)
- Make Lena's voice the trust anchor (not brand voice)
- Offer permission not to buy (reduces resistance)
- Realistic timelines with ranges (not fixed promises)
- Cite Kelly Mahler / SPARK research when relevant
- Mini-wins as success metrics (not "fully trained")
- End with peer-to-peer Lena moment + soft CTA

**DON'TS:**
- Lead with the product
- Use "easy/quick/simple" anywhere
- Promise outcomes tied to timelines
- Compare to neurotypical kids as standard
- Suggest the parent isn't doing enough
- Use level labels (Level 2/3) in copy
- Stack social proof early (save for later)
- Use urgency manipulation ("only 24 hours!")
- Use exclamation marks in body text (signals marketing)
- Use "we know how you feel" — too cheap
- End mails with hard-sell CTAs ("BUY NOW!")

**Source:** DOC1 lines 1523-1553

## 15. CONTRADICTIONS BETWEEN FILES

**Finding 1: Frequencies-Wording.txt vs. Frequencies-Wording-typo.txt**
- Both files are IDENTICAL (5,291 bytes each, same content). The "typo" version contains no corrections or differences. No contradictions found.

**Finding 2: 4 Avatars (Frequencies) vs. 4 Levels (DOC1)**
- The Frequencies file defines 4 avatars by EMOTIONAL TYPE (Deadline Pressure, Social Pressure, Overwhelmed, Autistic).
- DOC1 defines 4 levels by CLINICAL SEVERITY (Level 1 verbal, Level 2 mixed, Level 3 non-verbal, General Fallback).
- These are DIFFERENT segmentation axes. The Frequencies avatars are ad-creative segments (how to hook). The DOC1 levels are email-flow segments (which tonality). They map to different parts of the customer journey.
- Potential conflict: The Frequencies "Autistic Parent" avatar overlaps with DOC1's entire autistic parent audience (all levels). The Frequencies file treats "Autistic Parent" as one emotional type, while DOC1 splits it into 3 clinical levels with very different tonality needs.

**Finding 3: Frequency Stages vs. 4 Emotional Phases**
- Frequencies-Wording.txt defines 6 stages for AD CREATIVE (Mirror → Problem Amp → Hope → Mechanism → Calm → Action).
- DOC1 defines 4 phases for EMAIL JOURNEY (Slow Realization → First Attempt → Method Cycling → Resignation).
- These are complementary, not contradictory. The 6-stage curve is per-ad micro-structure. The 4-phase model is macro-journey structure. An email in Phase 3 (Method Cycling) would still use the 6-stage curve internally.

**Finding 4: Hope Percentages**
- DOC6 specifies exact hope percentages: L1=70%, L2=40%, L3=20%, General=50/50.
- Frequencies-Wording.txt does not mention percentages but describes tonal qualities (urgency, shame, exhaustion, anxiety).
- DOC1 describes tonality per flow stage but not per level.
- No direct contradiction, but the Frequencies file's "high stress / high urgency" for Deadline Parent doesn't map cleanly to any single DOC1 level — it could be Level 1 (Pre-K pressure) or Level 2 (tried many methods).
