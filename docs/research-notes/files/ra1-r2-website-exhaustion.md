- **Memorial Day Sale active** with free gifts on orders

### Materials, Sizing, Colors
- 100% cotton construction
- Leakproof reusable design
- Sizes available for toddlers (specific sizes not confirmed from scrape)
- Multiple color/design options — currently 3 new designs launched
- Soft, comfortable feel — designed to feel like real underwear

### Brand Voice & Visual Style
- **Voice:** Empathetic, parent-to-parent, emotionally resonant. Uses phrases like "messy middles," "not-quite-there-yet moments." Warm, supportive, non-judgmental.
- **Visual Style:** Clean, modern Shopify store. Bright colors, lots of product imagery. Heavy use of social proof (review counts, parent testimonials).

- Mission: "make potty training less stressful, more supportive, and truly child-led"

### Key Strategic Insights

1. **BKC's unique position is confirmed as nearly unoccupied.** No competitor combines reusable training underwear + wetness learning signal + autism/sensory focus. UpAiry comes closest on product but lacks autism positioning.

2. **UpAiry is the most dangerous direct competitor.** Nearly identical messaging ("bridge between diapers and underwear"), 100,000+ parent claims, expanding product line. BKC must differentiate hard on the 3-Layer Body-Signal Learning System and autism expertise.

3. **The autism market has no underwear competitor.** PottyFriend sells tools, SmartKnitKids sells sensory clothing, but no one else sells autism-specific training underwear. This is BKC's moat.
