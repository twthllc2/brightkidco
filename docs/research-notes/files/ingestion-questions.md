## SECTION G: IMPLICATIONS FOR EMAIL STRATEGY

### What the research CONFIRMS:
1. The interoception mechanism is scientifically sound (Nicholson et al. 2019)
2. The "wet and keep playing" problem is the #1 frustration — and BKC's sustained-sensation design directly addresses it
3. Shame/identity crisis is the emotional core, not logistics
4. The Oh Crap method gap for neurodivergent kids is real and underserved
5. Competitors (UpAiry, Snugkins) use similar language — BKC needs sharper differentiation

### What the research WARNS:
1. Don't overclaim — the 92%/87%/100K+ numbers are unverified marketing claims, not scientific facts
2. Don't use outdated expert names (Mitchell, Anderson) — they're not on the current site
3. Don't assume parents know "interoception" — introduce it as a new concept
4. Don't lead with cost savings — parents don't care about $25/month, they care about sanity
5. Don't use "#1 brand" without a source — use "rated 4.9/5 by 2,600+ families on Trustpilot"

### What the research REVEALS that wasn't obvious:
1. The daycare conflict is a MASSIVE frustration vector (12 mentions in Kommentare) — potential email angle
2. The poop problem is the most emotionally charged sub-problem — parents describe ER visits, MiraLAX protocols, 12-day holding
3. The "husband problem" is real — moms feel alone in the process, husbands take credit or blame
4. Night training confusion is widespread — parents don't understand it's hormonal, not behavioral
