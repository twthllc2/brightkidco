## CONFIDENCE RATING KEY

- **HIGH** — Multiple peer-reviewed studies or meta-analysis support this claim
- **MEDIUM** — Single peer-reviewed study or strong expert consensus
- **LOW** — Expert opinion, internal data, or derived inference

## CATEGORY A: INTEROCEPTION (Body Signal Awareness)

### PP-A1: The Core Deficit — Children Can't Feel the Signal
**Copy:** "Research shows children with autism have significantly reduced ability to sense internal body signals — including when they need to use the bathroom. They're not being stubborn. Their brain isn't receiving the message."
**Source:** Nicholson et al. (2019), Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 49(9):3625-3637. DOI: 10.1007/s10803-019-04079-w
**Confidence:** HIGH
**BKC Claims Supported:** "Pull-ups block the one external feedback loop," "Body-Signal Learning Layer preserves the uh-oh sensation"

### PP-A2: 59% Can't Signal When They Need to Go
**Copy:** "A study of 63 autistic children found that 59% couldn't reliably tell when they needed to urinate — and 71% couldn't signal when they needed a bowel movement. For most autistic toddlers, the 'I need to go' feeling simply doesn't arrive."
**Source:** Iqbal (2022), Pakistan Journal of Rehabilitation, DOI: 10.36283/pjr.zu.11.2/021
**Confidence:** MEDIUM (single study, small sample)
**BKC Claims Supported:** "Interoception support for autistic children," "Body-Signal Learning Layer"

### PP-A3: Only 19% of Autistic Children Recognize Thirst

**Source:** Iqbal (2022), Pakistan Journal of Rehabilitation, DOI: 10.36283/pjr.zu.11.2/021
**Confidence:** MEDIUM (single study)

## CATEGORY B: TOILET TRAINING (Statistics, Timelines, Barriers)

### PP-B1: Nearly Half of Autistic Preschoolers Aren't Trained
**Copy:** "Nearly half of autistic 4-5 year olds — 49% — are still not toilet trained. That's compared to just 8% of typically developing children the same age. This isn't a parenting failure. It's a documented, measurable difference."
**Source:** Wiggins et al. (2022), SEED-2 Study, Journal of Developmental and Behavioral Pediatrics (CDC-funded, N=743 ASD children)
**Confidence:** HIGH (CDC-funded, peer-reviewed, large sample)
**BKC Claims Supported:** "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds not toilet trained," "Toileting challenges in ASD"

---

### PP-B2: Autistic Children Are 6x More Likely to Still Be in Diapers
**Copy:** "Autistic children are 6 times more likely to still be in diapers at age 4-5 than their typically developing peers. The gap is real, it's measurable, and it's not because parents aren't trying."
**Source:** Derived from Wiggins et al. 2022 (49% vs 8%)
**Confidence:** HIGH (derived from peer-reviewed data)
**BKC Claims Supported:** "ASD toileting gap," "Why traditional methods fail"

---

### PP-B3: 65% Not Trained by Their Third Birthday

**Source:** Faulkner et al. 2017 (N=500 children on autism spectrum)
**Confidence:** MEDIUM (single study, reasonable sample)
**BKC Claims Supported:** "Toileting timeline," "Why starting early matters"

---

### PP-B4: The Long Haul — Accidents at Age 12
**Copy:** "5% of autistic children are still having toileting accidents at age 12. Without the right tools and approach, this isn't a phase they simply outgrow — it's a challenge that can last years."
**Source:** Faulkner et al. 2017
**Confidence:** MEDIUM
**BKC Claims Supported:** "Long-term toileting challenges," "Why intervention matters"

### PP-B5: Starting After 24 Months Predicts Slower Progress
