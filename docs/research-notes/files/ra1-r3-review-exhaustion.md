## Key Insights for BKC Email Copy

### 1. The "Feel Wetness" Mechanism is THE Differentiator
Multiple competitors (UpAiry, Gerber, training pants reviews) explicitly market the "feel wetness" concept. Parents understand and value this. BKC should lean heavily into the 3-Layer Body-Signal Learning System™ as the premium, engineered version of what parents already want.

### 2. Parents Are Desperate for Nighttime Solutions
Peejamas' entire brand is built on nighttime training anxiety. Parents use words like "desperate," "at our wit's end," and "secret hack." BKC's nighttime positioning can tap into this emotional pain point.

### 3. The "Big Kid" Motivation is Universal
Every competitor uses "big kid" language. It's the #1 motivator for toddlers. BKC emails should position the underwear as a "big kid" reward, not a training tool.

### 4. Softness is Non-Negotiable
"Soft," "gentle," "comfortable" appear in virtually every positive review. Parents of sensory-sensitive children especially need this reassurance. BKC should emphasize fabric quality in every email.

### 5. Absorbency Anxiety is the #1 Complaint
The most common complaint across ALL competitors is "not absorbent enough." Parents fear leaks. BKC should address this head-on: "Our 3-layer system absorbs small accidents while letting your child feel the learning signal — without the puddles."

### 6. The Pull-Ups Critique is BKC's Opportunity
Parents who understand potty training say Pull-Ups "keep kids too dry to learn" and are "just diapers in disguise." BKC's email sequence should validate this frustration and position BKC as the solution that actually teaches.

### 7. Eco-Friendly + Cost Savings Resonate
"Save money on disposables," "eco-friendly," "reusable" appear frequently. BKC should calculate the cost savings vs. Pull-Ups ($50–$100/month) in an email.

### 8. Confidence Language Converts
"Confident," "proud," "independent" appear in the most enthusiastic reviews. BKC emails should paint the picture of the confident toddler, not the struggling one.

### 9. The Autism/Sensory Angle is Underserved
SmartKnitKids reviews show parents of SPD/autism children are desperate for products that understand their needs. "No more tears, fights, or struggles" is powerful language. BKC can own this niche with targeted emails.

### 10. Parents Trust "Scientifically Proven" and "Doctor Recommended"
Dryly and Chummie both use authority language. BKC's "proprietary" system and "100,000+ satisfied parents" can serve the same function.
