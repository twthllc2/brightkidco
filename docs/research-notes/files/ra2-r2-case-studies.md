## Case Study 1: IMSE — Institute for Multi-Sensory Education
**Most Relevant to BrightKidCo** — Directly serves special needs / multi-sensory learning community

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Institute for Multi-Sensory Education (IMSE) — imse.com |
| **Industry** | Education / Multi-Sensory Learning (serves special needs educators) |
| **Location** | Michigan, USA |
| **Platform** | Mailchimp |
| **List Size** | 500,000+ educators |

### Challenge
- Massive email list (500K+) but extremely low engagement
- Open rates stagnating between 20-25%
- No segmentation — same email sent to all contacts regardless of role (VHS teacher in Ohio vs. school administrator in Texas)
- Text-heavy, image-poor emails
- Bot accounts imported into the list without cleanup
- No strategy, campaigns sent on autopilot

### Strategy Used
1. **List cleanup** — Ran reengagement campaign targeting inactive contacts; removed non-responders and bot accounts
2. **Segmentation by role and location** — Organized contacts by course completion, job title, and location; separated general education teachers from special education teachers and administrators
3. **Automated product flows** — Created dedicated marketing automation flows for new curriculum products
4. **Weekly "Freebie Friday" feature** — Free teaching resource became a reliable engagement touchpoint integrated into automations
5. **Canva integration** — Improved email design with visual content

### Results (with numbers)
- **Click rates tripled** (3x improvement) after segmentation
- **Open rates nearly doubled** — from 20-25% to approaching 40%+
- Bot cleanup and reengagement dramatically improved deliverability
- Consistent weekly engagement through "Freebie Friday"

### What Worked
- Cleaning the list first before segmenting (removing bots and inactive contacts)
- Segmenting by job title and role (special ed vs. general ed)
- Creating recurring valuable content (Freebie Friday) as an engagement anchor
- Using automations tied to product launches

### What Didn't Work (Before)
- Sending identical emails to 500K+ contacts
- Text-heavy, imageless emails
- No segmentation or personalization
- Keeping bot accounts and inactive contacts

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Directly applicable**: IMSE serves the SAME audience (educators working with children who have learning differences, including special needs). Their segmentation approach — separating special ed teachers from general ed — maps directly to BKC's need to segment autism parents from sensory-seeking parents from general parenting audiences.
- **List hygiene is step one** — Don't carry dead weight; reengage or remove.
- **Recurring valuable content** builds engagement habit (BKC could do "Sensory Tip Tuesday" or similar).
- **Role-based segmentation** is critical when your audience includes parents, therapists, educators, and caregivers.

### Source
- https://mailchimp.com/case-studies/imse-segmentation-strategy/

## Case Study 2: Gymwrap — Fitness DTC Brand
**Relevant for**: Email + SMS consolidation, small team strategy, seasonal revenue patterns

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Gymwrap (thegymwrap.com) |
| **Industry** | Fitness / E-Commerce |
| **Founder** | Nicole Ari Parker (actress) |
| **Location** | California, USA |
| **Platform** | Mailchimp (email + SMS consolidated) |
| **List Size** | 139,000+ subscribers |

### Challenge
- Email on one platform (Mailchimp), SMS on another (third-party)
- No unified view of customer behavior across channels
- Couldn't tell if SMS email non-responders were actually SMS-responsive
- Biggest revenue season (Q1: Jan-Mar, New Year fitness resolutions) was approaching
- CMO was skeptical SMS would add revenue vs. just cannibalize email

### Strategy Used
1. **Consolidated email + SMS in one platform** (Mailchimp)
2. **Cross-channel automation flows** — Used channel affinity to send messages where customers were most likely to engage
3. **Unified customer view** — Single dashboard for email + SMS behavior
4. **Dedicated Customer Success Manager** for strategic guidance
5. **Strategic SMS frequency management** — Not just blasting, but using SMS for specific high-impact moments

### Results (with numbers)
- **142x ROI on first SMS launch**
- **5x higher earnings per message (EPM)** vs. previous SMS provider
- **30% of total revenue attributed to Mailchimp** (email + SMS combined)
- Revenue now flows automatically — described as "money earned in sleep"
- SMS became additive revenue, NOT cannibalization of email

### What Worked
- Consolidating email + SMS in a single platform for unified data
- Using a CSM for strategic guidance (not just self-serve)
- Cross-channel automation that respects channel preference
- Timing SMS for high-impact moments rather than duplicating email content

### What Didn't Work (Before)
- Separate platforms for email and SMS = data silos
- Manual, time-consuming campaign coordination across platforms
- Unable to identify cross-channel customer preferences

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Email + SMS consolidation is critical** — Running them separately creates blind spots and wastes effort.
- **SMS is additive, not cannibalistic** — When done right, SMS drives NEW revenue rather than splitting existing revenue.
- **Small teams can execute** — Gymwrap's small team achieved massive results with the right platform consolidation.
- **Seasonal planning matters** — BKC should plan campaigns around key parenting moments (back-to-school, holiday sensory gift guides, autism awareness month in April).
- **139K subscribers generating 142x ROI on SMS** shows even modest-sized lists can drive outsized returns.

### Source
- https://mailchimp.com/case-studies/gymwrap-sms-email-marketing/

## Case Study 3: EZ Bombs — Family Recipe Turned Viral Brand
**Relevant for**: Small family business, social-to-email funnel, TikTok integration

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | EZ Bombs (ezbombs.com) |
| **Industry** | Food / E-Commerce |
| **Founder** | Tina Castaneda (mother and grandmother) |
| **Platform** | Klaviyo (migrated from Postscript) |
| **Growth** | Launched 2024, TikTok bestseller same year |

### Challenge
- Prior email provider (Postscript) had poor social platform integrations
- Manual process to sync TikTok contacts for retargeting/exclusions
- Hard to combine Shopify + TikTok Shop data
- List growth was "uphill battle" — TikTok users reluctant to leave app
- Email and SMS on separate platforms = reporting nightmare
- Couldn't accurately track cross-channel campaign performance

### Strategy Used
1. **Switched to Klaviyo** for unified email + SMS with TikTok integrations
2. **TikTok Lead Ads integration** — App-native sign-up forms so TikTok users never leave the app
3. **TikTok Audiences integration** — Seamless export of email segments to TikTok for exclusions/retargeting
4. **Channel-preference segmentation** — Segment by preferred retail channel (Shopify vs. TikTok Shop vs. retail)
5. **Full-funnel TikTok strategy** — Awareness ads → lead capture → email nurture → conversion

### Results (with numbers)
- **27% YoY growth in total ecommerce revenue** in 2025
- **22% YoY decrease in TikTok cost per lead** with Klaviyo integrations
- **2 pre-built Klaviyo-TikTok integrations** driving cohesive strategy
- True brand-new awareness achieved by excluding all prior purchasers across channels

### What Worked
- Native TikTok lead capture (users never leave the app = higher conversion)
- Cross-channel exclusion lists (don't waste ad spend on existing customers)
- Segmenting by retail channel preference for personalized messaging
- Consolidating email + SMS + social data in one platform

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Social-to-email pipeline is gold** — BKC likely gets discovery from Instagram/TikTok parenting communities. Integrating those platforms with email capture is critical.
- **"List growth was an uphill battle"** until they made sign-up frictionless — BKC should minimize friction in email capture (pop-ups, QR codes, in-app forms).
- **Cross-channel exclusions save money** — Don't retarget people who already bought.
- **Family-founded businesses can scale fast** with the right email infrastructure — EZ Bombs went from kitchen to TikTok bestseller in months.
- **Segment by how customers found you** — TikTok-discovered customers may have different needs than organic search or referral customers.

## Case Study 4: Half Magic — Beauty DTC Retention
**Relevant for**: Retention strategy, loyalty programs, repeat purchase growth, Customer Hub

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Half Magic (halfmagicbeauty.com) |
| **Industry** | Beauty / E-Commerce |
| **Founder** | Donni Davy (Emmy-winning Euphoria makeup artist) |
| **Platform** | Klaviyo (consolidated email + SMS + Customer Hub) |
| **Agency** | Lilo Social |

### Challenge
- Strong customer acquisition but weak retention
- Driving repeat purchases with broad-brush promotions instead of personalized messaging
- Email on Klaviyo, SMS on separate platform — fragmented cross-channel experience
- Customer accounts on Shopify's pages with limited dynamic content
- Didn't want to add a separate loyalty app (cost + fragmentation)

### Strategy Used
1. **Consolidated SMS into Klaviyo** for cross-channel automation
2. **Replaced Shopify account pages with Klaviyo Customer Hub** — personalized sidebar with subscriptions, favorites, recently-viewed
3. **Added Klaviyo Marketing Analytics** for RFM-based timing of messaging
4. **Native loyalty program** built within Klaviyo (no separate app) — tiers based on lifetime purchase count
5. **Cross-channel automations** — 8 cross-channel flows launched
6. **Dynamic content blocks** in campaigns showing loyalty status and rewards

### Results (with numbers)
- **5x YoY growth in repeat purchasers** in last 12 months
- **110% YoY growth in revenue from Klaviyo automations** in last 12 months
- **2x higher AOV from orders attributed to Customer Hub** in 90 days
- 8 cross-channel automations running
- Loyalty campaigns converting at higher rate than non-loyalty campaigns

### What Worked
- Consolidating tech stack in one platform (no separate loyalty app needed)
- RFM segmentation driving personalized nurture automations
- Customer Hub creating a personalized on-site experience
- Loyalty tiers as dynamic content in email campaigns
- Cross-channel flows that respect channel preference

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Loyalty programs don't require separate apps** — BKC can build tiers within Klaviyo based on purchase count (e.g., "Sensory Explorer" → "Sensory Pro" → "Sensory Champion").
- **RFM segmentation is powerful** — Send different messages to first-time buyers vs. repeat buyers vs. at-risk churners.
- **Customer Hub / on-site personalization** drives 2x higher AOV — BKC should consider showing personalized product recommendations based on past purchases.
- **Repeat purchaser growth (5x) came from personalization, not discounts** — BKC shouldn't default to discounting; personalized messaging based on purchase history works better.
- **110% flow revenue growth** shows automations outperform one-off campaigns when properly set up.

## Case Study 5: Manly Bands — DTC Jewelry Automation
**Relevant for**: Creative abandoned cart strategy, post-purchase automation, self-serve platform

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Manly Bands (manlybands.com) |
| **Industry** | Jewelry / E-Commerce |
| **Platform** | Klaviyo (migrated from Listrak) |
| **List Size** | ~500,000 contacts |
| **Founded** | Garage startup by husband-wife team |

### Challenge
- Previous platform (Listrak) required support calls for basic tasks
- Editing flows required navigating "spiderweb" node-based layout
- Not self-serve — even list/segment edits needed support
- Fast-growing DTC brand needed agility, not enterprise overhead

### Strategy Used
1. **Migrated to Klaviyo** (6 weeks including IP warming)
2. **Abandoned cart SURVEY flow** — Instead of purchase CTA, sends a 1-question survey: "Why didn't you buy?" Respondents get personalized follow-up with coupon
3. **Production-status post-purchase flow** — Manufacturer scans ring at each production stage → updates Google Sheet → Klaviyo API checks hourly → triggers status email to customer
4. **Full automation suite** — Welcome, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back

### Results (with numbers)
- **36% of overall revenue from email** in last 90 days
- **58% of Klaviyo-attributed revenue from flows** (automations) in H2 2022
- **4.8x median placed order rate** on abandoned cart survey flow vs. peer group
- Abandoned cart survey flow: highest-converting automation
- Migration completed in 6 weeks

### What Worked
- **Abandoned cart survey** instead of hard sell — understanding WHY customers don't buy is more valuable than generic discount offers
- Production-status emails during 3-5 week wait — keeps customers engaged and reduces "where's my order?" inquiries
- Self-serve platform empowers small teams
- API-driven automation (Google Sheet → Klaviyo) for real-time production updates

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Ask "why didn't you buy?" instead of just offering discounts** — BKC could survey cart abandoners: "Was it the price? The product? Not sure it's right for your child?" Then personalize follow-up.
- **Post-purchase engagement reduces support burden** — If BKC ships physical products, production/shipping status emails reduce WISMO inquiries.
- **36% of revenue from email** is achievable for a DTC brand — this is a realistic target for BKC.
- **58% of email revenue from automations** means flows should be the priority, not campaigns.
- **Small team (husband-wife founding) scaling with automation** — BKC is likely similarly sized; automation is how small teams compete.

- https://www.klaviyo.com/customers/case-studies/manly-bands-email-revenue

## Case Study 6: Tatcha — Luxury Beauty Omnichannel
**Relevant for**: Promotional strategy, SMS list growth, omnichannel coordination

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Tatcha (tatcha.com) |
| **Industry** | Luxury Beauty / Skincare (Unilever-owned) |
| **Platform** | Klaviyo (email + SMS + forms) |
| **Key Event** | Fukubukuro (New Year "Lucky Bag" promotion) |

### Challenge
- Wanted to grow annual Fukubukuro promotion revenue YoY
- Needed to expand SMS usage to drive growth
- Had to coordinate email, SMS, social, and paid media for one promotion
- Standing out during a time when other brands take a break (January)

### Strategy Used
1. **Omnichannel Fukubukuro campaign** — Email, SMS, social, paid media all coordinated
2. **Expanded campaign recipients based on purchase history** — Engaged segment + less-engaged subscribers who previously bought featured product category
3. **New site abandonment flow** launched during promotion
4. **Refreshed welcome and abandonment flow creative** to highlight promotion
5. **Smart Send Time** for optimal delivery timing
6. **Facebook integration** for lookalike audiences and purchaser exclusions
7. **Social unboxing content highlighted via email** — creating flywheel of enthusiasm

### Results (with numbers)
- **20% YoY growth in ecommerce revenue**
- **47% of ecommerce revenue from Klaviyo** during promotion
- **70% YoY growth in flow revenue** during promotion
- SMS drove incremental growth beyond email
- Site abandonment flow contributed meaningfully during event

### What Worked
- Using a unique cultural hook (Fukubukuro) to stand out from competitors
- Expanding beyond engaged segment to include past purchasers of featured categories
- Refreshing existing flows with promotional creative (not just campaigns)
- Cross-channel coordination (email + SMS + social + paid)
- Smart Send Time for optimal delivery

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Create your own "Fukubukuro"** — BKC could create a signature annual event (e.g., "Sensory Box Black Friday" or "Autism Awareness Month Special" in April).
- **Refresh automations with promotional creative** — Don't just send campaign blasts; update your welcome flow and abandonment flows to feature current promotions.
- **Expand recipients for special events** — Include less-engaged subscribers when the message is highly relevant (e.g., holiday gift guide for sensory products).
- **47% of revenue from Klaviyo** during a major promotion shows email/SMS can be the primary revenue driver, not just a supporting channel.
- **70% flow revenue growth** by updating automations during promotions — flows work harder when they're timely and relevant.

## Case Study 7: Kaylin + Kaylin Pickles — Platform Migration
**Relevant for**: Platform switching costs, cost management, onboarding support

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Kaylin + Kaylin Pickles (kaylinandkaylin.com) |

### Challenge
- E-mail list growing but Klaviyo costs becoming unsustainable ("ludicrously expensive")
- Approaching another cost threshold on Klaviyo
- Not using full Klaviyo feature set
- Lacking strategic support for effective platform utilization
- Risk of switching: learning curve, momentum loss during transition

### Strategy Used
1. **Switched from Klaviyo to Mailchimp** for better cost/value ratio
2. **Onboarding support from Mailchimp's Customer Success team** — hands-on, step-by-step guidance
3. **Custom audience growth solution** with popup forms and quizzes
4. **Tagging and segmentation** for targeted messaging
5. **AI-powered tools** for content optimization

### Results (with numbers)
- Significant cost savings (exact % not disclosed, but described as "saved a lot of money")
- Improved engagement metrics after migration
- Better strategic support from Mailchimp CSM
- Quiz-based popup forms improved list quality and segmentation
- Viral product demand driving consistent email-triggered sales

### What Worked
- Dedicated onboarding support minimized disruption during migration
- Quiz-based popup forms for better list quality
- Cost savings reinvested into better content and segmentation

### What Didn't Work (Before)
- Paying for Klaviyo features they weren't using
- No strategic guidance on platform optimization
- Cost scaling faster than revenue

- **Monitor platform costs vs. revenue** — As BKC grows, ensure email platform costs scale proportionally. Don't pay for features you don't use.

- **Strategic support matters** — Having a CSM or account manager who understands your niche can make a big difference vs. pure self-serve.

## Case Study 8: Totally Chocolate — B2B/D2C Segmentation
**Relevant for**: Audience separation, relationship-based email, post-event follow-up

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Company** | Totally Chocolate Promo (totallychocolate.com) |
| **Industry** | Promotional Products / Food Gifts |
| **Platform** | Mailchimp |
| **Market** | $24 billion promotional products industry |

### Challenge
- Two completely separate business units: D2C (direct to consumer) and B2B (through distributor partners)
- A SINGLE email mixing audiences could destroy distributor relationships
- No marketing infrastructure — everything managed through Outlook
- No segmentation, no automation, no consistent communication
- HubSpot CRM integration costs too high
- Distributors overwhelmed with supplier emails — most go to spam
- Food gifts only 1.3% of promotional products industry revenue

### Strategy Used
1. **Strict audience separation** — Completely separate lists for D2C and B2B
2. **Monthly newsletters** combining education with personality for distributors
3. **Post-trade-show follow-up automation** — Reduced follow-up time from days to hours
4. **Mailchimp + HubSpot integration** for CRM data sync
5. **Content that helps distributors sell** — Not just product pitches, but resources distributors can use with their own clients

### Results (with numbers)
- **40% open rate** (2x the industry average for promotional products)
- **95% reduction in follow-up time** after trade shows (from days to hours)
- Built marketing infrastructure from zero (previously managed through Outlook)
- Successfully maintaining strict audience separation with zero cross-contamination

### What Worked
- Treating distributors as partners, not just recipients
- Educational content that helps distributors grow their own business
- Automated post-event follow-up (massive time savings)
- Strict audience separation protecting business relationships
- Personality-driven content standing out in crowded inboxes

### What Didn't Work (Before)
- Manual Outlook-based email management
- No segmentation = generic messages to everyone
- Slow post-event follow-up losing momentum

### Key Learnings for BrightKidCo
- **Audience separation is non-negotiable** if BKC ever sells wholesale/retail alongside DTC — mixing audiences destroys trust.
- **40% open rate is achievable** with relevant, personality-driven content in niche markets.
- **Post-event automation** — If BKC attends autism conferences, sensory product expos, or parenting events, automated follow-up sequences can recover days of lost momentum.
- **Content that helps your audience do THEIR job** builds loyalty — BKC could create content that helps parents advocate for their children, find resources, etc.
- **Trade show / event follow-up** is a massive untapped opportunity for niche brands.

### Source
- https://mailchimp.com/case-studies/totally-chocolate-mailchimp-automations/

## Actionable Insights for BrightKidCo

### Priority 1: Foundation (Do First)
1. **List hygiene before everything** — Clean inactive subscribers, remove bots, run reengagement campaign (IMSE lesson: 500K contacts with 20% open rate is worse than 200K contacts with 45% open rate)
2. **Set up core automations** — Abandoned cart ($3.65 RPR average), welcome flow ($2.65 RPR), browse abandonment ($1.07 RPR), post-purchase ($0.41 RPR but critical for retention)
3. **Consolidate email + SMS in one platform** — Don't run them separately (Gymwrap: 142x ROI after consolidation; separate platforms = blind spots)
4. **Build quiz-based popup for segmentation** — "What's your child's sensory profile?" → auto-segment into relevant email tracks (Kaylin + Kaylin lesson)

### Priority 2: Segmentation Strategy
5. **Segment by parent/caregiver type** (IMSE lesson):
   - Autism parents
   - Sensory-seeking / SPD parents
   - Special needs educators / therapists
   - General parenting audience
   - Gift buyers (grandparents, family friends)
6. **Segment by purchase behavior** (Half Magic lesson):
   - First-time buyers → welcome nurture
   - Repeat buyers → loyalty program
   - At-risk of churn → win-back
   - VIP → exclusive early access
7. **Segment by acquisition channel** (EZ Bombs lesson):
   - TikTok/Instagram-discovered
   - Organic search
   - Referral/word-of-mouth
   - Conference/event leads

### Priority 3: Content Strategy
8. **Create recurring valuable content** (IMSE's "Freebie Friday" lesson):
   - "Sensory Tip Tuesday" — weekly sensory activity idea
   - "Tool of the Week" — product spotlight with use-case tutorial
   - Parent survival stories / community spotlights
9. **Personality-driven content** (Totally Chocolate lesson) — Don't be generic; let BKC's brand voice be warm, understanding, and expert
10. **Educational content that helps parents do THEIR job** — Advocacy tips, IEP advice, sensory diet ideas, therapist-recommended activities

### Priority 4: Advanced Tactics
11. **Abandoned cart survey flow** (Manly Bands lesson) — Ask "Why didn't you buy?" instead of just offering discounts. Possible responses: "Too expensive" → send value breakdown; "Not sure it's right for my child" → send age/sensory profile guide; "Just browsing" → send educational content
12. **Create signature annual event** (Tatcha's Fukubukuro lesson):
    - April: Autism Awareness Month mega-sale
    - Back-to-School: Sensory toolkit bundle
    - Holiday: "Sensory Gift Guide" series
    - January: New Year "Fresh Start" sensory diet kit
13. **Loyalty tiers within Klaviyo** (Half Magic lesson):
    - Tier 1: Sensory Explorer (1-2 purchases) — basic rewards
    - Tier 2: Sensory Pro (3-5 purchases) — free shipping, early access
    - Tier 3: Sensory Champion (6+ purchases) — exclusive products, VIP support
14. **Post-purchase production updates** (Manly Bands lesson) — If BKC ships physical products, automated "your order is being packed" → "shipped" → "delivered" → "how's your child liking it?" sequence

### Priority 5: Measurement & Optimization
15. **Target metrics** based on benchmarks:
    - Abandoned cart open rate: 50%+ (aim for 65% top 10%)
    - Abandoned cart conversion: 3.3%+ (aim for 7.7% top 10%)
    - Email revenue attribution: 30-47% of total ecommerce revenue
    - Flow revenue: 50%+ of total email revenue
    - Overall email open rate: 35-45% (niche audiences tend to be higher)
16. **A/B test everything** — Subject lines, send times, content blocks, CTAs. The Klaviyo benchmarks show huge gap between average and top 10% — that gap is optimization.
17. **Track RPR by flow type** — Know which automations make money and optimize those first

### What NOT to Do (Anti-Patterns from Case Studies)
- Don't send identical emails to your entire list (IMSE: 20% open rate)
- Don't run email and SMS on separate platforms (Gymwrap: blind spots)
- Don't keep inactive subscribers forever (IMSE: bots dragging down deliverability)
- Don't use generic "increased sales" messaging (Totally Chocolate: distributors ignore generic supplier emails)
- Don't default to discounts for cart abandonment (Manly Bands: survey-first approach converts 4.8x better)
- Don't ignore post-purchase communication (Half Magic: 110% flow revenue growth shows automations outperform campaigns)
- Don't pay for features you don't use (Kaylin + Kaylin: "ludicrously expensive" platform costs)
