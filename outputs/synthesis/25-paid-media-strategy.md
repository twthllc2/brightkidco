## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BrightKidCo (BKC) operates in a fragmented, underserved market. The product — a Body-Signal Learning Layer™ training underwear — occupies a unique position: no competitor combines reusable underwear + wetness learning signal + autism/sensory focus (Source: Research Compendium §1.1 — Competitive Landscape; Diagnosis Compendium §2.3 — Competitive Position). **Key strategic decisions:**

1. **Meta is the launch channel.** Facebook group density for autism parents (200K+ members in primary groups alone — Source: Research Compendium §4.2) combined with Instagram's visual storytelling capability makes Meta the highest-ROI starting point. TikTok is deferred to Phase 2 due to content production requirements.

2. **Google Search enters Phase 2.** High-intent queries (e.g., "autism potty training underwear", "best training pants for autistic child") have low competition. Phase 1 focuses on building awareness; Phase 2 captures search demand generated by that awareness.

3. **Pinterest is a long-term build.** The platform's visual search nature and growing autism parent community make it valuable for evergreen content, but the conversion cycle is longer. Invest in Phase 2-3.

4. **Email is the conversion engine.** All paid media funnels into email capture (pop-up, quiz, lead magnets). The Welcome Flow generates $44 per $1 spent (Source: Research Compendium §3.1 — Benchmarks). Paid media builds top-of-funnel; email converts.

5. **No direct competitor runs paid ads targeting autism parents.** Pull-Ups (46,250+ reviews, $50-100+/month ongoing) targets general parents. Super Undies ($22-$44) has minimal paid presence. The autism parent is invisible in paid advertising — this is BKC's first-mover advantage (Source: Research Compendium §1.4 — Competitor Email Strategy).

**Scoring rationale:**

- **Audience Match (25% weight):** Meta has the highest concentration of autism parent audiences (Facebook groups totaling 200K+ members, Instagram autism parent accounts with 400K-1.5M followers — Source: Research Compendium §4.2-4.3). Google captures search intent but relies on awareness being built elsewhere. TikTok has massive reach but less concentrated autism parent targeting.

- **Targeting Precision (20% weight):** Meta's interest-based targeting (autism parenting, sensory processing disorder, special needs parenting) and Custom/Lookalike audiences provide the most precise targeting. Google Search captures explicit intent. TikTok's interest graph is less developed for niche health/developmental topics.

- **Creative Potential (20% weight):** TikTok has the highest organic creative potential for storytelling and viral reach. Instagram Reels and Stories are strong. Google Search is text-limited. Pinterest is visual but less dynamic.

- **Cost Efficiency (15% weight):** Pinterest has lowest CPMs for visual content. Meta CPMs are moderate rising. Google Search CPCs are high for health-related keywords ($3-8/click). TikTok CPMs are low but conversion tracking is less mature.

- **Time to Results (10% weight):** Meta has the fastest path to conversion with mature pixel/API tracking. Google captures existing intent quickly. TikTok and Pinterest require longer audience-building periods.

- **Competitor Gap (10% weight):** ALL channels have minimal to zero autism-parent-specific ad competition. TikTok has the widest open gap — #autismpottytraining hashtag is essentially unowned (Source: Research Compendium §4.3 — Social Media). Pinterest has no autism potty training content saturation.

### 3.2 Channel Descriptions

#### Tier 1 — Meta (Facebook + Instagram) | Phase 1 Entry | 70% Budget

**Why it's #1:** Facebook groups are where autism parents congregate, ask questions, and share product recommendations. 15+ groups with 100K-200K members each — with Potty Training Autism/Special Needs (30-50K members) being the exact BKC use case (Source: Research Compendium §4.2 — Facebook Groups). Instagram's visual platform allows BKC to showcase the product, share parent testimonials, and build brand authority.

**Key advantages:**
- Facebook Interest targeting: "Autism Parenting", "Sensory Processing Disorder", "Special needs parenting", "Occupational therapy"
- Instagram Reels for educational content with high organic reach
- Facebook Lead Ads for direct email capture (bypassing landing page friction)
- Custom Audiences from email list + Lookalike expansion
- Facebook Groups integration for community-driven ads
- Mature conversion tracking with Meta Pixel + Conversions API

**Key disadvantages:**
- Rising CPMs in competitive verticals (mitigated by niche targeting)
- iOS 14+ attribution limitations
- Requires compelling visual creative — text-heavy ads underperform
- Meta's algorithm requires consistent testing to find winning audiences

#### Tier 2 — Google Search + YouTube | Phase 2 Entry | 15% Budget

**Why #2 for Phase 2:** Parents actively search for solutions. High-intent queries like "potty training underwear for autism" have lower competition than general parenting keywords. YouTube provides long-form educational content that builds trust — critical for the skeptical L2/L3 audience (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §6.1 — Level 2 Paradox).

**Key advantages:**
- Captures existing demand (awareness must be built first — hence Phase 2)
- Lower competition for autism-specific keywords
- YouTube tutorial content aligns with BKC's educational brand position
- Remarketing Lists for Search Ads (RLSA) to retarget site visitors
- Performance Max campaigns for cross-network reach

**Key disadvantages:**
- High CPCs for health/parenting keywords ($3-8/click estimated)
- Search volume is limited by niche size (~1K-10K monthly searches for core terms)
- YouTube production costs are higher than static creative
- No audience building — only captures existing demand

#### Tier 3 — TikTok | Phase 2 Entry | 10% Budget

**Why #3 with Phase 2 entry:** TikTok has the largest reach potential (top autism creators have 1.2M-3.1M followers — Source: Research Compendium §4.3). The #autismpottytraining hashtag is wide open. However, TikTok requires native-content-format creative (vertical video, trends, authentic UGC-style) which takes time to produce. Phase 1 is for content library building; Phase 2 is for paid amplification.

**Key advantages:**
- Lowest CPMs among all platforms
- Highest organic discovery potential
- #autismpottytraining is an unowned hashtag — first-mover advantage
- Spark Ads allow amplifying organic creator content
- TikTok Shop potential for direct purchase

**Key disadvantages:**
- Requires native-content creative (not repurposed from Meta)
- Less mature conversion tracking and attribution
- Autism parenting content faces moderation challenges
- Longer path to purchase — TikTok is primarily discovery
- Audience is younger than core BKC demographic (30-45 vs TikTok's 18-34 core)

#### Tier 4 — Pinterest | Phase 3 Entry | 5% Budget

**Why #4:** Pinterest serves as a visual search engine for parents planning and researching. Autism parents pin sensory diets, visual schedules, and potty training strategies. However, the conversion cycle is longer, and Pinterest requires high-volume content production (20+ pins/week per account). Entry in Phase 3 when content library is established.

**Key advantages:**
- Long content shelf life — pins drive traffic for months/years
- Lower CPCs than Google
- Visual search drives high-intent traffic
- Growing autism parent community on the platform
- Ideal for lead magnet distribution (visual schedules, checklists)

**Key disadvantages:**
- Requires dedicated content production (15-25 pins/week)
- Longer conversion cycle (weeks to months from pin to purchase)
- Less mature ad platform compared to Meta/Google
- Smaller addressable audience for the niche

## 6. CREATIVE TESTING FRAMEWORK

### 6.1 Testing Philosophy

BKC has zero paid media data. Every assumption must be tested. The testing framework uses a structured hypothesis-driven approach:

1. **Test one variable at a time** — isolate hook, format, offer, or audience
2. **Minimum statistical significance** — 95% confidence, minimum 50 conversions per variant
3. **Weekly creative refresh** — retire underperformers after 500-1,000 impressions with <1% CTR
4. **Creative fatigue monitoring** — watch frequency >4 impressions/week and CTR decline >20%

### 6.5 Forbidden Creative Approaches

Based on brand voice rules from Diagnosis Compendium §3 and Layer-04, these creative approaches are FORBIDDEN in paid media:

- **NO miracle/cure language** — "Fix your child's autism", "Cure potty training struggles" — triggers L2 skepticism and violates brand ethics
- **NO fear-mongering on adult diapers** — The adult diaper transition is the deepest emotional trigger in the ecosystem but CANNOT be used as fear-based selling (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §6.6 — Adult Diaper Transition)
- **NO NT comparison as standard** — "Just like other kids" is forbidden for all levels (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §3.3 — Forbidden Words)
- **NO BCBA criticism** — "ABA doesn't work" destroys trust with 65% of audience who rely on BCBA protocols (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §6.2 — BCBA Loyalty)
- **NO easy/quick claims** — "Potty train in 3 days" is the #1 trust destroyer for L2/L3 audiences who have heard every promise
- **NO math reframes for L3** — "Less than a coffee a day" is inappropriate when $34.99 is a significant commitment for 40% Medicaid households (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §6.5 — Medicaid Sensitivity)
- **NO urgency for L3** — Zero urgency. Dignity-first always. Countdown timers are NEVER appropriate (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §3.2 — Per-Level Tone Profiles)

### 7.3 Budget Guardrails

**Never-exceed rules:**

1. **Phase 1: Maximum $4K/month.** Stay within budget. The goal is proof-of-concept and data collection — not maximum spend. Escalate spend only after hitting CPA targets for 7 consecutive days.

2. **No single ad set >30% of daily budget.** Diversify testing across at least 4 ad sets per campaign to prevent single-point failure.

3. **Scale pacing: max +20% daily budget increase.** Following Meta's learning phase recommendations. Aggressive scaling triggers CPA volatility.

4. **Stop-loss rules:**
   - Cold audience: Pause any ad with CPA >$50 after 50 conversions
   - Warm audience: Pause any ad with CPA >$35 after 30 conversions
   - Video views: Pause any ad with >$0.10 per 3-second view after $50 spend
   - Any ad with frequency >5 AND declining CTR (<1%) = pause immediately

5. **Seasonal budget adjustments:**
   - May-August (Pre-K enrollment season) — Increase L1-targeted budget by 25%
   - September (school start) — Shift budget from Pre-K hooks to "regression recovery" content
   - November-December (holiday) — Maintain base spend; avoid competing with holiday advertisers
   - January (New Year resolution parents) — Increase by 15% for fresh-start framing

## 9. SCALING PLAN

### 9.1 Phase 1 — Proof of Concept (Months 1-2)

**Objective:** Validate that autism parents convert through paid media. Build baseline data.

**Actions:**
- Launch Meta only (Facebook + Instagram). No other channels.
- Create 10-15 ad creatures across 5 pillars (3 per pillar).
- Test 4-6 cold audiences (interest-based + broad).
- Goal: 50+ email signups and 20+ purchases from paid media.
- Collect 500+ 3-second video views for retargeting pool.
- Set up Meta Pixel + Conversions API for accurate attribution.
- Weekly creative refresh — kill losers, double down on winners.

**Exit criteria (move to Phase 2 when):**
- [ ] CPA on warm audiences <$25 for 7 consecutive days
- [ ] At least 2 winning creative pillars identified (CTR >1.5%, CPC <$1.50)
- [ ] Email list grown by 300+ from paid media
- [ ] 20+ purchases attributed to paid media
- [ ] Retargeting pools populated (1K+ website visitors, 500+ video viewers)

### 9.2 Phase 2 — Learning & Expansion (Months 3-4)

**Objective:** Expand to Google and TikTok. Build retargeting funnels. Find scalable audiences.

**Actions:**
- Launch Google Search (non-brand + brand + Shopping).
- Launch TikTok Spark Ads (amplify top organic content).
- Build retargeting funnels: Website visitors (1-7d, 8-30d), Video viewers (25%+), Email lookalikes (1% LAL).
- Scale winning Meta creatives to warm audiences (testimonial, guarantee, mechanism deep-dive).
- Test Pinterest with small budget ($250/mo).
- Implement UTM tracking and unified attribution model.
- Run creative A/B tests at scale (10+ concurrent tests).

**Exit criteria (move to Phase 3 when):**
- [ ] Consistent ROAS >2.0x on Meta warm campaigns
- [ ] Google Search CPA <$30 on non-brand keywords
- [ ] TikTok identifies at least 1 viral content format
- [ ] Email list grown by 1,000+ from all paid channels
- [ ] Retargeting pools: 5K+ website visitors, 10K+ video viewers
- [ ] Attribution model validated (last-click + view-through)

### 9.3 Phase 3 — Scaling (Months 5-6)

**Objective:** Scale budgets 2x. Optimize for efficiency. Expand creative production.

**Actions:**
- Increase Meta budget to $5,500/mo — focus on lookalike audiences + DABA (Dynamic Audience-Based Ads).
- Scale Google to $2,000/mo — add Performance Max campaigns, YouTube pre-roll.
- TikTok to $1,500/mo — scale Spark Ads with top creators, launch In-Feed conversions.
- Pinterest to $500/mo — Shopping Pins, Idea Pins, lead magnet distribution.
- Podcast sponsorship pilot ($500/mo) — Autism Parenting Secrets or The OT Schoolhouse.
- Implement creative sequencing (cold → warm → hot creative journey per user).
- Deploy dynamic creative optimization (DCO) for Meta catalog ads.

**Exit criteria (move to Phase 4 when):**
- [ ] Full-funnel ROAS >3.0x sustained for 30 days
- [ ] 3+ channels profitable (CPA <25% of AOV)
- [ ] Email list grown by 3,000+ from paid media
- [ ] Creative production at 8-12 new concepts/month
- [ ] Scaling CPA stable (not increasing with budget)

### 9.4 Phase 4 — Full-Funnel Machine (Month 7+)

**Objective:** Operate as mature paid media program. Scale to $15-25K/mo.

**Actions:**
- All four channels active with full-funnel attribution.
- Retargeting at all stages (cold → warm → hot → loyal).
- Creative library of 50+ proven concepts.
- Lookalike audiences from 2,000+ purchasers.
- Influencer pipeline active (2-3 creator partnerships/month).
- A/B testing machine running 15+ concurrent tests.
- Budget reallocation based on channel efficiency (not fixed percentages).

## 10. RISK REGISTER & MITIGATIONS

### 10.1 Key Risks

| # | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|------|------------|--------|------------|
| 1 | **Meta CPM inflation** — Autism parent targeting becomes competitive | Medium | High | Diversify to Google/TikTok early. Build email list as owned audience hedge. |
| 2 | **Low conversion volume** — Niche market has fewer purchasers than expected | Medium | Critical | Focus on LTV optimization. Maximize Welcome Flow ($44 per $1). Reduce paid spend, increase organic. |
| 3 | **Creative fatigue** — Limited autism parent audience sees same ads repeatedly | High | Medium | Weekly creative refresh. Frequency capping at 3/week. Segment creative by level. |
| 4 | **Attribution gaps** — iOS 14+ limits Meta tracking, inaccurate ROAS | High | Medium | Conversions API integration. GA4 as independent measurement. Incrementality testing. |
| 5 | **TikTok content moderation** — Autism content flagged as sensitive | Medium | Low | Pre-moderation review. Avoid triggering keywords. Text overlays on video. |
| 6 | **Brand safety concerns** — Autism community backlash against paid targeting | Low | Critical | Transparency in ad copy. No predatory targeting. Dignity-first always. Community-first ethos. |
| 7 | **Email dependency** — If email capture fails (pop-up <3%), paid media ROI collapses | Medium | Critical | Test multiple pop-up variants. Meta Lead Ads as backup. Lead magnets as conversion incentive. |
| 8 | **Domain warm-up conflict** — Zero sender reputation means email deliverability is fragile | High | Critical | Strict adherence to 6-week warming schedule. No promotional content in first 2 weeks. (Source: Diagnosis Compendium §7.1 — Domain Warming) |

### 10.2 Success Criteria — Go/No-Go Checkpoints

After each phase, evaluate whether to proceed:

**After Phase 1 ($6K spend, 2 months):**
- [ ] CPA <$35 on warm audiences? → Continue to Phase 2
- [ ] CPA $35-50? → Extend Phase 1 with revised targeting
- [ ] CPA >$50? → Pivot creative strategy or reconsider paid media viability

**After Phase 2 ($16K total, 4 months):**
- [ ] Blended ROAS >2.0x? → Scale to Phase 3
- [ ] Blended ROAS 1.0-2.0x? → Optimize, extend Phase 2
- [ ] Blended ROAS <1.0x? → Reduce paid spend, pivot to organic channels

**After Phase 3 ($36K total, 6 months):**
- [ ] ROAS >3.0x + CPA <$25? → Full funnel deployment, scale to $15-25K/mo
- [ ] ROAS 2.0-3.0x? → Continue optimization within $10K/mo budget
- [ ] ROAS <2.0x? → Restructure channel mix, consider removing lowest-performing channels
