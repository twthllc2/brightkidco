### 1.2 Hook Type A — Mirror Hooks (Scroll-Stop by Recognition)

These hooks work because the parent sees her exact situation in 0.5 seconds. Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.2-1.4, Layer 31 §2-4.

**Hook A1 — The Pre-K Panic**
> **Hook:** "Your child starts preschool in [weeks]. Still in pull-ups."
> **Emotional target:** Deadline Pressure Mom (Layer 31 §2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I have six weeks to get him fully potty trained or he can't go to Pre-K." (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM §8.4)
> **Level:** L1 (Sarah)
> **Why it works:** Names the specific calendar crisis. Every Deadline Mom fills in her own deadline.
> **Forbidden:** DO NOT say "Quick" or "Easy" — this is a panic trigger, not a solution frame.

**Hook A2 — The Couch Incident**
> **Hook:** "By 10am he peed on 7 couch cushions. I've been changing clothes since 6."
> **Emotional target:** Overwhelmed Mom (Layer 31 §4)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "By 10am he managed to pee on all 7 cushions on my couch." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.3)
> **Level:** L2/L3 (Lisa/Maria)
> **Why it works:** Specific physical detail creates visceral recognition. The number "7" triggers "that's my house."
> **Forbidden:** DO NOT follow with a quick fix promise. Match the exhaustion register.

**Hook A3 — The Playground Glance**
> **Hook:** "She's 5. Still in pull-ups. I'm starting to feel ashamed at the playground."
> **Emotional target:** Social Pressure Mom (Layer 31 §3)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I'm starting to feel ashamed when other parents see his diaper poke out." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.4)
> **Level:** L1/L2/GF
> **Why it works:** Names the specific shame trigger. The "playground" is the universal comparison arena.

**Hook A4 — The 3-Year Journey**
> **Hook:** "3 years of potty training. 3 years of accidents. 3 years of 'I've tried everything.'"
> **Emotional target:** Method-Cycling Mom (Layer 31 §4.6)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "We've been potty training for three years. It destroyed me." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.3)
> **Level:** L2 (Lisa)
> **Why it works:** Tripling creates rhythm that signals exhaustion, not panic. The parent who has been at this for years recognizes the tone immediately.

**Hook A5 — The Adult Diaper Moment**
> **Hook:** "He's outgrown size 7. The next size up is adult diapers. I cried in the store."
> **Emotional target:** L3 (Maria) — Deepest emotional trigger (Layer 31 §4.5, trigger 6)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "He's outgrown size 7. Moving to adult diapers. I cried in the store." (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM §8.4)
> **Level:** L3 only
> **Why it works:** Names the identity crisis moment without exploiting it. Dignity-first.
> **Forbidden:** NEVER use as sales pressure. This is an acknowledgment hook, not an urgency hook. (Source: Layer 30 §3.4)

### 1.3 Hook Type B — Counter-Intuitive Hooks (Scroll-Stop by Surprise)

These hooks disrupt the parent's existing belief framework. Source: Layer 20 (Language Pattern), Layer 31 §10.

**Hook B1 — The Wrong Problem**
> **Hook:** "You haven't failed potty training. You solved the wrong problem."
> **Emotional target:** All levels — universal reframe (Layer 31 §10.1)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I feel like a failure and don't know what to do." (Source: Layer 11 §D2)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** The word "failed" stops the scroll. The reframe "wrong problem" creates a curiosity gap that demands explanation.

**Hook B2 — The Pull-Up Trap**
> **Hook:** "Pull-ups keep your child completely dry. That's the problem."
> **Emotional target:** L2 (Lisa) — Lever 2 activation (GIULIANO-DEMANDS §L2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "If I don't remember to set her on the toilet, she will just pee in her underwear and not even tell me." (Source: Layer 11 §B1)
> **Level:** L2, L3, GF
> **Why it works:** Inverts the parent's assumption that pull-ups are helping. Creates immediate mechanism curiosity.

**Hook B3 — The Defiance Reframe**
> **Hook:** "He's not being stubborn. He genuinely can't feel it."
> **Emotional target:** Parents who think their child is refusing (Layer 9 §L1-C)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "She doesn't care AT ALL. She'll walk around with wet pants until I notice." (Source: Layer 11 §B1)
> **Level:** L1/L2
> **Why it works:** Names the parent's internal accusation ("my child is defiant") and reframes it neurologically. Absolves both parent and child.

**Hook B4 — The "Gets It But Doesn't Get It"**
> **Hook:** "He gets it. But he doesn't get it."
> **Emotional target:** L1 (Sarah) — The Level 1 marker phrase (MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §2.2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "He gets it, but he doesn't get it." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §2.2)
> **Level:** L1
> **Why it works:** This IS the verbatim Level 1 marker phrase. Every L1 parent recognizes it instantly.

**Hook B5 — The 49% Normalization**
> **Hook:** "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't toilet trained. You are not behind."
> **Emotional target:** All levels — Lever 5 activation (GIULIANO-DEMANDS §L5)
> **Source:** Wiggins et al. 2022, CDC-funded SPARK study (Source: MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM §2.2)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** The stat normalizes. It transforms the parent from "only one" to "one of many." The shame reduction is instant.

### 1.4 Hook Type C — Question Hooks (Scroll-Stop by Self-Identification)

**Hook C1 — The Exhaustion Check**
> **Hook:** "How many times have you changed wet clothes today? Before noon?"
> **Emotional target:** Overwhelmed Mom (Layer 31 §4)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "My washing pile is taller than me!" (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.3)
> **Level:** L2/L3
> **Why it works:** The specific number-in-a-question forces self-tracking. "Before noon" creates immediacy.

**Hook C2 — The Method Inventory**
> **Hook:** "How many methods have you tried? Sticker charts. Timers. Oh Crap. ABA protocols. Social stories. How many until you count it as 'everything'?"
> **Emotional target:** Method-Cycling L2 Mom (Layer 31 §4.7, insight 2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I've tried everything from the BCBA's protocol to OT sensory work to social stories. We're stuck." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §2.3)
> **Level:** L2
> **Why it works:** Lists specific methods she has tried. Creates "she knows my exact situation" recognition.

**Hook C3 — The Two-Question Split**
> **Hook:** "Does your child talk about the potty but won't actually use it? Or just sits there for 20 minutes and pees the second they stand up?"
> **Emotional target:** Cross-level — uses "Whether...Or..." pattern (Layer 20 §3, Instance 2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "He will sit on the toilet for 45 minutes and then pee the second he stands up." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §2.3)
> **Level:** L1/L2
> **Why it works:** The two-question split covers both major symptom profiles. Every parent identifies with at least one.

**Hook C4 — The Partner Question**
> **Hook:** "Is your partner still blaming you for the potty training not working?"
> **Emotional target:** Social Pressure Mom — Partner conflict trigger (Layer 31 §3.5, trigger 4)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "My partner is FURIOUS at me that she isn't potty trained." (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM §8.4)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** Names the unspoken shame source. Almost no one addresses partner dynamics in potty training ads. Source: Layer 11 §NEW-8 (112 mentions).

**Hook C5 — The Nighttime Confusion**
> **Hook:** "Your child is fully trained during the day. Why is night wetting still happening?"
> **Emotional target:** All levels — Nighttime Training Confusion (Layer 11 §NEW-1, 1,166 mentions — #1 by volume)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "He's been fully potty trained for a year and a half, but not night trained." (Source: Layer 11 §NEW-1)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** Names the #1 unaddressed objection. Parents conflate day and night training. The question hook creates curiosity for the answer: "They're two different systems."

### 1.5 Hook Type D — Story Openers (Scroll-Stop by Narrative)

**Hook D1 — The Specific Moment**
> **Hook:** "Last Tuesday, 3pm, my son peed on the couch. Again. I just sat down and cried."
> **Emotional target:** Overwhelmed Mom (Layer 31 §4.5)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I can't do this anymore. I need help." (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM §8.4)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** The specific time (3pm) creates a real-moment feel. The word "again" signals the cumulative exhaustion.

**Hook D2 — The First Signal**
> **Hook:** "After 6 years of zero reaction to wet diapers — he looked down. He felt it."
> **Emotional target:** L3 (Maria) — The win moment (Layer 31 §10.4)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "He looked down at the wet spot for the first time in 9 years." (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §2.4)
> **Level:** L3
> **Why it works:** The specific duration ("6 years") signals deep journey. The micro-win ("looked down") is credible hope.

**Hook D3 — The Daycare Confrontation**
> **Hook:** "The daycare called. Again. Their policy says no pull-ups. But underwear means accidents. What am I supposed to do?"
> **Emotional target:** Daycare-conflicted parent — NEW-2 (Layer 11 §NEW-2)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "Daycare has been an issue... They require a pull up against their skin unless fully potty trained." (Source: Layer 11 §NEW-2)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** Names the institutional conflict that standard potty training advice ignores.

**Hook D4 — The Regression Story**
> **Hook:** "He was fully trained. Then vacation happened. Now we're back to accidents. It feels like we lost everything."
> **Emotional target:** Regression-anxious parent — NEW-3 (Layer 11 §NEW-3)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "I need some help with a severe regression! My son... has been fully day trained since May last year... In the last week, he has been having 3-4 pee accidents." (Source: Layer 11 §NEW-3)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** Names the specific regression trigger (vacation). Creates "this happens to everyone" normalization.

**Hook D5 — The 2AM Google Search**
> **Hook:** "It's 2am. You're Googling 'potty training not working' for the 50th time. I've been there."
> **Emotional target:** All — universal research moment (MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.1, Behavioral Patterns)
> **Verbatim parent language:** "Googles at 2am" — Behavioral Pattern #1 (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM §1.1)
> **Level:** All
> **Why it works:** The specific hour (2am) captures the signature behavior of the autism parent. It signals "I know your life."
