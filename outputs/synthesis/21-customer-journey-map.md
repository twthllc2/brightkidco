## 1. Executive Summary

The BrightKidCo customer does NOT have a potty training problem. She has a **body-signal translation problem**. Her child's nervous system does not reliably translate the internal sensation of "I need to go" into the action of using the toilet. Everything she has tried — sticker charts, ABA protocols, Oh Crap, social stories, timers, bribes — has failed not because of lack of effort, but because she was solving the wrong problem. (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md Section 1.8; MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM.md Section 1.1)

**She is 95%+ female, age 27-48, primary caregiver of an autistic child (Level 1-3).** She has tried 40+ methods. She has googled at 2am. She has cried in store aisles. She has been told by pediatricians to "just wait until he's ready." (Source: 01-customer-avatar.md Section 2)

**The journey from Awareness to Advocacy spans 2-7+ years** — not weeks like neurotypical potty training. The average autistic child takes 3.3 years to complete toilet training vs 2.3 years for typically developing children. Only 35% are daytime trained by 36 months. 49% of autistic 4-5 year olds are NOT toilet trained (vs 8% neurotypical — a 6:1 gap). (Source: MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM.md Section 2.2 — Wiggins et al. 2022)

**BKC's role in the journey:** Not just a product seller. From first touchpoint to post-purchase, BKC reframes the parent's understanding of the problem. Every communication serves one purpose: shifting the parent from "behavioral" to "neurological" understanding of their child's potty training struggle. The product (3-Layer Body-Signal Learning Layer™) is the physical manifestation of this reframe. (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md Section 3.1-3.5)
