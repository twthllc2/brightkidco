### 2.1 What Is Interoception?

Interoception is the "eighth sensory system" — the body's ability to sense internal physical states. From MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM.md, Section 2.1, lines 206-207:

> "Interoception — the ability to sense internal body signals — is the eighth sensory system. Research consistently shows autistic children have significantly diminished interoceptive awareness."

It governs:
- Bladder fullness and bowel urgency (directly relevant to potty training)
- Hunger, thirst, temperature regulation
- Heart rate, breathing, emotional states
- Anxiety, frustration, excitement

Source: SCIENCE-PROOF-POINTS.md, Section Category A, PP-A7: "Interoception researcher Kelly Mahler calls it the 'eighth sensory system' — the sense that tells us when we're hungry, thirsty, anxious, or need the bathroom."
