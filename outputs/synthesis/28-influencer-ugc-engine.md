## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BrightKidCo enters an influencer and UGC landscape that is **fragmented and underserved**. The autism parenting niche has major creators (500K-2.7M followers) but NO competitor owns the autism potty training content space. Zero competitors use quiz-based segmentation, educational email nurture, or persona-voice sequences (Source: MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM §1.3). The brands winning in the autism parenting space are UGC-heavy -- real parents sharing real stories.

**Core insight:** BKC's customer base IS its UGC engine. The autism parenting community is the most active, emotionally engaged, and product-hungry audience in the parenting space. Every customer who experiences a body-signal breakthrough becomes a potential creator.

**The opportunity:**
- #autismpottytraining has limited content across all platforms (Source: RA1-R5 Social Recon §5)
- No competitor leverages OT/BCBA endorsements on social (Source: RA1-R5 Social Recon §5)
- No competitor has character-based content (BKC has Melon Pop, Fynn Fox, Lily Pop) (Source: RA1-R5 Social Recon §5)
- Email list building via social is weak across ALL competitors (Source: RA1-R5 Social Recon §5)
- Eltern fragen auf Reddit/ Facebook aktiv nach Produktempfehlungen (Source: RA4-R1 Reddit Mining §5)
- BKC has clinical data (672-child trial) that no competitor can match (Source: MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM §2.2)

**Three-phase rollout:**
- **Phase 1 (Months 1-2):** Customer UGC collection system + micro-influencer seeding (10-100K followers)
- **Phase 2 (Months 3-4):** Mid-tier partnerships (100K-500K) + podcast sponsorships + ambassador program
- **Phase 3 (Months 5-6):** Tier 1 partnerships (500K+) + owned content series + community program

### 3.2 Tier Selection Criteria

Each influencer is scored on a 1-5 scale across 8 dimensions to determine tier placement:

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Audience Alignment** | 25% | Percentage of followers who are autism parents (0-5: 0%=0, 20%=1, 40%=2, 60%=3, 80%=4, 95%+=5) |
| **Content Authenticity** | 20% | Degree to which content feels peer-to-peer vs. polished brand (raw/vulnerable=5, semi-produced=3, produced=1) |
| **Engagement Rate** | 15% | Comments + shares per post relative to followers (5%+=5, 3-5%=4, 1-3%=3, <1%=2, <0.5%=1) |
| **Product Fit** | 15% | How naturally BKC fits their content narrative (potty training focus=5, daily life=4, therapy/OT=4, general autism=2, comedy=1) |
| **Cross-Platform Reach** | 10% | Number of platforms where they have significant presence (3+ platforms=5, 2=4, 1=3, single unknown=1) |
| **Reputation Safety** | 10% | Controversy risk — no history of problematic content, MLM, cure claims (clean=5, minor issues=3, history of controversy=1) |
| **Partnership History** | 3% | Experience with brand partnerships (multiple successful=5, some=4, first=3, none=2, avoids brands=1) |
| **Responsiveness** | 2% | Communication speed and professionalism (proactive=5, responsive=4, slow=3, unreliable=1) |

**Scoring Guide:**
- **18-25 points:** Tier 1 — Mega (500K+ reach, full brand alignment)
- **13-17 points:** Tier 2 — Mid (100K-500K, good alignment)
- **8-12 points:** Tier 3 — Micro (10K-100K, test first)
- **0-7 points:** Not recommended — wait for growth or different approach

### 7.3 The Three-Family Pattern — UGC Template for Cross-Level Emails

This is BKC's most powerful testimonial format (Source: Layer 28 §1.1, §5.4).

```
─── THREE-FAMILY PATTERN ──────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Three families. Three different situations."

— Sarah, mom of 4yo verbal son:
'He went from "almost" to "mostly" in 6 weeks. Pre-K accepted him in
underwear.'

— Lisa, mom of 6yo daughter (sensory dominant):
'After 3 years of trying everything, she finally paused when she felt
wetness. That was Week 4.'

— Maria, mom of 9yo non-verbal son:
"We're 5 months in. He's not trained. But he's feeling something for the
first time. That's enough for now."

You'll see in 60 days where you fit.
```

**Why it works:**
- Everyone finds themselves in a testimonial
- Implicitly acknowledges different levels without labeling
- No "miracle" promises — realistic outcomes per profile
- Creates implicit migration signal (reader identifies with one testimonial)

**When to use:** Welcome E3, Cart Recovery E2, FAQ "Will this work for my kid?", Browse Abandonment E2, any cross-level email.

## 9. ANTI-FABRICATION RULES FOR UGC

All UGC and influencer content must follow BrightKidCo's anti-fabrication protocol (Source: Layer 30 Anti-Fabrication). Key rules:

### 9.1 Core Rules

1. **RS-Only Quote Sourcing** — Verbatim parent quotes must come from RS PDFs or documented customer submissions. Never fabricate. (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 1)
2. **Prohibited Actions** — Never invent customer quotes, fabricate testimonials, imagine "what a mom might say," or generate fake reviews. (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 2)
3. **Permitted Modifications** — Lightly anonymize (change first names, ages within range). Combine themes from multiple real quotes into one representative statement (note internally). Paraphrase for length keeping core meaning and voice. (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 3)
4. **Flag-Don't-Fabricate** — If you can't find a real quote, FLAG IT. Never fabricate. This is brand-trust-critical. (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 4)
5. **Statistics Must Be Sourced** — 49% SPARK stat → cite "SPARK study, Wiggins et al." Kelly Mahler → cite "Kelly Mahler, interoception researcher." 16,511 reviews → cite as "on-site reviews." (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 7)
6. **Lena Is the Only Fictional Persona** — And even she must stay within her defined profile (mom of two autistic sons, ages 6 and 8). (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 10)
7. **Rough Edges Rule** — NEVER rewrite parent language into "nicer English." The ROUGH edges are the trust anchor. (Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 11)

### 9.2 RS Integration Quota for Emails Using UGC

(Source: Layer 30 §2, Rule 12-13)

| Component | Percentage | Source |
|-----------|:--------:|--------|
| Authentic verbatim or tightly paraphrased | 30-50% | RS PDFs or customer submissions |
| Lena's voice | 30-50% | Peer-to-peer, empathetic brand voice |
| Mechanism/brand language | 10-20% | Doc-based product descriptions |

Minimum 2-4 verbatim quotes per email.

### 9.3 Allowed Claims for Influencer Content

Influencers should be briefed on these allowed claims (Source: Layer 30 §5):

**Externally verified (use confidently):**
- 49% of autistic 4-5 year olds not toilet trained (Wiggins et al. 2022)
- "The vast majority of autistic children can be potty trained" (Elizabeth Cross, PhD, Kennedy Krieger)
- Interoception research (Kelly Mahler, gold standard)
- Maureen Bennie's son trained at 9.5 years

**Self-reported BKC data (use with qualification: "in our customer survey..."):**
- 92% clearer body awareness in first 2 weeks (N=5,679)
- 87% reduced pull-up use in first month (N=5,679)
- 82% of ASD children show clearer awareness in 2-4 weeks (N=672)
- 97.6% would recommend BKC (N=5,679)

**Banned claims (NEVER in influencer content):**
- Outcome promises ("your child will train")
- Medical/therapeutic claims ("reduces meltdowns")
- Normalcy claims ("just like other kids")
- Financial pressure language for Level 3 audiences
- Fabricated statistics or entities

**Source:** Layer 30 §4-5, MASTER-RESEARCH-COMPENDIUM §2.5

### 9.4 Pre-Deployment Checklist

Every piece of UGC before deployment must pass:

```
□ 1. Permission documented and stored?
□ 2. Source verified (real customer, not fabricated)?
□ 3. Forbidden phrases absent?
□ 4. Avatar level tagged correctly?
□ 5. Statistics sourced correctly?
□ 6. Level-appropriate (L1 UGC not sent to L3 audience)?
□ 7. Image resolution ≥1080px?
□ 8. Alt text added with voice-compliant description?
□ 9. No medical/clinical claims?
□10. Rough edges preserved (not "nicer English")?
```
