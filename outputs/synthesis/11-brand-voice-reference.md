## 3. THE VOICE FORMULA

### 3.1 The Core 4-Step Formula

Every email from Lena follows this sequence:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  THE LENA VOICE FORMULA                               │
│                                                                      │
│   OBSERVATION ──→ VALIDATION ──→ MECHANISM ──→ PERMISSION           │
│       │               │               │               │              │
│       ▼               ▼               ▼               ▼              │
│   "He talks      "That's not      "His nervous     "Try it for      │
│    about the      defiance.        system          60 days. By      │
│    potty but      That's a         processes the    your             │
│    won't          signal-timing    signal — just    judgment."       │
│    actually       gap."            too late."                       │
│    use it."                                                        │
│                                                                      │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Step | What It Does | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Observation** | Name what the mom is experiencing | "He talks about the potty but won't actually use it." |
| **Validation** | Absolve guilt — it wasn't her | "That's not defiance. That's a signal-timing gap." |
| **Mechanism** | Explain WHY neurologically | "His nervous system processes the signal — just too late." |
| **Permission** | Give her an off-ramp | "Try it for 60 days. By your judgment." |

### 6.2 Level-Specific Forbidden Phrases

| Level | Forbidden Phrase | Why |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| **Level 1** | "Severely affected" | Labels the child; Sarah's son is verbal |
| **Level 1** | "Non-verbal kids" | Wrong segment; L1 parents have verbal children |
| **Level 1** | "Years of failed attempts" | Overblown for L1; she's tried 1-3 methods |
| **Level 1** | "Adult diapers" | Not her reality yet; this is L3 language |
| **Level 2** | "Easy" / "Quick" | She's been trying for years — rage triggers |
| **Level 2** | "Pre-K urgency" | L2 kids are 5-7; Pre-K pressure is L1 territory |
| **Level 2** | "Just like other kids" | Comparison wound; years of comparisons |
| **Level 2** | "We can train in X weeks" | She's heard this from every method |
| **Level 3** | ALL L1+L2 forbidden | Everything above applies |
| **Level 3** | "Don't give up!" | Toxic positivity for a resigned parent |
| **Level 3** | "Save him" | Implies he needs saving; violates V10 |
| **Level 3** | "Be diaper-free" | Her goal is dignity, not diaper-free |
| **Level 3** | "Independence!" | Empty promise word for L3 parents |
| **Level 3** | "He deserves better" | Implies his current life is deficient |
| **General** | No level labels | Unknown audience sensitivity |
| **General** | No diagnosis language | Symptom-first, not label-first |
| **General** | No fixed age | Every child develops differently |

| Approach | Forbidden | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|

### 15.3 Section Details

#### SECTION 1: Hook / Observation
- Use verbatim language from the mom's world
- Never start with the product — start with her
- For Phase 1: gentle observation ("Many parents notice...")
- For Phase 2: validation hook ("Sticker chart didn't work, did it?")
- For Phase 3: science hook ("You've tried everything. We're not going to tell you this is magic.")
- For Phase 4: dignity hook ("If you've stepped back from trying — I get it.")
- **Length:** 2-4 sentences
- **Voice Rule:** V1 (Peer-to-peer), V7 (Validate before solving)

#### SECTION 2: Validation
- Name the failed method specifically (Oh Crap, sticker charts, naked method, timers)
- Reframe the failure as neurological, not behavioral
- Never imply she could have done more
- For Level 1: light validation (1-3 methods)
- For Level 2: deep validation (3-5+ methods, name BCBA specifically)
- For Level 3: maximum validation (years of ABA, consultants, specialists)
- **Length:** 2-5 sentences
- **Voice Rule:** V7 (Always validate failed attempts before proposing mechanism)
- **Approved Phrases:** "It wasn't you. It wasn't your child. It was the method."

#### SECTION 3: Mechanism / Education
- Mechanism specificity over brand promises (V11)
- Use interoception science (Kelly Mahler, SPARK)
- Frame as "his nervous system works differently" — never as "the product fixes"
- For Phase 1: gentle education — "here's why"
- For Phase 2: validation + mechanism — "it wasn't you, here's why"
- For Phase 3: science-first with citations (Kelly Mahler, SPARK, BCBA)
- For Phase 4: gentle, no energy push
- **Length:** 3-8 sentences (varies by phase)
- **Voice Rule:** V11 (Mechanism specificity), V13 (Science-first), V10 (Autism = identity)
- **Approved Phrases:** "His nervous system processes the signal — just too late."
- "Interoception is the body's internal signal system. Some kids process those signals differently."
- "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't trained." (SPARK study)

#### SECTION 4: Testimonial / Social Proof
- Use real parent stories with specific details
- For Level 1: short-medium (3-8 sentences), "almost trained" stories
- For Level 2: long (10-15 sentences), BCBA-respectful, messy details
- For Level 3: long + messy (12-18 sentences), late-trainer stories
- For General: medium (variable), three-family format (migration anchors)
- **Length:** 3-18 sentences (varies by level)
- **Voice Rule:** V1 (Peer-to-peer)

#### SECTION 5: Product / Offer
- Frame as "adds the body-signal layer" — never as "fixes potty training"
- Present offers with pricing table

| Offer | Price | Badge | Save Tag |

| 3+3 FREE | $79.99 | BESTSELLER | Save 40% TODAY |

- Pricing display: `$34.99` (not `34.99$`), `$79.99` (not `$79,99`)
- Bundle notation: "3 + 3 FREE" with spaces around +
- "FREE" always in caps when used as offer modifier
- **Length:** 2-4 sentences + pricing table

#### SECTION 6: Guarantee / Risk Removal
- Always "60-Day Money Back" — NEVER 30 (V8)
- Frame as permission, not as a sales tactic
- For Level 1: "60 days. Pre-K in [X] months. You have time."

- For Level 3: "No pressure. If something shifts, it shifts. If not, you've done right."
- For General: "60 days to try it. By your judgment."
- **Length:** 1-3 sentences
- **Voice Rule:** V8 (60-day guarantee), V12 (Permission-not-to-buy)

```
✅ CORRECT: "He talks about the potty but won't actually use it. You're not alone in that."
❌ WRONG: "Many children struggle with potty training compliance at this age."
❌ WHY: Corporate voice, not peer voice. Clinical framing instead of naming her reality.
```

```
✅ CORRECT: "It wasn't you. It wasn't your child. It was the method."
❌ WRONG: "Don't worry, you just need to be more consistent with your approach."
```

```
❌ WRONG: "Our product fixes the potty training problem by helping children feel when they need to go."
❌ WHY: "Fixes" implies child is broken. Product-forward instead of mechanism-forward. V10 violation.
```

```
❌ WRONG: "Get your 30-day money-back guarantee today! Limited time offer!"
```

### 18.1 Checklist Usage Guide

**Before sending any email, run through all 20 points:**

1. **Read the email aloud.** Does it sound like a mom talking to another mom? If it sounds like a brand, rewrite.

2. **Scan for forbidden words.** Ctrl+F for: easy, simple, quick, guaranteed, miracle, cure, fix, solve. If any appear, remove them.

3. **Check the validation-mechanism order.** Did you validate her failed attempts BEFORE explaining how the product works? If not, swap the order.

4. **Check for NT comparisons.** Any mention of "other kids," "normal," "typically" in a comparative context? Remove.

5. **Check the guarantee.** Does it say "60-Day" and not "30-Day"? Always 60 per V8.

6. **Check the level calibration.** Is the hope/realism ratio right for this level? L1: 70/30, L2: 40/60, L3: 20/80, GF: 50/50.

7. **Check the sign-off.** Lena sign-off present? Profile line correct? P.S. within limits?

8. **Check brand constants.** Pricing correct? Footer links correct? No Trustpilot or Facebook links?
