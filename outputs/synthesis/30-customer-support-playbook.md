#### O1: Nighttime Training Confusion (1,166 mentions — #1 by volume)

**Observe:** "You're frustrated that daytime progress isn't carrying over to night. Your child does great in the day but wakes up wet every morning. You're wondering if the product works at night."

**Validate:** "Night wetting is a completely different system from daytime awareness. Daytime potty training is about body-signal learning — interoception. Nighttime dryness is hormonal — it's controlled by vasopressin, a hormone that matures on its own timeline, usually between ages 5 and 7. This is developmental, not behavioral."

**Explain:** "The Body-Signal Learning Layer works for daytime because it creates a gentle 'uh-oh' sensation when your child pees — teaching their brain to recognize the signal. Nighttime is different: they're asleep. No sensation can teach a sleeping brain. Keeping a pull-up on at night won't undo the daytime progress — they're two different systems. Many families see daytime awareness in 4-8 weeks and nighttime dryness follows months or even years later."

**Permit:** "Focus on daytime first. Nighttime will come when the vasopressin hormone matures. The product handles light-to-moderate nighttime wetting, but if your child is a heavy night wetter, use a pull-up at night and Body-Signal undies during the day. You're not failing — you're separating two completely different learning tracks."

### 7.2 When Support Cross-Sells / UpSells

**Allowed (Lena initiates):**
- Size exchange inquiry → "The 3+3 bundle covers multiple sizes if you want to future-proof"
- 1-pair buyer troubleshooting → "Most parents find 3+3 gives enough rotation for wash cycles"
- Happy customer on Day 30+ → "If you're seeing progress, the 5+5 bundle includes the e-book, targets, and progress chart"

**NOT allowed (Lena NEVER does):**
- Push upsell during return/refund (trust destroyer)
- Urgency-based upsell ("Limited time!")
- Upsell to L3 audience beyond 3+3 (Medicaid sensitivity)
- Hard-sell CTA in support context ("BUY NOW!")
- Use customer emotion as sales lever

### 8.2 Level Detection from Support Conversations

Support is a high-confidence level tagging opportunity. Listen for these markers:

**Level 1 markers:** "He gets it but doesn't get it", "He tells me AFTER he's already gone", "Some days great, some days zero", "Pre-K won't take him", "He just says no", "Oh Crap stressed us both out"

**Level 2 markers:** "We've tried everything from BCBA protocol to OT", "He holds for 4 days then accidents", "Prompt-dependent — only goes when I bring him", "Scheduled sits for 2 years, nothing", "Does great at school, not at home", "He's been in ABA for years"

**Level 3 markers:** "Never shown a single sign of feeling wet", "9 years in pull-ups, never reacted", "We did 40 hours of ABA for 2 years", "He's 9 — next size is adult diapers", "I've made peace with maybe never", "We spent $2500 on a consultant"

**General Fallback markers:** Mixed or unclear signals, "Just started looking", "Still figuring out what's going on", "Not sure if he's autistic", "Pediatrician says wait"

### 9.2 Anti-Fabrication Rules in Support

Every support reply must pass these checks:

1. **No fabricated parent quotes.** Any quote attributed to a customer must be from the approved RS PDFs, Kommentare extracts, or verified reviews. Never invent "what a parent might say."

2. **No outcome promises.** "Your child will..." is forbidden. Use "Many families see..." or "Some kids respond in..."

3. **No unverified claims.** Never claim medical/therapeutic benefits. Never say "developed with Kelly Mahler" (unverifiable partnership). Never say "#1 Rated" (no independent ranking).

4. **Self-reported data must be qualified.** "92% report clearer awareness" → "In a survey of 5,679 parents, 92% reported..." Never present as clinical data.

5. **Lena stays in profile.** She is Customer Support, Mom of two autistic sons. Never "I'm the founder." Never "I started this company."

6. **The rough edges stay rough.** Never sanitize parent language. "I sound like I'm talking to my dog and I hate it" is MORE trustworthy than polished copy.

7. **Sensitive areas get extra care.** Self-blame, Partner Conflict, Comparison Wound, Pediatrician Frustration, Financial Hardship, Child's Identity — handle each with the sensitivity mapped in Section 8 of Layer 30.

## 10. QUALITY ASSURANCE & TRAINING

### 10.1 Lena Support Persona — Role Play

When training new support staff to write as Lena:

**Voice anchors:**
- You are talking to a friend who is exhausted and overwhelmed
- You have lived this exact experience — use that
- You never sound like you're reading from a script
- You never sound like you're selling
- You end every interaction warmer than it started

**The Lena voice test:** Read your reply aloud. If it sounds like it could be from a customer service manual, rewrite it. If it sounds like a friend helping a friend, you've got it.

### 10.2 Common Support Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "Have you tried..." | She's tried everything — implies she hasn't done enough | "Some parents find that..." |
| "Don't worry" | Minimizes real struggle | "I know this is hard. You're not alone." |
| "Your child will train" | Outcome promise | "Some kids respond in..." |
| "Our product is guaranteed" | Over-promise language | "60-day money back guarantee" |
| "You should be more consistent" | Blames parent | "Consistency helps, but the signal has to exist first." |
| "Let me connect you to sales" | Breaks peer trust | Handled by Lena directly |
| "It's easy once you get the hang of it" | Forbidden word "easy" | "It's a learning curve, but many families see..." |
| "Our BCBA says..." | Lena is NOT a BCBA | "Your BCBA leads. We add alongside." |

### 10.3 Continuous Improvement

**Monthly review metrics:**
- Support tickets by objection category (most common)

## APPENDIX: QUICK-REFERENCE CARD

### The 4-Step Formula (Every Reply)
1. **Observe** — "I hear you saying..."
2. **Validate** — "That's completely normal..."
3. **Explain** — "Here's what's happening..."
4. **Permit** — "Here's what I'd do..."

### The 3 Non-Negotiables
1. **Peer, not brand** — Never sound like marketing
2. **Validate before solve** — Acknowledge before explaining
3. **Autism = identity** — Never frame child as broken

### The 5 Universal Truths in Support
1. "You haven't failed. You solved the wrong problem."
2. "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't trained. You're not behind."
3. "I have two autistic sons. I've been where you are."
4. "60 days, by your judgment. The risk is ours."
5. "Your timeline is your timeline. Late is not never."
