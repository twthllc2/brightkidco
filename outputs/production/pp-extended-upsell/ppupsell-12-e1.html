BODY-SIGNAL LEARNING

If you bought 1 pair, here's why you need 6

Most potty training advice ends at "get started." But the real signal comes from what happens after. The pattern your child builds in the first 30 days tells you everything about what comes next — and whether you're set up for it.

Whether your child has been wearing one pair or a full set for the last month, you've probably noticed something by now.

Maybe it's that wet feeling after a nap. Maybe it's the way they freeze mid-play when they feel something. Maybe it's a look — a little furrowed brow, a hand reaching down, a pause you almost missed.

These are body signals. And they're the foundation of everything we teach at BrightKidCo. Not stickers. Not charts. Just a pair of training pants that lets your child feel what's happening — and build awareness at their own pace.

Here's what we watch for in those early wins: Are they pausing? Are they reaching down? Are they telling you — even with just a sound or a gesture — that something is happening? That's the signal. That's the beginning.
