### Reframe — The Wrong Battle

Here's what I need you to understand: **every single one of those methods assumed your child CAN feel the signal.**

Oh Crap assumes it. Sticker charts assume it. Scheduled sits assume it. Every BCBA protocol that uses timer-based prompting assumes the internal "I need to go" sensation arrives on time, and that the issue is compliance, awareness, or motivation.

For 40+ methods, you've been fighting the wrong battle.

The research is settled: many autistic children have diminished body awareness (Nicholson et al. 2019, Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders). The brain doesn't receive body signals, bladder fullness, temperature, hunger, the same way neurotypical brains do. The "I need to go" message arrives. It just arrives too late.

That's not a training problem. That's a neurological signal problem. No method that assumes the signal works correctly can fix a broken signal.

**Interoception CAN be trained.** Research showed an 8-week targeted intervention produced measurable improvement in body awareness. The body-signal underwear creates the conditions for that training in everyday life.

The outer barrier contains accidents without creating "diaper mode." Your child feels the consequence of the full bladder release, feels it enough to learn from it, without the humiliation of a soaked couch or the disruption of a full clothing change every time.

### Typography Rationale

**Serif for emotional weight:** Georgia is used for headlines, subheaders, pull quotes, and the guarantee block, the sections where emotion and authority need to land. Serif faces slow the reader down naturally, signaling "this part matters." For L2's long-form science-driven copy, the serif/sans-serif shift creates a rhythm that keeps the eye moving.

**Sans-serif for clarity:** Body copy, lists, and CTA use Helvetica/Arial for maximum readability on mobile. The CTA weight (bold) at 17px ensures it stands out without shouting.

**No all-caps anywhere in the email.** L2 is in Phase 3 (Exhaustion), all-caps reads as yelling and triggers the "I'm being sold to" response. Sentence case throughout, even on the CTA button.

**Bold reading path (scan test):**

## MOTION / ANIMATION

### Approach: Zero Motion by Default

L2 parents are hyper-skeptical of marketing gimmicks. Animated GIFs, auto-playing videos, and motion effects will reduce trust. **Default state: static, no animation.**

### Conditional (GIF Replacement for Re-engagement)

If this email is sent to a segment that has opened all prior E1-E4 emails but not clicked (warm but unconverted), a single animated element can be included:

- **The Bridge SVG fills in.** The dots on the "Failed Methods" side appear one by one over 2 seconds (200ms per dot), creating a visual stacking effect. Then, after a 500ms pause, the single dot on the "Forward" side appears. Total animation: ~3 seconds. Loop: once, not infinite. Format: inline SVG with CSS keyframe animation (no GIF, reduces payload and respects reduced-motion preferences).

### Reduced Motion Preference

All animations wrapped in `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`, respect accessibility. When reduced motion is preferred, the static bridge illustration appears fully formed with no reveal animation.
