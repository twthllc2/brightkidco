## 8. ONE FIX

**Shorten the recap section by 40%.**

The recap is the conversion engine, but at ~200 words, it's dense for a mobile reader on scroll 3-4. Cut each bullet to one sentence max:

**Current:**
- The problem isn't behavior — it's neurology. His brain and bladder are sending signals on different frequencies. That gap is real, measurable, and documented across 49 studies (Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi 2025).
- The mechanism closes that gap. The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates a sustained 30-60 second "uh-oh" sensation — time his brain needs to register what's happening. Pull-ups wick that signal away completely.
- The proof speaks for itself. Research confirms interoception can be trained (Hample, Mahler & Amspacher 2020). The children who start earlier close the gap faster — Nicholson et al. (2019) found diminished body awareness is a childhood phenomenon that resolves with the right early intervention.

**Proposed:**
- **The problem:** His brain and bladder are sending signals on different frequencies. That gap is documented across 49 studies (Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi 2025).
- **The mechanism:** The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates a 30-60 second "uh-oh" sensation — the time his brain needs to register what's happening. Pull-ups wick that signal away.
- **The proof:** Interoception can be trained (Hample, Mahler & Amspacher 2020). Children who start earlier close the gap faster (Nicholson et al. 2019).

**Impact:** Saves ~100 words, reduces scroll depth by ~0.5 on mobile, keeps the strategic safety net intact while respecting Day 21 fatigue.
