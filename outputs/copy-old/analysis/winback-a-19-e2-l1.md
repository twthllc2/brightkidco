## 2. CONVERSION

**Offer:** 3+3 Bundle at $79.99, free shipping, WELCOME10 discount
- Re-frames the same product with updated context (Pre-K deadline)
- 62% savings vs singles = rational justification

**Objection Handling:** Covers the critical ones:
- "Didn't work" → normalized ("that's normal, nervous systems need more time")
- "Sat in a drawer" → destigmatized ("that's okay")
- "My child can't be trained" → neurology reframe

**Guarantee:** 60 days, restarts on re-attempt — reduces re-purchase risk for someone who already bought and didn't engage

**Friction Points:**

- No price anchor in the CTA itself — reader has to scroll back to the offer section.

**Verdict:** Solid conversion architecture. The objection handling is the strongest element. The CTA detour to stories page is a strategic choice that trades immediate conversion for trust-building — appropriate for a lapsed buyer, but will show lower attribution.

## 4. CREATIVE

**Structure:** Hook → Validation → Testimonial → Mechanism → Product → Guarantee → CTA → Sign-Off → P.S.

This is a well-tested winback arc:
1. Acknowledge the gap (90 days) without guilt
2. Peer proof (two testimonials — micro-win + Pre-K success)
3. Science reframe (interoception gap, not stubbornness)
4. Offer + guarantee
5. Soft CTA
6. P.S. re-engagement nudge

**Dual Testimonials:** Smart. First testimonial (micro-win: "He went today!") creates hope. Second testimonial (Pre-K deadline: "his teacher said he's ready") creates urgency and specific outcome. They serve different emotional functions.

**P.S. Strategy:** "My older son was 8 before we figured this out" = removes age anxiety. "60-day guarantee restarts whenever you're ready" = lowers re-entry barrier.

**Verdict:** Creative structure is excellent. The dual-testimonial approach is the standout creative choice. P.S. is doing heavy lifting for the re-engagement play.

## 6. STRATEGY

**Winback Position:** Email 2 of 3, Day 7 — the "evidence + social proof" email in a 3-touch winback sequence.

**L1 Calibration:**
- Urgency: HIGH (Pre-K deadline framing) ✓
- Hope/Realism: 70/30 ✓
- CTA Aggression: Medium ✓
- Email Length: ~450 words (medium) ✓
- Science Density: Low-Medium ✓

**Strategic Strengths:**
1. Pre-K deadline is the right urgency lever for Sarah — it's external, time-bound, and high-stakes
2. Dual testimonials (micro-win + Pre-K success) create both hope and specificity
3. "It's not stubbornness, it's neurology" reframe is the most important line in the email — it removes blame and opens receptivity
4. The guarantee restart language ("restarts whenever you're ready") is a smart re-engagement mechanic

**Strategic Risks:**
1. The email assumes the reader remembers purchasing. If they've forgotten, the opening "It's been 90 days since you first tried" could feel confusing rather than empathetic.
2. No urgency mechanism beyond the Pre-K framing — no deadline, no scarcity, no "offer expires." This is intentional (L1 = not pushy), but may under-convert on urgency-sensitive readers.
3. The CTA goes to stories, not product. For Email 2 in a winback sequence, this is defensible. But it means this email's conversion will be attributed to Email 3 (if they click stories, then buy later).
