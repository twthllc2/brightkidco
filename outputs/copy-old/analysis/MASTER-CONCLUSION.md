## What Works (Strengths)

### 1. Brand Voice is Bulletproof
Lena's voice scored 9/10 across nearly every file. The peer-parent positioning ("I have two autistic sons") is the single most differentiating asset in the entire program. No competitor owns this tone. Zero forbidden language violations after cleanup.

### 2. Emotional Strategy is Exceptional
The guilt-absolution arc ("It's not your fault. That's neurology.") consistently scored 8.5-9.5 across all flows and levels. The "Three-Family Testimonial Pattern" (Sarah/Lisa/Maria) is a creative innovation that creates universal identification without alienating any segment.

### 3. The Interception Angle is Genuinely Novel
The "signal gap" reframe — that pull-ups are actively blocking learning — is the program's intellectual centerpiece. It scores 8.6-9.0 across all segments and is the most memorable takeaway from every flow.

### 4. Level Calibration is Sophisticated
L1 (hopeful), L2 (BCBA-aware), L3 (dignity-first), GF (inclusive) — each level gets distinct creative treatments, CTA intensities, and emotional arcs. The "Whether...Or...If..." cross-level pattern is a standout innovation.

### 5. Permission-Based Framing
"No pressure. Your judgment." appears across every flow and consistently builds trust. The 60-day guarantee framed as "by your judgment" is the strongest trust-builder in the system.

## What's Broken (Weaknesses)

### 1. CTA Execution is the Systemic Failure
The #1 recurring issue across all 124 files: CTAs are buried, weak, or disconnected from the emotional momentum built by the copy.

- **Welcome Flow:** E1 asks for a sale before trust is established
- **Cart Flow:** E3 CTA is 550 words deep
- **Browse Flow:** E1 "Keep reading" CTA has no destination URL
- **Post-Purchase:** E1 cross-sell CTA creates tonal whiplash
- **Replenishment:** E1 tries to validate + explain + upsell all at once

**Pattern:** The copy builds desire brilliantly, then drops readers at the action point. Fix CTA placement across the ecosystem and the average score moves from 8.0 to 8.5+.

### 2. Mechanism Sections Are Too Dense
14 of 32 Welcome files, 8 of 38 Post-Purchase files, and 6 of 41 Lifecycle files recommend shortening the science explanation. The citations (Nicholson 2019, Hample 2020, Barmpagiannis 2025) are important for credibility but kill reading speed for tired parents.

**Fix:** Trim mechanism sections by 30-40%. Keep one key stat (49% or 19%), one citation, and the pull-up reframe. Move detailed science to a landing page.

### 3. Emails Are Too Long
Most files are 1,000-2,500 words. A real parent reading at 10pm on their phone will skim 30% and read 10%. The emotional peaks are in the first 300 words — everything after that is risk.

**Fix:** Target 400-600 words per email. The cart/checkout/browse flows (which scored highest) are the model — they're shorter, more focused, and more respectful of the reader's time.

### 4. Source Citations Leak Into Body Text
Despite cleanup, some files still show academic references that break the mom-to-mom voice. "Nicholson et al. (2019) from the Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders" is not how a tired mom talks.

**Fix:** Replace all citations with "research shows" or "studies found." Move full references to a footer link.

### 5. Product Photography is Absent
Zero emails contain real product images. Every email is text-only with colored placeholder divs. E-commerce emails live and die by product photography.

**Fix:** Add product hero shots, lifestyle images, and UGC photos to every email that mentions the product.
