# BrightKidCo — General Fallback (GF) Emotional Arc & Trust Meter

> **Reference document for Stage 1 subagents.** This is the sub-group-differentiated emotional map of every email a GF-tagged subscriber receives across the BrightKidCo lifecycle. GF is the union of five operationally distinct sub-mothers (Groups A–E per Giuliano Demands §1.2), and **the same mail produces different trust deltas in each sub-group**. This document treats the journey as five superimposed emotional curves rather than one blended line.
>
> Sources: `_notes/journey-gf-01-avatar.md` §1–10; `_notes/journey-gf-02-sequence.md` §2–7; `_notes/extracted-sections.json`; `demands/GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md` §1, §1.2, §8.5, §8.6, §13, §14 V16, §15.7.

## 0. How to Read This Document

**Five sub-mothers, five trust meters.** Trust is scored 0–10: 0–3 alienation (spam folder), 4–5 neutral, 6–7 trust building, 8–9 high trust, 10 peak trust (rare).

**Trust meter baselines at program entry** (from avatar §3):

| Sub-Group | Baseline | Why |
|---|---|---|
| A — Diagnostic Entrants (~25%) | 5.5 | Hope-heavy but unanchored; brand has done nothing to earn or lose trust. |
| B — Diagnosis-Rejecters (~15%) | 3.5 | Defensive. Trust must be earned by never labeling her child. V16 is the floor. |
| C — Sensory-Difference (~25%) | 5.0 | Neutral-positive. Reads mechanism-rich copy; autism framing is irrelevant to her. |
| D — Overwhelmed Non-Selectors (~25%) | 4.0 | Resigned but watching. Clicked past pop-up — trust is conditional. |
| E — Cart/Browse (~10%) | 3.0 | Lowest baseline. Never opted in. Brand knows almost nothing. |

A welcome mail that lands Group A at +1.5 trust may move Group B by +0.3 or −1.5 depending on whether copy passes V16.

**Per-email narrative structure:** (1) **BEFORE** — emotional state at inbox-open; (2) **HOOK** — what subject line and first 100 words do; (3) **TRUST-AFTER** — where each sub-group lands; (4) **DELTA** — net movement + sub-group vulnerability flag.

## 1. PRE-PURCHASE / WELCOME GF (Flow 01)

### E1 — Welcome + WELCOME10 Code + Lena Intro (Day 0, immediate)

**BEFORE.** Group A is at inbox-open excitement — she just opted in, she has the 10% code, the dopamine is real. Group B is at inbox-suspicion: she's reading because a discount landed but she's already scanning for "autism" or "Level." Group C is mildly curious, opens but doesn't commit. Group D is resigned — she clicked past the pop-up but gave her email, so a brand mail is expected. Group E is lowest engagement — she may not have intended to give her email at all.

**HOOK.** "Talk soon," Lena's intro with her "two sons" anchor. No level labels, no diagnosis. WELCOME10 code in the first 200 words. Three-Family Pattern referenced as a forward promise. Subject line is universal: "Welcome — here's 10% off your first order." No "Level 1 moms" framing anywhere.

**TRUST-AFTER.** Group A: 6.5 (+1.0). The code is concrete, Lena is a peer, no pressure. She feels welcomed into something. Group B: 4.0 (+0.5). Passes V16 because "two sons" is non-level-specific. She breathes. Group C: 5.5 (+0.5). Lena voice is universal. Group D: 5.0 (+1.0). She clicked past the pop-up, but this email doesn't label her — gentle landing. Group E: 4.0 (+1.0). The code reactivates her. **Net deltas:** Strong across all groups. E1 is structurally safe.

**DELTA.** A: +1.0. B: +0.5 (V16 clean). C: +0.5. D: +1.0. E: +1.0. **Vulnerability flag:** None. This is the safest mail in the entire GF sequence.

### E2 — Mechanism Reveal, "Holy Shit" Moment (Day 1)

**BEFORE.** Group A is fully engaged, hoping E1 wasn't a fluke. Group B is still guarded, scanning for diagnostic language. Group C is curious whether the mechanism will be substantive. Group D is cautiously opening — she wants validation, not cheerleading. Group E may not open at all.

**HOOK.** "Whether your child talks about the potty but doesn't produce. Or doesn't notice when they're wet. Whether they sit for 20 minutes and nothing happens, or they pee the second the pull-up comes off — the reason is the same. It's not behavioral. It's signal-timing." Interoception introduced gently. Three failed methods named without level-pinning. First migration anchor: "If your child is 5, verbal, and 'gets it but doesn't get it' — read Sarah's story."

**TRUST-AFTER.** Group A: 7.5 (+2.0). **This is her tipping-point email.** The mechanism lands, the science is real, and the symptom description matches her child. She's now emotionally invested. Group B: 4.5 (+0.5). The symptom language is exactly right — "doesn't notice when they're wet," "sits for 20 minutes" — she reads without defensiveness. If copy mentions "autistic children often can't feel body signals," B drops to 2.0 instantly. **V16 litmus test.** Group C: 7.0 (+1.5). Interoception is the right mechanism for her. She has read Mahler and now sees the brand reading her literature. Group D: 6.0 (+1.0). The three anchors (sits 20 min, pees when pull-up comes off, doesn't react to wet) cover her level without naming it. She may click the migration anchor here. Group E: 4.5 (+0.5). If she opens, the mechanism lands. If not, no delta.

**DELTA.** A: +2.0. B: +0.5 (V16-dependent; +1.5 if flawless). C: +1.5. D: +1.0. E: +0.5 (open-dependent). **Vulnerability flag:** R2 violation (fewer than 3 anchors). R1 violation ("autistic children often…"). Any autism label in body drops B to 2.0, A from 7.5 to 5.5. E2 is the most-trusted-but-most-fragile email in the welcome.

## 10. PP-LEVEL DETECTION (Flow 09, Day 14) — THE BIG LEVER

**BEFORE.** This is the single most important mail in the entire GF lifecycle. Day 14 trust is at peak (transaction-confirmed intent, mechanism understood, guarantee absorbed). For Group D (Overwhelmed Non-Selectors), this is the moment she is asked — gently — to choose her child's symptom profile. For Group A, she may have just received a diagnosis and is ready to migrate. 4. **Does Group D (Overwhelmed Non-Selectors) see a migration path without being nagged?** If no, she stays GF by inertia.
5. **Does Group E (Cart/Browse without Opt-In) find recovery framing + discount + minimal friction?** If no, she drops.
