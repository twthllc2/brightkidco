# AVATAR PROFILE — LEVEL 2: LISA

> **Reference document for Stage 1 subagents.** L2 persona in full depth for any L2-specific copy, design, or strategy. Grounded in `01-customer-avatar.md` (synthesis), `GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md` (§1, §8.5, §13, §15.7, §23), and `g3-brand-marketing-direction.md` (§2.6). Internal version: 2026-06-28.

## 0. ONE-LINE IDENTITY

Lisa is the **largest segment (~40%)**, the **most skeptical** buyer, and the **most methodically evaluated** prospect. 40+ methods tried, a BCBA on speed dial, thousands spent, every book read — she's one mechanism reframe away from converting, if we don't blow it with marketing polish first. **L2 axiom: marketing polish triggers skepticism; mechanism specificity converts her into a scientific evaluator.**

---

## 1. WHO SHE IS

**Demographics:** 31–44 (skews 33–40); 95%+ female primary caregiver; USA 65%, UK 15%, AU/NZ 15%, CA 5%; ~60–65% college-educated (many healthcare/teaching/social work — they speak the language of evidence); middle to upper-middle income, ~25% Medicaid; 3+3 bundle ($79.99) realistic entry. Employment: 45% working / 45% SAHM / ~10% reduced hours. **Family:** Married/partnered dominant; single moms ~15%. Neurotypical sibling is common — the contrast is daily, unspoken. Father involvement varies; when present he's often the more skeptical "show me the data" voice — Lisa carries the emotional load, he carries the financial scrutiny. **Never pit them against each other** (Demand V17). Extended family is mostly unhelpful: grandparents who say "be more consistent," aunts forwarding "potty train in 3 days" articles.
**Child (5–7, mixed verbal, moderate ASD):** Past the Pre-K deadline; school has adapted via IEP. Functional language for daily needs but cannot reliably report on body states — the disconnect is the L2 fingerprint. Has an IEP, likely self-contained special-ed or inclusion with 1:1 aide. Knows the *concept* of pottying, can follow visual schedules, may initiate sitting — but **cannot translate the internal signal into action**. The **"trained at school, not at home" paradox is the L2 fingerprint**: IEP + ABA prompting produce prompt-dependent success at school; at home, nothing fires without an external cue.

**Comorbidities — the "Double A cluster"** (Synthesis §1.1): SPD ubiquitous (bathroom = sensory minefield); GI issues high (129+ mentions — constipation, withholding, encopresis; "holds his poop for 4 days then has accidents in his sleep" — often the proximal reason pottying stalled); ADHD ~32.9%; Anxiety ~14.8%; speech delay milder than L3.

**Daily rhythm:** Mornings — sensory-diet routine, prompted bathroom (often unsuccessful — "sits 45 min then pees standing up"), outfit changes pre-loaded, school. Midday — Class Dojo/Seesaw reports; some days 100% prompted success, other days refusal/accidents/shutdown. Afternoons — OT, ABA in-home, speech, or sensory-friendly outing; by 3pm the kid is regulated-out. Evenings — bath, dinner (often GI-restricted), bathroom routine, pull-up, laundry. Bedtime — withholding risk peaks; Mom checks the pull-up at midnight, again at 3am. Late night — Lisa researches, 2am Google, Reddit, SPARK forum. **She has been doing this for years.**

**BCBA / OT relationship — the highest-trust authority.** The BCBA is her highest-trust authority for behavioral questions (not Google, not her pediatrician, not her mother) — 2–6 years with BCBA, 1–4 with OT. She carries **loyalty and resentment simultaneously** (loyalty because they've been there; resentment because the protocol hasn't worked). **She will not hear criticism of her BCBA** — the L2 unforgivable sin (Demand §8.5). She's probably already asked her BCBA about pull-ups, underwear, sensory tools, timers, rewards; the BCBA answered patiently, scientifically, with the protocol. Lisa still has the problem. **Strategic implication:** Give Lisa ammunition to bring back to her BCBA, not permission to override. The **bi-weekly BCBA fact sheet** (Demand §23) is the highest-leverage L2 conversion tool — it makes her a translator, not a rebel.

## 2. WHAT SHE'S TRIED

The single most important thing about Lisa: **she has tried more than you can list.** This is the core identity of L2.

### 2.1 The method inventory (≥3 must be named in every L2 email)

- **Behavioral/reward:** sticker charts, M&M rewards, prize box, "big kid" undies promise
- **Timed/scheduled:** every-30-min reminders, bathroom timer watches, hourly prompts
- **Books/social stories:** standard potty library, custom social stories, picture schedules
- **Methods/books:** *Oh Crap*, 3-day method, naked weekend, "don't ask just sit," EC adaptations
- **ABA protocols:** discrete-trial toileting, prompted sitting, reinforcement schedules, fading procedures
- **OT sensory:** sensory diet pre-bathroom, brushing, compression, weighted lap pad, foot stool
- **Specialty underwear:** multiple trainer brands, cloth trainers, every pull-up size, "feel the wet" marketing that didn't deliver
- **Environmental hacks:** musical/character potties, his own bathroom, no-flush rules, night lights, white noise, portable potties in every room
- **Consultants / dietary / school-IEP:** private potty consultants ($2,500+ invoices), OT intensives, behavioral feeding-GI crossover; Miralax, fiber gummies, hydration tracking, magnesium, probiotic trials; accommodated bathrooms, 1:1 escort, picture schedules home, parent-teacher data logs

### 2.2 How long, and what's worked

**Years.** 2–6 years of active attempts since diagnosis, often 1–3 years of typical-developmental attempts before that. By age 5–7 she's crossed from "phase" to "chronic gap."

**Partial wins:** prompted success at school (real win, but prompt-dependent); situational wins (bathroom at OT, morning if regulated, occasionally if Mom catches him mid-dance); reduced meltdown severity when sensory prep done; **"looks down at the wet spot" micro-moment** — first recognition something happened, even after.

### 2.3 The "trained at school, not at home" paradox

The most common L2 scenario (Synthesis §3.3, §7.2): Mom drops kid at school. Note: "Went 4/4 today!" Pickup — asks "Did you go potty?" Kid says "no" because in his frame he didn't *initiate* it. At home, no internal cue fires. Pull-up fills. Couch, floor, bed. Mom feels gaslit by the school report. School thinks it's solved. Mom knows it isn't. **The school success is prompt-dependent — it does not transfer home because the prompt is gone.** This paradox is the entry point for the interoception reframe. The word "failed" is wrong for everything else. **She didn't fail. The methods failed because they were solving the wrong problem.** This reframe is the entire emotional payload of the L2 brand voice.

## 3. WHERE SHE IS EMOTIONALLY AT PROGRAM ENTRY

### 3.1 Method-cycling exhaustion

Lisa is **exhausted in a way L1 and L3 are not.** L1 still has hope driving her (deadline pressure is energizing-painful). L3 has resignation, its own equilibrium. Lisa is in the middle: too tired to keep searching, too invested to give up. Physical exhaustion of caregiver, cognitive exhaustion of running a 24-hour data operation, emotional exhaustion of hoping and being disappointed on a 3–6 month cycle for years. **Copy must reduce her cognitive load, not add to it** — short sections, plain language, visual anchors, permission to skim.

### 3.2 The L2 paradox: highest motivation + highest skepticism

Lisa has spent **thousands** on consultants. Bought the potties, the underwear, the books, the timers. Returned them. Written 1-star reviews. Told her sister "don't bother." Her reflex on seeing a BrightKidCo pop-up is **transactional skepticism:** "How much is this really going to cost me?" "What promises can I not believe?" "If this were real, my BCBA would have heard of it." "How is this different from the last 10 things?" "Is this person a parent, or just a marketer?" Skepticism is the immune response of a motivated buyer who has been burned.

### 3.3 Trust asymmetry: science HIGH, marketing LOW

- **Trust in science: HIGH.** She reads research. Skims SPARK papers. Mahler on the nightstand. **This is our opening.** We can name Mahler, cite Wiggins 2022 (49%), reference SPARK, talk about interoception as a measurable construct. She will read carefully, verify, and trust when it verifies.
- **Trust in marketing: LOW.** Exclamation marks, "revolutionary," "transform your life," before/after, "join thousands," "don't miss out," countdown timers, all-caps, emojis in subjects, "amazing," "incredible," "breakthrough" — filtered as noise in 0.5 seconds. **This is why L2 cannot be sold with the L1 playbook.** L1 responds to Pre-K urgency and cheerleading; L2 reads the same email and feels patronized.

### 3.4 Hope / Realism: 40/60

Lisa enters with **40% hope, 60% realism** — still believes a solution exists (that's what makes her hyper-motivated), but burned enough that default is "show me, don't tell me."

- **40% hope:** opens welcome flow, reads mechanism, clicks through to science, considers showing BCBA
- **60% realism:** sizes up claim, scans for marketing tells, double-checks guarantee, assumes it won't work until she sees a reason

Ratio stays roughly stable until the **recognition moment** (§6), when it shifts. If post-purchase validates, hope climbs to 55–65/45–35; if post-purchase disappoints early, it can collapse to 25/75 and she joins the L3 winback.

## 4. HER TRUST THRESHOLD

### 4.1 What destroys trust instantly

Any ONE collapses the conversion:

| Trust-destroyer | Why it fails for Lisa |
|---|---|
| **No mechanism explanation** | Leading with the product = just another piece of underwear. Dismissed. |
| **"Easy," "Quick," "Simple"** | She's been trying for years. Insults her effort and intelligence. "Quick" is her most dangerous word (Demand §8.6). |
| **Fake urgency / countdown timers** | She knows urgency tactics. L2 countdowns NEVER (Demand §15.7). |
| **Aggressive BCBA criticism (any form)** | Unforgivable. She will defend her BCBA even when frustrated with the BCBA. |
| **"Just like other kids" / NT comparison** | She's accepted difference. NT comparison is a wound, not a goal. |
| **Marketing AI-isms** | "Revolutionary," "transformative," "leverage," "empower" — dialect of products she stopped trusting. |
| **L1 story comparison** | L2 stories need L2 contexts (BCBA, sensory, OT). Don't splice in L1 testimonials. |
| **"Just trust the process" / "Don't give up!"** | Patronizing cheerleading from an untrusted brand. |
| **Heavy math reframe** | "$79.99 less than two specialist copays" = salesy. Gentle in L2; forbidden for L3. |

### 4.2 What builds trust

| Trust-builder | Why it works |
|---|---|
| **Mechanism + Mahler "interoception researcher"** | "The interoceptive system senses internal body states. For autistic children, this signal often arrives late, weak, or not at all." She recognizes herself. Cite Mahler carefully (NOT "our partner"). |
| **Wiggins 2022 / SPARK (49%)** | "49% of autistic 4–5 year olds are not toilet trained. You are not behind." Her permission to stop pretending. |
| **BCBA acknowledgment — complement, never replacement** | "Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years. Body-signal learning is the missing layer their protocol can't address." **The L2 unlock sentence.** |
| **Risk-removal stack: realistic timelines + 60-day guarantee + permission-not-to-buy** | She trusts anyone who doesn't promise magic and gives her the exit. **The paradox: permission to leave makes her stay.** |
| **"Lena has two autistic sons" + verbatim parent quotes + "we don't replace pull-ups"** | Peer signal + anti-evangelism. MEDIUM sign-off variant. We complement the BCBA protocol, OT work, current pull-up routine. |

### 4.3 The 6-element L2 formula (mandatory in EVERY email)

Per Demand §23, **every L2 mail must contain all 6. Missing any one breaks conversion.** (1) REAL symptom recognition — name 3+ specific failed methods; (2) Mechanism specificity — interoception explained, not vague; (3) BCBA/OT acknowledgment — respects the 4-6 year relationship; (4) Realistic timelines — "8-12 weeks, some longer"; (5) 60-day guarantee — risk removal; (6) Permission-not-to-buy — respect over pressure. Pre-flight checklist: unchecked = DO NOT SEND.

**BCBA never rules** (Demand §23): always frame as complement, not replacement. ❌ "ABA doesn't work for potty training" → ✅ "Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years"; ❌ "Your BCBA missed this" → ✅ "ABA + Body-Signal = both pieces"; ❌ "BCBA protocols are flawed" → ✅ Frame as the missing layer the protocol can't address.

## 5. HER DECISION SPEED

### 5.1 The window: 7–21 days from signup to purchase

- **Days 0–3:** Welcome E1–E3. Reads mechanism carefully. Googles "interoception autism potty training." May forward to BCBA. No purchase yet.
- **Days 3–7:** Welcome E4–E5. L2-specific testimonials. Recognition moment may or may not land.
- **Days 7–14:** Welcome E6–E8. BCBA fact sheet. Trust deepens.
- **Days 14–21:** Decision point. Recognition + BCBA approval + guarantee → conversion. Else lingers in PP-Education or drops to winback.
- **Days 21+:** Winback re-engages; conversion drops sharply past day 30.

### 5.2 What accelerates vs what delays

**Accelerates:** recognition moment (3+ symptom matches); mechanism specificity that lands ("the signal arrives late, weak, or not at all"); L2-shaped testimonial (same age, same comorbidities, same BCBA context — not L1 "too cheerful," not L3 "too resigned"); the BCBA fact sheet (gives her language to bring to her BCBA); the 60-day guarantee surfaced at the right moment; permission-not-to-buy (counterintuitively accelerates — pressure relief lets her commit).

**Delays:** **the "one more method" trap** (Lisa is perpetually about to try one more thing — new OT rec, BCBA protocol revision, GI med adjustment; the most common delay); partner or BCBA consultation (don't presume, V17); reading the entire welcome flow before buying (rational for some); "what if it's another disappointment?" self-protection (delay is grief-management — respect it).

### 5.3 The "one more method" trap — explicit handling

Acknowledge directly once ("You might be thinking 'let me try one more thing first.' That's reasonable."), reframe the delay ("But the body-signal piece doesn't compete with what your OT is doing. It's the missing layer."), don't punish the delay (winback flows exist for this; re-engage gently after 30+ days), and never imply she's wasting time — the trap is grief.

## 6. HER HOPE/REALISM ACROSS JOURNEY STAGES

**The baseline.** Lisa opens the first email in **defensive evaluation** mode — not waiting to be persuaded; waiting to be allowed to trust. Her internal monologue: "If they say 'miracle,' I'm out. If they say 'works in 3 days,' I'm out. If they don't mention his nervous system, I'm out. If they badmouth my BCBA, I'm out." She over-weights negative evidence (one mention of "guaranteed" collapses the email), under-weights positive claims, looks for honesty about limitations, and expects condescension — anything confirming that expectation loses her.

**What converts her — recognition moment + mechanism click.** Conversion requires **two moments coinciding:** *Recognition (emotional)* — she reads the symptom inventory and counts 3+ items matching her kid (the "yes, yes, yes, exactly" reaction, the L2's mirror); and *Mechanism click (intellectual)* — she reads the interoception explanation and has the "oh, that's why" realization (reframe from "behavioral problem" to "body-signal gap"). When both fire in the same email (most often Welcome E2 or E4, sometimes PP-Education), hope/realism shifts from 40/60 to ~55/45. She's now in **scientific-evaluator mode**, not **defensive-evaluation mode.** Will read more carefully, check the science, consider showing her BCBA. The conversion event is the purchase; the **conversion shift** — skeptic to evaluator — is what the welcome flow is engineered to produce.

**What keeps her after purchase:** L2-specific testimonials in PP-Education and Replenishment (NOT L1 "potty trained in a week!" — those destroy credibility; L2 testimonials must include BCBA context, sensory language, slow progress, realistic timelines); recognition of her kid in the stories; troubleshooting that respects intelligence; permission to take it slow (L2 repurchase is driven by the kid's progress, not email cadence); Mary S. story at the right moment (Phase 2, founder-as-peer, Lena's MEDIUM or LONG sign-off).

### Hope/realism across stages (Demand §13.2)

| Stage | L2 Ratio | Tonality |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-Purchase | 40/60 | Education, mechanism depth, low frequency, no urgency |
| Conversion | 35/65 | Skeptical — guarantee + permission are the levers |
| Transactional | 40/60 | Warm realism, minimal sales talk |
| PP Early | 40/60 | Validation, "it's normal," reassurance |
| PP Mid | 35/65 | Patient, sensitive, never promise quick wins |
| Lifecycle | 35/65 | Fixed skepticism, even retained customers stay measured |

L2's hope ratio does NOT rise much over the journey — realism stays high by design; we're not converting her to an optimist but giving her a tool that works within her realistic frame.

## 7. WHAT SHE NEEDS FROM US

### 7.1 Mechanism specificity, not vague promises

Lisa doesn't respond to "helps your child learn to use the potty." She responds to **"the wetness-sensing layer triggers the interoceptive signal his nervous system can't generate on its own."** Every L2 email must explain **why** at least once. Lena voice Step 3 (Demand §7.2) is mechanism. For L2, mechanism depth **is the trust contract.** Cite (per Demand §9.5): Kelly Mahler as "interoception researcher" (NOT "our partner"); Wiggins et al. 2022 / SPARK (the 49%); Mahler's 8 interoceptive senses; "body-signal learning" instead of "training."
### 7.2 BCBA/OT acknowledgment — not replacement

**The L2 unlock sentence:** "Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years. Body-signal learning is the missing layer their protocol can't address." Every L2 email positions BrightKidCo as **complement** (not competitor) to the BCBA protocol, **tool** (not replacement) for body-signal learning, **bridge** between school-prompted success and home-internal success. The **bi-weekly BCBA fact sheet** is the highest-leverage L2 conversion asset — turns her from a buyer-overruling-her-team into a team-aligned translator.

### 7.3 Realistic timelines — 8–12 weeks, some longer

Lisa has been promised "results in days" by 40+ products. Every promise broken. She's stopped believing tight timelines. Anchor explicitly: **"8–12 weeks for most kids. Some longer."** **"First recognition can happen in days. Independent toileting often takes months."** **"The body-signal layer builds slowly. Like language. Like motor skills. No instant version."** Never promise: "In 30 days your child will…", "Quick results guaranteed," "Notice a difference in the first week," fixed timelines without the "or longer" hedge.
### 7.4 The 60-day guarantee — essential

Non-negotiable for Lisa: financial caution (absorbed $2,500+ in failed consultants), partner scrutiny ("But will it work?" "And if it doesn't?"), self-protection (cycled through hope enough times), BCBA proof-of-concept window ("I'll try it for 60 days and report back"). **Surface in every L2 email with any CTA.**

### 7.5 Permission-not-to-buy

Lisa has been sold to by ABA consulting firms, pediatric urologists pushing medication, school districts pressuring IEP compliance, family, and herself at 2am downloading a $40 app. **Allergic to pressure.** Any urgency/scarcity/"buy now" collapses trust. The Lena LONG sign-off: "If this isn't the day, that's okay. The product will be here next month. So will I." Guarantee + permission-not-to-buy create the L2 paradox: **the more we let her leave, the more she stays.**

## 8. WHAT SHE CANNOT HEAR

### 8.1 L2-specific forbidden phrases (Demand §8.5)

Any of these in L2 copy collapses trust:

| Phrase | Why forbidden for L2 |
|---|---|
| "Pre-K is coming!" / direct Pre-K urgency | School adapted via IEP. Pre-K deadline gone. L1 framing insults her. |
| "Just like other kids" / "Like neurotypical kids" | She's accepted difference. NT comparison is a wound, not an aspiration. |
| Aggressive BCBA criticism (any form) | Her BCBA is her highest-trust authority. Even soft implication that the BCBA missed something triggers defense. **Unforgivable.** |
| "We can train your child in X weeks" | Immediate skepticism. She's heard every timeline. |
| L1 story comparison ("when other kids his age…") | L2 stories need L2 contexts (BCBA, sensory, OT). |
| "Just trust the process" / "Years of regret" | Patronizing / implies she's failing. |
| "Don't give up!" / "You can do this!" | Conditional 🟡, leans 🔴. Cheerleading from untrusted brand = insulting. |
| **"Quick" / "Fast"** | **Her most dangerous word** (Demand §8.6). Years of trying. |
| "Easy" / "Simple" | Universal forbidden. Insults her effort. |
| "Magic" / "Miracle" / "Cure" / "Fix" / "Guaranteed results" | Brand-wide forbidden. Autism is identity, not disease. Use "60-day money back." |
| "Don't worry" / "We understand" (empty) | Empty empathy reads patronizing. |
| "Independence!" (without realism anchor) | Conditional 🟡. Must pair with "at his pace." |
| "Your child should…" / "Have you tried [obvious]?" / "Just be more consistent" | The three lines she's heard from mother, pediatrician, in-laws, teachers. Forbidden. |
| "The next size is adult diapers" | L3-only trigger. For L2, category error; she's still in pull-ups. |

### 8.2 The single most dangerous L2 email

> **"Easy potty training in just 3 days — even for autistic kids! Try now before this offer expires!"**

Or any equivalent — AI-ism marketing blitz, countdown timer, exclamation cascade, NT-comparison testimonial, "just like other kids" promise, BCBA-mocking tone. Lisa's immune system fires at "Easy" (universal forbidden), at "3 days" (L2 most-dangerous: "Quick"), at "autistic kids" in marketing voice, at "before this offer expires." Then she marks spam, leaves a 1-star review, tells her three Facebook groups "stay away." **The most dangerous L2 email is the most polished L1 email.** The shinier it is, the harder it collapses.

## 9. VISUAL CALIBRATION RULES (per G3 §2.6 + Demand §15.1, §15.7)

**CTA style** (Demand §15.7) — aggression LOW; muted green (same hue as #039902 but desaturated); 48px min height but **not** full-width like L1; softer edges, gentler shadow; soft-action wording ("See the science," "Read the full story," "Try it for 60 days," "Show your BCBA") — never "BUY NOW"; countdown timers **NEVER**. The L2 CTA is the visual equivalent of permission-not-to-buy.

**Spacing** — 44px section gaps (vs L1 48–64px, L3 56–72px); generous whitespace around the CTA; no clutter; cards with subtle shadows, 12–16px border-radius (Demand §15.4).

**Color energy** — backgrounds Mint (#DBFFCD) + white; **science callout boxes** in mint-bordered frames around stats, citations, mechanism explanations (the L2 visual signature — the science gets its own frame); Yellow accents (#F5C84B) sparingly for badges/trust signals; Cream (#FBF7EB) for card backgrounds; body text #1A1A1A on off-white #FAF9F7 with high contrast (L2 reads every word, often at 2am).

**Imagery** — kids aged 5–7 (L2 mom doesn't recognize a 3-year-old); sensory context visible (fidget toy, weighted lap pad, sensory-friendly bathroom, OT session); BCBA-respectful (professionals shown calm, competent — never antagonist); mixed-verbal cues (gesture, picture schedules, partial language — NOT full conversation, NOT non-verbal isolation); real parents/kids in real homes (Demand §15.6 — never stock, never cartoon illustrations); micro-moments as the L2 recognition image-language ("wet spot looked at," "Mom's hand on his shoulder during a sensory moment," "OT session calm-down"). NO: full children's faces, crying children, frustrated parent faces, bright cartoon colors, "before/after" comparisons. Image alt-text per V23: "calm/sensory-aware" for L2.

**Stat callout boxes — the L2 visual signature.** Any statistic, citation, or mechanism explanation gets a dedicated mint-bordered frame:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ★ 49% of autistic 4-5 year olds are not        │
│     toilet trained.                             │
│     Source: SPARK study, Wiggins et al. 2022    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Use for: the 49% statistic; Mahler interoception citations; BCBA fact sheet excerpts; visualized mechanism explanations; sourced testimonials; internal research summaries. **The boxed framing tells Lisa: "this is the science part. Slow down and read it."** Visual equivalent of the 6-element formula's mechanism specificity requirement.

**Photography vs illustration** — photography dominates for L2 (real kids in real bathrooms in real sensory contexts); illustration reserved for mechanism explainers; brand characters used sparingly in educational content only (no Melon Pop in CTA paths).

## 10. THE ONE THING

### 10.1 The single most important thing L2 moms need to hear

> **"Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years. Body-signal learning is the missing layer their protocol can't address. You're not failing potty training. You solved the wrong problem — and now there's a layer that addresses it."**

Contains: **Absolution** ("you're not failing") + **Mechanism** ("body-signal learning… the missing layer") + **BCBA acknowledgment** ("your BCBA has been doing the hard work") + **Complement framing** ("addresses it" — additive, not replacement) + **Wrong-problem reframe** (deepest L2 lever — Demand §6, lever 1). If L2 reads only one sentence and walks away, this is the sentence that converts her. Every other L2 sentence is in service of this one.
