## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Week 1 plateau is real. Hold the line. The brain needs static before it finds the station.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Second position in PP-Education Flow — the one-week checkpoint. Parent has been wearing the product for 7 days and is sitting here thinking, "Is anything happening?" This email must validate that nothing happening IS the expected outcome. The radio analogy (static → crackle → voice) is the structural innovation — it makes the neurological concept accessible without clinical language.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-9 (Whisper Tone) — gentle, quiet hero that matches the "nothing happened and that's okay" emotional register. No mechanism tease here — the parent already knows the mechanism from D0. This is pure reassurance.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) — long-form letter structure sustains the validation + education tone. Multiple paragraphs let the copy build from observation → validation → mechanism → tips → permission.
- SP-05 (Counter Testimonial) — three-family testimonial pattern with level-specific labels (L1/L2/L3 mom) visible in the wireframe but NOT in the email. The labels help the designer understand which testimonial maps where, but the email itself uses no labels.
- EDU-4 (Why It Works — 3 Reasons) — the radio analogy + mechanism re-explanation. Three reasons why nothing happening is expected, not failure.
- ProductShowcaseSoft — gentle product reminder, not a hard sell. The product is already in use.
- CTA-2 (Soft Link) — permission-based CTA. No purchase CTA in D7. Pure education.
- TRUST-5 (Promise Badges Strip) — lightweight trust badges below the CTA, not a full guarantee section.
- UTIL-07 (Section Header) — section dividers with descriptive headers.

**Differentiation Note:** D7 is the only email in the flow that must validate the absence of results. D0 resets expectations; D7 confirms that the absence IS the expected result. The radio analogy is unique to D7 — no other email uses this metaphor. D7 also introduces the "Three Ways to Support Week 1-2 Transition" practical tips, which is the only email in the flow that gives actionable wearing suggestions. D7 does NOT include a purchase CTA — it is pure education + reassurance.

## 5. Section Specs

### Section 1: HERO (HERO-9 — Whisper Tone)
- **Component:** HERO-9 — Whisper Tone
- **Category:** HERO
- **Copy:** "If nothing happened in week 1, that's exactly right" (headline) + "Your child's nervous system is doing exactly what it needs to right now. Here's what Week 2 looks like." (subtext)
- **Background:** #FFFFFF
- **Padding:** 34px top, 22px sides, 0 bottom
- **Typography:** H2 center, Questrial 28px/700, B.ink. Subtext: 17px, B.soft, line-height 1.7
- **Special:** No hero image above the headline — the whisper tone is text-first. Product image sits below the headline in its own block. The subject line doubles as the headline for brand consistency. The "exactly right" validation is the emotional anchor.

### Section 2: PRODUCT IMAGE (Below Hero)
- **Component:** Image block (not a named component)
- **Category:** PRODUCT
- **Image:** `/root/projects/brightkidco/raw/product-photos/flat-lay/diaper-woodland-creature-yellow.jpg`
- **Dimensions:** 280px height, centered, rounded 20px
- **Background:** #FFFFFF
- **Special:** Woodland creature pattern on cream — warm, approachable, matches the "hold the line" reassurance tone. Product is already in use; this is a gentle reminder, not a sales pitch.

### Section 3: LETTER-01 — THE OPEN (Observation)
- **Component:** LETTER-01 — Paragraph Stack
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):** "Whether your child is 3 or 10, verbal or not, diagnosed or wondering, if they've been wearing the Body-Signal underwear for a week and you're sitting here thinking, "Is anything happening?", I want you to hear this: That's exactly where you're supposed to be."
- **Background:** #FBF7F1 (B.cream), rounded 16px, padding 32px
- **Typography:** Body: Questrial 17px/1.7, B.soft. Key validation line: Questrial 28px, B.ink, 700 weight.
- **Special:** The "diagnosed or wondering" phrase covers all 5 GF sub-groups (A: no diagnosis, B: rejects labels, C: non-ASD, D: has but didn't share, E: unknown). The validation line "That's exactly where you're supposed to be" is the email's thesis statement.

### Section 4: LETTER-01 — WHY NOTHING IS NOTHING (Validation)
- **Component:** LETTER-01 — Paragraph Stack (continuation)
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):** "Week 1 isn't about signals. It isn't about progress. It's about something quieter: desensitization. Your child's nervous system has spent years (months, weeks, however long) feeling one way in their underwear. Dry. Comfortable. Familiar. Now there's a new sensation, a gentle feedback layer that creates awareness by being present. And the brain's first job isn't to act on it. It's to stop ignoring it. That takes time."
- **Background:** #FBF7F1 (B.cream), rounded 16px, padding 32px
- **Typography:** Body: Questrial 15px/1.7, B.soft. Key term: "desensitization" in B.ink. Insight: "It's to stop ignoring it" in italic. Duration: "That takes time" in 22px, B.ink, bold.
- **Special:** The desensitization reframe is the core educational payload. "Stop ignoring it" is the insight that changes the parent's mental model from "nothing is happening" to "the brain is learning to receive."

### Section 5: RECOGNITION ANCHORS (R2 — 3-5 Specific Situations)
- **Component:** Custom section — symptom-based recognition block.
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):**
  - "Your child doesn't seem to notice the underwear at all"
  - "They take it off after a few minutes"
  - "They're going about their day like nothing changed"
  - "You haven't seen a single pause, look, or signal"
  - "All of that is normal. Expected. Right on schedule."
- **Background:** #DBFFCD (Mint), rounded 16px, padding 22px
- **Typography:** Anchors: Questrial 16px/1.7, B.soft, dash "—" opener. Validation: Questrial 15px, B.ink, bold. Triple validation: "Normal. Expected. Right on schedule." in 22px, B.ink, 700.
- **Special:** 4 anchors (minimum 3 required per R2). Mint background signals "this is for you." The triple validation ("normal. expected. right on schedule.") is the emotional peak of the recognition section. Each anchor describes an absence of behavior — the parent recognizes their child by what ISN'T happening.

### Section 6: EDU-4 — WHY THE BRAIN NEEDS REPETITION (Radio Analogy)
- **Component:** EDU-4 — Why It Works (3 Reasons)
- **Category:** MECHANISM/EDUCATION
- **Copy (verbatim):** "The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates a gentle sensation lasting 30-60 seconds after every accident. But the brain doesn't know what to do with it yet — it's a new input. Week 1 is the "reception" phase, the brain learning to receive the signal before it can act on it. Think of it like a radio finding a station. First you hear static. Then a crackle. Then a voice through the noise. Week 1 is the static phase. Week 2-3 is when words start forming. The underwear is doing the teaching. Your job right now is simply to let it, consistently, daily, without pressure."
- **Background:** #FBF7F1 (B.cream), rounded 16px, padding 22px
- **Typography:** Body: Questrial 15px/1.7, B.soft. Key term: "reception phase" in B.ink. Radio analogy: italic emphasis. Permission line: Questrial 22px, B.ink, 700.
- **Special:** The radio analogy is unique to D7 — no other email uses this metaphor. It makes the neurological concept (interoceptive signal processing) accessible without clinical language. The analogy has three stages: static (Week 1), crackle (Week 2), voice (Week 3+). The permission line ("The underwear is doing the teaching") is the mechanism reassurance.

### Section 7: PRACTICAL TIPS (3 Ways to Support Week 1-2 Transition)
- **Component:** Custom section — actionable wearing suggestions.
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):**
  - "Wear for 2 hours after school or therapy, when the bladder is naturally fuller, the signal has more to work with"
  - "Try before bath time, the bathroom context helps the brain connect sensation to location"
  - "Wear during a calm activity like reading a favorite book, a quiet nervous system receives new signals more easily"
  - "Start with short intervals (30-60 minutes) if your child resists, build up tolerance gradually"
- **Background:** #FFFFFF (white), rounded 16px, padding 22px
- **Typography:** Tips: Questrial 16px/1.7, B.soft, B.green checkmarks (✓). Intro: Questrial 15px, B.soft.
- **Special:** D7 is the only email in the flow that gives actionable wearing suggestions. Tips are level-neutral — "after school or therapy" works for all levels. The tolerance-building tip (30-60 minutes) specifically serves L3. Tips are optional ("Try these if you want to help") — not directives.

### Section 8: SP-05 — THREE-FAMILY TESTIMONIALS
- **Component:** SP-05 — Counter Testimonial
- **Category:** TESTIMONIAL/SOCIAL PROOF
- **Copy (verbatim):**
  - L1 mom: "He's been wearing them all week and I haven't seen any change. But I'm not giving up yet. I keep reminding myself, the strategy doc said week 1 is just the warm-up."
  - L2 mom: "She takes the underwear off within 10 minutes. We're starting with 5-minute intervals and working up. Her OT says building tolerance is the first real skill, so that's what we're doing."
  - L3 mom: "He doesn't seem to notice the underwear at all, same as everything else. But I noticed he's keeping them on longer than day 1. Day 1 it was instant-off. Today he wore them for almost 45 minutes before I had to change him. That's something."
  - Bridge: "If any of these sound familiar, you're exactly where you should be. The timeline isn't the same for every kid. But the mechanism is."
- **Background:** #FFFFFF (white) for cards, #FBF7F1 (cream) card background
- **Card styling:** Rounded 12px, padding 22px, subtle shadow (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.06))
- **Separator:** 3px line, #DBFFCD (mint) between cards
- **Typography:** Quote: Questrial 16px/1.7, italic, B.soft, em-dash "—" opener. Level labels (L1/L2/L3): DESIGNER REFERENCE ONLY — not visible in email. Bridge: Questrial 15px, B.ink, bold.
- **Special:** The level labels in the wireframe are for the designer's reference to understand which testimonial maps where. The email itself uses NO labels. Each testimonial shows a different success metric: L1 = persistence ("not giving up"), L2 = tolerance building ("5-minute intervals"), L3 = micro-win ("45 minutes from instant-off"). The bridge line ("The timeline isn't the same for every kid. But the mechanism is.") unifies all three.

### Section 9: PERMISSION EXIT
- **Component:** Custom section — the emotional close.
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):** "If you're wondering whether this working, the honest answer is: you won't know in week 1. And that's not a flaw. That's the design. Week 2-3 is when the first signals start for most kids. A pause. A look down. A step toward the bathroom after the fact. A pull at the pants. Those are the first words in a new language the brain is learning to speak. Your job this week: put the underwear on. Let the body-signal layer do the teaching. Don't prompt. Don't pressure.
