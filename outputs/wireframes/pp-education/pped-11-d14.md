## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Two-week milestone — whether you've seen something or nothing, the signal is building. Seven recognition anchors help every parent find their child's version of progress. Graduated Three-Family testimonials prove the mechanism works across ALL levels, on different timelines.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Recognition → Equipment → Validation → Connection → Understanding → Permission. D14 is the "are we there yet?" email — it validates both seeing-something and seeing-nothing, provides concrete signals to watch for, and closes with the 60-day permission frame.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-19 (Timeline Shock) leads with the "two weeks in" milestone to create immediate temporal anchoring. LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) delivers the 7 recognition anchors and validation copy in clean, readable blocks. SP-06 (Before/After Testimonial) presents the Three-Family pattern with graduated outcomes. EDU-10 (How It Works) reinforces the mechanism explanation. CTA-6 (Social CTA) invites reply — educational, not transactional.

**Differentiation Note:** PP-Education emails use the brand teal (#2BAEB4) as primary, green (#5DD07A) as secondary accent. Neutral warmth background (#FBF7F1). CTA is reply-based (social), not product-focused. No product pricing in this email — it's a nurture touchpoint, not a sales touchpoint.

## 8. Level Markers — Email-Specific Differences

Since D14 is cross-level (R1-R6), there are no level-specific variants. All readers receive the same email. The following design choices ensure cross-level compliance:

| Marker | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| **R1 — Symptom Over Label** | Zero level labels. All signal examples use symptom descriptions. "Verbal or not" covers communication modes. |
| **R2 — 7 Recognition Anchors** | Full list spans L1 (staying dry 2 hours) through L3 (tolerating underwear longer). Every parent finds 1-2 matches. |
| **R3 — Age Ranges** | "Whether your child is 3 or 10" — inclusive range, no fixed ages. Testimonials span ages 4, 6, 9. |
| **R4 — Verbal/Non-Verbal** | "Saying 'wet' or showing you with a gesture" — paired variants in every signal example. |
| **R5 — Timeline Flexibility** | "4, 6, even 8 weeks" — ranges throughout, never fixed. "Week 2 is still early." |
| **R6 — Low-Bar Success** | "A pause, even one second" — success defined as any observable awareness, not "fully trained." |

## 10. Rendering Notes

- **Email client considerations:** Testimonial cards use table-based layout for Outlook compatibility. CTA button uses VML fallback for Outlook rendering. Gradient band falls back to solid #FBF7F1 in clients that don't support CSS gradients.
- **Mobile responsive notes:** Single-column layout. CTA button goes full-width on screens < 600px. Hero image scales to 100% width. Section padding reduces to 16px on mobile. Recognition anchor list stacks vertically with green bullet markers. Minimum 48px tap targets on all interactive elements.
- **Accessibility notes:** All images have descriptive alt text. Color contrast ratios: body text #4A6568 on #FBF7F1 passes WCAG AA (~4.8:1). CTA white on teal passes WCAG AA (~7.8:1). Pull-quote teal on white passes WCAG AA (~5.2:1). Testimonial text on white card passes WCAG AA.
- **Special instructions:** PP-Education D14 is a nurture touchpoint — NO product pricing, NO purchase CTA, NO urgency language. Reply-based CTA only. P.S. links drive engagement without sales pressure. Three-Family testimonials use graduated outcomes (L1 = full pee, L2 = pause, L3 = pause + look) to serve all levels simultaneously. Green (#5DD07A) is used for list bullets and testimonial borders — it signals growth/progress without the urgency of coral/red.
