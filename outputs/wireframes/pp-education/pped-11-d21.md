## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Three-week checkpoint — where you are depends on where your child's nervous system is. Six recognition anchors cover the full spectrum from "first pause" to "absolutely nothing." Exit options triage three paths (signs → next stage, nothing → within range, unsure → deeper guidance) that bridge to Flows 12 and 13.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Open recognition → Validation of position → Low-bar win celebration → Permission-based next step. D21 is the final PP-Education email — it transitions from pure education to soft bridges for extended engagement (Flows 12, 13, 22).

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-11 (Question Storm) leads with "3 weeks in, what are you seeing?" — a question hook that invites self-assessment. LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) delivers the observation, recognition anchors, validation, and exit options in clean blocks. SP-08 (Stacked Mini-Reviews) presents the Three-Family pattern as compact review-style cards. EDU-10 (How It Works) provides the mechanism reminder. CTA-2 (Soft Link) is the gentlest CTA — no button, just a text link for the extended upsell bridge.

**Differentiation Note:** D21 is the most spacious email in the flow — it uses more whitespace, slower pacing, and the softest CTA (text link, not button). The exit options triage is unique to this email — it creates three self-selected paths that determine the reader's next touchpoint. This is the bridge email between education and next-stage engagement.

## 10. Rendering Notes

- **Email client considerations:** Testimonial cards use table-based layout for Outlook compatibility. CTA text link renders as styled `<a>` tag — no VML needed. Gradient band falls back to solid #FBF7F1 in clients that don't support CSS gradients.
- **Mobile responsive notes:** Single-column layout. CTA text link goes full-width on screens < 600px. Hero image scales to 100% width. Section padding reduces to 16px on mobile. Exit option cards stack vertically with teal (#2BAEB4) headers. Minimum 48px tap targets on all interactive elements.
- **Accessibility notes:** All images have descriptive alt text. Color contrast ratios: body text #4A6568 on #FBF7F1 passes WCAG AA (~4.8:1). CTA link teal on white passes WCAG AA (~5.2:1). Pull-quote teal on white passes WCAG AA (~5.2:1). Testimonial text on white card passes WCAG AA.
