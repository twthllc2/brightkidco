## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "One small thing. Three days. No pressure." A gentleness-first re-engagement email that offers a single, concrete, low-bar action step — not a sales pitch, not a guilt trip, not another product push. The creative metaphor is a "soft reset": a quiet invitation to try again on the reader's own terms. The visual language mirrors the copy — spacious, warm, uncluttered. Every element says: "You're in control here."

**Emotional Arc Position:** E3 is the strongest re-engagement push in the sequence, but "strong" here means offering one specific, actionable thing — not increasing pressure. The arc: Recognition → Validation → Single Action → Permission → Quiet Door-Open. The custom illustration ("Soft Reset Still Life") provides visual warmth without product focus.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-9 (Whisper Tone): Custom illustration — "Soft Reset Still Life" (underwear draped over a small wooden chair with warm sunlight). Whisper tone hero: low visual weight, ambient warmth, no product focus.
- LETTER-03 (Letter + Pull-Quote): Multi-paragraph body with embedded pull-quote for the mechanism block. The "One thing worth trying" section gets its own bordered callout box.
- SP-04 (Trust Pill Strip): The 60-day guarantee and "door is open" messaging serve as the trust anchor.
- EDU-9 (Tip Cards): The practical suggestion ("3 consecutive hours, 3 days in a row") is framed as a bordered callout box with rose accent border.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: Implicit — the underwear is referenced but not showcased. No product photos, no price.
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): Primary button ("Try it for 3 days, no pressure") + secondary text link ("Or just tuck this away for later. No rush."). Rose accent (#D4A89B) on button.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): P.S. guarantee reminder with Lena's personal note.
- DESDEEP-14 (The Handwritten Touch): Warm, hand-lettered aesthetic throughout. The illustration and callout box border both use the same rose accent for cohesion.

**Differentiation Note:** E3 is the only email in the At-Risk sequence with a custom illustration. The "Soft Reset Still Life" provides a visual anchor that E1 (text-only) and E2 (card-based) deliberately avoid. The illustration communicates: "We made something beautiful for you to look at while you think about this." The rose accent (#D4A89B) is Lena's signature color — warm, personal, non-corporate.

- Background: #FFF9F5 (Warm Cotton — soft, non-clinical comfort)
- Alternate BG: #F5EFEA (Natural Linen — mechanism block, footer)
- Headline & Body: #3D2C2E (Deep Espresso — readable, grounded, warm-black)
- Body Accent: #5A4A4B (Warm Mocha — P.S. text, secondary links)
- Brand Rose: #D4A89B (Lena's Rose — CTA bg, border accent, divider)
- CTA Text: #FFFFFF (White on rose)
- Muted: #8C7A6B (Warm Taupe — footer, secondary CTA, small text)
- Accent Green: #8FA8A0 (Sage — plant in illustration, micro-accent)
- Divider: #E8E0D8 (Pale Linen — section dividers)
- Callout Border: #D4A89B (Rose — callout box border)
- Quote Card BG: #FBF7EB
- B.teal #2BAEB4 — not used (rose palette replaces teal)
- B.green #5DD07A — not used (sage green #8FA8A0 replaces)
- B.ink #1F2D2F — not used (body text is warmer #3D2C2E)

**Typography:**
- Headline: Helvetica Neue/Arial, 24px, Bold (700), #3D2C2E, letter-spacing -0.3px, line-height 1.3
- Body: Helvetica Neue/Arial, 18px, Normal (400), #3D2C2E, line-height 1.6
- Callout Heading: Same stack, 20px, Bold (700), #3D2C2E, line-height 1.4
- P.S.: Same stack, 15px, Italic, #5A4A4B, line-height 1.5
- CTA Button: Same stack, 16px, Bold (700), #FFFFFF, letter-spacing 0.5px
- Secondary CTA: Same stack, 14px, Normal (400), #8C7A6B, line-height 1.4
- Footer: Same stack, 11px, Normal (400), #8C7A6B, line-height 1.4

**Design Notes:**
- No custom fonts (Gmail strips @font-face), robust Helvetica Neue stack
- 18px body minimum for mobile readability
- 24px headline = visible hierarchy without shouting
- Generous line-height (1.6) creates "spacious, no-pressure" feel
- P.S. in italic signals "personal afterthought from Lena"
- CTA button has 0.5px letter-spacing for premium finish; all-caps avoided

**Border Radius:** CTA button: 25px (pill — gentle, approachable); Callout box: 8px
**Shadow:** None — warm palette provides all needed visual warmth
**Spacing:** Section gap: 40px; H-padding: 30px desktop / 20px mobile; Callout padding: 20px; Logo: 120px wide, centered, 20px top padding

## 5. Copy Blocks (Verbatim)

### Hook
"Hey, It's been a little quiet. Noticed you haven't opened our last couple of emails, and that's totally okay. Maybe the underwear is sitting in a drawer. Maybe you tried it for a few days and didn't see what you were hoping for. Maybe life got busy and this fell off the priority list. Whatever the reason, there's no judgment here."

### Validation
"I hear from parents every week who say the same thing: 'I wanted it to work right away. When it didn't, I put it aside.' That's not failure. That's being human. You've been at this for a while, you've earned the right to take a step back. Some kids need a slower ramp. Some need longer wear times before the body starts registering the signal. And some need a reset, a few days off, then a fresh start."

### Mechanism (Practical Suggestion)
"Here's one thing worth trying this week, just one: Put the underwear on for 3 consecutive hours, three days in a row. That's it. No timer sits. No pressure to use the potty. Just wear. Why 3 hours? Research on interoception suggests the body needs sustained exposure to the signal before the brain starts to register it. The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates that 30-60 second 'uh-oh' sensation after every event, but it takes repetition over hours, not minutes, for a nervous system that picks up signals slowly to begin picking it up. I've seen this small change shift things for parents who thought nothing would."

### CTA
"Try it for the next three days. See if anything shifts. If it doesn't, that's okay too. This email is the last one I'll send at this frequency. If you ever want to try again, the door is open. The 60-day guarantee doesn't expire, whenever you decide to give it a real try, it starts from that day. No pressure. Just one small thing to try. Your judgment."

### Sign-Off
"With care, Lena —— Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons. P.S. My older son was 8 before his body started connecting the dots. If this week doesn't change anything, that's not a closed door. Some nervous systems just need more time. When you're ready, we're here. 60 days, your judgment."

### Footer
"BrightKidCo · Built for the brain that learns differently. You're receiving this because you purchased from BrightKidCo and opted into email updates. Unsubscribe · Manage Preferences."

## 6. Component Briefs

### Component 1: Custom Illustration — "Soft Reset Still Life"
- **Purpose:** Visual anchor for the re-engagement email, communicating warmth without product focus
- **Style:** Flat vector with subtle texture (20% grain), warm beige background (#F5EFEA)
- **Elements:** Small wooden child's chair (warm oak #C9A88B), BKC underwear draped over backrest (white with blue-grey logo stripe), late afternoon sunlight from frame-right, single potted succulent (#8FA8A0) on floor
- **Composition:** Chair off-center left, facing right ("looking forward" energy). Underwear draped naturally, not staged. No human figures.
- **Alt-text:** "A small wooden child's chair with a pair of BrightKidCo underwear draped over the backrest, warm afternoon light falling across the scene."

### Component 2: Validation Block with Rose Border
- **Purpose:** Emotional anchor — "That's not failure. That's being human."
- **Style:** Left border: 3px solid #D4A89B (Lena's Rose), 18px text, #3D2C2E
- **Key Detail:** The rose border draws the eye to the emotional core without a headline

### Component 3: Mechanism Callout Box
- **Purpose:** Frame the practical suggestion as a contained, actionable nugget
- **Style:** 2px solid #D4A89B border, border-radius 8px, padding 20px, bg #FAF5F0

### Component 4: Rose CTA Button
- **Purpose:** Primary action — "Try it for 3 days, no pressure"

## 7. Motion / Animation

**Live email animation (GIF, <400KB):** Subtle micro-animation in the hero image area:
1. **Light ray shift (6-second loop):** Warm sunlight angle shifts 3° rotation over 3 seconds, holds 3 seconds, resets. Creates "breathing" quality — the scene feels alive.
2. **Frame rate:** 15fps (adequate for subtle ambient motion, small file size)
3. **Colour shift:** Chair wood grain highlight subtly warms (#C9A88B → #D4A89B over 3s, then back)

**Static fallback:** Outlook and Gmail display static version at frame 0 (warmest, most inviting frame). No information lost.

**Why this motion:** Serves the "time passing gently" metaphor. Not decorative, not distracting. Reinforces verbal message: "take your time, there's no rush."

## 8. Easter Egg / Hidden Detail

**The Chair's Shadow:** In the hero illustration, the shadow cast by the child's chair forms a subtle, almost-imperceptible shape — if the viewer looks closely, the shadow line traces the rough outline of a lowercase "b" and "k" (for BrightKidCo). A quiet brand signature: the brand is present, but it doesn't announce itself. Rendered in 15% opacity of the chair's color (#C9A88B at 15%).

**The "Open Door" HTML Comment:** In the email's HTML source, a hidden comment near the CTA reads: `<!-- The door doesn't lock. Come back anytime. -->` Invisible to recipients but embodies the brand's permission-not-to-buy philosophy (V12) at the code level.
