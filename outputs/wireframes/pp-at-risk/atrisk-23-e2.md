## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "Three doors, one opens." The email frames common roadblocks not as failures but as normal friction points that every parent hits. The central metaphor is practical troubleshooting — a conversation with a knowledgeable friend who has specific, actionable advice for each situation. Three diagnostic cards, each with a roadblock, explanation, and solution. No vague encouragement, no empty cheerleading. Just: here's what you're seeing, here's what it means, here's what to try.

**Emotional Arc Position:** E2 escalates from E1's pure check-in to practical value. The arc: Recognition → Validation → Diagnostic → Solution → Permission → Action. The parent sees their specific situation named, understands why it's happening, gets a concrete next step, and retains full agency over whether to act.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO: None — content IS the hero. The three troubleshooting cards are the visual anchor. No hero image needed; the numbered roadblock structure creates its own visual hierarchy.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack): Opening paragraph establishes Lena's observation voice. Transitions into the three card modules.
- SP-08 (Stacked Mini-Reviews): Three verbatim parent notes, one per roadblock. Each quote authenticates the corresponding diagnostic card.
- EDU-2 (Step Cards): Three roadblock cards — each contains a "What's actually happening" mechanism explanation and a "What to try" solution. The cards ARE the educational content.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: CTA button references "Keep working with your 3+3 Bundle" — gentle product tie-in, not a hard sell.
- CTA-5 (Product CTA): Primary button + secondary text link. Dual CTA: action-oriented button + low-commitment reply option.
- TRUST-3 (Covered Checklist Badges): 60-day guarantee referenced in P.S. with refund language.
- DESDEEP-8 (The Icon Story): Three line-art diagnostic icons (sensory tag, awareness eye, regression loop).

**Differentiation Note:** E2 is the most structured email in the At-Risk sequence. While E1 was a freeform personal letter, E2 adopts a "Troubleshooting Guide" layout with numbered diagnostic cards. The visual structure signals: "We've thought about your specific situation and have answers." This is the first email that offers concrete next steps.

## 5. Copy Blocks (Verbatim)

### Hook
"Lena here again. I hear from a lot of parents around this point in the process. The underwear arrived. Everyone had good intentions. And then, something got in the way. There are three roadblocks that come up most often. Here's what to do about each one."

### Roadblock 1 — Won't Wear
"Your child won't wear the underwear. This the most common one by far. Some kids feel the difference in texture or fit and reject it immediately. That's not defiance, it's sensory sensitivity."

**What to try:** "Start with 30-minute intervals. Put the underwear on for 30 minutes at a time during a preferred activity... Increase by 15 minutes every few days... Some parents find that washing the underwear a few times with unscented detergent softens the fabric."

**Verbatim parent note:** "He treats underwear like a diaper."

### Roadblock 2 — No Awareness
"Your child is wearing the underwear but showing no awareness signs. They're wearing it. They're having accidents. And they don't seem to notice."

**What's actually happening:** "The Body-Signal Learning Layer is working... Research shows autistic children have significantly reduced ability to sense internal body signals, including bladder fullness (Nicholson et al. 2019)."

**What to try:** "Increase wear time to 3+ hours continuously... Aim for 3-4 hours of continuous wear daily for at least 2 weeks before evaluating."

**Verbatim parent note:** "He doesn't have full bladder awareness yet and doesn't realise he needs to go until it's happening."

### Roadblock 3 — Regression
"Your child showed early progress, and now it stopped. You saw a pause, a look, maybe even a step toward the bathroom. And then, nothing."

**What's actually happening:** "This isn't regression. Progress in body-signal learning is not linear. The nervous system is recalibrating."

**What to try:** "Hold steady. Don't change anything. Don't go back to pull-ups full-time. Don't add pressure."

**Verbatim parent note:** "He paused. He felt something. After 4 years of nothing, that was everything."

### Closing
"The body-signal approach isn't an overnight solution. It's a learning process, for their nervous system and for you. Every child's timeline is different, and yours is the only one that matters. If none of these fit your situation, reply to this email. Tell me what you're seeing. I read every response."

### CTA
[Button: "Keep working with your 3+3 Bundle →"]
[Text link: "Or reply and tell me what's happening"]

### Sign-Off
"With understanding, Lena —— Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons. P.S. 60 days to try it. By your judgment. If nothing shifts in that window, full refund."

## 6. Component Briefs

### Component 1: Troubleshooting Cards (×3)
- **Purpose:** Three diagnostic cards with roadblock, explanation, and solution
- **Style:** White card on #F5F0EB bg, 6px left accent band (unique color per card), numbered heading
- **Typography:** Card heading: 18px Semi-Bold; Body: 16px Regular; "What to try:" bold accent color; Verbatim quote: 15px italic
- **Spacing:** 32px between cards, 24px internal padding
- **Key Detail:** Each card is self-contained — a parent can scan to their specific roadblock and read only that card

### Component 2: Diagnostic Icons (×3)
- **Purpose:** Visual anchors for each roadblock
- **Style:** Line-art, 2px stroke, rounded caps, 40×40px, centered above card heading
- **Icons:** (1) Clothing tag / sensory wave — #C46A4D; (2) Eye with spiral pupil — #5A7A9A; (3) Circular arrow / loop — #7A9A7A
- **Fallback:** Numbered circles (❶, ❷, ❸) in accent colors at 18px Bold

- **Purpose:** Authentic parent voices validating each roadblock
- **Style:** Italic, 15px, #666666, preceded by "Verbatim parent note:" label (13px, Bold, #888888)

- **Purpose:** Primary action (button) + low-commitment alternative (text link)

## 7. Motion / Animation

**Level 1 (Apple Mail / iOS Mail):** Subtle fade-in on the three cards at 300ms intervals as the reader scrolls. CSS: `@keyframes fadeIn { from { opacity: 0; transform: translateY(8px); } to { opacity: 1; transform: translateY(0); } }`. Progressive enhancement only.

**Level 2 (all other clients):** No CSS animation. Static layout only. The natural reading flow creates its own rhythm.

**Rules followed:** opacity + transform: translateY (safe properties), ease-out cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1). No layout-animating properties, no bounce, no auto-play GIFs.

**Scientific citation as Easter egg.** The Nicholson et al. (2019) citation in Roadblock 2 is embedded naturally in the copy, not called out or highlighted.
