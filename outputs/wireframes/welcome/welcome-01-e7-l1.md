## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Train the Signal, Not the Behavior — the invisible bridge between body and brain made visible. For L1 parents whose child is verbal, smart, and close — showing WHY the final connection hasn't been made.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Email 7 of 8. Second-to-last education push before the final decision email (E8). Sarah has been receiving emails since Day 0. She's met Lena, understood the mechanism tease, received validation, and explored the product. E7 is the deep dive: the "last 10% is the hardest" email that names her exact pain point and explains the neurological reason her child is stuck.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-10 (Bold Declaration): Opens with the bold declaration "He's been 'almost trained' for months" — directly naming Sarah's experience, matching the subject line
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack): Clean paragraph stack for the validation and mechanism sections, allowing Sarah to read at her own pace
- SP-01 (Yellow Quote Block): L1-specific parent quotes in warm yellow blocks — Sarah needs to hear from moms like her
- EDU-2 (Step Cards): BSL mechanism as a 2-panel Signal Bridge diagram (the custom contraption) — visual, not clinical
- ProductShowcaseFull: Hero product shot with the 3+3 Bundle, pricing, and green CTA button
- CTA-5 (Product CTA): Full-width green button (#4A7C59) with white text — warm, not aggressive
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): 60-day guarantee with shield icon — risk reversal without sales pressure
- UTIL-01 (Gradient Band): Sage green to warm cream gradient transitions between sections

**Differentiation Note:** L1 E7 is distinguished from GF/L2/L3 by: (1) L1-specific testimonials only (verbal child parents experiencing micro-wins), (2) Pre-K urgency framing in CTA ("Pre-K is coming"), (3) warmer color palette (sage green + cream, not teal), (4) higher hope ratio (70/30), (5) medium science density (2-3 proof points, not exhaustive), (6) sentence case throughout (no CAPS — Lena's peer voice), (7) the Signal Bridge custom contraption is a 2-panel diagram, not the 3-layer stack used in GF.
