## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** The signal is there, it's just arriving too late — a precision insight that reframes the interoception deficit from absence to timing.

**Emotional Arc Position:** This is the climax of the Welcome Flow's educational arc. E1 introduced the concept, E3 proved the science, and E7 delivers the mechanism clarity that L2 parents have been building toward. The reader transitions from "I know something is different" to "I now understand exactly why."

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-13 (Scientific Hook) establishes the mechanism-first tone. LETTER-09 (Double Column) handles the dense validation and mechanism copy with visual breathing room. EDU-8 (Mechanism Diagram) delivers the 3-layer BSL visual. SP-02 (Expert Endorsement) bridges to the mini-win testimonials. ProductShowcaseFull presents the 3+3 Bundle. CTA-5 (Product CTA) is a clear but low-pressure conversion point. TRUST-2 (Guarantee Detail Cards) provides the 60-day guarantee framing. 20.2 (Dot Pattern Divider) separates sections.

**Differentiation Note:** Unlike L1's deadline urgency or L3's spacious dignity, L2's layout is mechanism-dense with structured information blocks. The visual density is medium — enough to satisfy L2's intellectual curiosity without overwhelming. The BCBA acknowledgment section is visually separated with a subtle callout box. Green is the psychological anchor, signaling mechanism trust, not sale urgency.

### Section 3: Deep Mechanism (EDU-8 — Mechanism Diagram)
- **Component:** EDU-8 — Mechanism Diagram
- **Category:** MECHANISM/EDUCATION
- **Eyebrow:** "THE INTEROCEPTION GAP"
- **Exact copy:**
  "Let me explain what's actually happening, in plain language.
  Interoception is the 'eighth sensory system' — the sense that tells you when you're hungry, thirsty, or need the bathroom. For autistic children, this system operates on a different frequency.
  Research found that 59% of autistic children cannot reliably signal when they need to urinate. The signal is there, it's just too late or too weak for the child to act on it in time.
  Think of it like a phone call where the other person's voice cuts in and out. The Body-Signal Learning Layer is like improving the signal strength on that call. It doesn't change what the caller is saying. It makes sure the message arrives clearly and stays long enough for the brain to register it."
- **3-Layer Callout (visual diagram):**

  - **Layer 2, Smart Absorption:** Protects clothing and furniture without wicking the signal away.
  - **Layer 3, Leak-Resistant Barrier:** The outer layer provides security while the inner layers keep teaching.
- **Closing:** "The goal is to give their brain 30 to 60 seconds of repeated, reliable feedback until the interoception pathway strengthens."
