## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The last email isn't a close. It's a door held open."
**Emotional Arc Position:** Recap → Gentle invitation → Respectful off-ramp. This is the conclusion of a 21-day conversation — a respectful handoff from information to decision. The visual metaphor is a door held open: not pushing through, not closing behind you. Just standing there, letting the parent decide when to step through.
**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-3 (Permission Open) sets a gentle, non-pushy tone with natural photography. LETTER-03 (Letter + Pull-Quote) delivers the long-form recap and Lena's personal story. SP-02 (Expert Endorsement) provides BCBA-context validation and parent quotes. EDU-14 (Quick Reference Card) condenses the 3-layer mechanism into a scannable format. ProductShowcaseFull gives the 3+3 Bundle a restrained presence. CTA-4 (Gentle Invite) is a low-commitment "when you're ready" frame. TRUST-2 (Guarantee Detail Cards) presents the 60-day guarantee as a commitment, not a sales tactic. 20.2 (Dot Pattern Divider) provides section separation with warmth.
**Differentiation Note:** L2 is the only level with a Six-Check Scorecard (interactive-style checklist), BCBA-context social proof quotes, and a science-citation density that acknowledges the parent's research depth. L1 uses warmer, more playful visuals. L3 strips everything to bare minimum with maximum whitespace and zero social proof.

Pull-ups keep kids completely dry — the exact mechanism blocking progress. For a child whose brain doesn't register bladder fullness in time, the wetness signal is the ONLY feedback the nervous system can use. Pull-ups wick that feedback away entirely.

- **Layer 1**, Soft cotton inner creates a gentle "uh-oh" sensation after every accident. Just enough signal for the brain to register: something happened.

- **Layer 3**, Leak-resistant outer barrier.

The mechanism chain: Child pees → feels the gentle sensation → brain connects "I peed" → "I feel it" → "I should use the potty." Over time, awareness builds.
