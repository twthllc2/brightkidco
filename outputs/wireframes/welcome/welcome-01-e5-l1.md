## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** The method didn't fail your child. The signal did.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Fifth position in Welcome Flow — the moment L1's accumulated doubt from 3+ failed methods meets a direct, honest answer. She's skeptical not because she doesn't want to believe, but because believing and being let down hurts more than staying skeptical. E5 reframes failure as a mechanism gap, not a parenting gap.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-12 (Before Snapshot) — shows the "before" state (parent + child, warm, natural light) that L1 recognizes as her reality. No action shot — this is about processing doubt.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) — clean paragraph flow for the hook + validation + reframe. No pull-quote distraction; L1 reads straight through.
- SP-01 (Yellow Quote Block) — single L1-specific mini-win testimonial. One quote is enough for L1 (she's early in skepticism, doesn't need a wall of proof).
- EDU-4 (Myth vs Fact) — the Signal Gap Diagram: "What's happening now" vs "What BSL changes." L1 wants to understand the mechanism, not just hear about it.
- ProductShowcaseFull — hero product moment after mechanism has landed. 3-Layer System visual.
- CTA-5 (Product CTA) — full-width button with Pre-K context ("closes the gap before Pre-K").
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal) — standalone guarantee section for risk-reversal.
- UTIL-01 (Gradient Band) — section dividers.

**Differentiation Note:** L1 gets the warmest visual treatment of all levels — warmer colors, serif typography (letter-like intimacy), brighter imagery. L1's hope ratio (70/30) allows more warmth and optimism. The Signal Gap Diagram is unique to L1/L2 — L3 doesn't get a diagram (dignity-first, no mechanism pressure). L1 also gets the "3 methods" easter egg — "You've tried three methods" is calibrated specifically for L1's experience (2-4 methods typical).
