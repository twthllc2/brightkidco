## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The Feedback You Didn't Know Was Missing" — what you thought was a solution (pull-ups) is actually the barrier. For L1 (Sarah), this is framed through the lens of Pre-K readiness, not chronic severity. The visual narrative moves from "comfortably dry" → "too dry to learn" → "the signal that changes everything."

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 6 of 8 — same position as GF but with distinct emotional framing. For L1, the pull-up reframe is WEAKENED (per GIULIANO-DEMANDS §6). Instead of "pull-ups are destroying progress," it's "pull-ups were designed for a different child's nervous system." L1 parents haven't been fighting this for years — they're in Phase 1-2 (confusion/desperation). A strong reframe would feel like an attack on their choice. The gentle reframe creates curiosity, not guilt.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-19 (Timeline Shock): The Pre-K deadline IS the timeline shock. Calendar + question mark icon. "Pre-K starts in a few months" is the visual anchor — urgency within hope, not desperation.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack): Clean paragraph stack for the narrative arc. Less pull-quote density than GF (medium copy density for L1 vs. long for GF). L1 reads but needs the argument delivered efficiently.
- SP-01 (Yellow Quote Block): L1 responds to "kids like mine." Three short testimonials in yellow quote blocks — one shows the reframe ("I had no idea"), one shows the mechanism working ("he paused"), one shows the win ("he went today").
- EDU-4 (Myth vs Fact): The pull-up reframe is structured as myth-vs-fact. "Myth: Pull-ups help training. Fact: Pull-ups block the signal." This format works for L1's educated-but-not-immersed research style.
- ProductShowcaseFull: Hero product moment — E6 is the first full product reveal. Pre-K framing: "six pairs give you the learning window to close the gap before September."
- CTA-5 (Product CTA): "Claim Your 60-Day Trial, 3+3 Bundle $79.99" — action verb + benefit. Secondary: "Or start with 1 pair for $34.99" (lower commitment for Pre-K curiosity).
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Clean seal badge for the guarantee — simpler than GF's TRUST-2 detail cards. L1 needs the guarantee stated clearly, not elaborately.
- UTIL-01 (Gradient Band): Section transitions between pull-up and mechanism sections.

**Differentiation Note:** E6-L1 is visually differentiated from E6-GF by:
1. HERO-19 Timeline Shock (calendar icon) vs. HERO-7 Counter-Intuitive Truth (lifestyle image)
2. Amber `#E8923A` as accent color (revelation color) vs. coral `#D85A30` in GF
3. EDU-4 Myth vs Fact format vs. EDU-12 Comparison Matrix in GF
4. SP-01 Yellow Quote Block vs. SP-03 Stat Row in GF
5. Lighter, more playful visual density (L1 is younger audience segment)
6. TRUST-1 Seal vs. TRUST-2 Detail Cards in GF
7. Secondary CTA link ("Or start with 1 pair") — not present in GF

- Body: Georgia / system serif, 16px, Regular (400), line-height 1.6, color `#2C2C2C`

- Stat emphasis: Helvetica Neue, 18px, Bold (700), line-height 1.3, color `#1A2B3E`
- Section headers: Helvetica Neue, 14px, Bold (700), letter-spacing 1px, text-transform uppercase, color `#888888`

- Footer: Helvetica Neue, 12px, Regular (400), line-height 1.4, color `#888888`

**Design note:** Serif body (Georgia) in a promotional email is intentional — signals editorial authority, not marketing. L1 (Sarah) is educated and research-driven. Serif copy reads as "information to trust," not "sales pitch to distrust."

**Border Radius:** Cards: 20px, Buttons: 6px (rounded not pill), Images: 0px (FramedImage), Small (badges): 999px pill

**Shadow:** Primary: `6px 6px 0 #1A2B3E` (navy block shadow), Secondary: `3px 3px 0 #1A2B3E`

**Spacing:** Section gap: 30px, H-padding: 24px (mobile) / 30px (desktop), Card padding: 22px / 22px, Element gap: 14px
