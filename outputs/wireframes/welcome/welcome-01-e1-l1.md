## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "Two radios, one frequency — the moment the signals finally find each other." The visual metaphor for Welcome E1 L1 is a child standing at the center of two invisible communication lines, one from brain, one from bladder, that aren't connecting. The design expresses this through split-frequency imagery: organic, warm imagery on one side (the child, the parent, the home) and subtle signal-wave visual language on the other (connecting lines, pulse points, frequency arc motifs). The moment E1 creates is the awareness that the signals exist, they just need tuning.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 1 of 8 — Mirror confusion → Validate → Offer mechanism as explanation → Curiosity to continue. L1 Sarah is at Phase 1-2 dominant (hope intact, Pre-K driven). She's verbal, her child talks about the potty but can't follow through. The email mirrors her specific frustration: "He gets it, but he doesn't get it." Validates without patronizing, introduces interoception as the mechanism, opens curiosity for E2.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-20 (Handwritten Letter Opener): Feels personal, like a letter from Lena. L1 Sarah responds to peer-to-peer voice, not marketing. Handwritten style establishes relationship immediately.
- LETTER-02 (Handwritten Opener): Continues the letter metaphor. Plain paragraph stack for L1's medium copy density. No pull-quote (L1 prefers direct flow over decorative pauses).
- SP-01 (Yellow Quote Block): Single parent quote in a warm yellow accent block. L1 doesn't need three-family pattern (that's GF). One focused, relatable L1-specific quote about timing problems.
- EDU-7 (Did You Know): "Did you know" framing introduces interoception as new information. L1 is at the "learning about the mechanism" stage — educational but not clinical.
- ProductShowcaseMedium: Inline product card with Sarah-style context. Side-by-side image + info, clean. Not as heavy as Full (E1 L1 is still establishing the frame, not closing).
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): Permission-framed, medium aggression per L1 CTA calibration (§15.7). Full-width, Brand-Green, 52px height.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Risk elimination at the first product mention.
- UTIL-01 (Gradient Band): Warm gradient transition between sections. L1 warmer color warmth than GF.

**Differentiation Note:** L1 is visually brighter and more energetic than GF, L2, or L3. Brand-Green #039902 is at full saturation (not muted). Yellow stat accent is more saturated (mirrors 70/30 hope ratio). Spacing is tighter (40px vs 44px GF, 48px L2, 56px L3) because L1's decision timeline is shorter (2-7 days). CTA is full-width green because action is appropriate at this stage. No non-verbal imagery (L1 forbidden per §8.5). No BCBA language. No "severe" or "high support." Pre-K framing acceptable.

## 8. Rendering Notes

### Email Client Considerations
- **Gmail:** Strips CSS animations, classes. All styling must be inline. GIF animations degrade to static frames. Preheader text visible in preview. Max email size ~100KB (§15.3).
- **Outlook Desktop:** No SVG support. Gradient bands degrade to solid B.teal. Border-radius not supported on buttons (accept square corners or use VML). FramedImage border/shadow renders as solid.
- **Apple Mail:** Full CSS support. Gradients render. Best rendering environment.
- **Yahoo Mail:** Inline CSS required. Border-radius spotty on buttons.
- **Thunderbird:** Good CSS support. Gradients may not render.

### Mobile Responsive Notes
- **Breakpoint:** 480px (§15.3)
- **Max width:** 600px desktop, full-width mobile
- **CTA button:** Full-width on mobile (spans screen edges minus 30px padding), 52px height (L1 level calibration)
- **Hero image:** Full-width on mobile, maintains aspect ratio
- **Product showcase:** Side-by-side stacks to single column — image full-width above content
- **Font sizes:** Body 16px mobile (§15.5 accessibility minimum), Headlines 22px mobile
- **Spacing:** Section gap 32px mobile (from 40px desktop)
- **Quote blocks:** 100% width with reduced padding (20px vs 30px desktop)
- **Signal-wave divider:** Becomes thin horizontal rule (2px B.teal) on mobile
