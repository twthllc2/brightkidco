## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The door is open — no pressure, no deadline, just a warm, dignified off-ramp that preserves the relationship for future return." E3 is the final winback touch. It does NOT try to close. It leaves the subscriber with a warm, dignified exit that preserves the relationship for a future return.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 3 of 3 — The closing email. E3 completes the arc from recognition (E1) → reconsideration (E2) → open door (E3). The emotional register is warmth without pressure — "Not today, but maybe later, and that's okay."

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-9 (Whisper Tone): Soft, quiet opening — mirrors the "door is open" framing. No bold declarations for a winback closer.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack): Standard paragraph stack for the body-heavy letter format. The GF copy has 5 recognition anchors and multiple bridging paragraphs.
- EDU-10 (How It Works): Brief mechanism reminder — the Body-Signal Learning Layer explained in one clean block. Not a deep dive, just a reminder.
- SP-08 (Stacked Mini-Reviews): Stacked mini-review cards showing 3 parents across levels — universal proof that the mechanism works at different stages.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: Gentle product reminder — the 3+3 Bundle shown softly, no hard push.
- CTA-12 (Exit Close): Permission-framed CTA — "Check your order" not "Buy now." The door is open.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Standard guarantee seal — consistent with all Winback A emails.
- DESDEEP-4 (The Minimalist Statement): Minimalist decorative element — clean, spacious, no visual clutter for the final touch.

**Differentiation Note:** E3 GF uses HERO-9 Whisper Tone (vs. E1's HERO-9 same but different copy treatment, and E2's HERO-17 Social Proof Hero). The exit-close CTA (CTA-12) is unique to E3 — E1 uses CTA-11 Permission CTA, E2 uses CTA-11 Permission CTA. E3 is the only email with zero urgency and a "door is open" framing throughout.

## 8. Render Notes

### Dark Mode
- Background inversions: #FFFFFF → #1A1A1A, #FBF7F1 → #2A2A2A
- Text color inversions: #1F2D2F → #F0F0F0, #4A6568 → #B0B0B0, #8A9B9D → #707070
- Border adjustments: #E8E2DA → #3A3A3A
- Image handling: Images render at full opacity; no filter changes needed

### Responsive
- Breakpoint: 320px (minimum supported)
- Behavior at 320px: Heading 20px, body 15px, padding 16px left/right, images full-width
- Behavior at 420px: Default rendering — heading 26px, body 17px, padding 30px
- Behavior at 600px+: Email container max-width 600px, centered, background #FFFFFF

### Email Client Compatibility
- Outlook: Fallback font Arial for Questrial; border-radius on buttons may not render — use VML rounded buttons
- Gmail: CSS media queries supported; responsive breakpoints work
- Apple Mail: Full CSS support including border-radius, box-shadow
- Yahoo Mail: Basic rendering; test button appearance
- Samsung Mail: Test image scaling on small screens

### Special Instructions
- The "Whether...Or...If..." bridge in the hero subhead is critical — preserve verbatim
- The 5 recognition anchors in the SP-08 section use em dash bullets, not standard bullet points
- Lena's sign-off uses "With care," (not "With love," or "Best,") — per winback sign-off matrix
- P.S. section is the final trust-builder — ensure it renders with adequate spacing
- The 60-day guarantee text appears in both the body and the CTA subtext — both must be present
