## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "75 days in, the signal is wiring, even when you can't see it." The invisible progress of neural pathway-building after 75 days of consistent Body-Signal Learning Layer exposure.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Where are you at 75 days? → All outcomes are valid → The rotation is the engine → Keep going at your own pace.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-5 (Mirror Moment) mirrors the garden path visual metaphor from the copy strategy. LETTER-02 (Handwritten Opener) delivers Lena's warm, personal tone. SP-09 (UGC Photo Grid) provides the Three-Family Testimonial Pattern. EDU-3 (Interoception Explainer) covers the mechanism section. CTA-11 (Permission CTA) matches the low-pressure, no-urgency CTA approach.

**Differentiation Note:** Replenishment B E1 uses mint (#DBFFCD) hero gradient, cream (#FBF7EB) testimonial cards, and garden path illustration. Lower urgency than Welcome flows. CTA is permission-based, not action-driven.

## 8. Rendering Notes

- **Email client considerations:** Testimonial cards use table-based layout for Outlook compatibility. CTA button uses VML fallback for Outlook rendering. CSS hover on CTA only works in Apple Mail/iOS Mail.
- **Mobile responsive notes:** Single-column layout. CTA button goes full-width on screens < 600px. Hero illustration scales to 100% width. Section padding reduces to 16px on mobile. Testimonial cards stack vertically. Minimum 48px tap targets on all interactive elements.
- **Accessibility notes:** All images have descriptive alt text. Color contrast ratios: body text #4A6568 on #FAF9F7 passes WCAG AA (~5.2:1). CTA white on teal passes WCAG AA (~7.8:1). Testimonial text on cream passes WCAG AA.
- **Special instructions:** This is a lifecycle replenishment email, not promotional. No auto-playing GIFs in hero. Tone is reflective, not promotional. Motion should be minimal, tasteful, and never distracting. The winding garden path illustration is the visual anchor — it should feel like a journey, not a destination.
