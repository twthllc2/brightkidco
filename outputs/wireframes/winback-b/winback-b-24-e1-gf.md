## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The signal didn't arrive. That's not failure, it's neurology." This email is a permission-not-to-buy that also functions as a mechanism refresher and a dignity-anchored re-engagement tool. The core tension: 60 days have passed with no purchase, which could feel like "I missed my window" or "this must not be for me" or "I should have figured this out by now." The creative strategy reframes that silence as neutral biology, not guilt, not failure, not procrastination, just a nervous system that needs a different approach.

**Contraption (Design Mechanism):** "The Worry-Free Return Card" — A subtle visual device placed alongside the pricing block, a small, rounded card with soft shadow (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)), showing three icons in a horizontal row: laundry basket (Wear for 60 days), refresh arrows (Not seeing change?), speech bubble (Return. Your call.). Icon size: 36×36px circles, #F0F7EF background, #6B8E6B muted green stroke. Text below each: 13px Questrial 400, #4A4A4A. Serves as visual guarantee summary without needing a large "GUARANTEE" banner. Works cross-level because it removes risk without aggressive sales language.

**Emotional Arc Position:** This is the first re-engagement after 60 days of silence. The Welcome Flow educated, the 60-day gap gave space, and now the email gently re-opens the door. The emotional arc: Curious ("60 days. Still curious?") → Recognized (5 specific symptoms) → Validated ("That's not defiance") → Understood (mechanism explanation) → Free ("If today isn't the day, that's okay").

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-13 (Scientific Hook): Establishes the permission-first tone with the subject line echo. No urgency, no countdown.
- LETTER-09 (Double Column): Handles the dense observation anchors and validation copy with visual breathing room.
- ED8 (Mechanism Explainer): Delivers the interoception science — Kelly Mahler, 49-study meta-analysis, 19% thirst recognition.
- SP-02 (Parent Testimonial): Single GF parent story — "He knew the routine... signal was reaching his brain after he was already wet."
- ProductShowcaseFull: 3+3 Bundle with Worry-Free Return Card contraption.
- CTA-5 (Permission CTA): "See how body-signal learning works" — exploratory, not transactional.
- TRUST-2 (Guarantee Detail Cards): 60-day guarantee with permission framing.
- DESDEEP-16 (Paper Texture): Subtle decorative warmth.

**Differentiation Note:** GF serves all 5 sub-groups simultaneously. No level-specific visuals, no age-specific imagery, no diagnosis labels. The palette is muted, warm, inclusive — the LH side is "warmth," not "excitement." No bright yellows, no clock/deadline imagery, no child silhouettes. The email is text-dominant (~90:10 text-to-image ratio) for domain warming compliance and accessibility.

### Section 6: Validation — Research-Backed Reframe (Section 2 of Copy)
- **Component:** LETTER-09 continuation
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Exact Copy (VERBATIM):**
  "Research shows that children with autism have significantly reduced ability to sense internal body signals, including when they need to use the bathroom (Nicholson et al., 2019). It's not defiance. It's not stubbornness. And it's certainly not that you haven't tried hard enough.

  Nearly half of autistic 4-5 year olds aren't toilet trained, 49%, to be exact. That's compared to just 8% of typically developing children the same age (SPARK study, Wiggins et al., 2022). This isn't a parenting failure. This is a documented, measurable neurological difference.
