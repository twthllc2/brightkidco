## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The door is always open, but this my last knock." This email is the final touch in a 3-email winback sequence for a subscriber who has never purchased. The big idea is respectful closure with an open invitation, acknowledging that she's had 74 days to decide and it hasn't happened, while leaving the door open without pressure, guilt, or marketing manipulation.

For L1 (Sarah, Pre-K parent), the emotional core is: *You're running out of time before Pre-K, but I won't use that against you. If today isn't the day, that's your call. I trust you to know what's right for your child.*

**Contraption (Design Mechanism):** "The Final-Knock Visual Contraption" — A visual sign-off device shaped like a timeline arc running down the left margin of the email body. Three small dots connected by a thin line, with the final dot highlighted in Brand-Green #039902 + Brand-Yellow #F5C84B glow. Dot 1 (Day 0, You signed up) is faded gray. Dot 2 (Day 60, Winback started) is faded gray. Dot 3 (Today, My last email) is highlighted green + yellow glow. On mobile, the arc shifts to a horizontal progress bar above the hero, 3 stages, stage 3 highlighted.

**Emotional Arc Position:** E3 is the final email — "This my last email." For L1, the emotional arc is: Pre-K reality check → Permission to not have solved it → Gentle mechanism reminder → Permission-framed offer → Respectful farewell with reply invitation.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-20 (Handwritten Letter Opener): Personal, intimate. Lena's voice as peer who's been there.
- LETTER-02 (Handwritten Opener): Paragraph stack, tighter spacing for Pre-K parent speed-reading.
- EDU-1 (Definition Block): Brief mechanism reminder — Nicholson et al. 2019, signal-timing gap.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: 3+3 Bundle offer, permission-framed.
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): "Try the 3+3 Bundle, 60 days, your judgment" — larger button for L1 tap-friendliness.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Guarantee as safety net.
- DESDEEP-14 (Handwritten Touch): Warmth decorative element.

**Differentiation Note:** L1 uses warmer colors (Yellow #F5C84B prominent), tighter section spacing (40px vs 32px default), larger CTAs (52px height), shorter paragraphs (max 3 sentences), and Pre-K deadline framing in subject + body. The Final-Knock Timeline Arc contraption is unique to E3 L1.

## 8. Render Notes

### Dark Mode
- Background inversions: #FBF7F1 → #1A1A1A, #FFFFFF → #2D2D2D, #FBF7EB → #2A2A2A
- Text: B.ink → #F5F0E8, B.soft → #9CA3AF
- Yellow #F5C84B → #FFD54F (brightened for dark bg)
- Green #039902 → #4CAF50
- Timeline dots adjust opacity for dark backgrounds

### Responsive
- Breakpoint: 480px
- Timeline arc: desktop sidebar → mobile horizontal bar (16px height, above hero)
- CTA button: full-width on mobile, 52px height
- Section gap: 40px desktop, 32px mobile
- Font sizes: body 16px mobile minimum

### Email Client Compatibility
- **Gmail:** Inline CSS only. Timeline arc degrades to horizontal bar on all clients.
- **Outlook:** No border-radius on cards. Timeline dots render as squares. VML fallback for CTA button.
- **Apple Mail:** Full CSS support. Timeline arc sidebar renders. Yellow glow animation works.
- **Mobile clients:** Timeline becomes horizontal bar. All elements stack single-column.

### Accessibility Notes
- Body text minimum: 16px on mobile (WCAG AA)
- CTA minimum height: 52px (above standard 48px — L1 larger tap target)
- Color contrast: B.ink on #FFFFFF = 14.7:1. #039902 on white = 4.6:1 (WCAG AA).
- prefers-reduced-motion: Timeline pulse disabled, section fade-ins disabled

- Reply prompt: REQUIRED (§17.2). "Hit reply. You'll get me, not a bot."

- L1-specific: Shorter paragraphs (max 3 sentences), faster scan for time-pressed Pre-K parent
