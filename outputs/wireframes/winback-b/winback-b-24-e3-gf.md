## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The door doesn't close. It just stays quiet." This is the final email in a winback sequence, the last scheduled touchpoint before a subscriber moves to sunset. The big idea inverts the standard "last chance" urgency trope. Instead of scarcity or FOMO, the creative is built around absence-as-invitation: a quiet room, a door left slightly ajar, a single light source. The visual metaphor is a threshold — the subscriber is never locked out, never guilted, never rushed.

**Contraption (Design Mechanism):** "The Mini-Win Awareness Grid" — A 3×2 card grid that redefines success for the GF audience. Each card contains a flat minimal icon (universal, no human figure, no age-coded design), a short action phrase, and an on-hover micro-animation. The grid uses Mint #DBFFCD outlines, 12px border-radius, 16px internal padding. Background: White #FFFFFF. Icons: single-weight line art, 48×48px, in Brand-Green #039902. Purpose: Most email marketing defines success as "fully potty trained" — this contraption rewires the definition into observable, low-bar micro-wins that every parent can recognize regardless of their child's level.

**Emotional Arc Position:** E3 is the final email — "This my last email." The emotional arc is: Quiet recognition → Validation without diagnosis → Mechanism as permission → Redefining success → Permission-framed offer → Respectful farewell. The door metaphor runs throughout — never a sales funnel, always an open threshold.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-20 (Handwritten Letter Opener): Personal, intimate tone for final email. Lena's voice leads.
- LETTER-02 (Handwritten Opener): Text-dominant, paragraph stack for dense emotional copy.
- EDU-1 (Definition Block): Light mechanism refresher — BSL layer, interoception, without overwhelming science density.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: Minimal product framing, the offer is present but not emphasized.
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): "Try it for 60 days, your judgment" — permission-framed, not transactional.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Guarantee as trust anchor, not sales tool.
- DESDEEP-14 (Handwritten Touch): Subtle warmth decorative element.

**Differentiation Note:** GF serves all 5 sub-groups simultaneously. No level-specific visuals, no age-specific imagery, no diagnosis labels. The palette is muted, warm, inclusive. Text-dominant (~90:10 text-to-image ratio) for domain warming compliance and accessibility. E3 GF is the most emotionally weighted of the three winback emails — it carries the weight of "final contact."
