## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The piece your therapy is missing" — framing BSL as the sensory layer that BCBA protocols can't provide. The email is structured as a mechanism refresher with peer validation. L2 has invested heavily in therapy ($50k+ over years), and the email acknowledges that investment while introducing the one component that was never addressed: interoception.

**Contraption (Design Mechanism):** "The Missing Piece Card" — A visual metaphor card showing a puzzle with one piece highlighted in emerald. The card sits alongside the mechanism section, visually representing "the piece your therapy is missing." Three labeled sections: Behavioral Foundation (BCBA), Sensory Processing (OT), Body-Signal Learning (BSL — the missing piece). Illustration style: Clean line-art, no gradients, muted palette.

**Emotional Arc Position:** This is the first re-engagement after 60 days. L2's primary lever is mechanism specificity — she needs to understand WHY before she'll act. The email re-establishes trust through fresh science + peer validation, then leaves the door open. The emotional arc: Exhausted Recognition → Validated Heaviness → Mechanism Clarity → Cautious Micro-Hope → Permission Off-Ramp.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-13 (Scientific Hook): "The piece your therapy is missing" — mechanism tease, not deadline.
- LETTER-09 (Double Column): Dense validation + mechanism with BCBA acknowledgment callout.
- ED8 (Mechanism Diagram): Deep mechanism — Kelly Mahler, Nicholson, Iqbal, 3-layer BSL visual.
- SP-02 (Expert Endorsement): Single L2 parent story — "He initiated. Once. After 4 years."
- ProductShowcaseFull: 3+3 Bundle with BCBA-complement framing.
- CTA-5 (Permission CTA): "When you're ready" — lowest aggression.
- TRUST-2 (Guarantee Detail Cards): 60-day guarantee with realistic timelines.
- DESDEEP-16 (Paper Texture): Subtle decorative warmth.

**Differentiation Note:** L2's layout is mechanism-dense with structured information blocks. The BCBA acknowledgment section is visually separated with a subtle callout box. Muted tones — warm sage green, soft sand, deep charcoal. No bright accents. Science callout boxes make the mechanism scannable. The visual density is medium — enough to satisfy L2's intellectual curiosity without overwhelming.

### Section 7: Mechanism — Interoception Gap Deep (Section 3 of Copy)
- **Component:** ED8 — Mechanism Explainer (Deep)

- **Eyebrow:** "THE INTEROCEPTION GAP"
- **Exact Copy (VERBATIM):**
  "Here's what the research shows: autistic children process internal body signals differently. Interoception, the 'eighth sensory system' as researcher Kelly Mahler calls it, is the sense that tells us when we're hungry, thirsty, or need the bathroom. For many autistic children, this signal is delayed or absent.

  Nicholson et al. (2019) demonstrated that children with autism show significantly diminished body awareness, the brain doesn't register the bladder-fullness signal until it's too late. The message travels, but it arrives milliseconds after the event.

  This isn't stubbornness. It's neurology.

  Iqbal (2022) found that only 19% of autistic children could recognize when they were thirsty. If thirst doesn't register, how can we expect bladder fullness to?"
