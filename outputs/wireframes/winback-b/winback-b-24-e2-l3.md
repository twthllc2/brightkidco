## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The quiet path." Not a re-engagement push. A gentle arrival, returning to Maria's inbox not with urgency or new claims, but with the quietest form of communication: a shared truth the science has recently confirmed.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Observation → Deep Validation → New Science → Updated Offer → Permission. E2 for L3 is the shortest, quietest winback email — every word earns its place. Progress for L3 children doesn't look like a breakthrough. It looks like a pause.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-9 Whisper Tone for maximum dignity, LETTER-02 Handwritten Opener for warmth, SP-06 Before/After Testimonial for peer story, EDU-11 Science Citation for settled science, CTA-4 Gentle Invite for permission-framed CTA, TRUST-1 60-Day Seal for guarantee, DESDEEP-14 The Handwritten Touch for visual intimacy.

**Differentiation Note:** Uses HERO-9 Whisper Tone (same as E1, but E2 adds The Ripple Line contraption). Uses EDU-11 Science Citation instead of EDU-1 Definition Block (E1). Single-column, maximum breathing room, no product photo (Ripple Line only). CTA is text link "When you decide, it's here." — zero aggression.

### Block: New Science Citation
In 2025, Frontiers in Psychiatry published a full meta-analysis on interoception and autism. That's the gold standard of scientific evidence, a study of studies, with findings that carry more weight than any single paper.

What they found: autistic individuals process internal body signals, the feeling of hunger, thirst, needing the bathroom, measurably differently. The signal travels a different path. It's not absent. It's delayed. It arrives quietly, or late, or in a language the brain hasn't learned to translate yet.

What the same body of research also found: the window for building this awareness is open through childhood. The brain can learn to recognize these signals. The earlier the input, the more the pathway strengthens.

That's not a promise. That's what the science says.

## 8. Render Notes

### Dark Mode
- Background inversions: #FBF7EB → #1A1A1A
- Text color inversions: #2D2D2D → #E5E5E5, #9AA8A7 → #B0B8B7
- Mint #DBFFCD → #1A2A1A
- Image handling: images remain unchanged, subtle border in dark mode
- Ripple Line: opacity adjustments for dark background visibility

### Responsive
- Breakpoint: 320px (minimum supported)
- Behavior at 320px: spacing reduces by 25% (48px→36px, 40px→30px, 32px→24px), CTA stays 16px minimum, left border reduces 2px→1px under 360px
- Behavior at 420px: default rendering
- Behavior at 600px+: max-width 600px centered, content remains held

### Email Client Compatibility
- Outlook: MSO conditional comments for background colors
- Gmail: inline styles for all spacing
- Apple Mail: Ripple Line SVG renders crisply
- Mobile clients: stack all elements single-column at 320px
- All clients: no CSS animations in static fallback

### Special Instructions
- Ripple Line animation (AMP only): 6s loop — State A holds 2s, transitions 1.5s, State B holds 1.5s, reverts 0.5s, rest 0.5s
- Static fallback: both states shown side-by-side as described
- No bounce easing — use ease-in-out with gentle curve
- No scale transforms on Ripple Line
- No animation on text, CTA, or product image
- No bold weight anywhere — bold reads as insistence, Maria rejects it
- No italic — entire email is already at whisper volume
- No ALL CAPS — reads as urgency
- Maximum body width: 520px in 600px container
- Lines never exceed 70-75 characters
- Letter-spacing increases slightly (0.02em) in P.S. block
- No hero image, no product photo unless explicitly approved
- CTA text link: Dark Charcoal with Brand-Green underline, no button background
- Only place Brand-Green at full saturation: "Lena" signature mark
- Easter egg in footer: 30% opacity, no clickable link, 10px gray
- No math reframe ("less than a coffee a day" forbidden for L3)
- No shipping cost mentions
- Lena sign-off and P.S. must not split across mobile screen boundary
