## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The Missing Piece." A jigsaw puzzle with one deliberately missing piece — the body signal. The mom has assembled every piece (methods, schedules, charts, BCBA protocols) but the center piece is absent. The visual metaphor mirrors the emotional journey: from assembled confusion (all methods tried) to recognition (the signal was never there).

**Emotional Arc Position:** Objection D1 resolution — the most universal objection across ALL levels. Opens by naming exhaustion ("You've tried everything"), validates without minimizing, reframes as neurological gap (not parenting failure), then offers permission-based relief. This is the entry point to the FAQ library.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-11 (Question Storm): Opens with multiple recognition anchors — questions the reader has asked herself. Storm of self-doubt that names the objection directly.
- LETTER-03 (Letter + Pull-Quote): Long-form letter supports the ~450-word copy density. Pull-quote isolates the key reframe: "You didn't fail. You solved the wrong problem."
- SP-08 (Stacked Mini-Reviews): Three-family quote pattern bridges all cross-level sub-groups (A-E). Multiple perspectives show "you're not alone."
- EDU-4 (Myth vs Fact): Dismantles the "I've tried everything" myth — lists failed methods and explains why they all share the same assumption (working signal).
- ProductShowcaseMedium: Medium product presentation — the mechanism has been explained; product is the natural next step, not the opener.
- CTA-11 (Permission CTA): Permission-framed, not command. "See if the body-signal approach fits your child" — zero urgency, maximum permission.
- TRUST-5 (Promise Badges Strip): Multiple trust badges (60-day guarantee, full refund, peer support) in a horizontal strip.
- DESDEEP-3 (The Card Cascade): Decorative card cascade with puzzle-piece visual metaphor — scattered pieces converging toward the missing center.
