## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** The final FAQ email closes the series by addressing three seemingly unrelated objections — travel anxiety, poop-specific struggles, and communication barriers — and revealing they all share one root cause: the interoception gap. The email is structured as three distinct answers under a single unifying insight, demonstrating the mechanism's breadth without over-promising.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 6 of 8 — Series Close. The reader has worked through mechanism (E1-E2), fear (E3), external blockers (E4), and institutional systems (E5). E6 addresses the remaining practical and developmental objections that keep parents from moving forward. The emotional arc moves from curiosity ("three questions I hear every week") through practical relief (travel strategies), understanding (poop is harder), and hope (no words needed) to a permission close ("When you're ready, it's here").

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-3 (Permission Open): Opens with three questions as direct quotes — immediate recognition anchoring.
- LETTER-03 (Letter + Pull-Quote): Three objection sections (Travel, Poop, Communication) each with validation + mechanism + permission.
- EDU-10 (Mechanism Callout): Unifying reframe — interoception gap connects all three objections. 3-layer BSL system description.
- ProductShowcaseFull: 3-layer Body-Signal Learning Layer description (cotton inner, smart absorption, outer barrier) — mechanism-first product introduction.
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): "When you're ready, it's here." — zero urgency, zero pressure.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): 60-day guarantee with low-bar success metrics.
- UTIL-05 (LONG Sign-Off): E6 is the final FAQ email — LONG variant per §9.5 of Brand Voice Reference.
- 20.1 (Wave Divider): Signal-wave dividers between sections.

**Differentiation Note:** This is the ONLY FAQ email that addresses practical (travel), biological (poop), and developmental (communication) objections in one letter. The communication barrier section is the most L3-heavy content in the entire FAQ series — it centers dignity-first language and the "no words required" mechanism. LONG sign-off is exclusive to E6 — personal stories from both sons, "With care" variant.
