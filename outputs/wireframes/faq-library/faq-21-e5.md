## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** A letter that names the three systems every parent navigates — BCBA, school/daycare, and medical/GI — and shows that none of them address the sensory gap. The missing piece is the same in every case: the body signal. The email uses three-family testimonial stories (Sarah, Lisa, Maria) to show how the mechanism works alongside existing systems, not instead of them.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 5 of 8 — System Navigation. The reader has worked through personal objections (E4: partner, cost, brand). E5 addresses the institutional barriers: the BCBA who has a protocol, the school with a pull-up policy, and the GI doctor managing withholding. The emotional arc moves from overwhelm ("none of them are getting your child out of pull-ups") through mechanism clarity ("the interoception gap is the common thread") to permission ("Your BCBA leads. We add alongside.").

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-3 (Permission Open): Opens with the three systems named — BCBA, school, GI. Permission framing: "Here's why. And what to do about it."
- LETTER-03 (Letter + Pull-Quote): Three gatekeeper objection sections, each with validation + reframe. Pull-quotes isolate the key insight for each system.
- SP-05 (Three-Family Testimonials): Sarah (L1), Lisa (L2), Maria (L3) — peer stories covering all levels. Three separate testimonial cards with green left border.
- EDU-10 (Mechanism Callout): Interoception gap explanation, Nicholson 2019 citation, positioned after the three systems section.
- ProductShowcaseFull: 3+3 Bundle presentation with bridge strategy (underwear under pull-up).
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): "See the bridge strategy for your child's situation" — permission-framed.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): 60-day guarantee with low-bar success metrics.
- 20.1 (Wave Divider): Signal-wave dividers between major sections.

**Differentiation Note:** This is the ONLY FAQ email that directly addresses professional systems (BCBA, school, medical). The "alongside" positioning is critical — never "instead of." Three-family stories mirror the three gatekeeping systems: Sarah = BCBA success, Lisa = school bridge, Maria = medical alongside. L3 sensitivity: Maria's story centers dignity-first, not training outcomes.
