## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "150 days in, your first 10 pairs have earned their retirement." Celebration of the distance traveled, anchored in the Three-Family Testimonial Pattern so every level sees themselves.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Hook → Validation → Three-Family stories → Options (refresh, size up, gift, continue) → CTA → Lena sign-off.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-3 (Permission Open) matches the warm, non-urgent opening. LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) delivers the hook, validation, and options cleanly. SP-13 (Review of the Week) provides the Three-Family Pattern. EDU-10 (How It Works) reinforces the 3-layer mechanism. CTA-11 (Permission CTA) with dual CTA (reorder + gift).

**Differentiation Note:** Georgia serif throughout — warm, editorial, trustworthy, a letter from a person, not a brand. Three accent colors on testimonial cards (green, amber, clay) create subconscious emotional cues. "Pause dot" contraption between testimonials and options creates spatial permission moment.

## 8. Rendering Notes

- **Email client considerations:** Pause dots use CSS pseudo-elements — falls back to plain text "..." in Outlook. Numbered option blocks use table-based layout for Outlook compatibility. CTA button uses VML fallback. Georgia serif renders consistently across all major email clients.
- **Mobile responsive notes:** Single-column layout. CTA button goes full-width on < 600px. Option blocks stack vertically. Section padding reduces to 16px on mobile. Testimonial cards stack vertically. Minimum 48px tap targets.
- **Accessibility notes:** All images have descriptive alt text. Color contrast: body text #2D2A24 on #F9F6F2 passes WCAG AA. CTA white on green passes WCAG AA. Muted text #8A8278 on #F9F6F2 passes WCAG AA for large text.
- **Special instructions:** Georgia serif throughout — editorial, trustworthy, a letter from a person. Three accent colors on testimonial cards are NOT level labels — they're emotional color cues that work subconsciously. "Pause dot" contraption creates spatial permission moment between testimonials and options. Gift angle as secondary CTA, not primary — reorder is primary, gifting is optional. Double em-dash after "Lena" (——) is a brand voice detail for LONG sign-off variant.
