## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Progress at week 4 looks like small moments, not big milestones.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Second email in L1 5-email sequence (E2 of 5). The parent has been wearing the product for ~4 weeks. She's past the excitement, past the initial "doing something" feeling, and now deep in the "nothing is changing" window. This email must catch her at the exact moment she's considering giving up and reframe the plateau as the most important phase. The Pre-K deadline is the motivational frame — not to create panic, but to anchor her effort in a concrete future event she cares about.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO (Personal Greeting + Hook) — direct address by first name ("Sarah"), names the month investment immediately. L1 responds to personal, direct framing.
- TEXT/LETTER — Micro-signs reframe (progress list) — redefines success from "potty trips" to observable body-signal moments. The list format is scannable for L1's faster reading pace.
- MECHANISM — Documentation rationale + science anchor — interoception research (Nicholson et al. 2019) grounds the tracking approach in credibility. Medium-low density for L1.
- UTILITY — Tracking Checklist TABLE — tangible tool she can print/photograph. Alternate row shading (#FBF7EB cream) for visual rhythm. This is the actionable center of the email.
- TEXT/LETTER — Normalization + Pre-K Connection — names the "some days zero, some days 3" pattern explicitly. Pre-K deadline inserted as motivation without panic.
- CTA + OFFER — Product Offer Block (3+3 Bundle, $79.99) + CTA button — soft placement after education content. The P.P.S. product mention contextualizes the bundle within rotation necessity.
- SIGNOFF + FOOTER — Lena Short-Medium sign-off with personal reply invitation + P.S. (plateau normalization) + P.P.S. (bundle mention) + footer with guarantee.

**Differentiation Note:** This is the only email in the L1 E2 sequence that must simultaneously validate the "nothing is happening" feeling AND prevent abandonment by reframing what progress looks like. D0 set expectations; E2 catches her at the plateau. The checklist table is the structural innovation — it converts anxious observation into structured documentation, giving the parent something concrete to DO rather than just feel. The Pre-K deadline frame distinguishes this from generic "keep going" messaging.

## 5. Technique Specs

### Section 1: HERO — Personal Greeting + Hook
- **Component:** HERO (custom personal greeting)
- **Category:** HERO
- **Copy (verbatim):** "Sarah,\n\nYou've been at this for about a month now. If you're wondering whether anything is actually changing, let me show you what to look for.\n\nBecause here's the thing most parents miss: the first signs of body-signal awareness aren't trips to the potty. They're smaller. Subtler. And if you don't know what you're watching for, they're easy to miss entirely."
- **Background:** #FFFFFF
- **Padding:** 34px top, 22px sides, 0 bottom
- **Typography:** Name: Questrial 22px/600, B.ink. Subtext: Questrial 17px/700, B.ink, line-height 1.6. Body: 16px/400, B.soft, line-height 1.6
- **Badge:** Warm #FFF8E1 capsule, "PRE-K IS [X MONTHS] AWAY" in 12px Questrial 700, B.teal text, rounded 999px, padding 6px 16px
- **Special:** Direct first-name address creates immediate personal recognition. The badge establishes the Pre-K deadline frame from the very first visual element. Bracket notation "[X months]" creates a soft deadline — never bold, red, or exclamation. The hook names the month investment, validating her effort before reframing progress.

### Section 2: TEXT/LETTER — What "Progress" Actually Looks Like
- **Component:** TEXT/LETTER — Paragraph Stack + Card
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):** "What \"progress\" actually looks like at week 4\n\nYour child's nervous system is building new pathways. That takes time. But the evidence appears in small moments:\n\n✓ A pause mid-play. Just a second. He stops, looks down, then carries on. That pause is his brain registering a sensation it didn't notice before.\n✓ A hand to the waistband. Not a grab, just a touch. A subconscious check-in with his body.\n✓ A change in posture. Shifting weight. Squatting. Leg-crossing. These are his body's early attempts to respond to a signal it's only beginning to recognize.\n✓ A glance toward the bathroom. He doesn't walk there. He doesn't say anything. But his eyes go there. Something is connecting.\n\nNone of these look like \"potty training success.\" But in the context of body signal development? Every single one is a milestone."
- **Background:** Card: #FBF7F1 (B.cream), rounded 20px, padding 22px
- **Typography:** Heading: Questrial 28px/700, B.ink (bold only, no full caps). Body: Questrial 16px/400, B.soft, line-height 1.6. List items: B.green (✓) + 16px B.soft. Key reframe line: 16px, B.ink, 600 weight
- **Special:** The four micro-signs are ordered from most subtle (pause) to most explicit (glance toward bathroom). Each maps to a different stage of body-signal development without labeling stages. The card format makes the list scannable. The closing reframe ("every single one is a milestone") is the emotional anchor — it redefines success in micro-terms.

Each time your child's brain receives the sustained 30–60 second \"uh-oh\" sensation from the Body-Signal Learning Layer, it strengthens that pathway. But the progress is incremental — too slow to notice day-to-day. That's why tracking matters."
- **Background:** #FAF9F7 (overall bg), no card wrapper
- **Typography:** Heading: Questrial 28px/700, B.ink (bold only, no full caps). Body: Questrial 16px/400, B.soft, line-height 1.6. "uh-oh" in italic
- **Special:** Medium-low science density for L1. The interoception research reference is present but not overwhelming — one paragraph, not a deep dive. The "30–60 second" mechanism claim anchors the tracking approach in credibility. This section bridges the micro-signs list (what to look for) with the checklist table (how to document it).

### Section 5: TEXT/LETTER — What's Normal + Pre-K Connection
- **Component:** TEXT/LETTER — Normalization Card + Pre-K Anchor
- **Category:** TEXT/LETTER
- **Copy (verbatim):**
  - Normalization: "What's normal at week 4:\n✓ Some days you see 3 signs. Some days zero. Both normal.\n✓ He might go days without any visible awareness, then suddenly show 2 signs in one afternoon.
