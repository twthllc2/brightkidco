## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The signal is real, your child just can't feel it yet." This email reorients how the parent thinks about potty training — from "my child won't" to "my child can't feel the signal." The visual metaphor is a broken or invisible communication line between body and brain. For GF, the email must be universal, inclusive, and symptom-focused — no autism labels, no level references, no specific age ranges.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Position 1 of the extended education sequence — the opening email (D22+). Mirror confusion → Validate → Offer interoception as explanation → Curiosity to continue. The reader arrives after the core welcome flow; this email deepens the mechanism education.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-13 (Scientific Hook): Opens with a science-anchored symptom observation, appropriate for the extended education context where the reader already has baseline familiarity.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack): Long-form letter format supports the 200-500 word copy density for GF's broad audience.
- SP-06 (Before/After Testimonial): Single peer testimonial showing the interoception mechanism in action.
- EDU-8 (Mechanism Diagram): Visual interoception pathway explanation with the 59% urination stat from Iqbal 2022.
- ProductShowcaseMedium: Medium product presentation — the reader has already seen the product; this reinforces the mechanism-to-product bridge.
- CTA-4 (Gentle Invite): Permission-framed, curiosity-driven CTA appropriate for E1 of the extended flow.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Risk elimination, required in all GF emails.
- DESDEEP-5 (The Border Play): Subtle border-based decorative framing to differentiate this flow from the welcome sequence.

**Differentiation Note:** GF is the broadest audience — no diagnosis language, symptom-first framing only. Uses "sensory differences" and "nervous system differences" as umbrella terms. The hidden detail is an open door bridge icon (universal, welcoming). The color narrative moves from amber (problem space) through cream (validation) to teal (resolution/CTA).
