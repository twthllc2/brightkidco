## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** Prompt-dependency isn't compliance failure — it's neurology. When a child always receives an external prompt, the brain learns to prioritize that cue over the internal signal. The BSL Layer has been building the internal signal. Now comes the harder part: fading the adult prompts so the internal signal has room to emerge.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Frustrated skepticism → Naming the pattern → Mechanism explanation → Actionable protocol → Permission space → Cautious recommitment. E3 is the "he still doesn't go on his own" email at the Week 5-6 plateau. It names the prompt-dependency frustration, provides a concrete week-by-week fading protocol, and closes with the 60-day permission frame.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-19 (Timeline Shock) leads with the symptom hook that L2 Lisa immediately recognizes. LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) delivers the validation, mechanism, and protocol copy. SP-06 (Before/After Testimonial) presents Lena's personal story. EDU-10 (How It Works) reinforces the mechanism via neuroscience. CTA-6 (Social CTA) is behavioral — "Try the 30-second delay."

**Differentiation Note:** L2-specific: BCBA acknowledgment (respectful, not critical). Protocol-oriented copy (week-by-week structure). High mechanism density (neuroscience explanation). No Pre-K thread (L2 school already adapted). No L3 territory (no "adult diapers", no "years of failed").

### Section 6 — Protocol: Prompt Fading (Week-by-Week)
- **Component:** LETTER-01 — Paragraph Stack (protocol variant)
- **Section Header (verbatim):** "This is not about stopping prompts cold. That would confuse and frustrate both of you. It's a gradual transfer of responsibility from external cue to internal awareness."
- **Protocol Cards (4 cards, #E8F4F8 background, 8px radius):**

  **Weeks 5-6: Delay, Don't Remove**
  - Wait 30-60 seconds before prompting
  - Watch for ANY signal: posture shift, hand to waistband, pause in play
  - If you see it: "I noticed you shifted. Does your body feel like something's happening?"
  - If you don't see it: prompt normally

  **Weeks 6-7: Expand the Window**
  - Increase delay to 2-3 minutes
  - Move slightly farther away when you prompt (from standing next to them to across the room)
  - Physical distance subtly shifts responsibility toward their body awareness

  **Weeks 7-8: Reduce Prompt Frequency**
  - Cut one scheduled prompt per day
  - Replace with: "How's your body feeling right now?"
  - If they answer with unrelated body sensation (hungry, tired, excited), validate it
  - ANY body awareness is progress

  **Weeks 8-10: Observe the Shift**
  - Some L2 kids begin showing first independent signals
  - Walking toward bathroom unprompted, pausing mid-play with recognition
  - Saying "I think I have to go" (even if sometimes too late)
  - "These are not failures. These are breakthroughs that happen to arrive late."

- **Copy Block (verbatim):** "If progress stalls: Stay at the current prompt level for another week. Regression isn't starting over. It's sensory fluctuation. Hold steady."
