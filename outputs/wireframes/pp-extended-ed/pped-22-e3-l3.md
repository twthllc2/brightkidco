## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "Small signs that matter" — you've been looking for the wrong signs. The signs that matter for your child are smaller. A pause before sitting down. A glance down at the wet spot. A flap of the hands. Ten seconds of dry time that wasn't there before. These are the first sparks of a nervous system beginning to register information it never received before.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Quiet acceptance → Micro-win recognition → Dignity-centered progress → Redefined success. E3 is the "it didn't work either" preempt email at the Week 5-8 inflection point. It redefines what "progress" means for L3 children, provides a minimal tracking system, and closes with the 60-day permission frame.

**Component Selection Rationale:** HERO-19 (Timeline Shock) leads with the validation hook that L3 Maria recognizes — "the signs you might be missing." LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) delivers the micro-win categories and tracking system. SP-06 (Before/After Testimonial) presents Lena's personal L3 story. EDU-10 (How It Works) reinforces mechanism via dignity-first framing. CTA-6 (Social CTA) is purely invitational — "When you decide, it's here."

**Differentiation Note:** L3-specific: ZERO urgency, no deadlines, no cheerleading. Dignity-first framing throughout. 1 pair at $34.99 only — no bundle upselling. Permission-not-to-buy CTA. Sentences shorter than L1/L2. More white space. Each idea gets its own line. Lena at her quietest.

### Section 6 — Five Micro-Win Trackers
- **Component:** LETTER-01 — Paragraph Stack
- **Section Header (verbatim):** "Micro-wins you can track starting today"
- **Tracker List (verbatim):**
  1. **The Pause** — A hesitation, a brief stop in movement, right before or after voiding. Even half a second. This is the nervous system registering: something happened.
  2. **The Look** — Eyes moving toward the wet spot or the body. The brain is connecting the internal event to the external reality for the first time.
  3. **The Reaction** — A change in behavior at the moment of wetness. Not discomfort, that may never come for a slow-to-pick-up-signals child. But any shift. A flap. A vocalization. A small stillness.
  4. **The Duration** — Measuring in seconds, not hours. Was he dry for 30 seconds this week when last week it was zero? That is nothing. That is the signal pathway starting to hold.
  5. **The Awareness Window** — The time between "he voided" and "he shows awareness of it." The gap may be 10 seconds. Next month it might be 8 seconds. That's progress.
- **Closing Copy (verbatim):** "None of these mean your child is 'training.' They mean his body is communicating with his brain a way it hasn't before. That is the only outcome the Body-Signal Learning Layer is designed to create."
- **Background:** #FBF7F1 (warm cream)

- **Notes:** L3-specific: 5 trackers grounded in actual L3 neurology. "Slow-to-pick-up-signals" — dignity language, not "severe." Each tracker is a concrete, observable behavior. "That is nothing" — reframe as meaningful.
