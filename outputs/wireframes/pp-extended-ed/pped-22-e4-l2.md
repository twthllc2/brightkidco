## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "The 3-Week Gap" — between starting and seeing. Most L2 parents expect linear progress. The science shows a 3-4 week latency period before the body signal pathway begins to register. The creative strategy visualizes this latency, not as failure, but as a biological timer.

**Emotional Arc Position:** E4 of 5 in the PP-Extended Education flow (D43). L2 emotional arc: skeptical engagement → mechanism confirmation. E4 is the "realistic check-in" — where are you at day 21? The email validates the latency period and provides concrete troubleshooting for families seeing nothing yet.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-2 (Data Shock): Leads with data/science hook — "3 weeks in, here's what your child's brain is doing." L2 parents respond to evidence, not emotion.
- LETTER-09 (Double Column): Science-dense layout with mechanism explanation, research citations, and troubleshooting. L2 needs depth, not skimming.
- TRUSTDEEP-11 (The Research Bar): Primary research citation (Hample, Mahler & Amspacher 2020, Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi 2025) — L2 requires mechanism depth.
- EDU-11 (Science Citation): Detailed mechanism explanation with interoception pathway, BCBA complement framing, and 82% internal data stat.
- ProductShowcaseMedium: 3+3 Bundle ($79.99) as primary offer.
- CTA-11 (Permission CTA): "When you're ready, the body-signal learning guide is here" — permission-not-to-buy frame essential for L2.
- TRUST-2 (Guarantee Detail Cards): 60-day guarantee with specific day-count framing ("You have 39 more days to observe").
- DESDEEP-5 (The Border Play): Clean editorial borders between sections.

**Differentiation Note:** E4 L2 is visually differentiated by:
1. HERO-2 Data Shock hero — science-first, not emotion-first
2. Neutral color palette (no warm accents) — evidence-based, not aspirational
3. EDU-11 Science Citation with formal research citations — L2 respects academic rigor
4. CTA-11 Permission CTA — "When you're ready" framing, no urgency
5. Longer email (~480 words body) — L2 needs depth (500-800w spec)

### Block: Mechanism Deep-Dive
**What the first 3 weeks actually accomplish**

Most parents think if nothing obvious has happened by week 3, nothing is happening. That's not how interoception works.

For autistic children, the brain processes sustained tactile input differently. Autistic brains don't habituate to sensory input the way neurotypical brains do — they keep processing it, fresh, every time. That "nothing happened" feeling? The sensation is landing. The brain is just still deciding what to do with it.

Your BCBA built the behavioral foundation over months or years of ABA. What you're doing now is adding the sensory layer ABA can't reach — sustained, localized body-signal input that gives the brain something to attach the behavior to.

### Block: Research Citations
**What the research says about this specific window**

A 2020 study by Hample, Mahler and Amspacher showed that autistic children who received structured interoception training began showing measurable improvement in body awareness after 4-6 weeks. Not 3 days. Not 1 week. The nervous system needs exposure cycles.

Internally, across 672 families in our data, 82% showed clearer body awareness within 2-4 weeks of consistent wear. That doesn't mean "trained", it means the first signal registered. A pause before an accident. A look at the wet spot. A hand toward the pants.

That's not failure. That's the first synapse firing.

### Block: Troubleshooting
**If you're in the "nothing yet" camp, here's what to check**

1. **Wear time:** Is your child getting 2+ hours daily of consistent wear? Intermittent wear = intermittent input. The brain can't build a pathway on 20 minutes here and there.

2. **The environment:** Sensory-overloaded bathrooms (flushing echoes, bright lights, cold tile) can override the learning signal. If your child resists bathroom time, it may not be the underwear, it may be the room. Try pairing wear-time with a low-stimulus activity (reading, tablet time, a quiet corner) for the first 2 weeks before introducing bathroom association.

3. **Clothing choices:** If your child is already sensitive to clothing textures, the learning layer adds another sensation on top. Try wearing the body-signal underwear under loose sweatpants or soft shorts, minimize competing sensory input during early weeks.

4. **Daily rhythm:** The nervous system learns in patterns. Same time of day, same duration, same low-pressure context. Irregular schedules give the brain inconsistent data.
