## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** The science proves the brain can learn this — the timeline is neurological, not behavioral.

**Emotional Arc Position:** Fourth position in PP-Extended Education Flow — the month-1 mechanism deep dive. Parent has been wearing the BSL Layer for 4 weeks. Some visible signs, some none. Lisa is in the "method-cycling danger zone" — if nothing dramatic has happened, she may be ready to abandon. This email must answer the question she's been holding in for 4 weeks: *Is this actually working?* The Nicholson 2019 deep dive provides the scientific answer: the deficit is developmental, not permanent. The timeline is neurological. Her child is not broken.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-01 (Email Opening) — Lena's voice, warm but direct. "You're 29 days in" grounds the timeline immediately. No badge, no teaser — the parent has earned the right to the full picture.
- LETTER-01 (Paragraph Stack) — Opening validation + emotional normalization. "You're tracking. You're documenting. And you're wondering" — names the skepticism directly.
- MECH-02 (Study Deep Dive) — The core of the email. Full Nicholson 2019 breakdown: Experiment 2 (children, significant deficit) vs Experiment 1 (adults, gap resolved). This is mechanism-specificity at its highest.
- TABLE-01 (Neural Rewiring Table) — Week-by-week HTML table showing what's happening neurologically and what the parent might observe. Converts invisible brain changes into a visible timeline.
- LETTER-02 (BCBA Bridge) — Dedicated section acknowledging the BCBA's work, positioning BSL as complementary, not competitive. Gives concrete data-sharing protocol.
- LETTER-03 (Validation Anchor) — "Where your child is right now is normal." Normalizes whatever stage the parent is in, referencing the table.
- GUARANTEE-01 (60-Day Reminder) — "You're 29 days into a 60-day trial." Frames the guarantee as ongoing permission, not a hard reset.
- SIGNOFF-02 (Medium Sign-off) — "With understanding, Lena" + P.S. offering the Nicholson summary for BCBA/OT.

**Differentiation Note:** E2 is unique in the Extended Education flow because it's the only email that must simultaneously provide the full scientific mechanism AND normalize whatever timeline the parent is experiencing. Every other email either teaches or validates — E2 must do both in the same message. The Nicholson 2019 deep dive (Experiment 1 vs 2 breakdown) is the structural innovation: it provides the scientific proof that the deficit resolves, while the week-by-week table translates that science into a parent-observable timeline.

### Section 2: MECH-02 — Nicholson Study Deep Dive (Mechanism Diagram)
- **Component:** MECH-02 — Study Deep Dive
- **Category:** MECHANISM/EDUCATION
- **Copy (verbatim):** "What Nicholson et al. 2019 Actually Found. The study compared body awareness, the ability to sense internal body states, between autistic children and typically developing children. Experiment 2 (children): Autistic children showed significantly diminished body awareness. Moderate-to-large effect size — a measurable, statistically significant gap. Experiment 1 (adults): Autistic adults showed NO difference from non-autistic adults. The body signal gap was gone. *Early interoception difficulties are resolved or compensated for by adulthood in people with ASD.* The deficit is childhood-specific. It closes. Your child is not permanently disconnected from their body signals. The wiring is delayed, not absent."
- **Background:** #FFFFFF (white), rounded 16px, shadow 6px 6px 0 B.ink
- **Typography:** Heading: Questrial 26px/700, B.ink. Experiment cards: bg #FBF7F1 (cream), rounded 12px, padding 22px, 15px/600 weight B.ink headings. Pull-quote: bg #DBFFCD (mint), rounded 12px, padding 22px, 18px italic B.ink, em-dash opener. Conclusion: 17px, B.ink, 600 weight.
- **Special:** The Experiment 1 vs 2 structure is the core innovation. Two cards visually separate the child data from the adult data, making the comparison immediately clear. The mint pull-quote is the verbatim study conclusion — the highest-evidence moment in the email. "Delayed, not absent" is the phrase L2 parents will remember.
