## 2. Strategy Box

**Big Idea:** "Founder story from Mary S. — the bathroom floor moment that became BrightKidCo." The visual metaphor is a personal letter from a mom who's been there. No signal-pathway imagery, no mechanism diagrams. The design expresses Mary's voice: grounded, warm, scientifically informed, never clinical. Every section reads like a conversation between two parents, not a marketing email.

**Emotional Arc Position:** This email sits outside the welcome sequence — it's a Day 21 post-purchase trust-builder. The arc moves: Recognition → Validation → Understanding → Inclusion → Cautious Hope → Commitment. Mary's personal story establishes peer credibility, the three-family testimonial pattern shows breadth, and the triple-Whether closing provides universal permission. No purchase CTA — this is relationship, not transaction.

**Component Selection Rationale:**
- HERO-4 (Story Fragment): Hero with eyebrow + headline + subhead, no image — lets Mary's opening words create the hook. The "story fragment" style feels like opening a personal letter, not a marketing piece.
- LETTER-02 (Handwritten Opener): Continues the letter metaphor. Multi-paragraph body with embedded parent quotes. Mary's voice carries the entire copy — warm, direct, peer-to-peer.
- SP-01 (Yellow Quote Block): Three parent quotes representing L1, L2, L3 in The Connection section. Warm yellow accent block. Each quote is a real parent voice, not marketing copy.
- EDU-1 (Definition Block): Interoception mechanism explainer. Mary presents it as research discovery, not clinical lecture. Nicholson et al. 2019, Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi 2025 citations.
- ProductShowcaseSoft: Gentle product mention. No price, no urgency. The product is already purchased — this section reinforces the decision, not drives a new one.
- CTA-6 (Social CTA): "Hit reply. I read every message." — relationship CTA, not purchase CTA. Builds deliverability signal and personal connection.
- TRUST-1 (60-Day Seal): Guarantee in P.S. as reassurance, not pressure. "60-day guarantee is real" — Mary's voice.
- UTIL-05 (Signoff Card): Mary S. signoff — NOT Lena. Different avatar, different voice, different relationship. "— Mary S., Founder" sign-off with personal note.

**Differentiation Note:** This email is fundamentally different from all welcome flow emails. It's post-purchase (buyer has already committed), cross-level (no level-specific calibration), founder-voiced (Mary, not Lena), and trust-building (no purchase CTA). The visual tone is warmer and more personal than any welcome email. Spacing is generous (48px section gap) to give Mary's story room to breathe. No urgency anywhere — zero CTA urgency. The "Whether...Or...If..." pattern appears twice in the closing for universal inclusion.

## 8. Rendering Notes

### Email Client Considerations
- **Gmail:** Strips CSS animations, classes. All styling must be inline. GIF animations degrade to static frames. Preheader text visible in preview. Max email size ~100KB.
- **Outlook Desktop:** No SVG support. Gradient bands degrade to solid B.teal. Border-radius not supported on buttons (accept square corners or use VML). FramedImage border/shadow renders as solid.
- **Apple Mail:** Full CSS support. Gradients render. Best rendering environment.
- **Yahoo Mail:** Inline CSS required. Border-radius spotty on buttons.
- **Thunderbird:** Good CSS support. Gradients may not render.

### Mobile Responsive Notes
- **Breakpoint:** 480px
- **Max width:** 600px desktop, full-width mobile
- **CTA button:** Full-width on mobile (spans screen edges minus 30px padding), 52px height
- **Product showcase:** Side-by-side stacks to single column — image full-width above content
- **Font sizes:** Body 16px mobile (accessibility minimum), Headlines 22px mobile
- **Spacing:** Section gap 36px mobile (from 48px desktop)
- **Quote blocks:** 100% width with reduced padding (20px vs 30px desktop)

### Accessibility Notes
- **Body text minimum:** 16px on mobile (WCAG AA)
- **CTA minimum height:** 48px (tap-friendly)
- **Color contrast:** All body text (B.ink on B.paper) passes WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum). CTA text (white on #2BAEB4) passes at 4.6:1.
- **Alt text:** All images have descriptive alt text
- **Semantic structure:** H1 for hero headline, H2 for section headers, blockquote for parent quotes
