Motion philosophy for L3: If it can be still, let it be still.

L3 is the level where animation is most restrained. L1 can use a 3-4 second animated GIF of the signal wave pulsing. L2 can use a slow fade-in for the contraption. L3 uses ONE animation, at the lowest possible intensity.

E6 L3 Animation, Gentle Signal Pulse (GIF)

What moves:

- The signal wave's highest peak rises and falls by 2px (not by 5px or 10px, it's barely perceptible)

- A tiny glow (opacity 0.04 → 0.10 → 0.04) trails behind the wave's right edge, like a firefly

What does NOT move:

- The feather (easter egg) stays perfectly still

- The CTA text does not animate, pulse, or highlight

- No scroll-triggered animations

- No fade-in entrance effects

- No bounce, shake, or zoom on any element

Why this level of restraint:

L3 parents have been sold products that "flash and pulse" for years. Every animated "limited time offer" banner is a betrayal of trust. The signal wave moves because it IS the mechanism, the first sign of life in a flatline. But it barely moves, because the L3 parent's hope is fragile. A 2px movement is enough. Anything more would feel like pressure.

Fallback: If the GIF cannot be rendered within size limits, the SVG wave renders static with `@keyframes` animation. Both paths produce the same visual.

L3 design principles applied to this email:

1. Nothing shouts. Every element is quieter than its L1/L2 counterpart. If a design decision would be heard before it's seen, it's wrong for L3.

2. Permission is visual, not just copy. The white space, the low contrast, the slow animation, they all say "you can put this down and come back."

3. Dignity in every pixel. No element should make the reader feel inadequate. The design holds space for her exhaustion, her grief, her caution.

4. The signal is the star. No competing visual elements. The wave is the only graphic. Every other decision supports the reader's attention resting on the signal concept.

5. Text link is the ultimate L3 CTA. A button says "click me NOW." A text link says "this here if you want it." For L3, the latter is the only honest option.
