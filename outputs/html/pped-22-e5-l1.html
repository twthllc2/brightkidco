2. The pull-up-over-underwear system

Send him to school in a thin pull-up OVER his BrightKidCo underwear. This does two things:

- The pull-up protects against accidents in the classroom

- The underwear underneath still has skin contact, so the body-signal learning continues throughout the school day

Teachers understand pull-ups. They don't understand body-signal layers. Meet them where they are.

3. The morning and after-school rhythm

The school day can dysregulate sensory systems. Build two consistent checkpoints:

- Morning: 10 minutes of calm bathroom time before leaving. No pressure to perform. Just the routine of sitting with his underwear on.

- After-school: 30 minutes in BrightKidCo underwear only (no pull-up) when he gets home. This his body-signal learning window, when his nervous system is decompressing and most receptive.

Every single day of body-signal exposure is building a pathway that wasn't there before. That pause, that look, that single step toward the bathroom — those are the evidence.
