This gap has a name: interoception — the body's ability to sense internal signals like hunger, thirst, and the need to use the bathroom. Research shows autistic children show significantly diminished body awareness — a real, measurable neurological difference, not a behavioral issue. Over 90% of autistic children have sensory processing differences, which is why timed sits and sticker charts fail. You can't reward a child into feeling something their nervous system isn't delivering. ---
