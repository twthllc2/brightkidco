The underwear your child wore was designed around one idea: the body learns best when it gets the right signal.

If your child paused. Looked down. Walked toward the bathroom, even after. Stayed dry a little longer than before.

### MECHANISM REMINDER — The Signal, Gently Reframed

The body-signal underwear works by giving the brain a sensation it may never have received before, a gentle, sustained "uh-oh" feeling that lasts 30 to 60 seconds after the moment passes.

Pull-ups wick that sensation away. Regular underwear delivers it in a split second that the brain can't always catch. The BSL layer holds the signal long enough for a deeply nervous system that picks up signals slowly to register: something happened.

It doesn't force the brain to respond differently. It gives the brain the information it needs to make its own connections, in its own time.

### Pre-Send QA Checklist

Every visual element must communicate spaciousness, quietness, and permission. The L3 mom (Maria) has been burned by marketing promises for 7+ years. Her visual system is calibrated to detect manipulation. The creative must feel like a conversation with a trusted peer, not an email from a brand.

- Font: Questrial (weight 400 only, no bold, no heavy)

- Headline: 24px, letter-spacing -0.01em, line-height 1.3

- Body: 16px, line-height 1.7 (spacious)

- Pull Quote: 18px, italic, color `#1A1A1A` with left border `#039902` 3px

- CTA Text: 15px, uppercase letter-spacing 0.06em, no background button, simple text underline in `#039902`

Key Layout Rules:

- NO hero image at top. Header is simple wordmark + headline.

- Generous whitespace between sections (minimum 32px)

- NO countdown timers, progress bars, or urgency indicators

- NO exclamation marks in any visual element

- Maximum 60 characters per line of body text at 600px width

- LEFT-ALIGNED for body text. NEVER center-align paragraphs.

L3-Specific Image Rules:

- Hero image: None on E1. Use after the mechanism section in later emails if at all.

- If imagery is used: Photograph of a real mother (not a model, not studio-lit) sitting on a couch in soft natural light. Child not required in frame. Focus on the mother's expression, quiet, reflective, not smiling.

- Product shot: Flat-lay only. Underwear folded, on a neutral surface (wood, linen, cream). NOT on a child. NOT on a mannequin. NOT being held up.

- No child faces in Level 3 imagery unless explicitly approved (dignity risk too high).

- Color temperature: Warm (2700-3000K). No cool blues, no clinical white.

Absolute NOs for L3 Imagery:

- Children running, jumping, or "playing normally"

- Before/after transformation shots

- Side-by-side comparisons

- "Victory" poses or raised hands

- Smiling children on potties

- Adult diapers shown in frame (even partially)

- Urgency reds or bright yellows

- Cheerful illustrations with cartoon faces

- Tone: Soft, diffused, natural light. Slight desaturation (80% of original).

- Vignette: Subtle dark edge vignette (10% opacity) to draw eye inward.

- Border: None on images. No drop shadows. Let images breathe.

- Product Priority: Product shown only in context of use, folded in a drawer, resting on a bed, placed alongside a book. Never isolated on white like a commodity.
