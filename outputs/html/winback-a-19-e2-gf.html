Whether you bought the 3+3 bundle three months ago or one pair to try, whether your child is 3 or 10, verbal or non-verbal, still in pull-ups or somewhere in between, if you've been wondering whether Body-Signal learning is actually working, you're not the only one.

If you stopped using them, that's not failure. If you're not sure they're working, that's not ungratefulness. If your child seemed to regress after an initial sign, that's not starting over.

The interoception pathway doesn't build in a straight line. Some kids show awareness in 2 weeks. Others take 8. Both are normal. Some need a break and come back to find the signal clicks the second time.

None of these kids used a different method. They all used the same underwear, the Body-Signal Learning Layer, that creates a gentle, sustained "uh-oh" sensation after every accident. Not to punish. To inform.

Pull-ups wick that signal away. Sticker charts don't create it. Timed sits can't force it. The body has to learn to feel, and that starts with the right input.
