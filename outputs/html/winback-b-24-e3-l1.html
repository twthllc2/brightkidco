The "Final-Knock" Visual Contraption

A visual sign-off device shaped like a timeline arc running down the left margin of the email body, three small dots connected by a thin line, with the final dot highlighted in Brand-Green #039902 + Brand-Yellow #F5C84B glow.

Mechanism:

1. Dot 1 (top, Brand-Gray #9AA8A7): Labeled "Day 0, You signed up", faded/subtle, representing the start of her journey

2. Dot 2 (middle, Brand-Gray #9AA8A7): Labeled "Day 60, Winback started", still faded, representing the lost connection

3. Dot 3 (bottom, Brand-Green #039902 + Yellow glow #F5C84B): Labeled "Today, My last email", HIGHLIGHTED, glowing, the final intentional touch

Behavior:

- The arc reads visually as a time-sweep vertical timeline (left sidebar, thin 2px line, 12px dot diameter)

- Transitions from cold/gray at top to warm/green at bottom, visual metaphor for "she's been cold to us but we still reach out warmly"

- On mobile: the arc shifts to a horizontal progress bar above thero, 3 stages, stage 3 highlighted

- The contraption is positioned outside the main card on desktop (left margin, 40px wide), inside the card on mobile (top bar, full-width, 16px height)

Rationale: Prevents the "this just another email" reflex. The timeline arc gives a visual reason to pause, she sees three dots, recognizes she's been on a journey, and the final dot in warm green + yellow says "we still care" without words. For L1 specifically, the timeline reinforces Pre-K urgency without being pushy, the dots say "time has passed" factually.

Constraint: NO countdown timers, NO blinking text, NO intrusive movement. Per GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §17.1 (Warming Rules: NO countdown timers) and §19.2 (No urgency manipulation). Also forbidden for winback emails per Layer-17 §7 (Lifecycle forbidden: "We miss you!" / guilt-tripping / countdowns).

Animation Philosophy: Motion should feel like a person breathing, not a machine advertising. Slow, organic, barely-there. The pulse on the dot is the closest thing to a heartbeat, the brand's heartbeat, not an alarm.
