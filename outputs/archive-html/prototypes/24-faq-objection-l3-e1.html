Why no method has worked in 7 years

Not because you did anything wrong. Because the tools didn’t exist for your child’s nervous system.

You’ve been at this for years. 40 hours of ABA per week. OT. Social stories. Picture schedules. Reward systems. Specialists. Consultants. A dozen different approaches. And your child is still in pull-ups.

I know what that feels like. My son was 8 before he trained. I spent years wondering what I was missing.

Here’s what I eventually learned: every method you tried assumed one thing — that your child’s body sends a clear signal in time to reach the bathroom. For kids with high support needs, that signal often arrives too late, too faint, or it doesn’t arrive at all.

That’s not a failure of the therapies. They addressed behavior, communication, and compliance. Those are important. But nobody told you there was a sensory piece — interoception, the ability to feel internal body signals — that behavioral methods can’t address.

You haven’t failed. The tools for your child’s nervous system didn’t exist. Now they do.

What this looks like for each child is different. For some, it means starting to feel the signal for the first time. For others, it means pausing, looking down, or pulling at pants. Whatever progress looks like for your family is valid.

 

With warmth,

Lena S.
