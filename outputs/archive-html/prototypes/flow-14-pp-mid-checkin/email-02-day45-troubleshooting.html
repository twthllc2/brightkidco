A quick troubleshooting checklist

1. Are they wearing them consistently?
The body needs daily contact with the learning layer. Even one hour a day makes a difference. If they've had a break, that's okay — just restart.

2. Are you layering over a pull-up or going commando?
The underwear works best against the skin. Pull-ups wick away the wet signal, which is the opposite of what we want. If pull-ups feel necessary for outings, add the underwear under the pull-up for at least part of the day.

3. Are there new stressors at home?
Change in routine, sleep disruption, new school term — any stressor can pause progress. It's not regression, it's recalibration.

4. What time of day are you trying?
Many kids show more interoceptive awareness in the morning when their body is calmest. Try increasing wear time in the 1–2 hours after breakfast.

You're not troubleshooting alone

Other parents are walking this same path. Sometimes the most helpful thing is to hear someone say, "Us too. Week 5 was our hardest week. Week 7 is when it started to shift."
