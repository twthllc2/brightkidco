Two weeks in — here's what to look for

The mini-wins that mean more than "fully trained"

 

Hi {first_name},

Two weeks. That's 14 days of body-signal learning. Whether you've seen nothing or something — you've been building something real.

By now, some parents start to see the first tiny shifts. Not the toilet. Not dry underwear. But awareness. And awareness is the foundation everything else builds on.

Mini-wins checklist — how many apply to your child?

☐ They paused before or during an accident
☐ They looked down at the wet spot
☐ They pulled at their pants after going
☐ They stayed dry for 2 hours instead of 30 minutes
☐ They made a sound or gesture that indicated awareness
☐ They walked toward the bathroom (even after the accident)

If you checked even one box, that's signal recognition starting. The connection between "sensations in my body" and "something just happened" is being built. That's measurable progress.

 

If you haven't seen any of these yet

That's okay too. Some children take longer. Their nervous system needs more time to map the new sensation. For some it's 2 weeks. For others it's 6. The research is very clear on this — interoception training follows an individual timeline, not a calendar.

What matters is consistency. The more they wear the underwear, the more opportunities their brain has to connect the dots. Every accident is a learning cycle. Every time the fabric triggers awareness, a neural pathway is being reinforced.

Progress isn't linear. But it is happening — even when you can't see it.
