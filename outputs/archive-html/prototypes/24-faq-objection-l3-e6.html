A few more things you might be wondering

“I tried training underwear and it didn’t help.”

Standard training underwear catches the accident. That’s all it does. Pull-ups wick the wetness away so the brain never registers what happened. BrightKidCo’s Body-Signal Learning Layer is different: it keeps the sensation present for 30–60 seconds — long enough for a nervous system that needs more time to register the feeling. It’s not a different version of the same thing. It’s a fundamentally different mechanism.

 

“My child won’t wear them.”

We expect that. Start by leaving them next to the potty or in their room. Let them explore the fabric. Put them on over a pull-up for a few minutes at a time. The Learning Layer can still work through a pull-up. 100% cotton, hypoallergenic, thin fit. Gradual sensory acclimation is the goal. If they never tolerate them, that’s okay — the 60-day guarantee covers that.

 

“We have GI issues. It feels impossible.”

The withholding cycle is real — constipation, pain, fear, holding, impaction, more pain. It’s medical, not behavioral. No reward system or sticker chart can break that. The Body-Signal Learning Layer works alongside what your medical team recommends. It doesn’t replace GI protocols. It helps the body start recognizing the signals that get lost in that cycle.

Autistic children are 4x more likely to experience chronic GI symptoms (McElhanon et al., 2014). You’re not alone in this.
