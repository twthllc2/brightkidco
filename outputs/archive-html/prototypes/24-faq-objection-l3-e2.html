You haven’t failed

After years of trying, there’s a quiet thought that settles in: “Maybe his autism is too severe. Maybe I’m the reason this isn’t working.”

I need you to hear this: your child’s autism isn’t being treated. His nervous system is being supported. Those are two very different things. And you’ve spent years supporting your child in ways most people will never understand.

The research is clear: interoceptive differences — the ability to feel internal body signals — are a well-documented feature of autism. It’s not a parenting gap. It’s not a commitment gap. It’s a neurological reality.

If you’ve moved past active training — that’s not failure. If you’ve stopped hoping — that’s not giving up. It’s energy preservation for what’s actually possible. And you’ve been doing the right thing, regardless of what comes next.

Whether he trains in 8 weeks or never, you have been doing the right thing.
