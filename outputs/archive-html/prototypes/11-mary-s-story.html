A letter from our founder, Mary S.

Hi {{first_name}},

My name is Mary. I'm the founder of BrightKidCo, and I want to tell you why this company exists.

My son was four when we started potty training. We tried everything—the three-day method, sticker charts, reward systems, scheduled sits every 20 minutes. Nothing worked. He'd sit on the toilet and stare at the wall, then pee in his pull-up the moment I put it back on.

I remember Googling at 2am, completely defeated, wondering what was wrong with me. That's when I stumbled across something I'd never heard of: interoception—the body's ability to sense what's happening inside.

The more I read, the more it clicked. My son wasn't stubborn. He couldn't feel the signal. Every method we tried assumed that signal was arriving. For autistic kids, it often doesn't.

The moment everything changed

I realized the problem wasn't my son—it was the underwear. Pull-ups are designed to wick moisture away. Which is great for keeping a child dry. But for learning? The wetness signal is the only way the brain connects "I peed" with "I should use the potty." Pull-ups block that connection.

So I asked: what if there was an underwear that preserves the signal? A fabric that creates a gentle awareness while keeping the child comfortable and dignified?

I worked with a textile engineer and an occupational therapist to build the Body-Signal Learning Layer™. It took two years and countless prototypes. The first time my son wore it, he didn't train overnight. But three weeks in, he paused. Looked down. Something had shifted.

 

Today, over 100,000 families have learned what took me years to discover: their child wasn't failing at potty training. The methods were solving the wrong problem.

That's why I built BrightKidCo. Not to sell underwear. To give families the explanation they've been searching for.

Thank you for being part of this journey.
