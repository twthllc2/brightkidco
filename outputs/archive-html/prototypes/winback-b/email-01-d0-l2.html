The science in one paragraph

Interoception — the ability to feel internal body signals — is processed differently in many autistic children. Kelly Mahler, the researcher who pioneered interoception education, calls it the "eighth sensory system."

When your child can't feel the "I need to go" signal, methods that depend on that signal — sticker charts, timer sits, ABA protocols for toileting — can't work, no matter how well they're executed.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer™ doesn't replace your BCBA's work. It adds the sensory piece that behavioral methods can't address.
