Your package is here and the underwear is in your hands. If you're like most parents, part of you wants to get started right away — and part of you is wondering if this will be another thing that doesn't work. I understand both feelings.

Here's what I've learned from watching thousands of families: the first week is not about the potty. It's about comfort. Familiarity. Letting your child's nervous system notice that something is different on their body.

The Learning Layer™ works by giving your child's brain a sensory signal they can feel. But if they're not comfortable with the layer itself, the signal gets lost in the "new thing" anxiety. So start slow.

This week: Let them wear the underwear for 30 minutes. Over clothes. Under clothes. Just holding it counts. No expectations. No pressure. We'll build from there.

WEEK 1 — REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS

Before week 1, progress looks like any of these:

• Your child notices the underwear (touches it, looks down, pulls at it)
• They wear it without trying to take it off after 5 minutes
• They pause mid-activity (even if they still go in the underwear)
