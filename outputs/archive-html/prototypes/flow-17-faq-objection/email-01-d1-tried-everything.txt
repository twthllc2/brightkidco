THE MECHANISM · WHY METHODS FAIL

Every method you tried assumed your child ALREADY FEELS the "I need to go" signal — and just needs a reason to act on it. That's the hidden assumption behind sticker charts, timed sits, rewards, and every bootcamp protocol.

For many neurodivergent children, that signal NEVER ARRIVES. Or it arrives too late. Their nervous system processes internal body signals — interoception — differently. Research has confirmed this across multiple studies: autistic children consistently show reduced ability to sense internal body cues. (Nicholson et al. 2019; Barmpagiannis et al. 2025 — a review of 49 studies.)

Pull-ups make this worse. They're designed to wick moisture away — which means your child feels nothing, learns nothing. The feedback loop that builds body awareness never has a chance to start. (Simon & Thompson 2006 confirmed that undergarment type directly affects training outcomes.)

The Body-Signal Learning Layer doesn't train behavior. It RESTORES THE SIGNAL that every other method depends on. A gentle wetness feedback that lasts 30-60 seconds — enough for the brain to start making the connection.

If your child has never shown any awareness of being wet or dry, that's not a behavior problem. That's a signal problem. And signal problems need signal solutions.
