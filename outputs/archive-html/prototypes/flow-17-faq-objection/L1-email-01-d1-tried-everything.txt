FAQ · L1 · 1 OF 6

"He gets it, but he doesn't get it. What makes this different?"

Fair question. The answer might surprise you.

---

Hi {first_name},

Your child can talk about the potty. They know where it is. They've sat on it. They've even gone sometimes. And yet — weeks or months later — they're still having accidents. Still asking for a pull-up. Still not making the connection.

Here's why: It's not that they don't understand. It's that the body signal isn't arriving in time. Their brain processes the 'I need to go' signal — but too late. By the time they feel it, it's already happening.

For neurodivergent kids, interoception — the ability to feel internal body signals — works differently. Research shows autistic children consistently have reduced interoceptive awareness. (Nicholson et al., 2019.) Your child isn't being defiant. Their nervous system processes the signal — just not fast enough.

---
THE MECHANISM · WHY STANDARD METHODS FAIL VERBAL KIDS
Sticker charts, rewards, and timed sits all assume the child ALREADY feels the signal and just needs motivation. For kids whose signal arrives late or weak, rewards can't create a sensation that hasn't arrived yet. The Body-Signal Learning Layer™ changes this: sustained wetness feedback for 30-60 seconds gives the brain time to register the sensation — building the connection that sticker charts can't.
