Hi {first_name},

On 'tried underwear before': Standard training underwear catches the accident — that's its only job. It doesn't help the child learn to feel the signal. The Body-Signal Learning Layer is fundamentally different: it delivers sustained wetness for 30-60 seconds, giving the nervous system time to register what happened. Pull-ups do the opposite — they wick moisture away, blocking the feedback loop entirely.

On 'my child won't wear them': This is common. Start by leaving the underwear next to the potty. Let your child touch it, hold it, explore it. Next step: wear over a pull-up — the Layer still works through the pull-up for signal detection. Gradually move to wearing on its own. 100% cotton, hypoallergenic, fun character designs. Gradual is the goal.

On 'what if they rip them off': Sensory defensiveness is real. 90%+ of autistic children have sensory processing differences. (Leekam et al., 2007.) If your child needs to remove them, let them. The exposure still counts. Each time they feel the gentle wetness feedback, a tiny neural connection forms.
