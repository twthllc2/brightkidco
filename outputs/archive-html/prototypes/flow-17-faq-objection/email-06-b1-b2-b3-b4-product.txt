On 'tried underwear, didn't help': Standard training underwear catches the accident — that's all. It doesn't teach. On 'my BCBA has a protocol': This isn't a replacement for ABA. It's addition. Your BCBA built the behavioral foundation. The Body-Signal Layer adds the sensory piece — teaching the body to feel. Together they're powerful. Share our research with your BCBA.

On 'my kid will rip them off': We expect sensory acclimation. Start by leaving them next to the potty. Let your child explore them. Wear over a pull-up first — the Layer works through that. 100% cotton, hypoallergenic, fun character designs. Gradual is the goal.

On GI issues and withholding: The withholding cycle is real — and medical, not behavioral. Autistic children are 4x more likely to have GI symptoms. (McElhanon et al., 2014.) The Body-Signal Layer works alongside your medical team. It doesn't replace GI protocols — it supports them.
