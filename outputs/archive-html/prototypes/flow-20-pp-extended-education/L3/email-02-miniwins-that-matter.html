What progress looks like at this stage
Mini-wins count more than fully-trained claims.
[IMAGE: Child showing a small moment of body awareness]

Hi {first_name},

At this stage of the journey, the word "progress" can feel like it doesn't belong to you anymore. The milestones other families celebrate — first dry day, first independent trip to the toilet — may seem out of reach. I understand.

So I want to offer a different way of measuring success. Not the kind you see on social media, but the kind that actually matters for a child whose nervous system learns differently.

At this stage, a win is:

• Pausing mid-play — even for two seconds

• Looking down after the accident instead of ignoring it

• Tolerating the underwear for 10 minutes without distress

• Letting you change them without a meltdown

• Pulling at the waistband when wet

• Staying dry for 30 minutes longer than usual

• Tolerating the bathroom environment for even a moment

• Simply wearing the underwear — that's the starting line

One mother in our community told me: "He's never given me a signal in 8 years. But in Week 5, he paused. Just stood still. He didn't make it anywhere near the bathroom. But he noticed. For the first time in 8 years, he noticed his body telling him something."

That's not a small win. That's the entire mechanism beginning to work. The pause is the signal. Everything else — the walking to the bathroom, the independent initiation — comes after the signal establishes itself.

If your child has shown any of these micro-signals, that's real progress. If they haven't yet — that's also okay. Some children need 8, 10, even 12 weeks before the first awareness moment. The nervous system doesn't follow a calendar.

YOUR DEFINITION OF PROGRESS
Whatever progress means for your family — pause, look, tolerate — that's enough.

Gently,

Lena

Lena from BrightKidCo · Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons

P.S. If your child has never shown a sign — that's not failure. That's their neurology. The signal may come later, or it may look different than you expected. Both are possibilities that exist alongside each other.
