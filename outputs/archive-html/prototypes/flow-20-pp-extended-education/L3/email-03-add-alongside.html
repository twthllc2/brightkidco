Hi {first_name},

If you're reading this and thinking, "I don't have the energy to overhaul our routine" — I hear you. And I want to be clear: you don't have to.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer isn't designed to replace anything you're currently doing. It's designed to be added alongside — quietly, without disruption.

Here's what adding alongside looks like:

• Your child stays in their usual diaper or pull-up for most of the day

• You add the Body-Signal Layer for one short window — maybe 30 minutes after breakfast

• No schedule changes, no new bathroom battles, no pressure

•">If your child shows distress, you put the pull-up back on. No harm done.

• The next day, you try again. Even 10 minutes of wear gives the nervous system input it hasn't had before.

That's it. That's the entire starting protocol for this stage. No timers, no reward charts, no scheduled sits unless you already have them.

The reason this approach matters for Level 3 children is that their nervous systems are often more sensitive to change. Pushing too hard can trigger resistance, sensory overload, or behavioral escalation. Adding alongside — gently, incrementally — gives the nervous system time to adjust without triggering a fight response.

One mother described it this way:

"I put the underwear on my son for 15 minutes after breakfast for a whole week. He didn't notice it. Or if he did, he didn't react. By week two, he was wearing it for an hour. By week four, he wore it all morning. Nothing dramatic happened. But his nervous system was getting familiar with a new sensation. That familiarity matters."

There's no rush. The product will be here. Your child's nervous system will develop at its own pace. Your job is simply to offer the input — consistently, gently — and let the body do the rest.

If today isn't the day to try, that's okay. The underwear will be here when you're ready.

Gently,
