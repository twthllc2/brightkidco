Hi {first_name},

I'm going to share something I don't say lightly: your child's timeline is not closed.

I know it may feel that way. When you've been at this for years — when every method has failed, when you've stopped actively trying — it's natural to assume the window has passed. I understand that deeply. Both my sons went through extended periods where I believed the same thing.

But here's what I've learned from our community and from the research: late training is more common than most people realize.

"Maureen Bennie's son was 9.5 when he finally trained. They had stopped trying years before. She wrote about it — not as a miracle, but as a slow, unremarkable progression once his nervous system was ready."

Another story from our community: a mother who had stopped active training when her son was 7. She tried the Body-Signal Layer at 9, with no expectations. Three months later, he paused for the first time. At 11, he initiated consistently. That's 4 years from first wear to consistency. Not fast. But real.

I'm not telling you this to sell you on a timeline. I'm telling you because I want you to know: if you've stopped trying, that's legitimate. You've been doing this for years. You've earned the right to set the journey down for a while.

But if a small part of you wonders whether something might still shift — that's also legitimate.
