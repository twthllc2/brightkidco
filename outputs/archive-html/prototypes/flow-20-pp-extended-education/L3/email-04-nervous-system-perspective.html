Hi {first_name},

I want to share something that matters deeply to me, because I've seen how easy it is to misunderstand what we're doing here.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer is not a treatment for autism. Your child's autism is not something that needs to be treated. It's part of who they are — how they experience the world, how their body communicates, how their nervous system organizes information.

What we're doing is much simpler and more specific: we're giving the nervous system a type of sensory input it hasn't had before. That input — sustained, localized wetness feedback — may help the brain learn to recognize a signal it couldn't previously detect.

That's it. We're not promising a cure. We're not promising independence. We're not measuring success by the same standards as neurotypical potty training. We're simply saying: some nervous systems need more signal. Here's a tool that provides it.

What we are:

• A sensory support tool for nervous systems that process signals differently

• A complement to whatever else your family is doing

• A low-pressure option — try it, pause it, come back to it

What we are not:

• A cure, treatment, or therapy for autism

• A promise that your child will be fully trained

• A replacement for medical or therapeutic care

• Something that requires you to change how you parent

This distinction matters because I don't want you to feel like I'm asking you to believe in something. I'm not asking you to hope for a miracle. I'm asking you to understand a mechanism. If the mechanism makes sense for your child, you can decide whether to try it. If it doesn't, that's a legitimate choice.

A mother whose son is now 14 and still uses pull-ups at night once told me: "I stopped looking for solutions years ago. I just wanted someone to see us as we are, not as a problem to be fixed."

I see you as you are. Your child's autism is part of who they are. We're not changing that. We're just offering a tool that might help their nervous system feel something new — if that's something your family wants to explore.

With respect,
