A few weeks ago, I heard from Sarah, a mom whose son Sam is 6, Level 2, in ABA since he was 3. I asked if I could share her story because I think it captures something many Level 2 families experience.

Here's what she wrote:

"Sam has been in ABA for 4 years. His BCBA is incredible — she's done more for him than I ever thought possible. But potty training was the one thing we couldn't crack. We'd done scheduled sits for 2 years. He always went in his pull-up 10 minutes after getting off the toilet. His BCBA was honest with me: she didn't have another behavioral tool to try."
"I found BrightKidCo through an OT forum. I was skeptical — we'd spent thousands on products. But the mechanism made sense: if the signal isn't arriving, no behavioral protocol can fix that. So we tried it. I told his BCBA we were adding a sensory tool alongside the existing protocol."
"Week 3: nothing. Week 4: Sam paused mid-stride for the first time. Not fast enough to reach the toilet, but he noticed. His BCBA was fascinated — she'd never seen that before. She adjusted his protocol to reinforce that pause behavior. Now in Week 8, he's initiating 30% of the time. It's not fully trained. But it's the first real progress we've seen in 4 years."

I love this story because Sarah didn't choose between her BCBA and the Body-Signal Layer. She brought them together. The BCBA handled the behavior side — reinforcing the pause, shaping the response. The underwear handled the sensory side — teaching the nervous system to send the signal in the first place.

If you haven't spoken with your BCBA about the Body-Signal Layer yet, I'd encourage you to bring it up. Many of them are already familiar with interoception and will understand immediately why the sensory piece matters.
