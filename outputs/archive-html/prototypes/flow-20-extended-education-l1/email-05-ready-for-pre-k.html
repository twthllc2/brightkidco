A practical Pre-K prep checklist

✓ Talk to the school early. Set up a meeting before school starts. Explain that your child uses body-signal learning underwear and is building interoceptive awareness. Many Pre-K teachers have experience with delayed training.

✓ Create a bathroom communication plan. Work with the teacher on a non-verbal signal your child can use when they feel the urge — or a scheduled check-in if the signal is still inconsistent.

✓ Send spare pairs. Pack 2–3 pairs of BrightKidCo underwear in a labeled wet bag. Write a brief note: "These help him feel when he needs to go. Thank you for supporting this."

✓ Practice the routine. Before school starts, practice: "At school, when your body tells you it's time, you can raise your hand and say 'bathroom.' Let's practice." Role-play it a few times.

✓ Release the pressure. I know this is the hardest one. Remind yourself: the goal for Pre-K is participation and growth, not perfection. Accidents will happen. They're learning opportunities, not failures.

Where you are right now is enough

Pre-K readiness isn't a binary switch. It's a spectrum. Many children enter Pre-K with what schools call "emerging skills" — awareness with occasional accidents — and develop full independence over the first few months of school.

Your child's nervous system is learning every single day. The fact that you're reading this, that you've been consistent, that you're planning ahead — you are doing the most important work.

You have time. You have a plan. And you have a community of parents walking the same path.
