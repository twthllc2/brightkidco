The 4 questions every skeptical parent asks

Let me answer them directly.

“I tried training underwear. Didn’t help.”

Standard training underwear catches the accident. That’s it. Pull-ups remove the signal entirely — the wicking technology keeps them dry so the brain never registers being wet. BrightKidCo’s Body-Signal Learning Layer is different: it delivers sustained, localized wetness for 30–60 seconds. Enough time for a hypo-responsive nervous system to register the sensation. It creates the feedback loop that standard underwear and pull-ups actively prevent.

 

“My BCBA has a protocol. I trust that more.”

You should trust your BCBA. They’ve been doing the hard work with your child for years. This isn’t a replacement — it’s the missing piece. ABA addresses behavior: sitting on the toilet, following a schedule. The Body-Signal Learning Layer addresses the sensory piece that ABA can’t teach. Together, they’re powerful. Bring our research to your next BCBA session — most see the value immediately.

 

“My kid will rip them off or refuse to wear them.”

We expect sensory acclimation. Start by leaving them next to the potty. Let your child explore them. Wear them over a pull-up first — the Body-Signal Learning Layer can still work through it. 100% cotton, hypoallergenic, thin underwear-like fit. Fun character designs (Melon Pop, Lily Pop, Fynn Fox, Coco Cloud) help make it feel like a normal underwear choice. Gradual is the goal. No pressure.

 

“We have GI issues / withholding.”

The withholding cycle is real — and it’s medical, not behavioral. Constipation, pain, fear, holding, impaction, more pain. No amount of stickers or rewards can break that cycle. The Body-Signal Learning Layer works alongside what your medical team recommends. It doesn’t replace GI protocols. It helps the body start recognizing the signal that gets lost in the withholding-anxiety cycle.

Autistic children are 4x more likely to experience GI symptoms (McElhanon et al., 2014, Pediatrics). You’re not alone in this — and the solution isn’t another behavioral method.
