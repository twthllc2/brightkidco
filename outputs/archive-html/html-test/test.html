The science

The connection between bladder and brain — the interoceptive signal that says "I need to go" — arrives differently for many autistic kids. Research from the CDC-funded SPARK study found 49% of autistic 4–5 year olds aren't toilet trained yet (Wiggins et al. 2022). Not because parents didn't try. Because traditional methods assume a signal that isn't reliably arriving.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer in Bright underwear creates a gentle, sustained sensation — 30–60 seconds of feedback — that gives the brain time to register: something happened. Week 1 is about reintroducing that sensation.

Week 1 is about showing up. Nothing more. If your child wears the underwear for one hour today — even if nothing "happens" — you've done enough.
