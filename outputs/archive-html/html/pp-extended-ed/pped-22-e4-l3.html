Your child is growing. That's not a crisis.

The research tells us something important: the childhood window for interoceptive learning extends well beyond what most parents are told (Nicholson et al. 2019 — the deficit is in childhood, not adulthood). That means there is no deadline that passes while you're not looking. Your child's nervous system is still developing. It's still capable of learning to recognize new signals — just not on anyone else's schedule.

What changes during adolescence isn't the destination — it's the definition of progress.

A body that's growing means a nervous system that's still wiring. The interoceptive pathway — the one that carries the "I need to go" signal — is still forming. For a deeply hypo-responsive child, the goal isn't toilet training by a certain age. The goal is giving the brain enough information, consistently, over enough time, that it eventually learns to recognize the signal on its own.
