The part nobody explained

It was never about motivation.

Most potty training methods assume your child already feels the urge — and just needs a reason to act on it.

For many neurodivergent children, that assumption is the whole problem. They don't feel it in time. Not because they're stubborn. Not because they don't care. Their body signal simply arrives late — or not at all.

Rewards can't create a signal that isn't reaching the brain yet. That's why nothing you tried worked.

What Nicholson et al. 2019 Actually Found

The interoceptive gap is childhood-specific.
It closes. The brain finds a way.

The Science

The study compared interoceptive accuracy — the ability to sense internal body states — between autistic children and typically developing children.

Experiment 2 (children): Autistic children showed significantly diminished interoceptive accuracy. Moderate-to-large effect size. This wasn't a small difference — it was a measurable, statistically significant gap.

Experiment 1 (adults): Autistic adults showed NO difference from non-autistic adults. The interoceptive gap was gone.

— Nicholson et al. (2019), PMID 31127487, J Autism Dev Disord 49(9):3625-3637

"Early interoception difficulties are resolved or compensated for by adulthood in people with ASD."
— Nicholson et al. 2019, Conclusion

Your child is not permanently disconnected from their body signals. The wiring is delayed, not absent.

What This Means for Your Child's
Learning Timeline

01

The current delay is developmental, not fixed.

The interoceptive gap exists because the neural pathways for body-signal awareness haven't fully formed yet. The brain finds a way — but it needs the right input during childhood. Your child's nervous system is not failing. It's developing on a different timeline.

02

External feedback is the missing piece.

If the internal signal isn't reaching the brain on time, an external signal can fill the gap. That's what the Body-Signal Learning Layer provides. Think of it like this: the BSL isn't training behavior. It's giving your child's interoceptive system the repetitions it needs to build the connection.

03

Prompt-dependency will fade as the internal signal grows.

If your child currently goes only when prompted, that's not failure. That's exactly what interoception research predicts. Over time, the external prompt becomes less necessary as the internal signal gets stronger.
