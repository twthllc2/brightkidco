The research

Interoceptive differences in autism are a real, measurable neurological phenomenon. Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi (2025), in a systematic review of 49 studies, confirmed this is not theory — it's established science. And critically, Nicholson et al. (2019) found that interoceptive accuracy changes across development, which means the childhood window exists for the brain to learn new patterns.

Interoception can be trained. Hample, Mahler & Amspacher (2020) showed that an 8-week intervention produced measurable improvement in body awareness. The BSL system creates the conditions for that training — no more, no less.
