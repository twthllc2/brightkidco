What's actually happening

Your child's brain isn't receiving the body's signal early enough.

Research from Nicholson et al. (2019) shows that autistic children process internal body signals differently. The feeling of "I need to go" arrives AFTER it's already happened — not before.

This is why everything you've tried felt like it almost worked. Sticker charts assume he can feel the signal — and chooses not to act. Timers assume the body runs on a predictable clock. These methods work for kids whose brains receive the signal on time. For kids whose brains don't, they create frustration on both sides.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer (BSL™) is different. It gives the nervous system the feedback it's been missing. That sustained signal, repeated over and over, builds the neural pathway that was never wired in the first place.
