Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi (2025) reviewed 49 studies on interoception in autism. The conclusion: diminished interoceptive accuracy in autistic children is real, measurable, and well-documented. This is settled science, not a theory. The signal from his bladder to his brain doesn't arrive the way traditional methods assume it does. They weren't designed for his neurology, and nothing you did wrong caused that mismatch.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer works with the nervous system as it is, not as a method expects it to be. It creates a gentle, sustained "uh-oh" sensation, 30 to 60 seconds of feedback that gives a deeply hypo-responsive brain time to register that something happened. For a child who has never shown a single sign of feeling wet or dirty, that sustained window of sensation is the first information his nervous system has ever received about what his body is doing. Pull-ups, by contrast, wick that signal away completely. They were engineered for comfort, not for learning. The technology that makes them convenient for parents is the exact mechanism preventing progress for children who need the feedback most.
