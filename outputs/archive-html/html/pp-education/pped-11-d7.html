What's happening neurologically

The brain learning to receive before it can act.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer (BSL™) creates a sustained sensation — a quiet "uh-oh" — that lasts 30-60 seconds after every accident. But the brain doesn't know what to do with that sensation yet. It's a new input. A new channel of information.

Week 1 is the "reception" phase — the brain learning to receive the signal before it can act on it.

Think of it like a radio finding a station. First you hear static. Then a crackle. Then a voice through the noise. Week 1 is the static phase. Week 2-3 is when words start forming.

This isn't your job to speed up. The underwear is doing the teaching. Your job right now is simply to let it — consistently, daily, without pressure.
