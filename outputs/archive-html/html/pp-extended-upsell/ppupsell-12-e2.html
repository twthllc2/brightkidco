Here's why rotation matters — neurologically

More pairs = more signal exposure.

The interoceptive signal doesn't wire itself in a week. It takes repeated exposure — the 30-60 second "uh-oh" sensation, over and over — for the brain to learn: this feeling means I need the bathroom.

Every day without the signal is a day the neural pathway isn't firing.

Pull-ups wick the signal away. One pair in the wash means half a day without practice. The math is simple: more pairs = more consistent signal exposure = faster neural wiring.

Some kids show first signals in 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal. The common thread: the underwear needs to be there, every day, for the learning to stick.
