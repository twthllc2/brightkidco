─── STORY 1 ───

"He paused. Just paused, looked at his legs."

"We'd been using the pants for about two weeks. One afternoon he was
playing with his trains, and he just stopped. Looked down. Kept playing.
When I checked, there was a small wet spot. He felt it. He'd never done
that before — in four years."

— Parent of a 4-year-old · recently diagnosed ASD

─── STORY 2 ───

"We went from 10 accidents a day to 3."

"We tried everything — timers, rewards, social stories, BCBA protocols.
Nothing worked because nothing addressed the sensory piece. After three
weeks in the Learning Layer pants, he started showing awareness. Two
months in, we went from 10+ accidents a day to three. Nine years old and
I finally understood: it wasn't defiance. He genuinely couldn't feel
it."

— Parent of a 9-year-old · ASD, sensory processing disorder, ADHD

─── STORY 3 ───

"He felt the wetness for the first time in his life."

"I'd given up. After eight years of trying everything — every method,
every specialist, every piece of advice — I had accepted that my son
might never potty train. It broke my heart, but I was exhausted.

"A friend sent me a link to BrightKidCo. I almost didn't click. I was
done getting my hopes up. But something about the way they described
interoception — nobody had ever explained it that way.

"The first week was hard. He didn't like the feeling. But by week three,
something shifted. He said 'wet.' Not clearly — but he said it. Then he
started pausing. Then he started heading to the bathroom on his own. It
wasn't overnight. It was slow. But it was real.

"He felt the wetness for the first time in his life. I cried. My partner
cried. It wasn't a full success story yet — but it was progress. Real,
visible progress. After eight years of nothing."

— Parent of a 10-year-old · ASD, non-verbal until 6
