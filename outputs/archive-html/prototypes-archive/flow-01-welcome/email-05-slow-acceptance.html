Last time we talked about why the methods you tried couldn't work — not because you did anything wrong, but because they assumed a signal your child wasn't receiving yet.

Today I want to show you the thing that's been quietly making it harder — and it's probably in your child's drawer right now.

I'm talking about pull-ups.

Here's the thing about pull-ups: they're designed to keep your child dry. That's great for sleep. But for learning? It's the exact opposite of what your child needs.

The wetness signal — that brief "uh-oh" feeling — is the only way your child's brain can connect "I peed" with "I should use the potty." Pull-ups wick that sensation away before the brain can register it. They block the feedback loop.
