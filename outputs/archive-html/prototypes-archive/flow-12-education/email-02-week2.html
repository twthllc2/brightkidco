One week in. If you're thinking "nothing happened" — you're not alone. Most parents see nothing dramatic in Week 1. And the research says that's exactly how it should work.

The interoception research tells us something important: the brain needs time to build a new sensory pathway. In autistic children, the signal between body and brain often arrives too late, too weak, or not at all. That doesn't change overnight.

What Week 1 actually accomplished:

✅ The fabric is familiar now, not foreign
✅ Your child has felt the Body-Signal Layer™ at least once
✅ The nervous system is beginning to register a new sensation

These are real neurological milestones — even if they don't look like potty training yet.

Tracking the body signal — what to watch for

Week 2 is where the subtle signs start appearing. These are the signals that your child's brain is connecting the dots:

  • Pausing — mid-play, a sudden stillness. That's the signal arriving.
  • Looking down — glancing at their underwear after peeing. Recognition starting.
  • Pulling at pants — they feel something different and want you to know.
  • Longer dry stretches — 2 hours instead of 30 minutes. The brain is learning to hold.

"He paused. Just paused, looked at his legs. It was the first time I'd ever seen him register that he had peed."

— A BrightKidCo parent, describing their Week 2 moment

One mom in our community told us: "He KNEW he was peeing." That was Week 2. Before that, she was sure nothing was working.

If you see none of these signals yet: That's also normal. Every child's nervous system adapts at its own pace. Interoception research shows that for some children, it takes 3-4 weeks for the signal pathway to form. You haven't fallen behind.

What to do this week:

  • Increase wear time gradually. Try 1-2 hours at a stretch.
  • Watch for the pause. That one-second still moment is gold.
  • Name what you see: "I see you felt something. That's your body talking."
  • Keep the toilet nearby but don't prompt. Let awareness build naturally.

Remember: internal progress can't be seen on a sticker chart. But it's happening.
