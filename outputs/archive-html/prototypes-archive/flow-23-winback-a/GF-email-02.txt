Whether your child is 3 or 10, verbal or not, still figuring out what a signal feels like or deep into the learning process — the stories that come in are surprisingly similar. They are not about quick wins. They are about small moments that add up over time.

If you are wondering whether the Body-Signal Learning Layer is worth revisiting, these voices from our Language Bank might help you decide.

Here is what other parents at every stage share.

---

"He paused. Just paused, looked at his legs. It was not an accident — it was the first time he had ever noticed his body telling him something. I sat on the bathroom floor and cried."
— Sandra, mom of a 6-year-old

"We went from 10 accidents a day to 3. Not zero, not perfect, but three. That is seven fewer laundry loads. Seven fewer moments of frustration for both of us. That is real progress."
— Marcus, dad of a 4-year-old

"It clicked, suddenly, one day. I almost quit three times. The first week was nothing. The second week he screamed when I put them on. Then one afternoon he just stopped mid-walk, looked down, and walked to the bathroom. That was four months in."
— Fatima, mom of a 7-year-old

"I was not expecting anything. My daughter is non-verbal and I have learned not to set timelines. But after six weeks of wearing them, she started touching her waistband when she needed to go. That is a signal. She found her own way to tell me."
— Elena, mom of a 9-year-old
