A LETTER FROM OUR FOUNDER
WHY I BUILT THIS
The moment I realized everything was different.
A letter from Mary S., founder of BrightKidCo.

Hi {{ first_name }},

I'm Mary — the one who started BrightKidCo. You usually hear from Lena, and you should keep hearing from her. She's the heart of this company. But I wanted to write to you directly, just this once, to tell you why this exists.

Before any of this — before the Body-Signal Learning Layer, before the factory, before the first pair ever shipped — I was just a mom standing in a bathroom, crying.

My son was six. We'd been trying to potty train for three years. Three years of sticker charts, timer sits, naked weekends, bribery, pleading. Three years of washing sheets at midnight. Three years of family members asking "Isn't he trained yet?" in that voice that made me want to disappear.

And the worst part was — I thought it was my fault. I thought I wasn't trying hard enough, wasn't consistent enough, wasn't a good enough mom.

It wasn't until a developmental pediatrician used the word "interoception" that I finally understood. My son wasn't stubborn. His brain wasn't receiving the signal that told him he needed to go. All those methods assumed a signal that simply wasn't arriving.

That moment changed everything. And it's the reason BrightKidCo exists.

I didn't build this company to get rich. I built it because when I finally understood what was really happening with my son, I couldn't unsee it. And I couldn't find a single product on the market that addressed it.

Everything was either a pull-up (which actively hides the signal) or standard training underwear (which expects the signal to already be there). There was nothing in between that actually taught the brain to feel.

So I found a textile engineer who understood sensory processing. We spent two years developing a fabric layer that could hold a tactile signal long enough for the brain to register it — without being uncomfortable, without causing rashes, without breaking down in the wash.

The first prototype was sewn in my kitchen. The first 100 pairs were packed by my family around our dining room table.

Today, 100,000+ families have used BrightKidCo. Sixteen thousand five hundred eleven reviews. Four point nine stars. And every single review — every "he paused," every "she looked down," every "I finally feel like we're making progress" — is a validation of that kitchen-table belief that there had to be a better way.

You now have those pairs in your home. And whether your child's journey takes two weeks or two years, I want you to know: you didn't fail. The methods failed you. The product landscape failed you. And I started this company to change that.

Thank you for trusting us with something so important.
