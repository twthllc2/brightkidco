# Review: Winback A (L1/L2/L3) & Winback B GF

**Flow-23:** Winback A — 9 level variants (L1 × 3, L2 × 3, L3 × 3)
**Flow-24:** Winback B GF — 3 email variants
**Review Date:** 2026-05-26

---

## Overview

Two distinct winback flows targeting lapsed subscribers:

- **Flow-23 Winback A:** Three-level progressive winback (warm → science → late-to-train). Each level has a 3-email sequence. Total: 9 emails.
- **Flow-24 Winback B GF (Good Faith):** A single 3-email winback sequence with a discount incentive (WELCOME10), designed as a lighter alternative. Total: 3 emails.

---

## Part 1: Winback A — Level Differentiation

### L1 (Warm Re-engagement) — Emails 1–3

**Strategy:** Gentle, no-pressure reconnection. Assumes the child is in the 3–5 Pre-K window.

| Email | Subject Line | Core Content | CTA | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-----|-------|
| L1-e1 | It's been a while — and that's okay | Soft reconnection, 3-bullet BSLL explainer (body signal, pull-ups, 60-day guarantee) | "See How It Works →" | Best opener. Strong bullet structure. Warm tone. |
| L1-e2 | No pressure — but Pre-K timing still matters | Pre-K deadline framing, 3+3 bundle ($79.99), cost comparison vs pull-ups | "See the 3+3 Bundle →" | Adds mild urgency via Pre-K window. Includes cost-savings angle — new angle not seen in L1-e1. |
| L1-e3 | This is my last check-in — and one final thought | Final email, 49% stat (autistic 4-5yo not trained), 60-day guarantee | "60 Days to Try It →" | Strong closing. Green accent line at signature. |

**Level differentiation: L1 vs L2/L3**
- L1 is for recently-lapsed subscribers with Pre-K aged children (3–5).
- Tone is supportive but lighter — does not dive into research citations or older-child stories.
- All three L1 emails share a consistent structure (logo → divider → hero → body → CTA → footer). No colored background sections appear in L1.

### L2 (Science/Research-Backed) — Emails 1–3

**Strategy:** Evidence-driven messaging. Targets parents who are skeptical or have been through multiple methods with their BCBA.

| Email | Subject Line | Core Content | CTA | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-----|-------|
| L2-e1 | It's been a while — here's what the research says | Kelly Mahler / interoception framing, BCBA complement positioning | "See the Science →" | Strong positioning — ABA addresses behavior, BSLL addresses sensation. BCBA partnership framing. |
| L2-e2 | Zero urgency — just a quick science refresh | Simon & Thompson (2006) citation, pull-ups wick moisture away, neurological vs behavioral | "Read the Full Research →" | Specific citation. Directly addresses parents who have "tried a lot." Bring-30-days-of-data-to-your-BCBA angle. |
| L2-e3 | Last one — then I'll step back | Final email in L2, ABA vs sensation gap, 60-day guarantee | "See How It Complements BCBA →" | Closes the science arc. Similar structure to L1-e3 but BCBA-focused. Green accent line. |

**Level differentiation: L2 vs L1**
- L2 is heavier on research citations (Mahler, Simon & Thompson).
- Directly addresses the BCBA/ABA audience — positions BSLL as a complement, not replacement.
- L2-e2 introduces the "30 days of data" concept — unique to this level.
- L2 is noticeably longer text-wise than L1.
- L2-e2 has the strongest research hook with the 2006 citation.

**Strategy:** Emotional, permission-giving messaging. For parents of older children (5+) still in pull-ups.

| Email | Subject Line | Core Content | CTA | Notes |

## Part 3: Winback Messaging Analysis

### Core Message Architecture

Both flows revolve around the same 3-pillar value proposition:

1. **The body-signal problem** — Standard methods assume interoception that many autistic children don't have.
2. **Pull-ups block the signal** — Wick moisture away, preventing brain-body feedback loop.
3. **BSLL restores the feedback** — The training underwear preserves wetness signal intentionally.

### Messaging Variations by Level

| Angle | Where Used | Strength |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **No-pressure reconnection** | L1-e1, GF-e1 | Establishes trust before selling |
| **Pre-K deadline urgency** | L1-e2 | Creates mild time pressure for 3-5 age group |
| **Cost savings (vs pull-ups)** | L1-e2 | Practical, rational appeal unique to L1 |
| **BCBA complement positioning** | L2-e1, L2-e3 | Key for the therapy-aware audience |
| **Research citation** | L2-e2 | Authority-building through peer-reviewed evidence |
| **"30 days of data"** | L2-e2 | Empowers parent to test and evaluate |
| **Story-based evidence** | L3-e1, L3-e2 | Emotional resonance through peer narrative |
| **Permission/deep validation** | L3-e3, L3-e2 | Empathetic — "stop counting days" |
| **Discount incentive** | GF-e2 | Direct conversion lever |
| **Vivid symptom language** | GF-e3 | "Sits on toilet 20 min", "plays in wet pull-up without reacting" |

### Tonal Consistency

- **Voice:** All emails stay in Lena's first-person voice (from BrightKidCo). Consistent sign-off.
- **Sign-off differences:** L1/L3 final emails use "Warmly, Lena" + green accent line. L2 final uses "Warmly, Lena" but no formal sign-off structure. L1/L2 mid-sequence emails don't sign "Warmly, Lena" — they close after CTA.
- **Inconsistency note:** L1-e3 and L2-e3 include a green accent line decorative element above footer; L1-e1/e2 and L2-e1/e2 do not. L3-e2 and L3-e3 include it; L3-e1 does not. Pattern: only the final email in a level gets the accent line. This is consistent within L1/L2/L3, but GF has no accent lines at all.

## Part 4: Structural Observations

### HTML Template Quality

All 12 HTML files follow the same well-structured, email-responsive template:
- Outlook conditional comments (VML roundrect buttons, MSO fixes)
- Two breakpoints (600px and 480px)
- Consistent font stacks (Georgia for headings, Arial/Helvetica for body)
- mso-table-lspace/rspace:0 on all tables
- Proper max-width:600px with width:100% fallback

### Recommendations

1. **GF-e2 orange CTA (#E8865C):** The color shift is notable. If this is intentional as a visual differentiator for the discount offer, keep it. If unintentional, align with the teal (#2A7A7B) used elsewhere.

2. **L2-e3 lacks the "Warmly, Lena" sign-off** that L1-e3 and L3-e3 have. Standardize final emails across all levels.

3. **L1-e3 and L2-e3 could add a social proof element** to match L3's story-based strength. The 49% stat in L1-e3 is good but L3 has deeper emotional hooks.

4. **Green accent line inconsistency:** L2-e1 does not have it (correct — mid-sequence), but L2-e2 also does not. Only L2-e3 does. This is fine as-is since the pattern is clear: accent line = final email marker.

5. **GF sequence doesn't have a "Warmly, Lena" close** in GF-e1 or GF-e2 — only GF-e3. For consistency, either add a sign-off to GF-e1/e2 or ensure this is intentional (shorter, discount-focused flow may justify less personalization).

6. **CTA label variety is a strength** — "See How It Works →", "See the Science →", "Quietly See How It Works →", "60 Days to Try It →", "The Door Is Open →". Each email has a unique CTA. No duplication across the 12 emails.

7. **L3-e2 mentions "specialty diapers"** alongside visual schedules and AAC. This is a risky reference if the brand doesn't want to acknowledge competitors. Confirm positioning intent.

### Key Strength

The 3-level architecture in Winback A is well-differentiated. L1 (Pre-K window), L2 (BCBA/science), and L3 (late-training older child) each speak to distinct parent personas with mutually exclusive messaging. No single email tries to serve all audiences, which avoids the trap of generic winback copy.

### Key Gap

No feedback mechanism between Winback B GF and Winback A. If a subscriber receives Winback A (e.g., L1) and doesn't convert, they should logically funnel into B GF with the discount incentive. But the flows as-built have no routing logic documented — they appear as independent sequences without a dependency relationship.
