📋 YOUR DAY 1 PREP TIP

When the box arrives, here's what we recommend:

1. OPEN IT TOGETHER.
Let your child see what's inside. Pull out the underwear, let them hold it. Sensory familiarity starts with the eyes and hands before it reaches the body. For some children, seeing the product before wearing it significantly reduces first-time resistance.

2. LET THEM EXPLORE THE FABRIC.
The tag, the seams, the elastic — let them touch everything. Some children need to rub the fabric on their cheek, or hold it for a while before trying it on. Follow their lead. The more familiar the fabric feels, the more comfortable they'll be wearing it.

3. DON'T AIM FOR THE TOILET TODAY.
The only goal today is wearing it. A child who tolerates the underwear for 30 minutes has already made progress. Curiosity matters more than results on Day 1. The Body-Signal Learning Layer™ starts working the moment they feel the difference between wet and dry.

WHAT TO EXPECT:
• First wear: Start with 15-30 minutes. Build up gradually.
• First accident: Don't react negatively. This is the feedback loop starting.
• First sign: A pause, a look down, touching the wet fabric — all signal recognition.
• First week: Expect nothing dramatic. Progress is internal before it's visible.

Most parents tell us the first sign of awareness comes within 7-14 days. For some children it's faster, for others slower. Both are completely normal. The nervous system needs time to learn this new signal — it's building a connection that wasn't there before.
