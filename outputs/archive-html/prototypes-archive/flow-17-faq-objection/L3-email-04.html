Every method you tried — ABA protocols, OT sensory strategies, social stories, picture cards, timed sits, rewards systems, naked weekends — every single one assumed something about your child's body that wasn't true: that the internal "I need to go" signal arrives clearly and on time.

For children with significant sensory processing differences, that signal either arrives too late, too weak, or not at all. The research is settled on this — autistic children have measurably reduced interoception. It's not a theory. It's a documented neurological difference (Nicholson et al., 2019; Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi, 2025).
