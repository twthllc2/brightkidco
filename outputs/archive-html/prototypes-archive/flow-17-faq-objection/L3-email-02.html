THE SENSORY ENVIRONMENT
"The bathroom itself is the problem"

This is one of the most real barriers, and most potty training advice completely ignores it.

For 90%+ of autistic children, the bathroom isn't just a room — it's a sensory war zone. Bright lights. Cold toilet seats. Loud flushing. Echoes. Overhead fans. Hand dryers. These aren't minor discomforts — they're genuine barriers to the nervous system regulating enough to feel the body's signals.

Some adjustments that help:

• Dim the lights or use a night light
• Put a folded towel on the seat for warmth and texture
• Let your child flush from outside the room
• Turn off the fan or overhead noise
• Use a portable potty in a preferred room initially — the bathroom can come later

These small changes make the environment feel safer, which allows the nervous system to be more receptive to internal signals. The Body-Signal Learning Layer can work regardless of where the potty is — it creates the feedback loop wherever your child is.
