YOUR 3-STEP DAY 1 PLAN

STEP 1: Open the box together
Let your child see what's inside. Pull out the underwear, let them hold it. Sensory familiarity starts with the eyes and hands before it reaches the body. For many children, seeing and touching the product before wearing significantly reduces first-time resistance.

STEP 2: Let them explore the fabric
The tag, the seams, the elastic. Some children need to rub the fabric on their cheek, or hold it for a while before trying it on. Follow their lead. Don't rush this step — the more familiar the fabric feels, the more comfortable they'll be wearing it. Some children may want to wear it immediately; others need a day or two of just holding it. Both are fine.

STEP 3: Don't aim for the toilet today
The only goal today is wearing it. A child who tolerates the underwear for 30 minutes has already made progress. Curiosity matters more than results on Day 1. If they want to wear it for an hour and then switch back to pull-ups, that's fine. You're building familiarity, not compliance.

📌 REALISTIC WEEK 1 EXPECTATIONS

• Accidents will happen. That's how the feedback loop works. The underwear preserves the signal pull-ups block — a gentle wetness cue that tells the nervous system "something happened."

• Progress is internal first. You might not see anything dramatic in Week 1 — and that's completely normal. The brain is learning to recognize a signal it couldn't feel before.

• A 'pause' is a win. If your child stops mid-accident and looks down, the signal reached them. That's the first step of interoceptive awareness.

• Don't compare. Every child's nervous system learns on its own timeline. Some children show awareness in days; others take weeks. Both trajectories are normal and healthy.

• The first sign to watch for: pulling at wet pants, pausing mid-step, looking down at the floor, or touching the fabric after an accident. Any of these means the signal is getting through.
