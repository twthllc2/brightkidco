💡 While you wait — a quick prep tip:

When the box arrives, open it together with your child. Let them touch the fabric, feel the tag, explore it. Sensory familiarity helps them feel safe with something new. The goal isn't the toilet on Day 1 — it's curiosity. Just wearing the underwear is the first win.

Here's what you can expect in the first few days:

WEEK 1 OVERVIEW:
• Day 1: Let them explore the fabric. Try wearing for 15-30 minutes.
• Day 2-3: Extended wear time. Accidents will happen — that's the feedback loop working.
• Day 4-7: Watch for micro-signals: a pause, a look down, pulling at the pants.
• First milestone: The "uh-oh" moment — when they show awareness of an accident happening.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer™ works by preserving a gentle signal that tells the nervous system "something happened." Most parents see the first sign of awareness within the first two weeks — and that looks different for every child. A pause. A look down. Pulling at the fabric. All of it counts.

The science behind it: Interoception (the 8th sensory system) is the ability to feel what's happening inside your body — hunger, thirst, and yes, a full bladder. For many autistic children, this signal is delayed or absent. Our underwear bridges that gap by providing an external sensory cue that the nervous system can learn to recognize. Over time, that external cue becomes an internal awareness.
