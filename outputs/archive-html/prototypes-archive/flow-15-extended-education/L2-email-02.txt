4–8 weeks. You’re in the window.

If you’re feeling like nothing is happening at week 4 — you’re not alone. This is the most common experience at this stage.

Here’s the mechanism truth: For children who’ve been in ABA protocols for 2–5 years, the behavioral pathways are well-established. What hasn’t been built is the sensory pathway — the interoceptive awareness that tells the body "I need to go."

The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates sustained wetness sensation that the nervous system can’t ignore or wick away. Building that neural pathway takes repetition. Week 4 is when the repetition starts to register — not necessarily as results, but as the foundation for results.

"At week 4 I was ready to give up. Nothing had changed. Then week 5 — he paused. Week 6 — he looked down. Week 7 — he walked to the bathroom. Not quickly. But he walked there."
— Jennifer, mom of Marcus, 7

What to bring to your next BCBA session:

Note any pauses or looks — even split-second
Track how long your child tolerates the Learning Layer
Note any changes in bathroom refusal or willingness
If you’re seeing nothing, bring that too — it’s useful data

Many BCBAs integrate sensation-based feedback once they see how it complements their protocols. The Learning Layer does not replace ABA — it adds the sensory dimension ABA was never designed to address.

Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years. This adds the piece that completes the picture: ABA + Body-Signal = both pathways addressed.
