Hi {first_name},

Three weeks down. If you're like most Level 1 parents I talk to, there's a date on your calendar that you're counting toward. September. Pre-K. The enrollment deadline.

Let's talk about what's realistic between now and then — because the truth is more hopeful than you might think.

Here's what the research and real parent data show: Most autistic children who wear the Body-Signal Learning Layer™ consistently show their first reliable signal within 2-6 weeks. You're right at the beginning of that window. For Level 1 kids — verbal, cognitively engaged, Pre-K age — many start showing awareness between weeks 3 and 5.

What "awareness" looks like at this stage:
✔ Pausing mid-activity after an accident starts
✔ Looking down at the underwear after going
✔ Touching the front of the underwear and making eye contact with you
✔ A delayed reaction — noticing 5-10 seconds after they've gone
✔ Shorter accidents (they start and stop more quickly than before)

These aren't failures. These are the exact micro-wins that precede full training.

Your child is in the perfect window to learn this. At 3-5 years old, the autistic brain is still highly plastic for interoceptive learning. The window for body-signal development is wide open right now.

Pre-K reality check: Many schools require daytime training, not mastery. If your child can initiate some of the time, with occasional accidents, that often meets the enrollment standard.
