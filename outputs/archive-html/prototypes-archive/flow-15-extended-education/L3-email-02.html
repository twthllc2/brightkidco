8–12+ weeks. At your child’s pace.

If you are used to hearing "give it 2 weeks" or "you will see results in a month" from other products — we are not going to say that. Because for Level 3 children, that is not how the nervous system works.

Here is what the research tells us:

Interoceptive awareness — the ability to feel internal body signals — is built through repetition of sensation over time. For children with significant sensory processing differences, this can take 8–12+ weeks before any observable change appears. Some children take 6 months. Some take longer.

And some children may never fully develop independent toileting. That is not a failure of parenting. It is not a failure of the product. It is the reality of a complex neurological profile.

"Everyone told me ‘it will click eventually.’ After 9 years, I stopped believing that. What I needed to hear was: ‘It might not click. And that is okay. You can still make progress — different progress — without it clicking.’"

— Patricia, mom of Elijah, 10

What realistic progress looks like at this stage:

  • Increased tolerance of the Learning Layer (wearing it longer each day)
  • Less resistance during changes
  • Occasional pause or look during accidents
  • More calm during bathroom-related routines
  • Any new awareness — even if it does not lead to independent toileting

These are not marketing promises. These are real outcomes families at this stage report. Each one represents months of neural groundwork.

The product will be here. However long your child needs.
