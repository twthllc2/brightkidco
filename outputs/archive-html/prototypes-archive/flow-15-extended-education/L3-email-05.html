Late is not never

Maureen Bennie’s son was 9.5 years old when he finally toilet-trained. Non-verbal. Level 3. Seven years of intensive therapy, two years of giving up, and then something shifted.

We share this not as a promise that every child will train — because some won’t, and that has to be honored too. We share it because "late is not never" is the most honest message we have for families who have been told there is no hope.

"After 9 years of nothing, I stopped believing. Then at 9.5, something changed. Not because we found the magic product. Because his nervous system finally matured enough to make the connection. I wish someone had told me that ‘late’ does not mean ‘never.’ And also that if he never trained, I would still have done enough."

— Maureen Bennie, mom of two autistic sons

Elizabeth Cross, PhD, says: "The vast majority of autistic children CAN be potty trained — it just takes longer than the methods promise."

The question is not whether your child will train on anyone else’s timeline. The question is whether your child’s nervous system has enough sensory input, enough repetition, and enough time.

You have given your child years of care, advocacy, and love. Whether the Learning Layer becomes part of a training journey or simply a support for your child’s daily comfort — you have been doing the right thing all along.

If this resonates, you know where to find us. If it does not, no hard feelings. The product will be here whenever you need it.
