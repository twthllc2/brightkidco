Three weeks. Here is a quiet check-in.

If you are reading this, you have had the Body-Signal Learning Layer in your home for three weeks now. Whatever that has looked like — daily use, occasional, or set aside — it matters that you are here.

Let us be clear about what this stage is:

For Level 3 children — non-verbal, high-support needs, children who have never signaled in 7, 8, 9 years — three weeks is not a timeline. It is the beginning of a process that may take months. And that is not a failure. That is neurology.

The Learning Layer is not here to fix anything. Your child’s autism is part of who they are. What the Learning Layer does is provide sustained sensory input to support the nervous system — at whatever stage that nervous system is at.

"We tried the Learning Layer for 3 weeks and saw nothing. No pauses. No looks. No changes. I almost threw them out. Then my husband said, ‘He did not melt down when we put them on. That is new.’ He was right. Tolerance was the first win. We did not see a signal for 11 weeks."
— Maria, mom of Daniel, 9

What counts at this stage:

Tolerating the Learning Layer being worn
Allowing the Learning Layer to be changed without resistance
Any pause — even a second — during or after an accident
Looking at the wet spot, even briefly

If you are seeing none of these, that is also normal. Some children need 8–12+ weeks before the nervous system begins to register the input. The pathways take time.

Whether this leads to training in 8 weeks or never, you have been doing exactly what your child needs. That does not change.
