“Tolerated” is a win. Let us explain why.

In the world of autism potty training, most milestones are defined by output: "child peed in potty," "child stayed dry for 2 hours," "child self-initiated."

For Level 3 families, those milestones may not be the right ones. And saying they should be is a form of pressure you do not need.

Here are the milestones that actually matter at this stage:

Tolerated wearing the Learning Layer — This is not trivial. For a child with significant sensory sensitivities, accepting a new sensation against the skin is a neurological achievement. It means the nervous system is not in defensive mode. That is foundation-building.

Allowed the Learning Layer to be changed without meltdown — This signals reduced body-based anxiety around bathroom routines. The nervous system is learning these moments are safe.

Paused during an accident — Even a split-second pause is the body sending a signal and the brain beginning to register it. This is the earliest observable sign that interoceptive awareness is building.

Looked at the wet spot — The visual confirmation of sensation. A multi-step neural process: feel → locate → acknowledge. For a child who has never shown this, it is enormous.

"He let me clean him without biting today. That is not nothing. That is him telling me he is safer. And that is worth more than any potty win."
— Jasmine, mom of Terrence, 8

If your child is tolerating the Learning Layer, you are making progress. The nervous system is doing its slow, deep work. What looks like nothing on the surface may be profound shifts happening underneath.

Keep noticing the small wins. They are not small.
