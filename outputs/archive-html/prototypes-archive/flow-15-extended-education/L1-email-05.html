Summer is the single best season for body-signal training. Here's why:

☀️ Fewer layers. Less clothing means less between the Body-Signal Learning Layer™ and your child's skin. The wetness feedback loop is faster and more distinct.

☀️ More outdoor time. Kids who are less stressed about accidents in the house are more relaxed overall. A relaxed nervous system learns faster. Take the learning outside — the garden, the park, the backyard.

☀️ Longer days. More daylight hours = more wearing time = more repetitions of the feedback loop. Every extra hour of wear is an extra hour of neural pathway building.

☀️ Pre-K runway. Summer gives you 8-12 weeks of focused, low-pressure time before the school year starts. That's a full training cycle without the weekend-only constraint.

Summer-specific tips for Level 1 families:

• Use water play as a body-awareness tool. Splash pools, sprinklers, water tables — the sensation of water on skin builds general interoceptive awareness that transfers to potty signals.

• Practice commando-style on hot days. No pants, just the Learning Layer™ underwear. Maximum sensory feedback, minimum laundry.

• Create a "potty station" outside if you're spending lots of time in the yard. A small potty in a shaded corner reduces the friction of going inside.

• Use the relaxed summer schedule to fade prompts gradually. Less rushing = more space for their internal signal to emerge.
