One month in. Let's talk about timelines — because every Level 1 parent I've worked with asks the same question: "When will I know it's working?"

The honest answer: Most parents see their child's first reliable signal between weeks 2 and 6. You're right in the middle of that window right now.

But "first signal" doesn't mean "fully trained." Here's what the progression actually looks like:

Weeks 1-2: Adjustment period. The child gets used to the wetness sensation. May not react at all. This is normal.

Weeks 2-4: First awareness signs. A pause, a look, a reaction after the accident. The child starts connecting wetness with the feeling of going.

Weeks 4-6: Emerging anticipation. The child may start and stop mid-accident, or show signs of holding. Some begin vocalizing or signaling they need to go.

Weeks 6-8: More consistent initiation for many. Dry stretches lengthen. Accidents become the exception rather than the rule.

Beyond week 8: For Level 1 kids, many reach reliable daytime training in the 8-12 week range.

--- The timeline in real life ---

"I kept waiting for a switch to flip. But it didn't happen that way. Week 3 he looked down. Week 4 he paused. Week 5 he said 'uh oh' after. Week 7 he walked to the potty on his own. Each step felt tiny until I looked back and saw how far we'd come." — Megan, mom of a 4-year-old

Wherever you are on this timeline, you're on track. Every child's nervous system processes interoceptive input at its own pace. The Learning Layer™ is doing its job every single time your child wears it.
