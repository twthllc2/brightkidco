3 weeks in. Let’s talk about sensory recalibration.

You’re three weeks into using the Body-Signal Learning Layer. Maybe you’re seeing small shifts. Maybe nothing obvious yet. Either is completely normal at this stage.

Here’s what’s actually happening underneath:

The Body-Signal Learning Layer works by creating sustained sensory input — the kind of signal the nervous system needs to build interoceptive awareness. For Level 2 kids who’ve spent years in prompt-dependent training, the nervous system needs time to recalibrate.

"We’ve been doing scheduled sits for 2 years. He never goes on his own. But at week 3 with the Learning Layer, he paused. Just paused. That’s never happened before."

— Lisa, mom of Ethan, 6

Your BCBA has built the behavioral foundation over years of work. That foundation is not wasted — it’s essential. The Learning Layer adds the sensory dimension that behavioral methods don’t address.

What to watch for this week:

  • A pause — even half a second — before or during an accident
  • Looking down at the wet spot (that’s the signal registering)
  • Any change in reaction time, however small
  • Tolerance of the Learning Layer during regular activities

These are not failures. They are the nervous system beginning to wire a connection that’s never been made before.

Timeline reality: First signals typically emerge between weeks 3–6 for Level 2 children. Some take 8 weeks. Both are within normal range. The nervous system doesn’t follow a calendar.
