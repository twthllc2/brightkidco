Here are three specific strategies L1 parents use to bridge from awareness to Pre-K readiness:

Strategy 1: Build the vocabulary bridge

Your verbal child can tell you about their day, their feelings, their favorite toy. Use that language capacity to build potty vocabulary. Name the sensation: "I see you felt something wet. That's your body telling you it's time to go." The more language you pair with the body signal, the faster the cognitive connection forms.

Strategy 2: The 3-step prompt fade

Step 1: Prompt once, wait 10 seconds. Step 2: If no response, name what you see ("I see you shifting — that might mean potty"). Step 3: Let them process. The goal is to hand the initiation over to their internal signal. Many L1 parents find that around week 5-6, cutting prompts by half actually increases initiations.

Strategy 3: The "before Pre-K" simulation

Structured potty breaks at set times (transition times, like classroom schedule). Practice telling a teacher they need to go. Role-play the sequence. The familiarity reduces anxiety when school starts.

A Pre-K success story

You're building more than potty skills

Every vocabulary bridge, every prompt fade, every classroom simulation — you're teaching your child self-advocacy. That's a skill that will serve them far beyond Pre-K.
