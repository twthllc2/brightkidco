Working alongside your medical team

For many Level 3 families, potty training is not just a behavioral challenge — it is a medical one. GI issues, chronic constipation, medication side effects, and sensory processing differences all play a role.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer is designed to work alongside medical care, not replace it.

GI Health and Interoception
Chronic constipation is common in Level 3 children. When a child is chronically impacted, the nerve endings in the bowel become desensitized — making it even harder for the brain to register the need to go. The Learning Layer provides external sensory input that can help rebuild body awareness even when internal signals are compromised.

What to discuss with your medical team:

Share what you are seeing — pauses, looks, tolerance changes
Ask how constipation treatment and sensory input can work together
If your child is on medications that affect bowel function, discuss timing of sensory input

"His GI doctor noticed improvement at his 3-month follow-up. I had not even mentioned the Learning Layer. He said the bowel pattern looked better — less withholding, softer stools. I genuinely think the sustained sensation helped regulate his system."

The Learning Layer supports the nervous system. Your medical team supports your child’s overall health. When both work together, that is when families see the most meaningful progress — at whatever pace that progress takes.
