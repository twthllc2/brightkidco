Bridging body awareness and communication

For Level 2 children who communicate through AAC, scripts, echolalia, or a mix — the potty training challenge has a double layer: not just building the body signal, but finding a way to communicate it.

The good news: Body awareness and communication can develop together. As the nervous system begins to register the sensation, that awareness can be paired with whatever communication system your child uses.

Practical approaches to try:

  • Pair the signal with a word or icon. When you notice your child pausing or reacting to the Learning Layer, model the word "potty" or tap their AAC button for bathroom.
  • Use the pause as the communication moment. That split-second hesitation — even if an accident follows — is the signal arriving. That’s when to offer the word, the picture, the gesture.
  • Create a visual bathroom routine. A simple 3-step card: 1) Feel something → 2) Touch card → 3) Go to bathroom.
  • Accept non-traditional communication. Maybe your child brings you their AAC device. Maybe they tug your hand. Maybe they stand by the bathroom door. All victories when they didn’t exist before.
"She told me, with her AAC, ‘need bathroom.’ That’s a first. She didn’t make it in time. But she FELT it and she TOLD me. That was bigger than staying dry."

— Rachel, mom of Ava, 5

Not all Level 2 children will develop verbal bathroom communication. But many will develop some form of signal-communication — and that is real progress.
