One paragraph that explains everything

There is a signal that needs to come from the body. Most potty training methods — Oh Crap, sticker charts, timers, scheduled sits, ABA protocols — assume that signal arrives. But for many autistic children, it does not.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates sustained sensory input that the nervous system cannot wick away. It provides what the body is not yet producing on its own: a consistent, recognizable signal that the brain can learn to interpret as "I need to go."

That is the entire mechanism. It is not about rewards or schedules or pressure. It is about building a sensory pathway that was never there.

"Children with autism often have misfires between their brain and their bladders. It’s not that they won’t go — it’s that the signal does not arrive in time."
— Kelly Mahler, Interoception Researcher

Whether your child is 3 or 10, verbal or non-verbal, just starting or years into trying — if the signal is not arriving, no method that assumes it does can fully work.
