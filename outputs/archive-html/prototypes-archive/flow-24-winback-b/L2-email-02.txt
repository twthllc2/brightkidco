New sensory-friendly updates

Built for children with tactile defensiveness.

Hi {first_name},

In my last email I shared the clinical rationale for pairing sensory feedback with behavioral therapy. Today I want to talk about something equally important: the fabric your child will actually wear.

If your child has tactile defensiveness, you know that underwear can be a non-starter. Seams, tags, synthetic materials — these small irritants can make wearing anything under clothing feel impossible. We've spent the past year addressing exactly that.

What's changed. The BSL Learning Layer now uses a cotton-blend fabric preferred by children with sensory sensitivities (Kyriacou, 2023). Key updates include:

- Cotton-blend fabric — softer against the skin, less friction than synthetic blends
- Gentle waistband — reduced elastic tension, designed for children who avoid pressure around the abdomen
- Tagless construction — no scratchy labels, printed care information instead
- Flat seams — minimized internal stitching to reduce tactile irritation
- Thinner learning layer — maintains signal responsiveness while reducing bulk

We made these changes based directly on feedback from OTs and parents of sensory-sensitive children. The goal was to create something a child could wear comfortably for extended periods — because the learning layer only works when it's worn consistently.

For children who currently resist underwear or pull-ups due to sensory discomfort, these updates may make the difference between refusal and willingness to try.

If your OT has recommended cotton over synthetics for your child, this addresses that clinical guidance directly.

The mechanism is the same. The delivery is better.

---

Reference: Kyriacou, C. (2023). Sensory processing and toileting in autistic children. Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 53(4), 1421-1434.
