Your experience helps others

What you've learned matters — and another parent is waiting to hear it

 

I know how busy life gets. This is just a gentle nudge.

A few weeks ago we asked how things were going with the underwear. If you haven't had a chance to share your thoughts yet — no worries. We wanted to circle back because your experience, whatever it looks like, genuinely helps.

Whether your child is just starting to notice signals — pulling at their pants, saying 'wet,' or showing you with a gesture — or things haven't shifted yet, that's real information. The parents who read reviews aren't looking for perfection. They're looking for someone who's been where they are.

When you look at progress differently — not 'fully trained' but a longer dry stretch, a pause before the accident, a glance down — every family sees something different. That's exactly what another parent needs to hear.

 

"After three methods and a BCBA who was also stuck — this was the missing piece. It's not behavior, it's sensation."

— Lisa, mom of a 6-year-old
