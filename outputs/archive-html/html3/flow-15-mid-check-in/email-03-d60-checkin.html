The real timeline

Two months is real effort — but it's not the full picture

Here's a truth that doesn't get said enough: some children show first signals within weeks. Others need 8 to 12 weeks or more. Both are within the normal range of how the nervous system works.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer is not an overnight solution. It's a neurological training tool. The 'I need to go' signal is a body-brain connection that has to be built from scratch for many autistic children. That connection can take time — but once it forms, it's permanent.

If you've seen any signal at all — even once, even for a second — the connection is forming. It may just need more time than you expected. And only you can decide whether that time is worth investing.
