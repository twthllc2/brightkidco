Which one sounds like your child? More than one is normal. The common thread across all of them is the same: the “I need to go” signal isn't reaching the brain in time. That's not defiance. That's neurology.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer keeps the wetness signal present long enough for the brain to register it. Some children show first awareness within weeks. Others need months. Both are real progress.

For kids aged 3 to 10+, the mechanism is the same. The timeline is where it varies.

Look for any signal — a word, a gesture, a pause, or simply looking down. Whether your child communicates verbally or through body language, the first sign is the same: awareness.
