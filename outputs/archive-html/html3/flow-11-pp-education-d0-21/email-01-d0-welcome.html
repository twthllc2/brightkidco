Welcome to the journey

Body-signal learning — a different kind of path forward

 

If you've cycled through methods — sticker charts, timed sits, social stories — and none of them worked, the common thread isn't you.

I hear this from parents every single day:

"My son sits on the potty for 20 minutes and nothing happens. The second the pull-up goes on — pee."

"My daughter doesn't notice when she's wet. She'll play in a wet diaper all day."

"We've been trying for years with zero progress in awareness. Nothing seems to change it."

Which one sounds familiar? (More than one is completely normal.)

All of these have the same root: the "I need to go" signal isn't reaching your child's brain in time. The body sends it — but for many autistic children, the connection between body sensation and brain awareness can be delayed. That's not defiance. It's neurology. Whether your child tells you with a word, a gesture, or a pause — the signal starts the same way.

This isn't a training bootcamp. It's a framework for understanding how your child's nervous system processes body signals — and what you can do to support that process.

Some parents see a shift in as little as two weeks. Others need eight or more. Both are completely normal. Whether your child is 3 or 12, the mechanism works the same way. Your child's timeline is the right timeline.

 

"I tried everything. Sticker charts. Timers. Rewards. Nothing worked until I realized she just couldn't feel it coming. Total reframe."

— Sarah, mom of a 4-year-old

 

Why this is different

Traditional potty training methods assume the "I need to go" signal arrives reliably in the brain. For many autistic children, it doesn't.

Research has shown that children with autism often have a reduced ability to sense internal body signals — including the need to use the bathroom. Interoception researcher Kelly Mahler describes it as the eighth sensory system. The ability to feel what's happening inside your body.

The good news: that connection can be built. The nervous system learns. It just needs the right kind of feedback — and that's what we're here to explore together over the next few weeks.

 

If this makes sense so far, there's more to come. If today isn't the day to dive deeper, that's a real answer too. The mechanism won't change.
