Pre-K countdown:
3 strategies to build momentum

Practical shifts for the weeks ahead

Hi {first_name},

We're past the 5-week mark. For many of you, Pre-K enrollment is 6 to 8 weeks away. That's close enough to feel real — and close enough that every day counts.

I've heard from parents who are starting to worry: "He's just not consistent. Good days and bad days. I don't know if he'll be ready." Let me tell you what I've seen work for Level 1 families in this exact moment.

Strategy 1

Reframe "accident" as "data"

Every accident in the Body-Signal Learning Layer is not a failure. It's the only moment the brain gets the full feedback cycle: sensation → recognition → consequence. Each time your child feels wet and pauses, even for a second, the interoceptive loop strengthens.

One mom shared: "He started to pee, then ran (still peeing) to the potty. That had never happened. He'd never had that moment of 'I need to go' in time. It was just a second of awareness, but it was new."

That's not an accident. That's a shift in signal timing. If you see any version of that — a pause, a look, a partial run toward the bathroom — that's the connection forming.

Strategy 2

Introduce "check-in" language

For verbal L1 children, the bridge between feeling and saying can be built with low-pressure language. Instead of "Do you need to go potty?" (which they've learned to say "no" to), try:

✓ "How does your body feel right now?"

✓ "Is your tummy talking to you?"

✓ "Let's check in with your body."

✓ "If your body needed to go, what would it feel like?"

This shifts the focus from performance (did you go?) to awareness (what do you feel?). Over time, this small language shift creates a framework the child can use to identify the sensation before it becomes urgent.

Strategy 3

Talk to the school early

One of the most relieving things you can do right now is have a direct conversation with the Pre-K program about their actual requirements. Many schools are more flexible than their websites suggest.

You might ask: "My child is working on body-signal awareness for potty training. He has some awareness but still has accidents. Would that be okay for enrollment?"

Knowing the answer reduces anxiety — and often the answer is yes. Parents who've done this report that schools appreciate the proactive communication and the specificity of the approach.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is enough awareness for the school to say yes. Many schools say yes far earlier than parents expect.

You have more control than you think in these next weeks. Every day of consistent use builds the connection. You're moving in the right direction.
