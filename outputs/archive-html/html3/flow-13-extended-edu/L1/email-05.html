Why warm weather, fewer layers, and more time outdoors help build the connection

Hi {first_name},

This is my last check-in for this series — but not the end of the road. I want to leave you with something that could make all the difference for the weeks ahead: summer is genuinely the best season for body-signal learning.

Here's why: fewer layers of clothing mean the wetness signal is more noticeable. Warmer temperatures mean less resistance to wearing fewer clothes. More outdoor time means less pressure around "stay clean" expectations. And for many families, summer means a looser schedule — more time at home, more flexibility with accidents, less rushing.

One mom I remember put it simply: "Week 12: 80% daytime success. He could go to public bathrooms (sensory challenge for him before). Pre-K accepted him." She started in early summer. By fall, the connection was solid enough for school.

How to make summer work for you

Go commando under shorts at home

More skin exposure = more sensation. If your child is comfortable, let them wear just the BSL underwear or loose shorts with no outer layer. The direct feedback accelerates awareness.

Use outdoor time for low-pressure practice

In the backyard or at a quiet park, accidents are less stressful. Keep a change of clothes handy. The less tension around accidents, the more the brain can focus on the signal.

Hydration works in your favor

Kids drink more in warm weather. More fluid intake means more opportunities for the brain to practice the recognition cycle. Every accident or near-accident is a learning moment.

Wherever you are — you're further than 2 months ago

I want to take a moment to name what's true: whether your child is having more accidents than dry moments, or whether you've seen real shifts, you are not the same parent who started this journey two months ago.
