Four to eight weeks — the realistic signal window

One month of body-signal learning. Here's what to expect and when to adjust.

 

{first_name} — one month in. You're at the point where many parents start asking: "Should I be seeing more by now?"

The honest answer for Level 2 kids: most start showing first body-signal awareness somewhere between week 4 and week 8. Not full independence. Not initiation. But awareness — a pause, a look down, a hand to the underwear.

Your BCBA has been working for years on the behavioral layer. The scheduled sits, the data tracking, the prompt fading — that foundation is not wasted. What's different now is that for the first time, your child's nervous system is receiving feedback it can learn from.

As one mom shared: "We'd been doing the 90-minute sit protocol for 18 months. He'd sit. He wouldn't go. Then he'd wait until I changed him into a pull-up, and within 5 minutes, he'd pee. Classic prompt-dependency, his BCBA confirmed. After 5 weeks with the 3+3 bundle, he started pulling at the front of the underwear right before an accident."

If you're at week 4 and seeing nothing — that's within the normal curve. Simon & Thompson (2006) showed that undergarment type directly affects training outcomes, but the timeline depends on the child's sensory profile. Kids with more significant interoceptive differences may take 10-12 weeks. Some need longer.

The question to ask yourself is not "is my child trained yet?" It's "is my child registering the wetness sensation more than they were at week 1?" If the answer is yes — even once — the learning loop has started.

 

Troubleshooting with your BCBA

If you're at week 4-6 and want to bring data to your next BCBA session, here's what to track:

• How long after an accident does your child notice? (Seconds? Minutes? Not at all?)

• Any pauses, touches, or looks after wetting?

• Tolerance time — how long before requesting a pull-up or showing distress?

• Does your child go on scheduled sits but not initiate?

Many BCBAs incorporate sensation-based feedback into protocols once they see results. The data from weeks of body-signal wear gives them something they've never had: evidence of whether the child's nervous system is registering elimination.

"I brought my tracking notes to Ethan's BCBA appointment — she said she'd never seen this kind of data before. We adjusted his protocol to combine the scheduled sits with the wetness-awareness window she could now see."
