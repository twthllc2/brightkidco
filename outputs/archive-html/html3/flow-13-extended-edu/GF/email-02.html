You're about a month in. If your child hasn't shown any awareness yet — please hear this: that is completely normal and does not mean it isn't working.

Body-signal learning is different from behavioral training. The nervous system needs time to build a connection it never had before. Some children show first signals in two weeks. Others need eight weeks. Some take twelve. All of those are real, valid progress timelines.

The mistake most parents make at this point is thinking "nothing is changing." But the changes at this stage are internal — and they're happening whether you can see them or not.

What "progress" looks like by week 4

• No visible change yet. That's okay. The brain is still learning to recognize the signal.

• A single pause — just a second where your child stops mid-accident — is a signal that awareness is beginning.

• Your child looks down at the wet spot or touches the underwear after an accident. That's interoception starting.

• Dry stretches are getting slightly longer — 45 minutes instead of 30. That's the signal arriving a little earlier.

• Your child tolerates the underwear for longer without asking for the pull-up. Sensory adjustment is happening.

If none of those are true yet — keep going. Week 4 is still early in a 2-to-12 week window.
