Why the timeline matters

The body sends the signal. But for many kids, the brain doesn't catch it in time. Our underwear preserves that gentle wetness signal so the connection can form. It takes time for a nervous system to learn something it's never done before.

Some kids show first signals within two weeks. Some need eight weeks. Some take twelve. All of those are within normal range. The nervous system learns on its own schedule — and that schedule is different for every child.

What matters is what you're looking for. Not "is he trained yet" — but "is he noticing anything new?"
