At this stage — about 7 weeks in — I want to address something that often comes up for parents of high-support children: how does body-signal learning fit with everything else your child has going on medically?

Many Level 3 children have a full medical team — a GI doctor, neurologist, BCBA, OT, speech therapist, pediatrician. And some of the challenges your child faces are medical, not just sensory. Withholding, chronic constipation, impaction — these are real GI issues that need medical management.

I want to be clear: the Body-Signal Learning Layer is not a medical intervention. It does not treat GI issues. It is not a replacement for your medical team.

What it is: a sensory tool that helps the nervous system register wetness in a way that pull-ups and diapers don't. That is all. And that is enough.

How to talk to your medical team about body-signal learning

If you're considering mentioning the underwear to your child's doctor or therapist, here's a framework:

The framing: "This is additive — it works alongside everything we're already doing. It's not a replacement for medical management or therapy."

Most doctors and therapists will understand the value of body awareness training. Many of them wish they had more tools for interoception work.

The underwear supports:
