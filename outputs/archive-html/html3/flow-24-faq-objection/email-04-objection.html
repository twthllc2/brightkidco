When your partner isn't on board

This is more common than you'd think. And it makes sense — your partner is protecting your family from another disappointment. Another method that didn't work. Another thing that felt like a solution but wasn't.

Here's what I'd suggest: don't frame this as "I found the solution." Frame it differently. Say: "I found something that explains why nothing has worked. Can I show you the mechanism before we decide anything?" The mechanism is neutral. It's not another training method — it's an explanation for why training methods failed. That's a conversation, not a sales pitch.

And if your partner still isn't convinced — the 60-day guarantee exists for exactly this reason. It's not a risk. It's a try.
