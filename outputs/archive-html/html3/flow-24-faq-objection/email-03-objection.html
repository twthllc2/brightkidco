How pull-ups block the signal

Pull-ups are designed to wick moisture away. That's great for sleep and comfort. But for learning? The wetness signal is how the brain connects "I peed" with "I should use the potty." Pull-ups block that connection before it can form.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer works differently. It preserves a gentle wetness signal for 30-60 seconds — just long enough for the brain to register it. For a child with sensory sensitivities, this is key: the signal is present but not overwhelming. Research found that autistic individuals consistently prefer cotton for its sensory properties (Kyriacou et al., 2023). That's why our underwear uses cotton-based fabric with flat seams and no tags.
