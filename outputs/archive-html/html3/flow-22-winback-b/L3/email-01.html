What we know about the signal gap

Traditional potty training methods assume an internal signal arrives reliably — that the child feels the "I need to go" sensation. Research has shown that children with autism often have a reduced ability to sense internal body signals. The signal either arrives too late, or it doesn't arrive at all.

This is not defiance. It is not something you failed to teach. It is a signal-timing gap inside the nervous system. The Body-Signal Learning Layer is designed to preserve a gentle wetness signal for 30-60 seconds so the brain can start building the connection between the feeling and what it means — at whatever pace your child's neurology allows.

Some kids show first signals in 2 weeks. Some need 8 weeks or more. Some respond, some don't. You'll know within 60 days. That's what the guarantee is for.
