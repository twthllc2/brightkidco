The Mechanism

Signal time is learning time

Your child's body sends the signal. But for many autistic children, the brain doesn't catch it in time. The connection is there — it's just not formed yet. And like any connection, it can be built.

The more hours your child wears the underwear, the more opportunities the brain has to learn the signal. One pair limits you to a few hours a day. Six pairs mean you can rotate through wash cycles and keep the signal present all day.

Research has shown that the type of undergarment a child wears directly affects potty training outcomes (Simon & Thompson, 2006). Sensory awareness of accidents is essential to the learning loop (Azrin & Foxx, 1971). The mechanism is grounded in decades of research.
