What makes this different

Every method you've tried — sticker charts, timer sits, reward systems, social stories — assumes one thing: that your child can feel the signal when their body says "I need to go." For children whose nervous systems process body signals differently, that signal isn't arriving the way standard methods depend on.

This isn't about behavior. It's about signal-timing. The Body-Signal Learning Layer is designed to create a gentle feedback loop that standard pull-ups and underwear can't provide — helping the brain connect the sensation with the action, at your child's own pace.

Some kids show signs of body awareness in 4 weeks. Others take 8 to 12 weeks. Both are normal. Progress looks different for every child — a pause before the accident, a look down at the wet spot, a hand on the pull-up. Those are real wins.
