The BrightKidCo customer does NOT have a potty training problem. She has a **body-signal translation problem.** Her child's nervous system does not reliably translate the internal sensation of "I need to go" into the action of using the toilet. Everything she has tried — sticker charts, ABA protocols, Oh Crap, social stories, timers, bribes — has failed not because of lack of effort, but because she was solving the wrong problem. (Source: MASTER-INGESTION-COMPENDIUM.md §1)

**The reframe that powers every email:** "It's not behavioral. It's neurological."

**The core insight (single most important sentence in the entire business):**

## 5. Key Strategic Decisions

### Decision 1: 4-Level Segmentation, No More, No Less

**Decision:** Exactly 4 avatar levels (L1, L2, L3, GF). No additional segments for emotional dimensions in Phase 1.
**Justification:** More segments would create complexity that kills execution before we have data. The 4 levels map to real neurological differences in the child. Emotional dimensions (Deadline, Social, Overwhelmed) *inform copy* but don't get separate tags or flows until Phase 2 at earliest. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §1; MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §5.1)
**Risk:** GF is ~50% of real volume — 5 sub-groups inside it. Migration strategy is essential to get 65-75% to level-specific paths.

### Decision 2: Lena Voice as Primary Brand Voice, Not Founder

**Decision:** Every email ends with Lena S. (Customer Support, Mom of 2 autistic sons). Founder Mary S. appears ONLY in a dedicated email in Phase 2.
**Justification:** Peer-parent voice yields 40-60% higher engagement than expert/founder voice. Trust increases 200%+. The customer needs "a mom who lived this" not "a founder who built this." (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §3.1; GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §7)
**Risk:** Brand becomes person-dependent. Lena must be a real, sustainable role.

### Decision 3: Pop-Up for Level Capture (Not Multi-Step Quiz)

**Decision:** 1-step pop-up with 4 symptom-based buttons. Level tags assigned from button selection.
**Justification:** Single-step reduces friction. 4 buttons cover the 4 levels. Symptom-based language ensures self-identification accuracy. Multi-step quiz would increase abandonment. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §10)
**Risk:** Level assignment accuracy — never tag below 70% confidence. Uncertain cases go to GF.

### Decision 4: 60-Day Guarantee (Not 30-Day as Website Shows)

**Decision:** Email program says "60-Day Money Back Guarantee." Website currently shows 30-day.
**Justification:** GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md and all diagnosis docs specify 60-day. This is critical for Level 2 (hyper-skeptical) and Level 3 (dignity-first) conversion. The 30-day on the website is a P0 discrepancy that needs resolution. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §6; Layer-38-brand-constants.md §2)
**Resolve with:** Yurishizu — does the website need updating to 60 days, or do the docs need correcting to 30?

### Decision 5: Welcome Flow is Level-Specific, Remaining Phase 1 Flows are Cross-Level

**Decision:** Only the Welcome Flow has 4 level variants in Phase 1. Cart Abandonment, Browse Abandonment, Checkout Abandonment, and transactional flows use single cross-level copy calibrated by 6 rules (R1-R6).
**Justification:** Welcome Flow sets the frame and must resonate precisely. Abandonment/transactional flows need speed over precision — one well-calibrated email works for all levels if it follows the 6 calibration rules. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §3-4; MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §4.3)
**Risk:** Cross-level emails that violate calibration rules destroy trust with specific segments. R1 violation (labeling the level) is worst.

### Decision 6: Email Moat as Primary Competitive Advantage

**Decision:** Invest heavily in quiz-based capture, avatar-segmented nurture, educational content, and Lena persona voice — because zero competitors do any of this.
**Justification:** The email marketing gap between BKC's planned architecture and every competitor is the single largest competitive moat. No competitor uses: quiz-based segmentation, educational email nurture, or persona-voiced automated sequences. (Source: 02-competitor-landscape.md §7; 16-executive-master-plan.md §1.4)
**Risk:** Competitors can copy. Need to build the moat while continuously innovating.

### Decision 7: Anti-Fabrication Rules Are Non-Negotiable

**Decision:** Every email must contain 30-50% authentic verbatim parent language, 30-50% Lena voice, 10-20% mechanism/brand language. Minimum 2-4 authentic quotes per email. Never fabricate.
**Justification:** Trust is the only asset this business has. One fabricated quote destroys everything. The autism parent community is hyper-vigilant — they will catch and expose inauthenticity. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §9; MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §3.5)
**Risk:** Slows down email production significantly. But non-negotiable.

### Decision 8: Phase 1 is Level-Only, No Emotional Dimensions

**Decision:** Emotional dimensions (Deadline, Social, Overwhelmed) inform copy selection in Phase 1 but do NOT get separate tags or flows.
**Justification:** Complexity management. Insufficient data in Phase 1 for reliable dimension tagging. Level × Phase × Dimension would create 48+ cells — impossible to manage. (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §1.4; GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §1.1)
**Risk:** Some nuance lost for Deadline Pressure moms (25.2% of audience) who convert at 15-25%. Can address through copy tonality within level-specific flows.

### Decision 9: Domain Warming First, Full Program Second

**Decision:** 6-week domain warming before any promotional content. Welcome + transactional only in weeks 1-2.
**Justification:** Sender reputation is zero — no emails have been sent from this domain. Warming protects deliverability for the long term. Code Red protocol (Double Opt-In → Gated Welcome → VIP-only sends) ready if reputation drops. (Source: MASTER-DIAGNOSIS-COMPENDIUM.md §7.1)
**Risk:** Delays revenue from welcome flow conversion by ~6 weeks.
