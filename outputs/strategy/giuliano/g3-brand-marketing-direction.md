## 1. EXECUTUTIVE SUMMARY

BrightKidCo occupies a unique position in the market: **no competitor combines reusable training underwear + wetness learning signal + autism/sensory focus.** (Source: Research Compendium §1.1 — Competitive Landscape; Diagnosis Compendium §2.3 — Competitive Position)

The brand exists to solve a Body-Signal Translation Problem — not a behavioral problem. Every "failed" method assumed the child could feel body signals their nervous system literally cannot detect. (Source: Ingestion Compendium §2.1 — Autistic Parent Core Profile; Diagnosis Compendium §1.8 — Deep Psychology)

**The opportunity in numbers:**
- 49% of autistic 4-5 year olds not toilet trained — 6x the rate of neurotypical children (Wiggins et al. 2022, CDC-funded SPARK study)
- Zero competitors targeting autism parents with educational email, quiz-based segmentation, or persona-voice sequences
- Zero paid media spend currently targeting this audience — 6-12 month first-mover advantage
- 15+ Facebook groups (200K+ members), 5+ subreddits (65K+ subscribers), millions of underserved parents

## 2. BRAND IDENTITY — VISUAL SYSTEM

### 2.1 Brand Foundation

**Who BrightKidCo IS:**
- Founded by Mary S. on her personal journey; led by Lena (Customer Support), Mom of two autistic sons
- Clinically informed by science (Mahler, SPARK), emotionally raw — knowledge-based, not hope-based
- Peer-to-peer, never corporate — autism is identity, not disease

**Who BrightKidCo is NOT:**
- A "wellness brand" with positive vibes
- An "expert brand" that knows everything
- A "miracle product brand" with breakthrough promises
- Polished, perfect, predictable

(Source: Brand Identity Guidelines §1.1 — Brand Foundation; Ingestion Compendium §2.2; Context Notes §B1)

### 2.4 Photography & Imagery Style

**Photography direction:**
- Real children, real moments — never stock photography of generic toddlers
- Autism-friendly aesthetics: muted backgrounds, soft lighting, no flash
- Focus on micro-moments: child looking at wet spot, parent's relief, small victories
- Body-language photography: non-verbal cues, gestures, sensory interactions
- NEVER: crying children, frustrated parent faces (exploitative)

**Illustration characters (owned by BKC):**
Melon Pop, Lily Pop, Fynn Fox, Coco Cloud, Milo Wale, Pip Penguin, Lumi Unicorn, Bloomie, Raffy, Moonie, Benny Buddy — these are exclusive brand assets. No competitor has character-based content. (Source: Research Compendium §1.4; RA1-R5 Social Recon §5)

(Source: Brand Identity Guidelines §5 — Photography; §6 — Illustration)

### 2.5 Logo Usage Guidelines

**Primary logo placement:**
- Email headers: top center, max height 60px for mobile
- Social profile photos: square crop, full logo on white/green background
- Website header: left-aligned, links to homepage

**Do NOT:**
- Change brand green (#039902) to any other color
- Place on busy photographic backgrounds without cream (#FBF7EB) backing
- Use with drop shadows or 3D effects
- Add taglines, slogans, or decorative elements

(Source: Brand Identity Guidelines §4 — Logo Usage; Layer 33 §5 — Email Design)

### 3.4 Lena Sign-Off Rules

**Three variants — choose by context:**

| Variant | Text | When To Use |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **SHORT** | "Thanks for being here. If you have a question, hit reply. You'll get me — not a template, not a bot. I read everything. Lena" | Welcome E1, transactional emails |
| **MEDIUM** | "I have two autistic sons. One's 6, still in the middle of it. The other took us until he was almost 8. If your kid is anywhere on this curve — that's normal here. Lena" | Emotional / education mails |
| **LONG** | "Whatever you choose — thanks for reading this far. If this isn't the day, that's okay. The product will be here next month. So will I. I have two autistic sons. I know what it's like when 'later' turns into another year. But I also know what it's like to push too early. Trust your read. Lena" | Cart Recovery, Last Day |

**Non-negotiable rules:**
- Sign-off body: MAX 2-3 sentences
- P.S.: MAX 2 lines
- NEVER mix Mary S. (founder) into Lena sign-off
- Sign-off closing words per position: Emails 1-2 → "Talk soon,", Emails 3-5 → "With understanding,", Emails 6+ → "With care,"
- EVERY email ends with Lena — NEVER founder framing

(Source: Brand Voice Reference §9 — Sign-Off Variants; Giuliano Demands §7.3 — Sign-Off Decision Tree)

```

║                                                                              ║
║  GATE 1: SCROLL-STOP (0-2 seconds)                                          ║
║  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐   ║
║  │ MUST trigger "That's me" OR "That's my kid" within 0.5 seconds.      │   ║
║  │ Uses parent verbatim language, symptom-specific scenario.             │   ║
║  │ ❌ "Tired of potty training struggles?" (generic — doesn't stop)     │   ║
║  │ ✅ "By 10am he peed on 7 couch cushions." (specific — stops)         │   ║
```

### 7.4 UGC Collection System

**Collection pipeline:**
1. **Trigger:** Post-purchase Day 28 email — "How's it going? Share your story."
2. **Collect:** Direct reply to Lena (email), #MyBrightKidCo hashtag (social), or dedicated form
3. **Permission:** Simple permission form — "Can we share your story on our channels? Yes / No"
4. **Incentivize:** Featured parent gets $50 gift card + free bundle refill
5. **Categorize:** Tag by level (L1/L2/L3/GF), by emotion (relief, win, struggle), by format (photo, video, text)
6. **Repurpose:** UGC → email testimonial → social post → ad creative (Spark Ads) → landing page

**Anti-fabrication rules for UGC:**
- NEVER fabricate testimonials. If you cannot find a real parent quote, FLAG IT.
- Combine themes from multiple real quotes = allowed (note internally)
- Change first names and ages within range = allowed
- Heavy adaptation to fiction = FORBIDDEN
- UGC must follow the same integration quota: 30-50% verbatim parent language

(Source: Influencer & UGC Engine §9 — Anti-Fabrication Rules; Giuliano Demands §9 — Anti-Fabrication)

### 7.5 Influencer Creative Direction

**Creator brief must-haves:**
1. Hook with a specific scenario (NOT "potty training is hard")
2. Mention the 49% stat or body-signal learning mechanism
3. Show the product in real-life use (NOT staged studio shots)
4. Include a micro-win moment (first awareness, looking at wet spot)
5. End with "Try it for 60 days — by your judgment" (NEVER "Buy now")
6. Tag @brightkidco and use #MyBrightKidCo + #autismpottytraining

**Creators MUST NOT say:**
- "Easy", "Simple", "Quick", "Guaranteed"
- "This will fix your child"
- "Finally a solution that works for everyone"
- Any Level labels ("Level 1/2/3") — symptom language ONLY

(Source: Influencer & UGC Engine §6 — Creator Brief Template)
