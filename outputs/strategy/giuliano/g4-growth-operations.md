### 1.5 Parent Ammunition Scripts

**Source: Synthesis 18 §7**

Three scripts that equip parents to get BCBA buy-in:

**Script A — The Mechanism Script** (for ABA-loyalist BCBA):
> "I read about body-signal learning underwear. It works with interoception — something ABA doesn't address. I want to understand if you see this as complementary to our protocol. My goal is to follow your lead, but I want to make sure we're not missing the sensory piece."

**Script B — The Data Script** (for skeptical BCBA):
> "We've been doing the protocol for X months and we're not seeing body awareness. I found something that claims to train the interoceptive signal — research from Nicholson 2019 and Hample/Mahler 2020. Can we trial it alongside our current program for 60 days and track the data?"

**Script C — The Trial Script** (for the direct conversation):
> "I'm not asking you to endorse a product. I'm asking if we can add this as an adjunct for 60 days. If the data doesn't show improvement, we stop. Here's the research summary."

### 2.4 Forwardable Email Strategy

**Source: Synthesis 20 §5**

Create 5 forwardable email templates — one per partner archetype. Each template:
- Is addressed FROM the mom TO her partner (she forwards it)
- Explains the mechanism in plain language (for The Skeptic)
- Includes the cost reframe (for The Frugal)
- Positions BKC as complementary to ABA/BCBA (for The ABA Loyalist)
- Requires zero action from partner (for The Disengaged)
- Is non-confrontational (for The Overrider — avoids triggering power struggles)

**Decision:** Build forwardable email templates accessible via a "Share with your partner" button on the product page and in Welcome Flow E3/E4.

### 2.5 Partner-Focused Landing Page Elements

**Source: Synthesis 20 §6**

Add a "For Skeptical Partners" micro-section to the product page containing:
1. Mechanism explanation in 3 bullet points (for the Skeptic)
2. Side-by-side cost comparison: Pull-Ups $876/yr vs BKC $79.99 one-time (for the Frugal)
3. BCBA complement positioning: "Here's how it fits with your existing therapy" (for the ABA Loyalist)
4. The 60-day guarantee front and center: "If it doesn't work, you get every penny back"

### 2.6 Email Copy Rule — The "You Decide Alone" Framework

**Source: Synthesis 20 §10, L6 Lever**

The single most important copy rule for partner-sensitive emails:

> **"60 days. By your judgment. You don't need anyone's permission to try something that could help your child. If it doesn't work, every penny back — no one needs to know you tried."**

This permission frame must appear in:
- Welcome Flow E4 (when trust is building for Level 2-3 segments)
- Cart Abandonment E2 (when price objection + partner block activate together)
- Every email to Level 2-3 segments where partner conflict is highest

### 4.4 Return & Warranty Handling

**Source: Synthesis 30 §4.0**

**Guarantee policy:** 60-Day Calm Progress Guarantee. Full refund if parent doesn't see progress.

**Return handling scripts by scenario:**
- **Product doesn't work:** Full refund, no questions. "This wasn't the right tool for your child's nervous system."
- **Child refused to wear:** Full refund. "Some kids need time. 60 days gave you the data you needed."
- **Size issue:** Free exchange. "Let's get the right fit."
- **Damage/defect:** Immediate replacement + apology + $10 credit.

**Decision:** Never argue a return. The guarantee is the brand's most powerful trust signal. Less than 1% of purchasers use it — honoring it without friction reinforces trust. **Source: GD §9.6 (note: "less than 1% use it" is flagged as potentially unverified — confirm with data)**
