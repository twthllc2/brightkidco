### SECTION 2 вҖ” NEW SCIENCE CITATION (The "Updated Offer" Anchor)

**Line 4, Fresh Citation (New Science Angle per S2 В§1.21 Strategy)**

One finding from that review stood out: body signal differences are present across the full autism spectrum, from children who are verbal and academically on track to children with high support needs. It's not a "severe" thing. It's an autism thing.

A 2019 study from Nicholson and colleagues tested this directly: autistic children showed significantly reduced accuracy in sensing their own heartbeat, a basic internal signal that most people process unconsciously. The researchers called it "diminished body awareness," and it resolved by adulthood. In other words: the signal arrives. It just arrives late. And that delay, those missed milliseconds, is the entire reason conventional potty training struggles.

**Line 5, Pull-Up Signal Block (New Angle on "Why Now")**

And here's where pull-ups add another layer: their moisture-wicking technology, the feature parents pay a premium for, actively removes the one external signal the brain could use to learn. Pull-ups keep the child completely dry, which means the only feedback the body can register ("something happened") is wicked away before the brain can process it.

It's not that your child won't learn. It's that the feedback loop has been interrupted at both ends, the internal signal arrives too late, and the external signal is engineered away.

## 2.4 CUSTOM CONTRAPTION

### The "Neural Germination" Visual System

A custom illustration series that uses botanical germination as a visual metaphor for body signal learning:

**Contraption Element 1, The Seed in Dry Soil (Hero)**
- Illustration: A single seed in cracked, dry earth. No visible moisture. The seed is intact but nothing is happening.
- Text overlay: "Right seed. Wrong conditions."
- Color palette: Warm browns (#8B6914), dry ochre (#D4A76A)
- Significance: Represents the child's intact body signal potential in conditions not designed for it.

**Contraption Element 2, The Neuron-Seed with Signal Thread (Mechanism Section)**
- Illustration: A seed splitting open, with a delicate thread (neural pathway) reaching downward toward roots while a signal pulse travels upward.
- The seed is half botanical (roots/soil) and half neural (axon/dendrite at the sprout tip).
- Color palette: Transition from earth tones to teal/green (#2A9D8F) at the sprout
- Significance: The BSL creates the "moisture" (gentle sensation that lasts) that lets the body signal seed finally germinate.

**Contraption Element 3, Three Sprouts of Different Heights (Testimonial Section)**
- Three small potted sprouts at different growth stages:
 - Sprout 1 (L1): Small but visible green shoot above soil
 - Sprout 2 (L3): Strong root visible below soil, modest above-ground growth
 - Sprout 3 (GF): Multiple small shoots from one seed, varied, organic
- Color palette: Varied greens, chartreuse (#CCFF00), deep forest (#1B4332), sage (#84A98C)
- Significance: Wins look different on different timelines. Each sprout is growing; none is "done."

**Contraption Element 4, The Signal Pulse Ring (CTA section ghost element)**
- A subtle, very faint ring graphic around the CTA button, suggesting a ripple or signal pulse emanating
- Animation: Very slow pulse (3-second cycle), the signal is arriving
- Color: Transparent white overlay on the teal CTA button

## 2.5 COLOR NARRATIVE

### GF Inclusive Palette

The GF variant uses a **neutral-warm base with teal accent**, deliberately avoiding:
- Pink/purple tones (stereotypical "mom" marketing)
- Bright primary colors (child-focused, not parent-focused)
- Clinical white/blue (hospital association)
- High-contrast pairings (anxiety-triggering for overwhelmed parents)

| Color | Hex | Role | Rationale |
|-------|-----|------|-----------|
| **Warm Sand** | #F5E6D3 | Background | Inclusive base вҖ” skin-tone neutral, non-clinical, evokes calm |
| **Deep Teal** | #2A9D8F | Primary accent (CTA, links) | Trust color вҖ” bridges clinical (blue) and organic (green). Matches BKC brand. |
| **Stone Gray** | #6B7280 | Body text | Readable, non-aggressive. Softer than pure #333. |
| **Warm Amber** | #D4A76A | Recognition anchor icons | Recognition + warmth without alarm. |
| **Charcoal** | #1F2937 | Headings | High contrast with warm sand background. |
| **Sage** | #84A98C | Testimonial quote borders | Calm, organic framing for parent voices. |
| **Off-White** | #FAFAF8 | Quote card interiors | Soft contrast to sand background. |
| **Terracotta** (ghost) | #E07A5F | P.S. accent | Gentle warmth, never alarm. One small accent per email. |

**GF-Specific Principle:** No color that suggests level labels. Avoid clinical green (associated with medical/therapy settings) as primary. The palette should feel like a living room, not a clinic.

### Motion Philosophy

- **Staggered reveals for testimonials**, creates a "conversation" feeling rather than a wall of text.
- **Reduce motion preference respected**, `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` disables ALL animations, showing static layout.

### GF-Specific Motion Rules

- No fast animations (вүӨ0.3s feels urgent/aggressive, GF has low urgency)

- No parallax or complex CSS 3D transforms (rendering issues in Outlook/older email clients, and can trigger vestibular issues in some parents)
