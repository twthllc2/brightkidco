### Key Typography Decisions for L2

1. **NEVER use bold**, The brand rule (Weight 400 only) is especially important for L2. Bold/emphasis says "marketing." L2 parents read bold as a sales signal. Use italics for testimonials, spacing for hierarchy.

2. **Wider line-height (1.6)**, L2 emails can run 500-800 words. The extra leading gives the eye room. The tonality calibration for L2 specifies "deliberate, evidence-based" sentence style, the typography should support a measured reading pace.

3. **Stat highlight uses size, not weight**, The "49%" stat is rendered at 22px instead of bold. This follows the brand rule while creating visual hierarchy through scale.

4. **Forbidden: exclamation marks**, The L2 typographic rule extends to punctuation. No excitement markers. Periods and em-dashes only.

5. **Email-safe fallback**, Questrial fails in Gmail and Outlook. Use `Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif` as fallback chain.

## 7. HIDDEN EASTER EGG

### The "Two-Dot" Signature

In Lena's sign-off block, between "With care," and "Lena", there is a subtle typographic gesture: two dots, rendered in white at 40% opacity, positioned to the right of the closing line.

```
With care, · ·
Lena —,
```

**Meaning:** Two dots, representing Lena's two autistic sons. One slightly higher than the other (one trained at 6, the other at almost 8). They are off-center, asymmetrical, intentionally not perfect. Like progress with neurodivergent children.

**Visibility threshold:** Visible at normal reading distance (not hidden in HTML). Legible enough to be seen, subtle enough not to demand attention. An L2 parent, who is the most detail-oriented, BCBA-aware reader, will notice it on the second or third read, and the asymmetry will register subconsciously as "this wasn't designed by a marketer."

**Cross-email continuity:** The two-dot motif appears in all Winback Path B emails (L2 variant), but each email uses it slightly differently:
- E1: Dots equal size, equal spacing (first contact, balance)
- E2: Dots slightly farther apart (widening gap, second contact)
- E3: One dot higher, one lower (asymmetry, finality of difference, acceptance)

## 8. MOTION / ANIMATION

### One Gentle Motion: The Signal Dot Pulse

The dot at the end of the Signal Bar (top of email) has a single animation:

```css
@keyframes pulse-dot {
 0% { opacity: 0.6; transform: scale(1); }
 50% { opacity: 1; transform: scale(1.15); }
 100% { opacity: 0.6; transform: scale(1); }
}
.signal-dot {
 animation: pulse-dot 3s ease-in-out infinite;
}
```

**Why this matters for L2:**
- The pulse is slow (3s cycle), not a rapid blink (which reads as alarm or interruption)
- The scale change is minimal (1.15x max), perceptible but not demanding
- The opacity fade is gentle, like a heartbeat, not a notification
- **Starts by default**, loads already pulsing (no click required)
- **Stops on hover**, when the reader engages, the motion pauses (respects her agency)

**Why one animation, not more:**
- L2 parents have sensory-overloaded lives. Too much motion = overwhelm = unsubscribe.
- The single pulse echoes the "sustained 30-60 second signal" the product itself creates, motion as mechanism metaphor.
- No scrolling parallax, no reveal animations, no hover effects on CTAs. L2 readers scan for information; motion that serves understanding is welcome, motion for decoration is noise.

**Fallback:**
If animated GIF/AMP isn't supported by the client: static image of the tapered line + dot with micro-copy. No motion lost, the metaphor survives without animation.
