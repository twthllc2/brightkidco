## CREATIVE STRATEGY

### Big Idea

**"The signal doesn't lie"**, Sensory honesty over salesmanship.

This email abandons all persuasion tactics built on urgency, scarcity, or social proof. Instead, it bets entirely on one thing: the interoception mechanism itself. The new science citation (49-study review) acts as the structural spine, if the mechanism is real, the product is just the delivery system for that mechanism. The copy doesn't convince. It just presents better evidence.

For L1 Sarah at Day 67, the persuasion loop has already closed. She's read the Welcome flow (8 emails), she's been through the FAQ Library, she's seen Winback E1. Any additional attempt to "convince" her would be noise. The only remaining move is to lower the barrier to a point where her own curiosity overrides her hope-fear.

> Rationale: Winback E2 can't repeat Welcome or E1. For L1, the Pre-K deadline was already raised in E1, repeating it would feel desperate. The new angle is: better evidence + lower risk.

### Contraption

**The "Two Doors" choice architecture.**

Oppose another brand's winback that would say "Last chance, 20% off expires tomorrow," this email builds a visual and rhetorical contraption where both paths are valid.

Door 1 ($34.99, 1 pair): "Just test the signal." No commitment, no routine change, no method. Pure curiosity purchase.

Door 2 ($79.99, 3+3 Bundle): "This the full approach." The bestseller. The proven path.

The contraption works because:
1. Door 1 feels like cheating, she's buying something that barely counts as buying
2. Door 1 removes the "decision weight" that's been paralyzing her for 67 days
3. Once Door 1 is in play, her brain switches from "should I buy?" to "which door?"
4. Many L1 moms will pick Door 1 and upgrade to Door 2 within 14 days once the signal shows

This the "Yes, and..." contraption, not a hard fork but an invitation to start anywhere.

> Rationale: Contraption is specific to the E2 "updated offer" requirement. E1 had one offer (3+3 Bundle). E2 adds the low-barrier option, a structural shift that creates the two-door effect.

The number 67 is precise (not "about two months"), which creates a subtle trust violation-negative space, the reader expects vagueness from a brand. Getting the specific number right is a micro-proof attention.
