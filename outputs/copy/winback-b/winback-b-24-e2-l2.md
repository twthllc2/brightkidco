## Motion / Animation

### Animated Elements (Email-Compatible)

Since most email clients do not support CSS animations beyond simple GIFs, motion is used minimally and only where it enhances the L2 reading experience:

1. **Reveal sequence (default: static):** Each section could be designed as a "scroll-triggered reveal" in a web version, but in email, sections render statically. The section hierarchy (Hook → Science → Timeline → BCBA → Offer → Testimonial → Permission) creates an implicit reveal sequence: each section builds on the last.

2. **No GIF for this email type.** Winback emails, especially for L2, benefit from stillness. Animated GIFs signal "promotional" to L2 readers, triggering skepticism. The email reads more like a research update than a marketing promotion, and that stillness is intentional.

3. **If animated CTA (future web implementation):** The text-link CTA could have a subtle underline animation on hover, the underline extends from left to right over 0.3 seconds. This would signal interactivity without the aggression of a button pulse or bounce. For email, the underline is static.

4. **Guarantee callout (static-optimized):** If CSS-animated email were possible, the guarantee box border could gently fade in 0.5s after the offer text renders, creating a "and by the way, there's zero risk" reveal moment. In practice, all email clients render it immediately, which is functionally equivalent.
