**"The Final Invitation, Clear, Warm, Unhurried."**

**Font Stack:**

## DECORATIVE FURNITURE

**Minimal, every element earns its place.** No decorative flourishes that don't serve the message.

1. **Top accent line:** A thin 2px horizontal line below the logo in the terracotta accent (#B55A3B). Subtle, warm, confident. Sets the tone that this a designed experience, not a mass email.

2. **Social proof left bar:** 4px vertical bar on the left side of the testimonial block, in terracotta (#B55A3B). Signals that this a quoted/borrowed voice, not brand copy.

3. **Guarantee panel soft border:** 1px border in sage green (#D0E0D0) around the guarantee section. Soft enough to not shout, present enough to frame the risk reversal.

4. **Section divider dots:** Between major sections (recap → social proof → product), a centered row of three small dots (· · ·) in the footer gray (#8A8580) at 70% opacity. Creates breathing room without heavy horizontal rules.

5. **Lena's signature flourish:** Below the sign-off, a subtle SVG signature stroke in warm brown (#2C2420) at 60% opacity. A simple swoosh that reads as a hand-signed letter without needing to load an actual signature image.

**Anti-patterns avoided:**
- ❌ No confetti, stars, or celebration graphics (this a soft close, not a party)
- ❌ No countdown timers or urgency clocks (L1 allows urgency in copy tone, but the *design* should not scream)
- ❌ No product photography in the email itself (redirecting to site for imagery; keeps email lightweight and loading fast)
- ❌ No gradient backgrounds (competes with the clean, resolved feeling)

## HIDDEN DETAIL / EASTER EGG

**The "3+3" Micro-Animation in the CTA Button**

When the email is viewed on a client that supports CSS animations (Apple Mail, iOS Mail, Outlook for iOS):

The CTA button has a subtle, slow (3-second cycle) pulse on the border, a gentle, warm glow in the sage green (#D0E0D0). It's not a flashy animation. It's a warmth signal. A heartbeat. A reminder that the offer is still alive.

On clients that don't support animation, the button simply renders as a solid, high-contrast green CTA, no degradation, no broken experience.

**Hidden text easter egg for those who read every line:** The first letter of each section header spells "L-E-N-A." (Hook → Hook, E,no, wait... let me not. That requires a different approach.)

**Better easter egg:** In the social proof section, the three parent quotes each start with a word whose first letter matches the three-layer system: **S**ignal (Sarah quote starts with "He says", not ideal), let me just embed it in a different way.

**Final easter egg:** In the guarantee panel, if a subscriber reads the fine print carefully, they'll notice the text reads: "Your judgment, your timeline, your call.", the first letters of each phrase (Y, Y, Y) don't spell anything. But the *last* letters of each phrase (t, e, l) quietly spell "tel", short for "tell me." It's a subconscious nudge to hit reply.
