#### VALIDATION (L2-Specific Symptom Recognition)

You've been told pull-ups are "part of the process." The pediatrician said it. The ABA consultant said it. The Facebook groups all recommend them. And you've watched your child sit in a pull-up for hours — dry, comfortable, and completely unaware.

You're not wrong to use them. Every parent does. But here's what no one told you:

Your BCBA's protocol assumes your child can feel when they need to go. Pull-ups were designed to keep kids comfortable during potty training **for neurotypical kids who can already feel the signal.** For a child with interoceptive differences — a child whose nervous system doesn't reliably register bladder fullness — pull-ups don't just fail to help. They actively prevent learning.

---

#### MECHANISM (The Pull-Up Reframe — L2 Deep)

The science is clear: undergarment type directly affects toilet training outcomes. Simon & Thompson (2006) demonstrated that the type of underwear children wear during training influences how quickly they acquire the skill. The mechanism is simple — the brain needs feedback to learn.

For autistic children with interoceptive differences, that wetness signal is the ONLY feedback the brain can use. It's not a nice-to-have. It's the entire curriculum.

Pull-ups remove that feedback entirely. The moisture-wicking layers — engineered by billion-dollar companies to keep children "dry and happy" — pull the sensation away before the brain can register it. Your child stays too dry to learn. The interoceptive connection that should be forming — *"I feel something warm → that means I peed → I should use the potty"* — never gets a chance to build.

Nicholson et al. (2019) showed that autistic children have significantly diminished interoceptive accuracy. The signal from bladder to brain arrives — but milliseconds too late, or too faintly to register. Pull-ups don't give those sluggish milliseconds a fighting chance.

The $50-100 you're spending every month on pull-ups? You're paying to keep your child's brain from learning.

**"The signal your child needs is the one pull-ups take away."**

Every visual choice reinforces this reversal: comfort vs signal, dryness vs learning.

### HIDDEN DETAIL / EASTER EGG

**The "Uh-Oh" Textured Background.**

Across the Body-Signal product intro section, the background uses an extremely subtle repeating pattern — a 2mm grid of tiny, barely-visible "uh-oh" text strings rendered in `opacity: 0.03` in the warm amber color. It's not visible to the casual reader. But if someone zooms in or inspects the email, they'll find the word "uh-oh" repeated hundreds of times — a hidden reference to the "30-60 seconds of gentle 'uh-oh' sensation" that is the core mechanism of the Body-Signal Learning Layer.

**Why:** The L2 parent reads everything. She's hyper-researching, hyper-skeptical. Finding this hidden detail rewards her thoroughness and signals: *this brand is built by people who care about the small things.* It's a brand trust signal for the most skeptical segment.

Also: The "60-day guarantee" badge has a tiny footnote asterisk that links to — wait for it — a single sentence at the very bottom of the footer: `* We mean it.` Only visible on inspection. Another signal for the hyper-skeptical L2 parent.
