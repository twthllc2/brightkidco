### Validation

Interoception researcher Kelly Mahler calls it the "eighth sensory system", the internal sense that tells your body when you're hungry, thirsty, or need the bathroom. For most of us, these signals arrive automatically. We feel the tug of a full bladder, and we act on it.

For autistic children, this sense works differently.

Not missing. Not broken. Different.

The signal from bladder to brain follows a neurological pathway that operates on a different frequency. Your child isn't ignoring the feeling, they may not be receiving it at all, or it arrives milliseconds too late to act on.

### Mechanism

Let me give you a number that stopped me cold: **Only 19% of autistic children can recognize when they're thirsty.**

Think about that. Thirst is one of the most basic internal signals — a survival cue. If a child can't feel thirst, how can we expect them to feel a full bladder?

The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates a sustained, gentle "uh-oh" sensation that lasts 30-60 seconds after every accident, giving the brain enough time to register: *something happened. That feeling connects to that action.*

This why prompt-dependency is so common in L2 kids. Compliance isn't awareness. The protocol can't bridge the interoception gap. The Body-Signal Layer can.

### Product Intro

The Body-Signal Learning Underwear is a 3-layer system, and each layer has one job:

**Layer 1, Signal Creation (cotton inner)**
The cotton inner allows a gentle "uh-oh" sensation to reach the skin, not overwhelming, not cold, not alarm-based. Just 30-60 seconds of gentle sensation that lasts that says *"something happened"* to a nervous system that might otherwise miss it entirely.

**Layer 2, Protection Without Blocking (smart absorption)**
Enough absorbency to protect furniture and clothing, but not so much that it wicks away the learning signal. Unlike pull-ups, which are engineered to keep kids 100% dry, Layer 2 preserves exactly enough moisture for the brain to register the event.

**Layer 3, Leak Resistance Without Diaper-Mode (outer barrier)**

**"The bridge across the interoception gap."**

A visual showing a neural signal traveling from bladder to brain, but instead of a straight path, there's a gap in the middle. The Body-Signal Learning Layer appears as a warm, luminous bridge spanning that gap, transforming a broken dotted line into a solid, glowing connection.

This not a "before/after" transformation. It's a *mechanism reveal*, the parent finally sees WHY it hasn't worked and HOW this different.

### Motion / Animation (client-constraint aware)

BrightKidCo's email program must account for varied ESP capability. The following motion treatments are **enhancements**, the email must function perfectly without any animation.

**Hero illustration pulsing (enhancement):**
- The bridge glow in the Interoception Gap Diagram pulses between 70% and 100% opacity over 3 seconds, looped gently
- Implementation: CSS `@keyframes pulse { 0% { opacity: 0.7; } 50% { opacity: 1; } 100% { opacity: 0.7; } }` on the SVG glow layer
- Fallback (no CSS animation support): Static image at 85% opacity, still communicates the bridging concept

**Stat number reveal (enhancement):**
- The "19%" stat fades in with a 0.3s ease-in when the email loads (if CSS animations are supported)
- Implementation: `animation: fadeIn 0.3s ease-in;`
- Fallback: Stat is visible immediately, no loss of information

**CTA button micro-interaction (enhancement):**
- On hover (desktop): Button background shifts from `#D4893D` to `#B87325` (10% darker amber) over 0.2s
- On mobile: No hover state, tactile feedback comes from the tap interaction
- Implementation: `transition: background-color 0.2s ease;` on the `<a>` or `<td>` element

**Typography restrictions:**
- No animated text (no sliding, no typing effects, too distracting for scientific content)
- No parallax, no scroll-triggered animation (not supported in email clients)
- No auto-playing video/GIF in thero, static illustration only (GIF autoplay is unreliable across clients)

**Client compatibility:**
- Apple Mail (iOS/macOS): Full CSS animation support, all enhancements work
- Gmail app: No `<style>` block support, all enhancements degrade gracefully to static
- Outlook desktop: No animation support, static fallback on everything
- Samsung Mail, Yahoo: Partial support, test before deployment
