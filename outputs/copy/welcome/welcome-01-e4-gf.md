### Mechanism
### Mechanism

Here's what all three families have in common: their children weren't getting the body signal.

Interoception — the eighth sensory system — is how your brain knows when you're hungry, thirsty, or need the bathroom. For children with sensory processing differences, this signal is delayed, weakened, or absent. Research confirms this is a real, measurable neurological difference.

The Body-Signal Learning Layer creates that feedback — a gentle sensation that lasts 30-60 seconds after every accident. Enough time for the brain to register: *something happened*.

### Product Introduction

The BrightKidCo Body-Signal Learning Layer is a 3-layer system built for sensory-sensitive children:

- **Layer 1 (Cotton Inner):** 100% cotton, the fabric research consistently shows autistic individuals prefer (Kyriacou et al. 2023). Creates a gentle, noticeable "uh-oh" sensation that lasts 30-60 seconds, sustained enough for a slow to pick up signals brain to register. 
- **Layer 2 (Smart Absorption):** Protects furniture and floors without wicking the signal away completely. Unlike pull-ups that keep kids "too dry to learn," this layer gives the brain feedback. 
- **Layer 3 (Outer Barrier):** Leak-resistant outer layer so parents can actually leave the house while the body-signal learning happens. 

PFAS-free. BPA-free. Designed alongside OT practitioners who understand sensory processing. 

**For reference:** some kids show first signs in 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both normal. The key is repetition, 30-60 seconds of sensation after every accident, building neural pathways over time.

## Layout Architecture

**Beyond single column, a responsive testimonial showcase layout:**

## Custom Illustration / Contraption (Unique Visual Element)

**The "Signal Bridge" illustration**, a custom visual at the top of the mechanism section showing: a child figure on the left (nervous system represented as a warm glow in the body), a dashed pathway from body to brain, with a small "Bridge" device (styled as a soft glowing ring around the waist). **The "Three Doors" visual micro-narrative:** Hidden in thero section's background pattern (visible on desktop, subtle on mobile) are three tiny door icons, slightly different shapes (one arched, one square, one rounded). ## Motion / Animation (Client-Constraint Aware)

**Email animation is severely limited by client support, this design respects that while maximizing what CAN move:**

| Element | Animation | Clients | Constraint |
|---------|-----------|---------|------------|
| Hero section | **None** — static image | All | Hero uses a static warm lifestyle photo. No GIF animation in hero (Gmail strips autoplay on many GIFs, Outlook doesn't support) |
| Testimonial cards (Apple Mail / iOS) | Gentle 0.8s fade-in on scroll into viewport | Apple Mail, iOS Mail, Outlook iOS app | Supported via CSS animation. Falls back to static render in Gmail/Outlook desktop |
| "Signal Bridge" illustration | Subtle pulse glow on the waist ring (CSS animation: 3s infinite alternate opacity .7→1) | Apple Mail, iOS Mail only | Degrades gracefully to static illustration in Gmail/Outlook |
| CTA button | **None** — static button | All | CTA animation is a not-worth-it risk. Button copy and color carry conversion weight |
| Divider rules | **None** | All | Decorations must not delay render time or break layout |

**Motion philosophy for BrightKidCo:** Calm, not flashy. A pulsing glow or a soft fade-in feels appropriate for a sensory-aware brand. Bouncing, sliding, or spinning elements would violate the tone of dignity and calm that Lena's voice establishes.

**Dark mode consideration:** Use Apple Mail's `@media (prefers-color-scheme: dark)` to swap thero background from #F5EDE3 to #2A2723 (warm dark), ensuring the sand color reads as "warm dim" not "muddy." Testimonial cards shift from white to #2D2D2D. The Signal Bridge glow shifts from amber to a soft cyan for better visibility on dark backgrounds.
