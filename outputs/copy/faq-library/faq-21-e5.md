## OBJECTION HANDLING FRAMEWORK

### Objection B2 — "My BCBA has a protocol. I trust that more."

| Step | Body Section | What It Does |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **1. NAME** | "Your BCBA has been doing the hard work for years." | Opens by validating the BCBA relationship. NEVER criticizes. |
| **2. VALIDATE** | "ABA addresses the behavioral component... What it can't address is the sensory component." | Distinguishes behavioral from sensory without dismissing either. |
| **3. REFRAME** | "Your BCBA can't prompt a sensory signal into existence." | Names the limitation of behavioral-only approaches factually. |
| **4. ABSOLVE** | "Your BCBA leads. We add alongside. Show them the data after 30 days." | Gives permission with a concrete bridge — brings BCBA into the loop. |

**NEVER say:** "ABA doesn't work for potty training," "Your BCBA missed this," "BCBA protocols are flawed." Always complement, never compete.

### Objection NEW-2 — School/IEP/Daycare Pull-Up Policy

| Step | Body Section | What It Does |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **1. NAME** | "Your school or daycare has policies based on neurotypical timelines." | Names the institutional barrier without blaming the institution. |
| **2. VALIDATE** | "Pull-ups actively remove the wetness signal your child needs to build body awareness." | Explains WHY the policy undermines progress. |
| **3. REFRAME** | "Underwear under the pull-up: daycare sees the pull-up, your child still feels the feedback." | Offers the proven bridge strategy. |
| **4. ABSOLVE** | "Your school has policies. We work around them." | Permission — you don't have to fight the system, just navigate it. |

### Objection B4 — GI/Medical Withholding

| Step | Body Section | What It Does |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **1. NAME** | "Your GI doctor manages the medical piece — withholding, constipation, ER visits." | Names the medical reality directly, not minimized. |
| **2. VALIDATE** | "That's critical care. But the same interoception gap that blocks pee signals also blocks poop signals." | Validates medical need while connecting to sensory mechanism. |
| **3. REFRAME** | "Your GI doctor can't prescribe body awareness." | Names the gap without dismissing medical care. |
| **4. ABSOLVE** | "Your GI doctor manages the medical piece. We handle the sensory learning layer alongside." | Alongside positioning — never "instead of." |
