### L1-Specific Design Decisions

1. **Yellow `#F5C84B` used as accent throughout**, divider, bullet points, urgency cues. Cross-level defaults use more muted accents; L1 gets brighter, more energetic touches that signal forward momentum without being aggressive.

2. **CTA button at 18px bold**, larger than the 16px standard. L1 Sarah is deadline-driven and benefits from a more prominent action target. 

3. **Section spacing at 24px**, tighter than the 32px cross-level default. Creates faster vertical scan velocity, matching L1's sense of Pre-K urgency. 

4. **Mechanism section lighter than L2**, L1 doesn't need deep science or Kelly Mahler citations. Simple "here's why" mechanism language suffices. 

5. **Two short testimonials** vs one longer one, L1 scans faster. Two quick proof points from relatable L1 moms. 

6. **Pre-K anchoring in subject line + body**, directly addresses L1's primary emotional driver (deadline pressure) without being manipulative. 

7. **No guilt, validation only**, The email never says "we miss you" or "come back." It validates the pause and repositions it as part of the learning process. 

8. **"By your judgment" clause**, The permission-not-to-buy framing that is the #1 trust builder for L1. 

9. **WELCOME10 auto-apply**, removes friction. L1 Sarah wants speed and simplicity. Auto-applying the discount signals "we made this easy for you." 

10. **"Starting again isn't starting over"**, the emotional core of the email. Directly counters the "I failed/wasted money" objection that silent L1 lapsed buyers carry.
