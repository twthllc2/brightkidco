### MECHANISM (Body-Signal Refresher)

> The Body-Signal Learning Layer works by creating a gentle, sustained wetness sensation — 30 to 60 seconds of feedback that gives a hypo-responsive nervous system time to register: *something happened.*
>
> Pull-ups wick that signal away in seconds. Regular underwear doesn't create enough of it. The three-layer design is calibrated specifically for autistic children whose brains process body signals on a different frequency.
>
> When a child stops wearing them — whether after a week or a month — that learning pause is real, but it's not permanent. The neural pathways that started forming don't disappear. They just go dormant.
>
> Starting again isn't starting over.

## BIG IDEA

**"Starting again isn't starting over."**

After 90 days away, Sarah needs to hear that the investment she made wasn't wasted — that the time away was part of the process, not a reset. The big idea reframes a "failed attempt" as a "learning pause" and positions the restart as a continuation, not a do-over.

This aligns with L1's hope-intact profile (70/30 hope/realism) and responds to her Pre-K deadline-driven urgency. The visual narrative is: *the bridge is still there — you just stepped off for a rest.*
