## EMAIL COPY

### PREHEADER

Just a quiet check-in. You decide what's next.

---

### BODY

It's been a while since we last wrote. You bought a pair of Body-Signal underwear — and maybe they're sitting in a drawer. Maybe you tried them once or twice and it didn't click. Maybe you never even opened the package.

That's okay.

You know your child better than anyone. If the timing wasn't right, or if the sensation was too much, or if life simply got in the way — none of that is failure. It's information. You've spent years gathering information about what works and what doesn't for your child.

This is just one more data point.

---

### WHAT THE BODY-SIGNAL LEARNING LAYER DOES

The Body-Signal underwear works by creating a gentle, sustained sensation when wetness happens — 30 to 60 seconds of feedback that a deeply hypo-responsive nervous system can register. It's not a quick fix. It's not a replacement for your current routine.

It's a signal.

For some children, that signal is the first time their brain has ever received the message: "Something happened." The pause. The look down. The moment before the meltdown used to start — that's not nothing.

For others, the signal still isn't enough. And that's real, too.

---

### 1 PAIR — $34.99

If you still have the pair you bought, they're ready when you are. Wear them alongside your current diapers — no need to replace anything. Just add one layer of information to your child's day.

If you need another pair, or a different size, they're here.

**1 Pair: $34.99**
Free shipping over $35.

---

### THE GUARANTEE

60 days. No questions. By your judgment.

If the Body-Signal underwear doesn't do anything for your child — if you try them consistently for two months and see nothing — full refund. Not because we expect that. But because you should be able to try something new without adding another disappointment to the pile.

You've earned the right to be skeptical. We mean it when we say your judgment is the only one that matters.

---

### CLOSING

This is the last email in this sequence. Not because we're giving up on you — but because we respect your timeline.

If you're ever ready to try again, the product is here. The science is here. I'm here.

And if not — you've been doing right by your child every single day. No email from me changes that.

With care,

Lena
Customer Support · Mom of two autistic sons

## CREATIVE STRATEGY

### Emotional Arc

**Start:** Quiet — Acknowledging the lapse without blame.
**Middle:** Informational — Mechanism reminder, dignity-first framing.
**End:** Open — Permission to do nothing, permission to try again, permission to close the door.

E3 completes the winback arc from "reminded" (E1) → "considering" (E2) → "deciding on my own timeline" (E3).

### Tonality Settings

| Element | Setting |
|---|---|
| Hope/Realism Split | 20% / 80% |
| Urgency | ZERO — never present |
| Empathy Intensity | Very High |
| CTA Aggressiveness | Very Low — permission-not-to-buy |
| Science Density | Medium — mechanism explained, not debated |
| Sentence Style | Short, spacious, dignified cadence |
| Forbidden Elements | Exclamation marks, "fix"/"cure", cheerleader language, timeline promises, urgency of any kind, "don't give up!", "he deserves better" |

### Subject Line Rationale

**"Checking in — no pressure"** — Validation hook with permission frame. L3 subject lines must never carry promise, urgency, or outcome language. This subject line does exactly what it says: checks in, removes pressure. Aligned with the Winback A E3 L3 subject line specified in S2 §1.16.

### Section Structure

```
PREHEADER — Permission context
↓
HOOK / DIGNITY ACKNOWLEDGMENT — "It's been a while. That's okay."
↓
MECHANISM REMINDER — "The signal still exists. The science hasn't changed."
↓
PRODUCT — "1 Pair $34.99. Add alongside. Don't replace."
  (Product appears AFTER mechanism — never before)
↓
GUARANTEE — "60 days. No questions. Your judgment."
↓
CLOSING — "Door is open. You decide."
↓
SIGN-OFF — "With care, Lena" + P.S.
```

### Offer Rationale

**1 Pair $34.99** — As specified in the L3 offer architecture (26-pricing-offer-architecture.md §10; 15-level-messaging-matrix.md §4.7). Single pair framing removes commitment pressure. "Add alongside" language ensures the parent feels no need to replace their current system. Cost reframe is NEVER used for L3 (Medicaid reality — ~40% of L3 audience has partial pull-up coverage; cost math feels tone-deaf).

### Key L3 Compliance Checks

- [x] Zero urgency — no deadlines, no countdowns, no "time is running out"

- [x] Product after mechanism — mechanism explained before product is mentioned
- [x] 1 Pair $34.99 — correct offer for L3
