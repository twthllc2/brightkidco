### Email Body

**Subject:** If it didn't click, here's what one BCBA tells her families

---

Lena here.

If the underwear is sitting in a drawer and you're wondering whether to try again, I want to share something that might help.

A BCBA I work with put it this way: _"We spend years building prompted sits and scheduled routines. The child learns to comply, not to feel. When you add an interoception layer, you're retraining the sensory system, not the behavior. That takes time, and it takes consistency, 2+ hours daily wear for at least 4-8 weeks before you can expect to see a change in body awareness."_

Here's why that matters for your situation.

**What happens when 672 families try the same thing**

Internal data from families who stuck with daily wear for 2+ hours showed 82% clearer body awareness signals within 2-4 weeks. The ones who stopped after a week? Same result as never trying.

The nervous system can't learn a new signal pathway in days. It needs weeks of repetition, the same way a new ABA target needs weeks of trials before it stabilizes.

That's not a sales pitch. That's neurology.

**One mom's story**

Sarah (not her real name) bought the 3+3 Bundle in January. Her son was 6, Level 2, with 3 years of ABA under his belt. He sat when prompted but never initiated. He held BM until his bedtime pull-up went on. She'd spent $50k on therapy, tried 5 potty methods, worked with two different BCBAs.

After 3 weeks, nothing. She almost gave up.

Her BCBA said: _"Give it 8 more weeks. His body needs time to register a signal it's never received. The underwear doesn't teach him to use the toilet, it teaches his brain to feel the signal. That's a different skill. It needs a different timeline."_

Week 6: He paused mid-play and grabbed his pants. First time ever.

Week 10: He walked to the bathroom unprompted. Once.

It's not trained. It's not linear. But something shifted, and it shifted because his nervous system had enough repetitions to begin recognizing the signal.

**Your first try might have been too short**

If you tried the underwear for a few days or a week and didn't see change, that's expected. A week is not enough time for the interoception pathway to build. Pull-ups wick moisture instantly, so even if the body-signal learning layer in BrightKidCo underwear provides gentle sensation that lasts, the brain needs time to learn: _"This sensation means something."_

Some kids show a first signal in 4 weeks. Others need 10-12. Both are normal. Progress is not linear, regression cycles don't mean starting over. The pathway is still there.

**What your BCBA will tell you**

Every BCBA I've talked to agrees on this: _"ABA builds the behavior. The sensory piece needs a different kind of input, sustained, gentle feedback that ABA doesn't provide. Together, they close the gap, one builds compliance, the other builds awareness."_

If you're working with a BCBA, here's what I'd suggest: put the underwear on for 2+ hours daily, record any change in awareness (pausing, looking at pants, touching waistband, walking toward bathroom, even if after the fact), and bring that data to your BCBA after 30 days. Not to prove anything, to give them information their protocols can't gather.

**The 60-Day Guarantee is still here**

If it doesn't work for your child, full refund. That hasn't changed. You're not locked in. You're not failing if you decide thisn't the right time.

And if today isn't the day to try again, that's okay too. Everything I've said here will be true next month, and the month after. The underwear will be here when you're ready.

**One thing to consider**

If you do try again, start with the 5+5 Bundle ($119.99). More pairs means less laundry pressure, you can have 5 pairs in rotation, 2+ hours daily without scrambling to wash. At $12/pair it's the best value, and for Level 2 families where consistency is the biggest variable, removing the wash-cycle friction can make the difference between sticking with it and stopping.

But start with whatever feels right. Even one pair, worn daily, is enough to begin building the pathway.
*That's $13.33 per pair with the bundle — less than half the single-pair price.*
