## Email Body

### Section 1: Shipment Notification
Good news, your Body-Signal Learning Layer underwear shipped today. Your tracking number is below so you can follow the package as it makes its way to you.

Depending on where you are, delivery usually takes 3-7 business days. Some carriers update tracking faster than others, if the number doesn't show movement right away, that's normal and usually updates within 24-48 hours.

### Section 2: What's in the Package
Your package from BrightKidCo contains your Body-Signal Learning Layer underwear, designed with three layers:
- A cotton inner layer that creates a gentle, sustained signal
- A smart absorption layer that protects without blocking the feedback
- A leak-resistant outer barrier

### Section 3: When It Arrives — First Steps
When the package arrives, here's what helps: Wash the underwear before first use, cold water, gentle cycle, no fabric softener. The Body-Signal Layer works best when the cotton is clean and absorbent.

Then let your child wear them for 1-2 hours at home. Don't prompt. Don't pressure. Let the sensation do the teaching. The learning starts from the first wear, each time the Body-Signal Layer creates that gentle "uh-oh" sensation, the brain gets a piece of information it may never have received before.

### Section 4: What to Expect in Week 1
Week 1 is about familiarity, not progress. Your child might not react at all at first, that's normal. The nervous system needs time to recognize a new sensation. Some kids show first awareness in 2 weeks. Others need 8 weeks. Both are normal.

The goal in the first week isn't the potty. It's awareness. If your child pauses for a moment when they feel wet, even once, that's the signal starting to wire.

### Section 5: You Made the Right Decision
If you're wondering whether this will work, you don't have to hope. You have 60 days to see what happens. The guarantee isn't a sales tactic. It's the only honest thing to offer when the outcome depends on your child's nervous system. If you don't see the shifts you're hoping for, you get every dollar back.

## Creative Strategy — Visual Direction

### The Big Idea
The email is structured like a package journey, a dotted trajectory line travels from left to right across the email header, beginning at a warehouse icon and ending at a home icon with a glowing "delivered" pin behind the CTA area. The visual metaphor: a signal is traveling toward your home, just as the Body-Signal Learning Layer will help a signal travel toward your child's brain.

### Layout Architecture
**Archetype:** Custom single-column with a split personality, top half is tracking/transactional information (direct, functional), bottom half is warm Lena education (organic, breathing room).

**Above the fold:**
- Package icon (small, top-left) + "Your order shipped!" headline
- Tracking number in a rounded, bordered callout card (emerald hairline border, cream fill)
- Estimated delivery window: "Arrives in 3-7 business days"

**Below the fold:**
- "What's in your package" section with a gentle illustration of the 3-layer system (exploded view, minimal line art, not product photography)
- "When it arrives" section with a small wash-icon tip
- "Week 1 expectations", short, spacious paragraph
- Lena sign-off with profile subtitle
- Footer (standard)

**Mobile collapse:** All cards stack vertically. The tracking callout card becomes full-width with increased padding (24px side padding vs 16px desktop). Font sizes scale down 2px for body text. The 3-layer illustration simplifies to a stacked icon set on mobile.

### Custom Illustration / Contraption
**The Package Journey Line.** A horizontal dotted SVG path running across the top section of the email:

```
🏭 · · · · · · · 📍 · · · · · · 🏠
[Warehouse] [In Transit] [Your Home]
```

The line has small signal-wave bumps drawn along the dotted path, a subtle visual callback to the "signal traveling to the brain" concept. Each wave bump is a soft emerald at 40% opacity.

**The 3-Layer Exploded View.** Below "What's in your package," a minimal line-art SVG showing three concentric ovals slightly separated, with labels:
- Layer 1 (innermost): Signal Layer, Gentle cotton feedback
- Layer 2 (middle): Smart Absorption, Protection without blocking
- Layer 3 (outer): Leak Barrier, Security without diaper-mode

Style: Ethereal Glass meets Soft Structuralism from the design reference, thin emerald outlines with subtle cream fills, no harsh lines.

### Color Narrative
**Palette for this email: Warm cream anticipation, soft, expectant, calm.**

- Background: Warmer cream #F9F7F4 (slightly off-neutral for reading comfort)
- Primary accent: Emerald #039902 (the BrightKidCo signal color, used sparingly for the tracking callout border and the journey line dots)
- Secondary accent: Soft amber #E8B84B (warmth of anticipation, used for the home icon glow and the "arrives" badge)
- Text: Dark charcoal #2C2C2C (body), slightly lighter #555555 (secondary info like tracking number)
- All borders: hairline (1px) in emerald at 50% opacity
- Pure black and white avoided throughout, backgrounds are tinted cream, not white; text is charcoal, not black

The emerald accent is deliberately restrained in this email, thin lines and small icons only, because the energy should be quiet anticipation, not urgent call-to-action. The amber accent adds warmth without triggering purchase anxiety.

### Typography as Design
**Headlines:** Questrial (font stack: 'Questrial', 'Helvetica Neue', sans-serif)
- H1 ("Your Body-Signal underwear is on its way!"): 24px desktop / 20px mobile, regular weight, letter-spacing 0.02em (slightly open for breathing room)
- H2 section headers ("What's in Your Package"): 18px desktop / 16px mobile, regular weight, emerald left hairline border

**Body:** Helvetica/sans-serif stack ('Helvetica Neue', 'Arial', sans-serif)
- 16px desktop / 14px mobile, line-height 1.6
- Tracking metadata (order info): 13px, secondary gray #666, clean sans-serif

**Expressive text:** None for this transactional email, the typography stays clean and functional. The "signal waves" visual motif carries the expressive weight instead.

**Mobile scaling:** All body text drops 2px on mobile. H1 drops to 20px. Section headers keep the emerald left border but border reduces to 3px width.

### Decorative Furniture
- **Dividers:** Hand-drawn SVG wave squiggle (not a horizontal rule) between "What's in Your Package" and "When It Arrives", a single gentle curve in emerald at 30% opacity, 2px stroke
- **Tracking callout:** Rounded card (12px border-radius) with emerald hairline border, cream fill, tracking number in monospace font stack ('SF Mono', 'Courier New', monospace) for scannability
- **Bullets for what's-in-box:** Signal-wave SVGs (small emerald dots with a trailing wave, 12px × 12px) instead of standard circles
- **Lena sign-off divider:** A thin emerald hairline rule, full-width, 1px at 30% opacity
- **Background texture:** Micro-noise SVG overlay at 3% opacity (cream-on-cream) for paper feel, applied to the full email background
- **Product care tip section:** A soft amber-left-border (4px, rounded) with cream fill, visually different from the education sections to signal "this practical, not persuasive"

**Shape language:** All rounded corners (12px cards, 50% icons). No sharp angles. Organic, gentle, soft.

### Hidden Detail / Easter Egg
**The tracking dot that moves one hop.** In the Package Journey Line illustration, the "In Transit" dot (📍) is slightly off-center between warehouse and home, exactly one hop forward, not yet arrived. This mirrors the Level 1 child who's "almost trained", one step forward, not yet at the destination.

**Cross-level interpretation:**
- L1 (Sarah): Sees the dot nearly home, "almost there" resonance with her Pre-K timeline
- L2 (Lisa): Sees the dot in mid-journey, mirrors her child's partial progress after years of attempts
- L3 (Maria): The dot has moved, any movement is progress, even if not yet at the destination
- GF: A universal "in transit" experience, every package arrives when it arrives, no pressure

This single visual element communicates R5 (timeline flexibility) and R6 (low-bar success) without a single word.

### Motion / Animation (GIF)
**Animated Tracking Dot Pulse.** The "In Transit" dot on the Package Journey Line gently pulses, a 2-second cubic-bezier scale animation (1.0 → 1.15 → 1.0) with a soft glow expanding outward in emerald. The animation is:
- CSS-based: `@keyframes pulse { 0%, 100% { transform: scale(1); opacity: 0.6; } 50% { transform: scale(1.15); opacity: 1; } }`
- Custom cubic-bezier: `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1)`, gentle ease-in-out, no jitter
- Only transform/opacity animated (no layout-triggering animations per design reference)

**Fallback behavior:**
- Apple Mail: Full CSS animation supported, pulsing dot visible
- Outlook desktop: Static dot (no CSS animation support), still visible, just not animated
- Gmail app: CSS animation NOT supported, falls back to static dot position

**Why this animation for transactional:** The pulse is subtle enough to not distract from the transactional purpose, but alive enough to create a sense of movement and anticipation. No buttons, no hover effects, no loading bars, this email is about waiting, not doing.

### Level Calibration (Visual)
Single visual direction calibrated via R1-R6, inclusive, not level-specific. No level-specific imagery.

**Cross-level visual approach:**
- **Imagery:** No photos of children (avoids age, ability, or level assumptions). Use abstract/minimal illustrations only, the package icon, signal dots, and 3-layer SVG work for all readers regardless of child's profile.
- **Color:** Warm + neutral (cream dominant) with restrained emerald accent, no urgency colors (red, orange), no cold clinical colors (blue, gray), no gender assumptions (pink, blue).
- **Tone:** The emerald is calm, the amber is warm, neither urgent nor festive. The overall visual temperature is "quietly expectant."
- **Typography:** Open letter-spacing, generous line-height, ample padding, gives the eye space to rest (L3 dignity requirement) without feeling sparse (L1 engagement requirement).
- **No product callout photos:** The 3-layer diagram is an abstract SVG, it describes without showing. This avoids triggering any "my child doesn't look like that" disengagement across levels.
- **CTA absence:** The email has no CTA button, pure fulfillment per S2. The "action" is waiting and opening the package when it arrives. This naturally satisfies R12 (permission-not-to-buy) without stating it.

**R1-R6 compliance check:**
- R1 (Symptom Over Label): ✅ Labels never mentioned. Language describes shipping experience.
- R2 (3-5 Recognition Anchors): ✅ N/A, transactional, not persuasion (per Layer-18 transactional table)
- R3 (Age Ranges): ✅ "Your child", never an age. The 3-layer illustration is abstract, no age cues.
- R4 (Verbal/Non-Verbal): ✅ N/A, transactional (per Layer-18 transactional table)
- R5 (Timeline Flexibility): ✅ "3-7 business days" + "some 2 weeks, others 8" for awareness timeline
- R6 (Low-Bar Success): ✅ "If your child pauses for a moment... even once, that's the signal starting to wire"
