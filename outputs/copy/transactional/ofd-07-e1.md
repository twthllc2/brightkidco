## Email Body

### Section 1: Anticipation — It's Here

Your package is out for delivery today. That box on the truck? It's carrying more than underwear — it's carrying the piece that's been missing.

Whether you've been trying for weeks or years, verbal or non-verbal, 3 or 10 — if your child doesn't feel when they need to go, the Body-Signal Learning Layer is about to give their brain the feedback it's been missing.

### Section 2: What to Do When It Arrives

Here's what to do when the box lands on your porch:

1. **Wash before first wear.** The cotton inner layer is the signal-creator. Wash once on gentle cycle — no fabric softener, it coats the cotton and dulls the sensation.

2. **Let your child hold it.** Open the package together. Let them touch the fabric, explore it, get familiar. Some kids need a day or two of sensory familiarity before wearing. That's normal.

3. **Start with 1-2 hours at home.** No prompts. No pressure. Just wear — let the underwear do the teaching. The gentle "uh-oh" feedback after every accident is how the brain learns to connect sensation to action. (Source: S2 §1.6; 04-mechanism-positioning.md §4.1)

### Section 3: What to Expect in Week 1

Most kids don't show first signs in the first week. That's not delay — that's desensitization. The nervous system needs time to register a new sensation.

Some kids show awareness in 2 weeks. Others need 8. Both normal. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §4 R5)

Look for the small wins: a pause before an accident, looking down at the wet spot, pulling at their pants afterward, staying dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes. These aren't failures — they're the signal wiring. (Source: layer-18-cross-level-calibration.md §R6)

### Section 4: A Gentle Reminder

Pull-ups wick moisture away completely. That's great for furniture, but it removes the one piece of feedback the brain can use — the feeling of wetness. The Body-Signal Learning Layer lets that signal through. Just enough. Just long enough. (Source: 04-mechanism-positioning.md §3.2)

Your job isn't to teach. Your job is to let the underwear do the teaching — and be there when the signal finally arrives.

### Guarantee / Risk Reversal

60 days to try it. By your judgment. If it doesn't work for your child, full refund. No questions. (Source: GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md §6)

## Creative Strategy — Visual Direction

### The Big Idea

The email is structured like an unboxing moment — warm anticipation building from the top as a tracking-package icon transforms into an open box revealing the underwear, with a gentle "signal path" dotted line flowing from the package toward the bottom CTA, symbolizing that the signal is finally on its way to the child's brain.

### Layout Architecture

**Above the fold:**
- Full-width hero with a package illustration (cardboard brown #C19A6B tones, minimal) and a "Tracking: Out for Delivery" status bar — warm, functional, not marketing-heavy
- Beneath the hero: concise "It's arriving today" statement in Lena's voice
- Tracking button (green #039902 pill, 48px height)

**Below the fold:**
- Three-step visual sequence in card layout (Wash → Hold → Wear) — three rounded cards (#FBF7EB on #FAF9F7 background) with icon + short instruction
- "What to Expect" section — two-column layout on desktop, stacked on mobile
- Gentle mechanism reminder callout (mint #DBFFCD highlight box)
- Lena sign-off block with her subtitle
- Footer

**Mobile collapse:** Single-column stacking. Cards become full-width with 16px padding. Hero image shrinks proportionally. Buttons full-width 48px min height.

**Layout archetype:** Warm Editorial Split — functional tracking info top-left, warm instructions cascade below. (Source: consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — Editorial Split archetype)

### Custom Illustration / Contraption

**Tracking Package Icon with Signal Path:**
A simple, warm illustration of a cardboard box at the top. A soft dotted line (the "signal") emerges from inside the box and trails downward through the email sections — like a neural signal path from package to brain.

The dotted line changes color as it moves:
- Top (package): soft gray #9AA8A7
- Middle (wearing): transitions to green #039902
- Bottom (learning begins): solid green glow

This creates a visual narrative: the package delivers the tool, wearing it activates the signal, and the signal reaches the child's awareness. (Source: consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — premium illustration concept; Ethereal Glass + Soft Structuralism hybrid)

**Icon set for the 3-step sequence:**
- Wash: Gentle water droplet icon (hairline stroke, rounded)
- Hold: Two hands holding fabric icon
- Wear: Simple underwear outline icon with a small dot (signal) pulsing near the waistband

### Color Narrative

**Palette for this email:**

- **Background:** #FAF9F7 (off-white) — warm, non-clinical, paper-like
- **Card backgrounds:** #FBF7EB (cream) — sensory warmth, gentle
- **Hero element:** Soft cardboard tones (#C19A6B, #E8D5B7) — evokes the package/unboxing
- **Primary accent:** #039902 (Brand Green) — used ONLY for tracking button and signal-dot illustration
- **Mint callout:** #DBFFCD — for the mechanism reminder highlight box
- **Text:** #1A1A1A (body), #4A4A4A (captions)
- **Divider lines:** #9AA8A7 (soft gray, thin 1px hairlines)

**Emotional arc of color:** Warm neutrals (package arriving, grounded anticipation) → mint highlight (clarity, understanding) → green accent (action, tracking). No urgency colors. No bright yellows or oranges — this is transactional, not promotional.

(Source: 11-brand-voice-reference.md §14.1-14.2 — locked brand palette; consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — tinted neutrals, single accent rules)

### Typography as Design

- **From name:** Questrial, 400 weight, #1A1A1A, 14px — "Lena from BrightKidCo"
- **Subject in preview:** Questrial, bold implied, 17px — functional and clear
- **H1 (Hero statement):** Questrial, 28px desktop / 22px mobile, letter-spacing: -0.02em (tight for warmth), #1A1A1A
- **Section headers:** Questrial, 18px desktop / 16px mobile, letter-spacing: normal
- **Body text:** 16px mobile / 17px desktop, line-height 1.5, #4A4A4A — accessible, easy to read
- **Step instructions:** 15px, line-height 1.4, #1A1A1A with icon aligned left

**Expressive text treatment:** The word "signal" wherever it appears uses a soft green #039902 italic treatment — subtle enough to not feel designed, but noticeable on second read that every appearance of "signal" ties the visual thread together.

(Source: 11-brand-voice-reference.md §14.3; consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — Questrial 400 only, NO bold body text)

### Decorative Furniture

**Dividers:** Hand-drawn wavy line (SVG with cubic-bezier approximation) in #9AA8A7 at 50% opacity — between major sections. Not a horizontal rule. Organic, human, non-corporate.

**Signal dots as bullets:** Instead of traditional bullets, use small green #039902 dots (8px diameter, 30% opacity) for the 3-step sequence. On hover (Apple Mail): pulse gently.

**Highlight box:** Mint #DBFFCD background with 16px border-radius, 4px left green border — wraps the mechanism reminder. Gives it visual weight without urgency.

**Borders:** Hairline 1px emerald #039902 left border on the "What to Expect" paragraph — subtle anchor, frames it as important but not urgent.

**Shape language:** All cards and buttons use border-radius 12-16px (rounded but not pill — except the CTA button which is pill at 50px). Organic, sensory-friendly shapes. No sharp corners.

**Background texture:** 3% micro-noise overlay (CSS noise via SVG filter) on the overall #FAF9F7 background — barely visible, adds paper texture feel. (Source: consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — Double-Bezel nested architecture, micro-texture principles)

### Hidden Detail / Easter Egg

**The Signal Dots Easter Egg:** In the three-step card sequence, each card has one dot in its corner — step 1 has one dot, step 2 has two dots, step 3 has three dots. The dots form an ascending pattern that mirrors the growing body awareness:

- This is only noticeable on second read — the dots are small (#9AA8A7, 6px)
- Different levels can interpret them differently:
  - L1: "Growing progress toward Pre-K readiness"
  - L2: "Accumulating sensory awareness data"
  - L3: "Small steps accumulating, no rush"
  - GF: "Building blocks, whatever the timeline"

This satisfies the cross-level easter egg rule with a universal symbol of accumulation that each level can read differently. (Source: plan Output Template — §Hidden Detail / Easter Egg; layer-18-cross-level-calibration.md §R1-R6)

### Motion / Animation (GIF)

**The Tracking Signal Path Animation:**
A short loop (2-3 seconds, under 500KB) showing a small dot traveling the dotted line from the package icon through the email — from gray to green — and settling at the CTA button.

- The dot moves along a curved cubic-bezier path matching the dotted line
- Transitions from #9AA8A7 (unaware) to #039902 (signal received)

**Respects constraints:** Transform/opacity only. No layout-triggering animations. Apple Mail supports CSS animation; Gmail/Outlook get the static fallback state. (Source: consolidated-design-skills-trimmed.md — motion principles, email client constraints)

### Level Calibration (Visual)

Single visual direction calibrated via R1-R6 — inclusive, not level-specific. No level-specific imagery.

**R1 — Symptom over label:** No level labels anywhere. The copy describes behaviors ("doesn't feel when they need to go") not diagnoses.

**R2 — Recognition anchors:** The "What to Expect" section includes a recognition anchor with L1/L2/L3-spanning wins: pause before accident (L1-L2), looking down at wet spot (L2-L3), pulling at pants (all levels), staying dry 2 hours (L1), staying dry 30 minutes (L2-L3).

**R3 — Age ranges:** "Whether you've been trying for weeks or years...3 or 10" — age-inclusive language throughout.

**R4 — Verbal/non-verbal inclusivity:** "Let your child hold it...explore it" — non-verbal accessible. No assumption of verbal instruction. The do-this-together framing works for any communication style.

**R5 — Timeline flexibility:** "Some kids show awareness in 2 weeks. Others need 8. Both normal." — no fixed promises.

**R6 — Low-bar success metrics:** "A pause before an accident, looking down at the wet spot, pulling at their pants... staying dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes" — all mini-wins, not "trained."
