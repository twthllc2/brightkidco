#### BODY COPY

You've been looking for the wrong signs.

The ones that matter for your child are not the ones the books describe. Not the ones therapists measure. Not the ones other parents post about.

The signs that matter for your child are smaller. So small you might have missed them.

A pause before sitting down.

A glance down at the wet spot instead of walking through it.

A flap of the hands at the moment of changing.

Ten seconds of dry time that wasn't there before.

These are not failures. These are not "almost there" nothings. These are the first sparks of a nervous system beginning to register information it never received before.

**What micro-wins look like for L3 children**

Most tracking systems measure the wrong things. Your child's nervous system processes differently. For your child, a win looks different:

| Instead of asking | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "Did he train?" | "Did he pause at all today?" |
| "Did he tell you?" | "Did anything different happen around changing?" |
| "Is he dry?" | "Was he dry 30 seconds longer than last week?" |
| "Did he initiate?" | "Did he react to wetness sooner than before?" |

Each question acknowledges that his body is learning a language it never learned to speak. The first signs will be barely visible.

**Micro-wins you can track starting today**

1. **The Pause**, A hesitation, a brief stop in movement, right before or after voiding. Even half a second. This the nervous system registering: something happened.

2. **The Look**, Eyes moving toward the wet spot or the body. The brain is connecting the internal event to the external reality for the first time.

3. **The Reaction**, A change in behavior at the moment of wetness. Not discomfort, that may never come for a slow to pick up signals child. But any shift. A flap. A vocalization. A small stillness.

4. **The Duration**, Measuring in seconds, not hours. Was he dry for 30 seconds this week when last week it was zero? That is nothing. That is the signal pathway starting to hold.

5. **The Awareness Window**, The time between "he voided" and "he shows awareness of it." The gap may be 10 seconds. Next month it might be 8 seconds. That's progress.

None of these mean your child is "training." They mean his body is communicating with his brain a way it hasn't before. That is the only outcome the Body-Signal Learning Layer is designed to create.

**The tracking system**

Take one note per day. Just one.

Pick one micro-win category from the list above. At the end of each day, ask yourself one question: "Did I see it today?" Yes or no. That's it.

Write it down. A single word. A tally mark on a sticky note. A note in your phone.

At the end of the week, look back. Not for a trend. Not for evidence of progress. Just to see: did I notice anything this week I wouldn't have noticed before?

Some weeks the answer will be no. That is real. That is not failure. That is the honest pace of your child's nervous system.

Some weeks the answer will be yes. A single yes. And that single yes is a signal your child's brain is doing something it never did before.

**What this means alongside your life**

You already track everything. Medications, appointments, IEP goals, sleep hours, stool patterns. You do not need another system. You need permission to change what you count as a signal.

The BSL underwear you added alongside your diapers, it is not here to transform your daily reality. It is here to provide the sustained 30-60 seconds of gentle feedback that your child's nervous system that picks up signals slowly needs to begin registering what it previously could not.

One pair. $34.99. Add alongside your current routine. No replacement of anything. Just an addition.

If you see one micro-win 60 days, one pause, one look, one shift, you will know the signal is reaching a brain it never reached before.

If you don't see anything, that is also information. A full refund stands. No questions.

One L3 parent told us: "He's not trained. But he looked down at his wet underwear for the first time in 9 years. That's nothing. That's a real change."

**When you decide, it's here.**

Lena ——

## PART 2 — CREATIVE STRATEGY

### Strategy Rationale

**Position in emotional arc:** E3 of 5 in the PP-Extended Education flow for L3. By D36, Maria has had the product for 5+ weeks. She has likely seen zero overt signs of progress. Without intentional reframing, the natural conclusion is "it didn't work either." This email preempts that conclusion by redefining what "progress" means for her child.

**Why this strategy works for L3:**

1. **L3 does not measure success the way other levels do.** L1 measures by "he went today!" L2 measures by "he initiated on his own." L3 measures by "did his nervous system register anything?", and until this email, no one has given her permission to count that as meaningful.

2. **The tracking system is deliberately minimal.** Overwhelmed parents (L3 dominant emotional state) cannot absorb another complex regimen. One question, one note, one look-back at end of week. That is sustainable.

3. **Micro-wins are dignity-first.** They do not promise "fully trained." They promise "your child's brain is doing something new." That is a promise the mechanism can keep.

4. **No urgency. No timelines. No cheerleading.** The email validates the possibility of "no" answers. It frames "no" as useful information, not failure. This maintains trust by refusing to sell a guarantee of outcomes.
