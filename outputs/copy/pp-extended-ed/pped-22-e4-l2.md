**What the first 3 weeks actually accomplish**

Most parents think if nothing obvious has happened by week 3, nothing is happening. That's not how interoception works.

For autistic children, the brain processes sustained tactile input differently. Autistic brains don't habituate to sensory input the way neurotypical brains do — they keep processing it, fresh, every time. That "nothing happened" feeling? The sensation is landing. The brain is just still deciding what to do with it.

Your BCBA built the behavioral foundation over months or years of ABA. What you're doing now is adding the sensory layer ABA can't reach — sustained, localized body-signal input that gives the brain something to attach the behavior to.

**What the research says about this specific window**

A 2020 study by Hample, Mahler and Amspacher showed that autistic children who received structured interoception training began showing measurable improvement in body awareness after 4-6 weeks. Not 3 days. Not 1 week. The nervous system needs exposure cycles.

Internally, across 672 families in our data, 82% showed clearer body awareness within 2-4 weeks of consistent wear. That doesn't mean "trained", it means the first signal registered. A pause before an accident. A look at the wet spot. A hand toward the pants.

That's not failure. That's the first synapse firing.

**If you're in the "nothing yet" camp, here's what to check**

1. **Wear time:** Is your child getting 2+ hours daily of consistent wear? Intermittent wear = intermittent input. The brain can't build a pathway on 20 minutes here and there.

2. **The environment:** Sensory-overloaded bathrooms (flushing echoes, bright lights, cold tile) can override the learning signal. If your child resists bathroom time, it may not be the underwear, it may be the room. Try pairing wear-time with a low-stimulus activity (reading, tablet time, a quiet corner) for the first 2 weeks before introducing bathroom association.

3. **Clothing choices:** If your child is already sensitive to clothing textures, the learning layer adds another sensation on top. Try wearing the body-signal underwear under loose sweatpants or soft shorts, minimize competing sensory input during early weeks.

4. **Daily rhythm:** The nervous system learns in patterns. Same time of day, same duration, same low-pressure context. Irregular schedules give the brainconsistent data.

**If you're seeing small signs, you're ahead of the curve**

Some parents at week 3 report: a pause mid-play, a glance at the wet spot, a step toward the bathroom. These are not coincidences. The body signal pathway is forming. The 49 studies review (Barmpagiannis & Baldimtsi, 2025) confirmed that body signal differences are one of the most well-established features of the autistic nervous system, and also that these pathways respond to training.

The signal isn't arriving fully yet. But it's starting to arrive at all. That's the bridge.

**"The 3-Week Gap", Between Starting and Seeing**

The core visual and narrative concept for PP-Edu E4 L2 is the gap between expectation and neurological reality. Most L2 parents expect linear progress. The science shows a 3-4 week latency period before the body signal pathway begins to register. The creative strategy visualizes this latency, not as failure, but as a biological timer.

The tagline working with the visual: *"Nothing is still something. The signal path is building."*

### Hidden Detail / Easter Egg

**The Unresolved Equation, "The 21-Day Gap" as a sensory processing diagram**

At the very bottom of thero timeline visual, placed at small scale but visible on desktop (12-14px, `#888888`), a simplified sensory processing equation:

```
Sensory Input (30-60s sustained wetness)
 ↓
[t(0) → t(1) + Δt] where Δt = latency period (14-28 days typical)
 ↓
Brain Registration (first awareness signal)
```

Or, in plain-text as it would appear in the design:

```
Input(S) → [Latency(Δt)] → Output(A)
 where Δt ≈ 14-28 days (individual variance ±50%)
```

**Meaning for L2 parents:** This mirrors the "tried everything, still stuck" feeling. Even the science has an unresolved variable, the exact latency period varies per child. It tells the L2 parent: *You're not failing. The signal is in transit. The *Δt* is different for every nervous system.*

**Placement:** In the lower-right of thero image area, at roughly 50% opacity, over the timeline strip. Discoverable but not distracting. On mobile, shifts to below the timeline, 11px, center-aligned.

**Rationale:** Hidden details reward close reading, which L2 parents do, they read every word, test every claim. The equation shows respect for their intelligence and BCBA awareness. It signals "we understand the science at the level you do."
