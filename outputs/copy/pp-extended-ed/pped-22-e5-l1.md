---

**Validation**

Here's what I want you to hear first: your child's progress in the last seven weeks is real. Even if it doesn't look like "trained" yet.

That pause he takes when he feels the uh-oh sensation? That's his nervous system registering a signal it didn't receive seven weeks ago. That look down at his pants? That's interoception building — his brain learning to connect the body signal to the awareness.

Research shows autistic children have significantly reduced interoceptive accuracy — the ability to sense internal body states like bladder fullness (Nicholson et al., 2019, Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders). What you're seeing over these weeks isn't failure. It's a nervous system literally building new pathways.

Pre-K doesn't require perfection. It requires that his teachers understand what's happening — and that you and they are aligned on how to support him.

---

**Mechanism — School Readiness Protocol**

Here are three things you can start this week:

**1. The teacher conversation template**

Most Pre-K teachers have never seen body-signal learning. They know sticker charts and timed sits. You need to frame it for them.

What to say:

> "My son is autistic. He's learning to connect body signals to the bathroom — but right now the signal arrives AFTER he needs to go. We use BrightKidCo underwear that gives him a gentle sensation so his brain learns. When he has an accident, please just help him change quietly. No shame, no pressure. We're building awareness, not compliance."

**2. The pull-up-over-underwear system**

Send him to school in a thin pull-up OVER his BrightKidCo underwear. This does two things:
- The pull-up protects against accidents in the classroom
- The underwear underneath still has skin contact — so the body-signal learning continues throughout the school day

Teachers understand pull-ups. They don't understand body-signal layers. Meet them where they are.

**3. The morning and after-school rhythm**

The school day can dysregulate sensory systems. Build two consistent checkpoints:
- **Morning:** 10 minutes of calm bathroom time before leaving. No pressure to perform. Just the routine of sitting with his underwear on.
- **After-school:** 30 minutes in BrightKidCo underwear only (no pull-up) when he gets home. This is his body-signal learning window — when his nervous system is decompressing and most receptive.

I can't tell you he'll be fully trained by September. What I can tell you: every single day of body-signal exposure is building a pathway that wasn't there before. That pause, that look, that single step toward the bathroom — those are the evidence.
