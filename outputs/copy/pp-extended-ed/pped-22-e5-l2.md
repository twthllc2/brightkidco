### The Three Most Common L2 Plateaus — And How to Navigate Each

**Plateau 1: Prompt-Dependency Persists**

*What it looks like:* He still won't initiate. You prompt, he sits, he gets up, nothing happens. You've been doing this for 7 weeks and it seems like he's just waiting for your cue.

*Why BCBA protocols created this:* Prompt-dependency is not a failure of the Body-Signal approach. It's the natural result of years of scheduled sits where the adult cue was the trigger, not the internal signal. The BCBA protocol built compliance; now we need to transfer that compliance to self-awareness.

*What to try:*
- Fade the prompt. Instead of "Time to sit," try a non-verbal cue — touch his shoulder, set a timer that beeps, or leave the bathroom door ajar with the light on. Let the environment cue him, not your voice.
- Extend the window between prompt and sit by 30 seconds. Give his nervous system time to register the BSL™ signal before you give the instruction.
- Celebrate the pause. If he stops mid-play, even for a second — that's the internal signal arriving. Name it: "You felt something." The word builds the cognitive bridge.

**Source:** S2 L2 Extended Education E3 — "Prompt-dependency reversal: strategies for fading adult cues while building internal signals."

**Plateau 2: Sensory Habituation**

*What it looks like:* The first few weeks, he seemed to notice the wetness signal. Now he doesn't react at all. It's like the underwear is background noise.

*Why this happens:* This is actually a good sign in disguise. Initial signal sensitivity is high because the sensation is new. After several weeks, his nervous system starts to calibrate — distinguishing between "this is wet" (sensory detection) and "I need to act on this" (motor response). The habituation means his brain is processing the signal faster, but the output pathway isn't built yet.

*What to try:*
- Increase wear time. If he's wearing BSL™ for 3-4 hour stretches, try 5-6 hours. The sustained signal over longer windows gives the brain more data points.
- Layering check: make sure nothing between the BSL™ layer and his skin (no cotton liner, no other underwear). The signal needs direct skin contact.
- Add a post-accident ritual. When an accident happens (and it will), don't rush to clean. Instead: pause for 5 seconds, say "Your body let you know something," then clean together. This 5-second window is where the neural connection forms. (Source: L9 §6.4 L2-D — "He's trained to comply, not trained to feel. There's a difference.")

**Plateau 3: Environment-Specific Regression**

*What it looks like:* He does well at home but has accidents everywhere else — school, grandma's, the store. You feel like you're starting over every time you leave the house.

*Why this happens:* Environment sensitivity is real and well-documented. The bathroom at school smells different, has different lighting, and echoes. The adult cue doesn't exist there. The schedule is different. His nervous system has to learn the same body-signal awareness in a new context — it's not regression, it's generalization. (Source: L9 §6.4 L2-F — "He can do it. Just not everywhere. Yet.")

*What to try:*
- Send a familiar BSL™ pair to school with a note for the teacher: "These help my child feel when he needs to go. If he pauses or touches his pants, please offer the bathroom." (Source: OH §11)
- Practice one new environment per week. Same routine, different room. Living room → kitchen → backyard → playground. Each context is a separate learning curve.
- Lower expectations for outings. The goal outside the home is not dry — it's awareness. If he pauses once during a 2-hour trip, that's a win.

### Proposed Animation

**Animated Element:** The "Signal-Timing Diagram" (Section 3, Illustration 2)

**Animation Description:**
- A subtle 2-second looping GIF
- The wavy dotted line in the **"Signal Travel"** lane pulses gently — fading from 100% opacity to 60% and back — as if the signal is "trying" to cross the threshold.
- The **"BSL™ Window"** band has a slow 4-second breathing animation (pulse from 30% opacity to 50% and back) — showing sustained feedback.
