**CTA path:** One clear button ("Shop the 3+3 Bundle →") + P.S. reply incentive (teacher info sheet)

- "Bundle" in CTA assumes familiarity — she's Day 50, she knows it, so this is fine

## 8. ONE FIX — Bridge the CTA to the Mechanism

**The problem:** The 3-step protocol teaches her how to use the product at school, but the CTA section jumps to "Shop the 3+3 Bundle" without connecting why the bundle enables the protocol. The mechanism says "here's what to do" and the CTA says "buy this" — there's a missing sentence that makes the CTA feel like a sales pivot rather than a natural continuation.

**The fix:** Add one bridge sentence between the mechanism and CTA sections:

After: "That pause, that look, that single step toward the bathroom, those are the evidence."
Before: "The 3+3 Bundle is still the best place to start..."

Insert:

> "These three steps work best when you have enough pairs to rotate — school days, after-school learning time, and laundry days covered. That's exactly what the 3+3 Bundle is built for at this stage."

**Why this works:**
- Makes the CTA feel like a natural consequence of the protocol, not a sales pitch
- "Rotate" language = practical, not pushy
- "At this stage" = she's not being sold to, she's being equipped for where she is
- Maintains the education-first Phase 3 tone while gently converting

**Expected impact:** +0.5 points on Conversion (8→8.5), making the overall score 9/10.
