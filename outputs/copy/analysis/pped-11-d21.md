## 8. ONE FIX

**Rewrite the mechanism reminder section as a micro-story, not a product explanation.**

The current text:
> "The Body-Signal Learning Layer works through repetition. Each 'uh-oh' sensation, sustained for 30-60 seconds, tells the brain: *something happened here.* Week 3 is when some nervous systems start registering that message."

This is clinical language in an otherwise warm, observational email. It breaks the emotional contract with the reader — who has just been told that "nothing" is success — by pulling them into product mechanics.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Your child's brain is learning something it's never known before: what wetness feels like from the inside. Some nervous systems hear that message by week 3. Others are still tuning in. Both are exactly where they need to be."

**Why this is the single most impactful fix:**
- It maintains the emotional register (warm, observational) instead of dropping into clinical mode
- It reframes "mechanism" as "journey" — the reader doesn't need to understand the product, they need to understand their child
- It preserves the timeline flexibility (R5) without the clinical "30-60 seconds" detail
- It costs nothing to implement and affects every reader who scrolls past the testimonial block (which is most of them)
