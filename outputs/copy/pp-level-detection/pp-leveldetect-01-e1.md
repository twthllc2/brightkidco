## PART 2, FULL CREATIVE STRATEGY

---

### BIG IDEA

**One tap tells us everything.**

The central insight: parents don't want to fill out forms. They want to feel seen. The level detection email turns segmentation into recognition — each button is a mirror, not a survey. When a parent taps the card that describes their child, they feel understood before any follow-up email arrives.

The creative strategy: **make the selection feel like a conversation, not a quiz. Four clear options, one that says "not sure," zero pressure.**

### CONTRAPTION

**1. Hero as permission frame**
- "Quick question" badge signals low commitment
- "One tap. There's no wrong answer." removes anxiety
- No CTA button — the cards ARE the interaction

**2. Level cards as mirrors**
- Each card uses recognition language, not labels
- Emoji icons provide instant visual recognition
- Color-coded circles create visual hierarchy
- Ink borders + box shadows maintain brand consistency

**3. Dashed "Not sure" variant**
- Visually distinct from solid-border cards
- Mint background signals "this is also welcome"
- Prevents abandonment from parents who can't categorize yet

**4. No CTA button**
- The task specifies: "NO CTA button — the buttons ARE the interaction"
- This is a segmentation email, not a conversion email
- Each button links to a level-specific landing page or tag update

### LEVEL CALIBRATION (R1-R6)

| Rule | Requirement | How This Email Satisfies It |
|------|-------------|----------------------------|
| **R1: Symptom Over Label** | No level labels in copy | Uses symptom language: "Can tell you when they need to go," "Inconsistent signals," "Doesn't react to wet." NO "Level 1," "Level 2," etc. |
| **R2: 3-5 Recognition Anchors** | Every mail needs recognition anchors | 4 anchors: verbal+aware, sometimes aware, rarely aware, not sure. Each card is a recognition moment. |
| **R3: Age Ranges** | Never use fixed ages | No ages mentioned at all. Language is symptom-based, age-agnostic. |
| **R4: Verbal/Non-Verbal Inclusivity** | Always pair verbal + non-verbal | Level 1 covers verbal ("Can tell you"), Levels 2-3 cover non-verbal ("pauses," "doesn't react"). |
| **R5: Timeline Flexibility** | Never promise outcomes on fixed timelines | No timelines. "Two weeks in" is the only time reference, and it's contextual. |
| **R6: Low Bar Success** | Define success as mini-wins | "Still figuring it out, and that's completely fine" validates GF. No success metrics in this email. |

---

### POST-PURPOSE

This email serves two purposes:
1. **Segmentation** — each button click tags the subscriber with their level
2. **Recognition** — the act of choosing a card makes the parent feel seen

Post-click behavior: Button links to a Klaviyo flow trigger or landing page that updates the subscriber's tag (L1/L2/L3/GF). Subsequent emails in the flow are level-calibrated.
