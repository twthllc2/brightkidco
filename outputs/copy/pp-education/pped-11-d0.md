### SECTION 2 — WEEK 1 GUIDE: WHAT TO EXPECT

Here's the approach for the first seven days:

**Put the underwear on like regular underwear. Let your child wear it for 1–2 hours daily. No prompts. No pressure. No timer sits. No "tell me when you need to go."**

Just let the sensation do the teaching.

For Week 1, the only goal is: *let your child's nervous system notice a new feeling.* That's it. Not the toilet. Not a "success." Not a change in bathroom behavior.

If your child touches the underwear, looks down, pauses during play, or shows any curiosity — that's enough. If they show nothing for the entire week — that's also exactly right.

### SECTION 5 — TIMELINE FLEXIBILITY (R5) + LOW-BAR SUCCESS (R6)

**When will something happen?**

Timelines vary — and that's not a bug, it's how nervous systems work:

— Some kids show first awareness in 1–2 weeks (a pause, a look down, a step toward the bathroom)
— Others need 4–8 weeks of consistent wear before the signal starts registering
— Some require 12+ weeks

All of these are normal. The nervous system doesn't follow a calendar.

**What counts as a win this week:**

- They wore the underwear for any amount of time without a fight
- They paused — even once — during an accident
- They touched their pants while wet or pulled at them
- They showed curiosity about the underwear
- They tolerated it for 30+ minutes
- They said "wet" or showed you with a gesture
- They stayed dry 2 hours instead of 30 minutes

That's a real win. Not "fully trained." Not "accident-free." Any one of these.

Week 1 is about reintroducing that sensation. Nothing more, nothing less.

## BIG IDEA

> **"Week 1 is not about the toilet. It's about the nervous system learning a new language."**

The first post-purchase educational email must reset expectations immediately. The conventional parent mindset is: "I bought the underwear → my child should start using the potty." The Big Idea is **permission to do less** — to observe instead of prompt, to notice instead of push. This is the exact opposite of every marketing email that says "3 easy steps to training." It builds trust through restraint.

## CONTRAPTION (Email Architecture)

The "Contraption" — the mechanical structure that makes this email work across all 4 levels simultaneously:

1. **Inclusivity Funnel (Top)** — "Whether...Or...If..." opener captures all 4 levels + all 5 GF sub-groups in one sentence. No one feels excluded.

2. **Expectation Reset** — "Week 1 Guide" explicitly tells the parent to do nothing except let the child wear the underwear. This prevents the L1 mom from over-prompting (which creates frustration) and the L3 mom from feeling pressure (which creates disengagement).

3. **Three-Family Bridge** — Three testimonial vignettes in a single block. L1 mom finds the "Day 6 pause" story. L2 mom finds the "screamed when we tried" story. L3 mom finds the "haven't seen anything" story. GF mom finds whichever fits. The block makes every segment feel seen without labeling any.

4. **Recognition Anchors Block** — Five symptoms listed by dash-separators. Mom scans, finds 1-2 that match, thinks "they know my kid." Trust built in 10 seconds.

5. **Timeline Spectrum** — Three time-ranges presented side-by-side: 1-2 weeks, 4-8 weeks, 12+ weeks. Each maps to a level (L1, L2, L3) without naming the level. GF gets the full spectrum.

6. **Low-Bar Wins** — Seven mini-wins that span the full ability range. "Tolerated for 30+ minutes" (L3 win) sits next to "stayed dry 2 hours" (L1 win). No hierarchy, no comparison.

7. **Mechanism Anchor** — The science paragraph does double duty: validates the reframe AND proves the product is built on real research. SPARK study citation is the authority signal.

8. **Soft CTA** — "If your child wears the underwear for one hour today, you've done enough." Zero pressure. Permission to proceed at her own pace. The secondary CTA (progress log) is an optional tool, not a requirement.

*(Source: Layer-18 §1 — cross-level framework; §2 — 6 core rules; §3 — GF writing approach; 2-full-email-copy-strategy.md §1.10 — E1 structure)*

## EASTER EGG (Hidden Experience Layer)

The Easter Egg in this email is the **"Three-Family Testimonial Pattern"** itself — but there's a deeper layer:

**The Hidden Calibration Table in the Testimonials:**

| Testimonial | Level Target | Symptom Mapped | Emotional Hook |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| #1 — "Day 6 pause after 3 years" | L1 + Sub-group D | Verbal child, "almost trained" but inconsistent | Hope: "maybe this time" |
| #2 — "Screamed when we tried" | L2 + Sub-group C (SPD) | Sensory resistance, method-cycling parent | Patience: "keep going" |
| #3 — "Day 10, nothing seen yet" | L3 + Sub-group A (pre-diagnosis) | Years of trying, no visible progress | Normality: "this is expected" |

The Easter Egg design: each testimonial card contains a **trigger phrase** that connects to a specific cross-level rule. The GF mom doesn't see the levels — she sees her experience.

**Additionally:**
- The opening "Whether...Or...If..." pattern encodes 3 dimensions simultaneously: **age** (3 or 10) → R3, **communication** (verbal or non-verbal) → R4, **journey stage** (new or years in) → R5. A reader at any point on any dimension finds herself in the sentence.

- The "What counts as a win" list is **ordered from most achievable (L3) to most advanced (L1)** without any labeling: "wore without fight" (L3) → "paused" (L2-L3) → "touched pants" (L2) → "curiosity" (L2) → "tolerated 30+ min" (L3) → "said wet or showed gesture" (R4 verbal/non-verbal) → "stayed dry 2 hours" (L1). GF readers find multiple touchpoints.

## MOTION (Animation Guidelines)

For HTML email (ESP platforms that support CSS animation — Klaviyo, Mailchimp, etc.):

| Element | Animation | Trigger | Duration | Notes |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|-------|
| **Logo fade-in** | Opacity 0→1 | On load | 0.8s | Easing: ease-out. Don't delay content below. |
| **"Week 1 Guide" badge** | Slide-up + fade | 0.2s after load | 0.5s | Badge slides 8px up into position. |
| **Testimonial cards** | Sequential fade-in | Scroll reveal or timed 0.3s delay between cards | 0.6s per card | Cards stack vertically; each fades in after previous. |
| **Win checklist** | Staggered line-by-line appear | On scroll into view | 0.3s per line | Creates a reading rhythm — feels like checking items off. |
| **CTA button** | Gentle pulse | 2s after page load, then every 8s | 1.5s pulse | Opacity 1 → 0.85 → 1. Very subtle — not urgent. |
| **Stat highlight ("49%")** | Background-color flash | On scroll into view | 1.2s | #F5C84B highlight expands from center. |
| **Sign-off text** | Opacity 0→1 | 1s after CTA | 1s | Lena signature fades in last — personal close. |

**Motion Rules:**
- No autoplay GIFs or video — risk of sensory overload for parent reader
- No infinite loops except the CTA pulse (and even that is extremely subtle)
- All motion must be **accessible** — respect `prefers-reduced-motion` media query
- Motion is **warmth, not excitement** — slow, organic easing curves (cubic-bezier with gentle deceleration)
- Mobile: reduce or eliminate staggered animations (increases cognitive load on small screens)
