# EMOTIONAL ARC & TRUST METER — LEVEL 2 (LISA)

> **Reference document for Stage 1 subagents.** The deepest section of the L2 journey engineering — per-email emotional arc (Before / Hook / Trust Transaction / After) and the running Trust Meter across all four temporal phases. Same structure as the L1 emotional-trust map, calibrated to L2's harder-to-gain-but-more-durable trust threshold. Grounded in `journey-l2-01-avatar.md`, `journey-l2-02-sequence.md`, `extracted-sections.json`, and locked copy at `outputs/copy/welcome/*.md`. Internal version: 2026-06-28.

## 0. HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Trust is the **only product we sell to Lisa before we sell her the product.** Every email either moves the meter or threatens it. The 6-element L2 formula (Demand §23) governs *what* each email must contain; this document governs *how* she experiences each one.

### 0.1 The L2 trust axiom

> **Marketing polish triggers skepticism; mechanism specificity converts her into a scientific evaluator. The brand that sells her the wrong thing — even with good copy — loses her permanently. The brand that earns her trust gains it for years.**

Lisa enters at **40/60 hope/realism** (avatar §3.4) — motivated enough to keep showing up, skeptical enough to reject any sentence that confirms her expectations of marketers. Her trust threshold is higher than L1's, lower than L3's, but the **most durable once earned.** Where L1 trust is volatile (one bad email collapses it), L2 trust becomes structurally reinforced after 3+ emails honoring the 6-element formula. She is too invested in her own analysis to walk away from a brand that has passed her tests.

### 0.2 Reading the meter

| Metric | Range | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust (0–100)** | Cumulative running score | How likely is she to buy / stay engaged / defend the brand in her BCBA conversation? |
| **Trust Delta (–2 to +2)** | Per-email change | Did this email move the needle forward, backward, or sideways? |
| **Mode** | Defensive-evaluation / Scientific-evaluator / Retained / Detached | What posture is she in when she opens? |
| **Hope/Realism** | From avatar §3.4 | Her internal ratio. |

### 0.3 Trust delta scoring

- **+2:** Recognition moment (3+ symptom matches) AND mechanism click both fire. L2 unlock sentence delivered cleanly.
- **+1:** Recognition OR mechanism deepens OR BCBA-respect lands.
- **0:** Steady-state transactional. No arc movement.
- **–1:** Polite friction (wrong timing, slightly salesy, but no forbidden phrase).
- **–2:** Forbidden phrase, BCBA criticism, fake urgency, "Quick"/"Easy"/"Miracle." May mark spam or leave 1-star review.

### 0.4 Starting trust level

**Lisa opens Email 1 at trust = 38/100** — lower than L1's starting trust. She is not waiting to be persuaded; she is waiting to be allowed to trust. The Welcome Flow's job is to move trust from 38 to **~48/100** by Welcome E8, at which point she has either converted, lingered into PP-Education, or moved to Winback B.

## 6. RISK FLAGS

### 6.1 Negative delta risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Marketing polish** (reads as sales contamination) | Pre-flight 6-element check; clinical-warmth energy |
| **BCBA criticism** (any form — soft or hard) | BCBA never rules; complement framing only |
| **"Quick"/"Easy"/"Simple"** (L2 most-dangerous word) | Forbidden-phrase audit before send |
| **Fake urgency** (countdown timers, "limited time") | Forbidden |
| **AI-isms** ("revolutionary," "transformative," "leverage") | Forbidden |
| **L1 testimonial splice** | L2 needs L2 contexts; cross-level testimonial audit |
| **Empty empathy** ("Don't worry," "We understand") | Forbidden |
| **Pre-K urgency** | School adapted via IEP; forbidden for L2 |

### 6.2 Trust-below-30% analysis

The starting trust of 38 is already low. **No email in the L2 sequence drops trust below 30** if the 6-element pre-flight is honored. The lowest state is never-bought L2 at end of Winback B (~54/100). The brand is structurally protected because the L2 unlock sentence and BCBA-respect frame are mandatory throughout.

> **CRITICAL SAFETY:** If a single L2 email contains both a forbidden phrase AND a BCBA criticism, trust can drop 4+ points in one send — from ~48 to ~44 in WELCOME E4, for example. By E8 the trust would land at ~36 instead of ~48. The 6-element pre-flight checklist is the structural defense.

### 6.3 Suspicious >+2 jumps

| Email | Why +2 is justified | Risk if lands as +3 |
|---|---|---|
| **WELCOME E2** | Recognition + mechanism + BCBA all fire | If over-explained (3+ mechanism citations), Lisa may perceive overproduction as marketing. Keep to one Mahler cite + one Wiggins stat. |
| **WELCOME E4** | Recognition + L2 unlock + wrong-problem reframe | If paired with "Quick"/"Easy," +2 collapses to –2. Pre-flight mandatory. |
| **FAQ E5** | L2 unlock sentence in pure form | If CTA pushes purchase, trust frame corrupted. Must be paired with permission-not-to-buy. |

### 6.4 The single most dangerous L2 email

> **"Easy potty training in just 3 days — even for autistic kids! Try now before this offer expires!"**

This is the **mirror image** of the L2 unlock sentence. Every trigger fires:
- "Easy" → universal forbidden
- "3 days" → L2 most-dangerous: "Quick"
- "autistic kids" in marketing voice → brand-wide forbidden
- "before this offer expires" → fake urgency forbidden

If Lisa ever receives this email (or any equivalent), her immune system fires. She marks spam, leaves a 1-star review, tells her three Facebook groups "stay away." **The most dangerous L2 email is the most polished L1 email.** The shinier it is, the harder it collapses.

## 7. THE L2 EMOTIONAL SIGNATURE — RECAP

### 7.1 The emotional moves that define L2

| Move | Where it appears | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Mirror, don't pitch** | WELCOME E1, E2; PP-Edu E1 | Pitched by 40+ brands; mirror is the entry. |
| **Name the methods** | WELCOME E2; Cart E2; FAQ E1 | Tried them; naming proves she has been heard. |
| **Absolve, don't motivate** | WELCOME E4; FAQ E1, E6 | "You didn't fail. Your BCBA didn't fail." Releases guilt. |
| **Mechanism, not promise** | WELCOME E2, E5, E7; PP-Ext Edu L2-1 | Science is the L2 trust contract. Vague copy collapses. |
| **BCBA as complement, never replacement** | WELCOME E2, E4; FAQ E5; PP-Ext Edu L2-2 | BCBA is highest-trust authority. Brand as additive, never critical. |
| **Permission, not pressure** | WELCOME E8; Cart E3; Checkout E2; Winback A L2-3; FAQ E6 | L2 paradox: the more we let her leave, the more she stays. |
| **Lena-as-peer, not Lena-as-brand** | WELCOME E1, E8; PP-Mary S. Story; PP-Ext Edu L2-2; Sunset E2 | "Mom of two autistic sons" is the deepest L2 trust signal. |
| **Long sign-off, when earned** | WELCOME E6+; PP-Ext Edu; Winback; Sunset | "With care, Lena" is the trust peak. Earned, not assumed. |

### 7.2 The 3-Layer Trust Architecture

L2 trust is built in three layers, each reinforcing the next:

1. **Intellectual trust** (WELCOME E2, E5, E7; PP-Ext Edu L2-1): Mechanism explained. Citations cited. Science respected.
2. **Emotional trust** (WELCOME E4, E8; FAQ E1, E6): Absolution given. Wrong-problem reframed. Permission granted.
3. **Relational trust** (WELCOME E1, E8; PP-Mary S. Story; PP-Ext Edu L2-2; Sunset E2): Lena-as-Lena. "Mom of two autistic sons." The door never closes.

Each email must serve at least one layer. The strongest emails serve two. The strongest moments serve all three — WELCOME E2 serves intellectual + emotional; WELCOME E8 serves emotional + relational; PP-Mary S. Story serves intellectual + relational.

### 7.3 The Trust Threshold Test

Before any L2 email sends, the brand must answer three questions:

1. **Does this email pass Lisa's 30-second filter?** (No exclamation cascade, no "Quick," no "Easy," no AI-isms, no countdown timer)
2. **Does this email name her experience?** (3+ failed methods, or 1+ sensory/BCBA anchor)
3. **Does this email let her leave?** (Permission-not-to-buy explicit or implicit)

If any answer is no, the email is not L2-calibrated. Send blocked.
