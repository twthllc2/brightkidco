# Cross-Level Sync Report — BrightKidCo 4-Level Master Journey Audit

> **Audit date:** 2026-06-28
> **Auditor:** Stage 1 subagent (cross-level consistency)
> **Documents audited:**
> - `journey-l1-MASTER.md` (Sarah, verbal 3–5, Pre-K deadline, 51 emails)
> - `journey-l2-MASTER.md` (Lisa, BCBA-equipped, ~46 emails)
> - `journey-l3-MASTER.md` (Maria, non-/minimally-verbal, 38–58 emails)
> - `journey-gf-MASTER.md` (General Fallback, 5 sub-groups, ~50–55 emails per journey)
>
> **Audit dimensions:** (1) same-week planning conflicts, (2) same-customer-different-level migration jarring shifts, (3) shared email consistency, (4) conversion path consistency, (5) sign-off progression.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The four level-specific master journey documents are **broadly well-synchronized** but contain **8 HIGH-severity conflicts**, **9 MEDIUM-severity conflicts**, and **6 LOW-severity conflicts** that, if not remediated, will produce jarring customer experiences during the most fragile moment in any lifecycle — the migration from `avatar_general` to a specific level tag (GF → L1/L2/L3 via PP-Level Detection at Day 14). The single most consistent dimension across all four documents is the **sign-off progression**, which is structurally identical in every document and serves as the master example of how cross-level synchronization should work. The most divergent dimension is the **49% SPARK statistic** and **Pre-K calendar language**, which are deliberately allowed in L1, allowed (with citation) in L2, and banned by name in L3 and GF — a correct asymmetry, but one whose violation is unforgivable on any cross-level email that does not respect the destination level's voice. Below is the full audit.

## 3. Cross-Level Conflict List

### HIGH SEVERITY (8)

#### H1. The 49% SPARK Stat Is the Largest Cross-Level Landmine

- **L1 §2, §7, §12:** 49% SPARK stat appears in 70%+ of L1 emails. Wiggins et al. 2022 cited. Framing changes by phase (introduction, reframe, identity, normalization, recap, regression, refresh).
- **L2 §9, §12.1 E5, §18.1 Cross-Flow Threads:** 49% Wiggins / SPARK appears in callout boxes — one of the 11 cross-flow threads. Explicitly named autism ("49% of autistic 4–5 year olds are not toilet trained. You are not behind.").
- **L3 §11.4 FAQ E4, §16 Cardinal Sins #9, §20.1 Stage 2 Checklist:** Math reframe including "less than a coffee a day" is FORBIDDEN. SPARK 49% may appear in PP-Education D14 / FAQ E1 (per L3 §11.4) but NOT in GF or in subject lines.
- **GF §11 Forbidden Phrases, §31 Death Spirals #1, §34 V16 Rule:** "49% SPARK stat — NEVER used in GF copy because it names autism explicitly." Instead: "almost 1 in 2 kids with nervous system differences aren't trained by age 5."

**Migration risk:** A Group A or Group D subscriber migrates from GF to L1 at Day 14 PP-Level Detection (Button 1: "sits 20 minutes, nothing happens"). Her inbox shifts from the safe "almost 1 in 2 kids with nervous system differences" (GF) to "49% of autistic 4-5 year olds" (L1). For a Group A Diagnostic Entrant, this shift is welcome — it confirms what she suspected. For a Group D who clicked past the pop-up to avoid labeling, this stat may read as confrontational.

**Resolution:** Standardize the parenthetical framing on the stat: *"49% of autistic 4–5 year olds are not toilet trained (SPARK, Wiggins et al. 2022)* — *or as we sometimes frame it for parents who aren't using clinical language: about 1 in 2 kids with nervous-system differences."* This way the citation is honest, but the bridge to a less-labeled frame is offered for the post-migration customer.

#### H2. The Mary S. Story Opener Differs Across Levels

- **L1 §9.6:** "I started BrightKidCo because my own child couldn't feel the signal..."
- **L2 §13.5:** Same opener, plus "we worked with our BCBA, not against her" + "it took us 9 months."
- **L3 §10.1:** **EXPLICITLY BANS** this opener: *"'Why I started this. — Mary S.' (NOT 'I started BrightKidCo because my own child couldn't feel the signal...' — that reads as marketing)."*
- **GF §18:** Uses the L1/L2 opener; explicitly states "Mary S. is the brand's GF archetype — 'didn't know what label to use' parent."

**Migration risk:** A GF-tagged customer who received "I started BrightKidCo because my own child couldn't feel the signal..." in PP-Mary S. Story (D3 PP) and then migrates to L3 at any post-purchase migration point will receive a NEW Mary S. Story — but **per L3 §10.1, Mary S. Story is one-time and does not re-fire**. So the customer will *not* re-receive a different opener. However, if the customer had NOT yet received Mary S. Story (purchased at D7, say, before Mary S. fired at D3+), and migrates at PP-Level Detection (D14) to L3, she will receive the L3 variant opener — a tonal upgrade she did not request.

**Resolution:** L3's opener ("Why I started this. — Mary S.") is a strict upgrade in dignity. The brand should consider adopting L3's opener as the **brand-wide standard** for Mary S. Story. The "I started BrightKidCo because my own child couldn't feel the signal..." line is on the borderline of L3's forbidden "marketing dialect" and the more dignified version is safer cross-level. Alternatively, define two Mary S. Story variants (L1/L2 opener + L3 opener) and have the GF architecture route customers to the appropriate opener based on destination level post-migration.

#### H3. Pre-K Calendar Language Is Banned in 3 of 4 Levels but Permitted in L1

- **L1 §7.5, §8.5, §15:** Pre-K calendar anchors are allowed in subjects ("Six weeks until Pre-K starts," "Pre-K starts in 12 weeks. Here's the math," "It's that time of year again"). L1-only.
- **L2 §6.1, §9 Forbidden Phrases, §22 Visual Calibration:** "Pre-K is coming!" / direct Pre-K urgency is FORBIDDEN. "School adapted via IEP" — the school/home paradox.
- **L3 §16 Cardinal Sins, §20.1:** "The 'pre-K frame' anti-pattern: Any L1-style Pre-K urgency... in an L3 email is an instant trust break."
- **GF §11 Forbidden Phrases, §15 Welcome E3, §34:** "Pre-K urgency FORBIDDEN in GF (L1-only allowance)."

**Migration risk:** A Group A customer whose child is on the Pre-K trajectory and who clicked Button 1 at Day 14 has been receiving GF Welcome E1–E6 (Pre-K forbidden) for 14 days. Her inbox has had no Pre-K framing. Now she receives L1 Welcome E6 (Pre-K bridge) and beyond. **The shift from "no Pre-K" to "Pre-K math" is the *intended* unlock of migration — it's what she signed up for.** This is not a bug, but it requires that the L1 copy at Welcome E6 acknowledge that she's coming from a non-Pre-K frame: "By now you've read about the mechanism. Here's how the Pre-K deadline interacts with it..."

**Resolution:** Add a **migration-bridge paragraph** (~50 words) at the top of L1's Welcome E6 for any subscriber who migrated from GF (i.e., a flag `migrated_from=gf` set at PP-Level Detection click time). The bridge acknowledges she's coming from a no-Pre-K frame and reframes Pre-K math as one possible use-case, not the only use-case. This softens the tonal jump.

#### H4. Brand-Green CTA Saturation Drops Sharply on Migration to L2/L3

- **L1 §12 Dimension 8, §19 L1 Hard Rules:** Full-width, Brand-Green #039902 at **full saturation**, white text, 48px+ height.
- **L2 §11 CTA Style, §21 Visual Calibration:** Muted green (desaturated #039902 hue), **NOT full-width**, 44px section gaps.
- **L3 §14.2 CTA Button Spec (locked):** **NEVER use bright green #039902 as CTA.** Muted gray or muted brown; auto-width, never full-width; no shadow; soft border-radius 8-12px.
- **GF §12 Visual Calibration, §34 Design Canvas:** Brand Green #039902 at **80–100% saturation** for primary CTA (variable by sub-group — full for A/E, reduced for C/D).

**Migration risk:** A GF customer (Group A) whose CTA button has been full-saturation green at 80–100% for 14 days migrates to L3 at Day 14. From D14 onward, every CTA she clicks is muted brown or muted gray, auto-width, no shadow. This is a **dramatic visual shift** that can read as "the brand has cooled on me" — the opposite of the intended trust-deepening. L3's visual restraint is correct for L3-native customers; for post-migration customers, it can feel like the brand has changed personalities.

**Resolution:** Define a **migration-aware CTA gradient**: post-migration from GF to L3, the customer's first two L3 emails should use a **medium-saturation muted green** (between GF's 80–100% green and L3's muted brown) to soften the transition. By L3 email #3 (D22+ for migrated Group D, equivalent to D14 if migrated late), the customer is fully on the L3 visual palette. The same gradient applies to L2 migration (start with desaturated green matching L2 spec, not full-saturation like GF).

#### H5. The Three-Family Pattern Includes Levels Names by Default

- **L1 §9.9 PP-Mid E1:** "Three-Family Testimonial (Sarah/Lisa/Maria) + Lena reply invitation."
- **L2 §14.1 PP-Mid Check-In E1:** "Three-Family Testimonial (Sarah / Lisa-customer / Maria)."
- **L3 §10.4 PP-Mid D30:** "Three-Family Pattern with Maria's story as the L3 anchor."
- **GF §15 Welcome E6, §20 PP-Level Detection:** Three-Family Pattern is symptom-described without level labels. "Universal cross-level stories. The Three-Family Pattern (one L1-style, one L2-style, one L3-style story, all symptom-described). No level labels."

**Migration risk:** A GF customer who has received the symptom-described Three-Family Pattern in Welcome E6 (or in Cart E2) — without level names — migrates to L1. Her next PP-Mid E1 (D30) is the L1 version with explicit level labels (Sarah/Lisa/Maria). The level names appear for the first time in her inbox, attached to stories she's already read. The level labels themselves are not forbidden in L1 (they're just internal Three-Family Pattern markers used in the testimonial library), but they should not appear in the subject line or body copy in a way that re-introduces the level hierarchy she just opted into.

**Resolution:** Verify that L1/L2/L3 PP-Mid E1 emails do NOT use the words "Level 1 / Level 2 / Level 3" in subject lines or visible body copy — only in internal copy briefs. The Three-Family Pattern is an internal testimonial-routing tool, not a customer-facing label.

#### H6. The L1 Welcome E5 Uses Shape G (Urgency-Driven) at D10 — A Cadence Conflict with L2/L3/GF E5

- **L1 §7.5 Welcome E5:** 1,186 words, **Shape G (Urgency-Driven)** with Mechanism deep middle. Pre-K math + 3-layer product education. Mixed rhythm.
- **L2 §12.1 Welcome E5 (D10):** "Mahler, Wiggins, SPARK — and what they tell us." Science deep-dive. Pure mechanism education. No product pitch. Read-only CTA. NOT Pattern G.
- **L3 §8 Welcome E5 (D10):** "The smallest signs. They count." Welcome trust peak. Mini-wins reframing. NOT Pattern G.
- **GF §15 Welcome E5 (D10):** Interoception deep dive, Pattern F (Education-First) + mechanism teaser. NOT Pattern G.

**Same-week planning conflict:** L1 alone uses Pattern G at Welcome E5 (D10). L2, L3, and GF all use Pattern F (Education-First). This is the ONLY Welcome email where the four levels diverge in section pattern. If a customer has a sub-folder showing all four E5 sends side-by-side (rare, but happens in lifecycle QA), L1's urgency register reads as off-key.

**Migration risk:** Moderate. A Group D customer who migrates from GF to L1 at Day 14 has just received GF Welcome E5 (Pattern F, education-first, no urgency) two days earlier. She migrates, then receives L1 Welcome E6 (Pattern D, Social Proof) on D14 — fine. The L1 E5 doesn't reach her because she's past it. But if a Group A customer receives GF Welcome E5 (Pattern F) and then L1 PP-Extended Ed E1-E5 — those are also Pattern A / education-first. The urgency register does not return.

**Resolution:** L1's Welcome E5 should be moved from Pattern G to **Pattern C (Problem-Solution) with calendar bridge** — the calendar math is allowed (L1-specific) but the urgency pattern itself should match the system. Pattern G should be reserved for L1 Welcome E7 ("Last Call") where the WELCOME10 expiration is the actual urgency.

#### H7. The D14 Cross-Flow Collision Differs in Resolution Between GF and L1/L2

- **L1 §17 Cross-Flow D14:** "Welcome E6 + PP-Ed D14 (early buyers) — PP-Ed D14 takes priority (customer is post-purchase)."
- **L2 §18 Cross-Flow:** No specific D14 collision mentioned; L2 is already tagged, never receives PP-Level Detection.
- **L3 §12 Cross-Flow Priority Hierarchy:** D14 not specifically flagged; standard priority chain applies.
- **GF §39 Day 14 Inflection (THE BIG ONE):** "PP-Level Detection is HIGHEST. Welcome GF E6 + PP-Education D0-21 E3 + Replenishment A1 ALL SUPPRESSED for GF purchasers." Three-to-four-way collision; PP-Level Detection wins.

**Same-customer-different-level conflict:** When a Group D customer migrates at PP-Level Detection (D14), her migration event *itself* suppresses multiple flows. From D14 forward, she is L1/L2/L3-tagged and the cross-flow priorities reset to the level's rules. But during the D14 transition window (the 24 hours when PP-Level Detection fires), the customer's inbox is processed by the GF architecture — even though, post-click, she's tagged as L1/L2/L3. If the implementation is not atomic, she could receive both PP-Level Detection (GF architecture) AND Welcome L1/L2/L3 E6 (level architecture) within the same window.

**Resolution:** Document a **migration atomicity rule**: when a customer clicks any button on PP-Level Detection, the tag update MUST be processed before any subsequent email decision is made. From the click moment onward, all flow decisions use the new tag. This is operationally critical — a 1-hour race condition could produce a customer receiving both PP-Level Detection and a level-specific Welcome E6 within hours, which would be alarming to a Group D customer who just experienced the catharsis of self-identifying.

#### H8. The Cart Abandonment Suppression Rule Treats L1's E3+ and GF's E3+ Differently

- **L1 §7.3, §17:** "Welcome E3 — SUPPRESSED if cart is still active."
- **L2 §12.3 Cart Abandonment:** "Cart Abandonment SUPPRESSES Welcome E3+ during active cart."
- **L3 §9.2 Cart Abandonment:** "Cart Abandonment received post-warming; suppression rules apply."
- **GF §16 Flow 02:** "Suppresses Welcome GF E3+ during active cart."

**Same-customer-different-level conflict:** If a customer is in L1's Welcome E3-suppressed state (cart active), she is functionally receiving only Cart E1-E2/E3 + Browse E1-E2/E3. After she converts (Cart E3 → purchase), she transitions to Order Confirmation + Shipping + PP-Education. There's no migration path from this suppression state to a different level. But: **if a Group D customer has cart active AND browses during the cart window, she receives both Cart E1 (mechanism reminder, R2 anchors) and Browse E1 (mechanism-light education).** Both are L1-aware but for Group D the L1 framing may not yet be appropriate.

**Resolution:** Add a per-level Cart Abandonment variant for migrated customers. A Group D who migrates at Day 14 and then later has a 2nd cart (e.g., 5+5 upgrade) should receive the L1 Cart E1-E3 if she migrated to L1, L2 if to L2, etc. **Verify that the existing level-aware content blocks already handle this** — per L2 §13.4 PP-Direct Upsell: "Level-aware content blocks IF tag is known." Confirm this logic extends to Cart/Checkout/Browse for post-migration customers.

## 9. The One Paragraph — How the 4 Levels Work Together

The 4 levels work together because they share a **single gravitational center** — the body-signal mechanism ("It's not behavior. It's signal-timing. GF is the gateway, not a destination: it exists to honor the 70%-confidence floor (Group B is permanent, Group C is permanent, Group A/D migrate), and PP-Level Detection at Day 14 is the moment a customer's lived experience is finally matched to her copy. When migration fires, the four levels behave as **one brand with four voices**, each voice tuned to a different hope/realism ratio and a different child profile, but all four tuned to the same mechanism, the same guarantee, and the same Lena-as-peer trust contract. The remaining work is to smooth the migration edges (H1-H8 above) so that the seam between voices is felt as deepening rather than redirection — and to verify, by code audit, that level-aware content blocks (Cart, Checkout, Browse, Replenishment, Winback, FAQ, PP-Extended Education) properly route to the destination level post-migration. The brand is **structurally sound** (sign-off perfect, transactional consistent, forbidden-phrase rules consistent, mechanism consistent, guarantee consistent) and **operationally distinct** (each level's voice is genuinely calibrated to its customer's emotional baseline). The score is 7.5/10 because the destination differentiation is working as designed, but the **bridges between destinations** need scaffolding. With the 8 HIGH-severity resolutions implemented, the sync score would rise to **9/10**, and the brand could confidently promise that "wherever you entered, wherever you migrate, BrightKidCo sounds like BrightKidCo."
