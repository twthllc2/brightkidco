# Flow Behavior — General Fallback (GF)
## Conversion Gates, Dead-Ends, Cross-Flow Continuity, and Migration Mechanics

> **Audience:** Stage 2/3 subagents and the lifecycle architect building operational logic on top of GF copy. The other four GF documents cover *who she is* (avatar), *what she receives* (sequence), and *how she feels* (emotional arc). This document covers **what each email is allowed to ask for, what happens if she walks away, what else is sitting in her inbox that week, and — uniquely to GF — when the tag itself changes**. GF is the only level with a migration touchpoint, and the only level where one email can simultaneously be the conversion lever for Group D, the dead-end for Groups B and C, and the cross-flow collision point for three other Day 14 sends.

> **Inputs:** `journey-gf-01-avatar.md` (5 sub-groups, V16, R1–R6, hope/realism baselines), `journey-gf-02-sequence.md` (22 flows, ~45 emails, PP-Level Detection), `journey-gf-03-emotional-trust.md` (per-sub-group trust deltas, peak moments, death spirals), `demands/GIULIANO-DEMANDS.md` §2.5 (cross-flow suppression), §14 V16 (symptom language only), §17 (deliverability + warming), §18.4 (PP-Level Detection mechanics).

## 0. EXECUTIVE FRAME — THE FOUR QUESTIONS PER EMAIL

For every email a GF-tagged subscriber (`avatar_general`) is scheduled to receive, this document answers four operational questions:

1. **CONVERSION GATE — what kind of ask is allowed?** GF's gate is **sub-group-differentiated**. Group A (Diagnostic Entrants, ~25%) tolerates "buy now" because she is hope-heavy (65/35) and label-tolerant. Group B (Diagnosis-Rejecters, ~15%) does not tolerate any "buy now" that mentions what she's buying *for*. Group C (Sensory-Difference, ~25%) tolerates mechanism-dense sales copy but no ASD framing. Group D (Overwhelmed Non-Selectors, ~25%) is realism-anchored and needs permission-not-to-buy. Group E (Cart/Browse, ~10%) tolerates discount-first framing because she never committed. The gate is the *brightest line each email can approach without tripping at least one sub-group's BS detector*.

2. **DEAD-END — what happens if she ignores this?** GF dead-ends are sub-group-differentiated. A Group B non-click is a *permanent GF resident* decision. A Group D non-click at PP-Level Detection is a *missed goldmine moment* (organic migration still possible later). A Group E non-click is the *most likely Sunset outcome*. Each email's dead-end describes what the system does **and what tag the customer carries** when the dust settles.

3. **CROSS-FLOW CONTINUITY — what else did she just get, and how must this email feel *distinct* from it?** GF's inbox is uniquely dense because she receives a level-specific Welcome variant *plus* every cross-level flow *plus* the GF-only PP-Level Detection. On Day 14, PP-Level Detection (Flow 09) AND Welcome E6 (Flow 01) AND PP-Education D0-21 E3 (Flow 11) AND Replenishment A1 (Flow 16) all try to fire. **PP-Level Detection wins the suppression chain for that day (§2.5).**

4. **MIGRATION MECHANICS — when does a GF email become an L1/L2/L3 email?** This is the **GF-unique fourth question**. Other levels don't ask it. The mechanics depend on the migration wave: Welcome-stage story link click (E2/E4/E5), Day 14 PP-Level Detection, PP-Extended Education story link (D22+), PP-Support At-Risk E3 soft link (D30+), or organic re-engagement. Once migration fires, *every subsequent email re-routes*. **Groups B and C never migrate** — they are permanent GF residents.

The reference frame: **§2.5 cross-flow suppression, §14 V16 (symptom language only), §17 deliverability/warming, and §18.4 PP-Level Detection mechanics** — together define max 1 flow email per subscriber per day, Cart Abandonment pauses Welcome E3+, Winback pauses nurture but allows transactional, PP-Level Detection at D14 takes priority over ALL other Day 14 content, Sunset is permanent exit, V16 forbids "autism/ASD/Level 1/2/3" for Group B, R1–R6 enforce symptom language / 3–5 anchors / age 3–10+ / verbal-nonverbal / timeline flexibility / low-bar metrics, 7-week warming with Lena-only from-address Weeks 1–6, 90-Day Engaged minimum, 120-day Sunset (0 opens AND 0 clicks AND 0 orders AND ≥20 emails), and §18.4 PP-Level Detection 70%-confidence floor with symptom-based buttons.

## 4. PP-LEVEL DETECTION — `pp-level-detection-09` (Day 14) — THE BIG LEVER

**Phase 1 state at trigger:** Day 14 post-purchase. Customer has `purchased = true` AND `level = avatar_general`. **This is the single most important email in the entire GF lifecycle.** Per §2.5 cross-flow suppression and §18.4, PP-Level Detection takes priority over ALL other Day 14 content — Welcome E6, PP-Education D0-21 E3, Replenishment A1.

**CONVERSION GATE:**
- **Hard CTA: NO.** Story links styled as text-link or low-emphasis button. **Lowest CTA aggression of any GF mail.** This mail does not sell. It listens.
- **No offer.** Pure detection.
- **Subject line symptom-framed:** "Some kids it clicks right away. Others it takes longer. Where's yours?" / "Three parents wrote in this month. Does any of this sound like your house?" / "Quick question — 14 days in, how's it going?"
- **Lena sign-off: SHORT variant.** "Talk soon," or "With understanding," — short.

**DEAD-END (sub-group-differentiated):**
- **Group A — Diagnostic Entrants (~25% of GF):** If she received a diagnosis between purchase and D14, she likely clicks Button 1 (L1 symptom profile) or Button 2 (L2). **Click → tag updates to `l1` or `l2`. Welcome GF E6/E7/E8 already suppressed. PP-Education D0-21 E3 SUPPRESSED. Resumes at E4 (Day 21) with level-aware content block. PP-Extended Education switches from GF themes to L1/L2 themes.**
- **Group B — Diagnosis-Rejecters (~15% of GF):** **The litmus test.** Buttons 1–3 imply the diagnosis she rejected. She does NOT click Buttons 1–3. She clicks Button 4 ("Still figuring out") or no button. **Tag remains `avatar_general` for life.** GF mail continues permanently.
- **Group C — Sensory-Difference (~25% of GF):** Buttons 1–3 imply ASD framing she does not accept. Clicks Button 4 or no button. **Tag remains `avatar_general` for life.**
- **Group D — Overwhelmed Non-Selectors (~25% of GF):** **THE GOLDMINE.** 40–50% click Buttons 1–3 and migrate. **Trust-after click: 9.5–10.0 (peak trust moment in entire lifecycle).** Click → tag updates to `l1`/`l2`/`l3`. Future flows re-route.
- **Group E — Cart/Browse (~10% of GF):** Revealed almost nothing. Clicks Button 4 most often, or no button. **Tag remains `avatar_general` for life** (or until organic migration later).
- **If customer does NOT click any button:** Single touchpoint fired. PP-Level Detection does NOT re-send at D21, D30, or D60. Migration becomes dependent on organic story link clicks in PP-Extended Education, PP-Support At-Risk E3, FAQ/Objection Library narrative prompts. **Estimated 5–10% organic migration post-PP-Level Detection vs 40–50% at the touchpoint itself.**

**CROSS-FLOW CONTINUITY — THE DAY 14 COLLISION:**
**What else fires at Day 14?**
1. **Welcome GF E6** — SUPPRESSED for purchasers per §2.5 + §18.4.
2. **PP-Education D0-21 E3 ("Week two reality")** — SUPPRESSED for non-migrated GF purchasers. Resumes at E4 (Day 21) with level-aware content if migrated, or with GF block if not migrated.
3. **Replenishment A1 (Day 14 wash cycles)** — per S01 §3.1, fires D14 for 1-pair buyers. **Suppressed for GF purchasers** because PP-Level Detection takes priority.
4. **PP-Education D0-21 E3** — same as above, suppressed.

**Why PP-Level Detection wins:** §2.5 priority chain places PP-Level Detection above ALL other Day 14 content. §18.4 confirms: "Takes priority over ALL other Day 14 content (Welcome E6, PP-Education, Replenishment)." For a GF purchaser, Day 14 is **one email day**, not three.

**MIGRATION MECHANICS — THE FULL FLOW:**
1. **Click Buttons 1–3:** Tag updates from `avatar_general` to `l1`/`l2`/`l3` per §18.4 70%-confidence rule (single click on a single story is sufficient signal given the same symptom language was used at pop-up).
2. **Welcome GF E6 already suppressed** — she does not receive it.
3. **PP-Education D0-21 E3 already suppressed** — she does not receive it.
4. **Welcome GF E7 and E8** — suppressed. She does not receive them.
5. **PP-Education D0-21 E4 (Day 21) resumes** with level-aware content block.
6. **PP-Extended Education (Flow 14, D22+)** switches from 5 GF themes to 5 L1/L2/L3 themes.
7. **PP-Extended Upsell (Flow 12), PP-Mid Check-In (Flow 13), Replenishment (Flow 16), Review Request (Flow 17), Winback A (Flow 18), FAQ/Objection Library (Flow 20), Referral (Flow 21), Sunset (Flow 22)** all use level-aware copy from D22+ onward.
8. **No retroactive mail re-send.** She does NOT receive Welcome L1/L2/L3 retroactively. Her Welcome GF sequence is what it was.
9. **Click Button 4:** Tag remains `avatar_general`. GF mail continues permanently. Migration opportunities remain in PP-Extended Education story links, PP-Support At-Risk E3, FAQ narrative prompts.
10. **No click:** Tag remains `avatar_general`. Migration becomes organic-only.

**Cumulative Migration Math (per §2.5 + journey-gf-02 §5.5):**

## 10. QUICK-REFERENCE DECISION CARD

When in doubt about any GF email's operational behavior, ask these 6 questions in order:

1. **Does it pass V16?** Could Group B (Diagnosis-Rejecters) read this without encountering "autism," "ASD," or "Level 1/2/3"? If no, rewrite.
2. **Does it satisfy R1–R6?** Symptom over label (R1), 3–5 anchors (R2), age 3–10+ (R3), both verbal modes (R4), flexible timeline (R5), low-bar metrics (R6).
3. **What is the conversion gate for THIS sub-group?** A tolerates "buy now." B needs guarantee + permission. C needs mechanism. D needs realism. E needs discount.
4. **What happens if she ignores this?** Cross-level fires regardless (no pre-purchase At-Risk). Post-purchase At-Risk fires at 3–21 days no open. Sunset at D120+.
5. **What else is firing same day?** Welcome E6 + PP-Ed E3 + Replenishment A1 all collide at D14 — PP-Level Detection wins. PP-Mid E1 + Replenishment A2 + Review E1 collide at D30 — three distinct framings.
6. **Is this a migration touchpoint?** Welcome E2/E4/E5 anchors + PP-Level Detection D14 + PP-Extended Ed story links + PP-Support At-Risk E3 + FAQ E6 + Winback Theme 3 = migration moments. Everywhere else, no migration.

If all 6 pass, the mail is GF-ready AND operationally clean. If any fail, fix before send.
